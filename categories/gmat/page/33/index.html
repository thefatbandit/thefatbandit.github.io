12.1 Introduction

Lesson Content Unlike many other types of Critical Reasoning questions, Method of Reasoning questions focus on how an argument is structured, not on the merits of the argument. In other words, when we consider the stimulus of a Method of Reasoning question, we don’t need to focus on whether the argument is sound or convincing or weak. 💡MUST KNOW: When solving a Method of Reasoning question, our job is to focus on how the argument is organized and analyze the argument’s structure.

12.2 Two-Part Method of Reasoning Questions

Lesson Content Many Method of Reasoning questions are based on dialogues between two speakers. Each part of the dialogue is considered a separate argument, generally with its own evidence, assumption(s), and conclusion(s). The first speaker’s argument may or may not be sound. On the other hand, the second speaker may respond effectively to the first speaker. The two speakers could believe that they agree (or disagree), when in fact they are talking about different things.
