## Question Format

Although William Pereira first gained national recognition for his movie set designs, *including those for Reap the Wild Wind and Jane Eyre, future generations remember* him as the architect of the Transamerica Tower, the Malibu campus of Pepperdine University, and the city of Irvine.

(A) including those for Reap the Wild Wind and Jane Eyre, future generations remember

(B) like that for Reap the Wild Wind and Jane Eyre, future generations will remember

(C) like those for Reap the Wild Wind and Jane Eyre, future generations remembered

(D) including that for Reap the Wild Wind and Jane Eyre, future generations remembered

(E) including those for Reap the Wild Wind and Jane Eyre, future generations will remember

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡 **Option (A)** is the ***original sentence*** always.
20% of the time the answer is (A). So we shouldn’t neglect it completely.
{{< /alert >}}

## Q Timing (40s - 80s)

Guess on the remaining options beyond the 2 min mark.

## Step 2: Read for meaning

- Keep an eye on both grammar and meaning issues.
- **Objective:** To spot overall issues with meaning or sentence structure
- Identify the subject, verb, preposition, conjunctions & participles. Don’t have to identify grammar function of each word through.

**Eg:** *“Anne and Millie went to the movies in her car.”*

This sentence is grammatically correct. But doesn’t convey the meaning clearly as to who’s car did they go in. 

#### Step 2.5 Predict Answer

- Predict the correction in the answer choice.
- Might not be possible at all times but should be tried since it saves a lot of time.

{{< alert type="danger" >}}
🚨 <u>DO NOT</u> READ THE 1ST ANSWER CHOICE
{{< /alert >}}

### Step 3: Finding A Starting Point

An SC question will be testing us on multiple fronts and will have many discrepancy points that could be identified. 

There are likely 2 starting points:

1. An error (or suspected error) in the original sentence
2. Splits/differences in the answers

Ensure that there is at least 1 split for the suspected issue.

### Step 4: Eliminate All Incorrect Choices

During practice, if we’re down to 2 answer choices, we should simply pick of the two and move on.

💡🚨 But during its review, make the following considerations:

- Why is the right answer right? Why are each of the 4 others wrong?
- How would someone mistakenly justify eliminating the right answer?
- How would someone mistakenly justify picking any of the wrong answers?

{{< alert type="danger" >}}
🚨 ***DO NOT*** go for shorter answer always. Focus on grammar & meaning 

(Most of the times concision errors have a ***meaning/idiom error***)
{{< /alert >}}

### Step 5: Repeat

{{< alert type="danger" >}}
🚨 The correct answer doesn’t necessarily have to sound perfect to us too
{{< /alert >}}

### Step 6: Read the Answer Choice back into the sentence

This will help you decide which answer communicated the meaning of the sentence most effectively.

## When Stumped

- Read the answers back into the sentence, again.
- Shorter answers are generally better.
- Strike-off passive voice options. They are seldom the right answer.
- Avoid answers with redundancy.
- Answers with ***“being”*** are to be avoided.
