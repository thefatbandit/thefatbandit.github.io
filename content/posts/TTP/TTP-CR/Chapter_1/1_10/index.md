## Lesson Content
When answering a Critical Reasoning question, even if we think that we have found the correct answer, we should go through all the five choices before choosing a correct answer.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Even if we think that we have found the correct answer, we should go through all the five options before choosing a correct answer.
{{< /alert >}}

Notice that Critical Reasoning question stems ask us to find the “best” choice among the five choices provided.

For example, Critical Reasoning question stems read:

> “MOST seriously weakens”

> “provides the MOST support”

> “BEST explains”

> “is MOST strongly supported by”

Therefore, even if we have found a choice that we think answers the question, there may be in the remaining choices another choice that answers the question in a much better way.

That said, to avoid any misconception, we’d like to say that our extensive research into Critical Reasoning questions has shown that, although the questions ask us to find the “best’ answer,” there will be only one truly correct answer for every question, and the difference between the correct answer and the “runner-up” will be significant.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Critical Reasoning questions, there will be only one truly correct answer for every question, and the difference between the correct answer and the “runner-up” will be significant.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s now talk about what to do if we’ve eliminated all of the answer choices.
