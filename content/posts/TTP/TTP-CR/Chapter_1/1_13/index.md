## Lesson Content
Many students have learned a gimmicky strategy for answering Critical Reasoning questions: read the question passage, read the question stem, and then “pre-think” what the correct answer will be.

Let’s make this very simple – do not pre-think.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Do not “pre-think” answers by attempting to read the question passage, read the question stem, and then “pre-think” what the correct answer will be. Doing so will likely limit your ability to become skilled at answering Critical Reasoning questions.
{{< /alert >}}

Here’s why pre-thinking doesn’t serve us well.

First, pre-thinking takes time.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Pre-think? Nah - you are not playing an elementary school game; you’re seeking to get into business school.
{{< /alert >}}

Second, even though we may be well aware that a pre-thought answer may not turn out to be the correct answer to a question, having a pre-thought answer in mind when we go through the answer choices can distract us and hamper our ability to find the correct answer.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Often, a test-taker who pre-thinks goes through all the answer choices in a question looking for a pre-thought answer only to have to go through all the choices again when none of the choices matches the pre-thought answer.
{{< /alert >}}

Finally, the test-maker has massive amounts of data on the mistakes that we are likely to make if we pre-think answer choices, and this data very surely is used to create trap answer choices.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When we pre-think, we’re liable to be affected by one of a number of extremely well-researched cognitive biases, and choose trap choices.
{{< /alert >}}

TTP also has large amounts of data on how students incorrectly “pre-think” the answers to the questions, and yes, to better prepare you, we’ve used this data to engineer trap answer choices in our Critical Reasoning questions.

So, pre-thinking can make it significantly harder for us to arrive at the correct answers to Critical Reasoning questions and, at the same time, make it significantly easier to fall into a trap.

💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Let’s now discuss another gimmicky strategy not to use, eliminating or choosing an answer choice on the basis of the presence of one word.
