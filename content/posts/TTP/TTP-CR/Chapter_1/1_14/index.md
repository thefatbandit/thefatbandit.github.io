## Lesson Content
Since a single word in a Critical Reasoning answer choice can have a significant effect on the meaning conveyed by that choice, people often get the impression that it makes sense to eliminate or choose Critical Reasoning answer choices because of the presence of particular words in the choices.

For instance, since answer choices that use words such as “all,” “none,” “always,” or “never” state things that are extreme, people often use the strategy of eliminating any choice that uses such a word, terming such choices “extreme choices.” 

Another word the presence of which people sometimes feel constitutes a reason to eliminate a choice is the word “some.” People eliminate choices that include “some” because they assume that a choice that uses “some” says something too vague to be a correct answer.

The issue with using such an approach is that, while it is true that “extreme choices” and some other types of choices that people often eliminate because of the presence of a particular word are more often than not incorrect choices, such choices are not always incorrect.

They aren’t mastering Critical Reasoning; they are playing the odds, and the results they get reflect what they are doing.

So, what should we do instead?

Rather than eliminate a choice because it uses a particular word, we can suspect that a choice is incorrect because it uses a particular word and carefully determine whether, in using that word, the choice does or does not do what the correct answer to the question must do.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Eliminating Critical Reasoning answer choices on the basis of the presence of single words is an unreliable, low quality approach to answering Critical Reasoning questions. For reliable results and high scores, we have to carefully evaluate the effect of every Critical Reasoning answer choice regardless of what words it uses.
{{< /alert >}}

Remember, Critical Reasoning questions, and GMAT verbal in general, test the level of sophistication of the thinking we use in answering the questions, and eliminating a choice because the choice uses a certain word is not a sophisticated method; it’s a gimmick.

Let’s now discuss another move we should never make in answering a Critical Reasoning question.
