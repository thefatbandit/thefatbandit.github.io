## Lesson Content
Many people get the impression that they should robotically eliminate any Critical Reasoning answer choice that brings in new information that is different from what is discussed in the passage.

> <em>“The author never said anything about ants in the argument. ”</em>

> <em>“This answer choice brings in new information. ”</em>

> <em>“What does the Mediterranean Sea have to do with Lake Michigan?”</em>

> <em>“This answer choice seems off-topic. ”</em>

> <em>“This answer choice came out of nowhere. ”</em>

> <em>“This answer choice is out of scope. ” </em>

Sometimes, eliminating an answer choice that brings in information that was not discussed in the passage does make sense.

However, the truth of the matter is that many correct answers to Critical Reasoning questions bring in new information that is rather different from anything said by the passage, and if we eliminate choices just because they bring in new information, we won’t maximize our scores.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Many correct answers to Critical Reasoning questions bring in new information that is different from anything said by the passage.
{{< /alert >}}

For example, correct answers to Weaken the Argument and Strengthen the Argument questions – the two most common types of Critical Reasoning questions – often bring in information that is quite different from anything said by the passages.

Therefore, we cannot eliminate an answer choice simply because it brings in new information that is different from what is discussed in the passage.

We’ll talk more about this idea later, but to get off to a good start, just remember that we cannot eliminate an answer choice simply because it brings in new information that is different from what is discussed in the passage.

##  Example 6 

Over the past year, brown bears that inhabit the nearby mountains have been coming into the Town of Silverton with greater regularity than ever before.

Which of the following, if true, most seriously weakens the argument?

1. [x] (A) One year ago, a number of farms in the town began for the first time growing blueberries, which are a favorite food of brown bears.
2. [ ] (B) People living in the town have grown increasingly nervous because of the increased presence of the brown bears.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) Over the past year, no more mountain lions have been sighted in the town than were sighted during previous years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) The owners of the town’s honeybee farms have reported seeing brown bears opening their beehives to eat the honey found within.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Brown bears find honey irresistible because it is high in both sugar and calories, two things that they require in order to maintain a healthy weight.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

##### Question Level: easy



Let’s break down the argument.


<u>Background</u>: Over the past year, brown bears that inhabit the nearby mountains have been coming into the Town of Silverton with greater regularity than ever before.


<u>Premise</u>: About a year ago, three farms in the town began for the first time producing honey, which brown bears find irresistible.


<u>Conclusion</u>: It must be that the reason why brown bears are coming into the town with greater regularity is that they are coming to consume the honey produced by these farms.


The question asks, “Which of the following, if true, most seriously weakens the argument?”


So, our goal is to weaken the support for the conclusion that bears are coming into the town more regularly now because of the honey being produced there. Anything that casts some doubt on this conclusion will weaken the argument.


We need to keep in mind that we won’t disregard an answer choice simply because it presents new information different from anything the passage says. In other words, even if an answer choice presents information different from anything the passage says, if that answer choice weakens the argument, it will be correct.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) One year ago, a number of farms in the town began for the first time growing blueberries, which are a favorite food of brown bears.
</b>


 Many people just starting to learn about Critical Reasoning quickly eliminate this answer choice. They say, “<em>This is an argument about honey. Blueberries have nothing to do with honey. Therefore, what this answer choice says is out of scope.</em>”


Not so fast.


The conclusion is that the reason why brown bears are coming into the town with greater regularity is that they are coming to consume the honey that farms have started to produce.


How could we cast doubt on this claim? Couldn’t we do so by finding another plausible reason why brown bears are coming into the town with greater regularity? Definitely, right?


Notice that, although this answer choice is about blueberries, something not mentioned in the passage, what this answer choice says absolutely weakens the argument, because it brings up an alternative possible reason why the brown bears are coming into the town with greater regularity.


In other words, the author claims that it is the honey. This answer choice casts doubt on that claim by bringing up the possibility that it might not be the honey at all; it might just be the blueberries!


> <b>(B) People living in the town have grown increasingly nervous because of the increased presence of the brown bears.
</b>


 Although what this answer choice says is very plausible given what the passage says, the fact that people living in the town have grown increasingly nervous because of the increased presence of the brown bears does not weaken the argument.


In other words, even if what this answer choice says is true, it still makes sense to conclude that the reason why bears are coming into the town is that they are coming to consume honey.


> <b>(C) Over the past year, no more mountain lions have been sighted in the town than were sighted during previous years.
</b>


 Notice that this answer choice is quite different from answer choice (A). Whereas choice (A) brings up new information, and that new information weakens the argument, this choice brings up new information, and that new information does NOT weaken the argument.


After all, even if, over the past year, no more mountain lions have been sighted in the town than were sighted during previous years, that fact does not cast doubt on the author’s claim about the brown bears and the honey. In fact, if anything, the fact that bears, which find honey irresistible, are coming into the town more, while mountain lions do not seem to be coming into the town more, tends to confirm that the presence of the honey is the reason for the change in the bears’ behavior.


So, yes, we will eliminate this answer choice. Notice, however, that we are not eliminating it because it brings in new information. We’re eliminating it because what it says does not weaken the argument.


> <b>(D) The owners of the town’s honeybee farms have reported seeing brown bears opening their beehives to eat the honey found within.
</b>


 What this answer choice says is in line with the conclusion of the argument. The fact that the owners of farms in the town have reported seeing brown bears opening their beehives to eat honey fits perfectly with the conclusion that the reason why brown bears are coming into the town with greater regularity is that they are coming to consume the honey produced by the farms. Therefore, what this answer choice says certainly does not weaken the argument.


> <b>(E) Brown bears find honey irresistible because it is high in both sugar and calories, two things that they require in order to maintain a healthy weight.
</b>


 What this answer choice says explains a premise in the argument.


Notice that the passage says that “brown bears find honey irresistible. ”


So, what this choice does is simply explain why brown bears find honey irresistible. They find it irresistible because it is high in both sugar and calories, two things that they require in order to remain at a healthy weight.


What this answer choice says certainly does not weaken the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
