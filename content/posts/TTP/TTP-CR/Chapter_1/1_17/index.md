## Lesson Content
Test-takers often miss Critical Reasoning questions for reasons that were predictable and avoidable.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A key aspect of improving Critical Reasoning performance is discovering and then addressing habits that lead to choosing incorrect answers.
{{< /alert >}}

Look at the below list of common reasons people miss Critical Reasoning questions that they could have gotten correct.

> I missed important details in the passage.

> I failed to read the entire passage.

> I failed to read entirely an answer choice that I eliminated / chose.

> I attempted to answer the question without fully understanding the passage.

> I chose an answer that answered a question different from the question asked.

> I failed to note / lost track of the conclusion.

> I eliminated the correct answer because it seemed irrelevant.

> I attempted to pre-think the answer choice.

> I made up a convoluted, unsupported story to support my choice.

> I chose a trap choice because it was worded to sound like what the passage says.

> I chose a trap choice because it seemed to have the effect I was looking for.

> I ran out of time.

> I did not keep all of the information straight.

Each time you get a question incorrect that you should have gotten correct, it’s extremely important to make a note of why you got the question incorrect.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The TTP Course makes it easy for you to log your errors. Each time you get a question wrong, tell the course why. Then, we’ll create a powerful error log for you to study with.
{{< /alert >}}

Your goal is to track and recognize all of the reasons why you miss Critical Reasoning questions and then address those reasons to maximize your Critical Reasoning performance.

Let’s now discuss the fact that we don’t need any specialized, outside knowledge to answer critical reasoning questions.
