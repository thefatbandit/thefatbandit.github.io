## Lesson Content
Correctly answering Critical Reasoning questions does not require any outside, specialized knowledge.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Correctly answering Critical Reasoning questions does not require any outside, specialized knowledge. Any outside information necessary for answering these questions is considered “common knowledge” among people with common sense and a basic level of education.
{{< /alert >}}

In other words, yes, we will encounter passages on economics, science, music theory, politics, and psychology, to name a few topics, but any outside information we’ll need to answer Critical Reasoning questions will be information that is considered generally known, and widely accepted, by the general population.

For example, we might need to know that profit = the money taken in by selling something – the cost of selling that thing, but we would never be required to know the relationship between mortgage interest rates and bond yields.

For example, we might need to know that depression is a condition that can involve a person’s feeling unhappy, but we would never need to know that depression can be caused by the environment or food choices.

Realizing that we are not expected to have any outside, specialized knowledge to answer these questions can help us a lot.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Any answer choice that would require our having advanced or specialized knowledge in any particular field to choose that answer choice as the correct answer can’t be the correct answer to a Critical Reasoning question.
{{< /alert >}}

Now, that all said, Critical Reasoning questions do often discuss themes, concepts, plans, and processes that are well-outside of the range of what is considered common knowledge, but still, any specialized knowledge that we might need to correctly answer the question will be contained within the four corners of the question; we won’t be required to have that specialized knowledge committed to memory.

If Critical Reasoning questions don’t require any specialized, outside knowledge, then why are Critical Reasoning questions challenging? Well, what’s challenging is the logical reasoning we have to do in order to answer them.

Let’s now talk about timing and Critical Reasoning questions.
