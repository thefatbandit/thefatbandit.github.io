## Lesson Content
The verbal section of the GMAT allots 65 minutes for answering 36 questions, or an average time of 1.8 minutes per question (about 1 minute and 48 seconds per question).

Of course, if you’ll need to average 1.8 minutes per question and yet may spend 2 minutes per Critical Reasoning question, where will the extra time come from? In general, Sentence Corrections take less time, on average, than Critical Reasoning questions.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

On test day you’ll likely spend about 1.75 to 2 minutes on each Critical Reasoning question. However, some of the more difficult Critical Reasoning questions may take you up to three minutes to answer.
{{< /alert >}}

That said, answering Critical Reasoning questions when you are taking the GMAT and practicing answering Critical Reasoning questions when you are preparing to take the GMAT are two different activities.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When you are practicing, you should answer the Critical Reasoning questions you see untimed.
{{< /alert >}}

This information is very important.

No matter what you have heard from anyone else about how to practice Critical Reasoning, <strong>do Critical Reasoning practice questions untimed, focus on understanding and accuracy, and let speed come with skill.</strong>

One of your main aims in practicing Critical Reasoning is to learn to see and clearly articulate what makes incorrect choices incorrect and correct answers correct.

As is the case with other types of GMAT questions, you won’t learn to succeed in Critical Reasoning by getting questions incorrect in two minutes each.

Let’s now talk about the fact that Critical Reasoning questions have been consistent over time.
