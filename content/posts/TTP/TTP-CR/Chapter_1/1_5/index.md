## Lesson Content
The conclusion of the argument is the sentence that states the argument’s point of view.

An author’s conclusion is always a statement of opinion.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The conclusion of the argument is the sentence that states the argument’s point of view. An author’s conclusion is always a statement of opinion. In other words, whenever an author sets forth a conclusion, she’s presenting what she believes to be true.
{{< /alert >}}

Conclusions come in the form of ideas, viewpoints, recommendations, suggestions, and plans of action, to name a few.

Consider the following simple argument:

> <em>Critical Reasoning is like a game. Games are fun. Critical Reasoning must be fun.</em>

The conclusion of the above argument is "Critical Reasoning must be fun." It's the main point of the argument, the part of the argument that the other parts lead up to.

Authors often use specific keywords to indicate to the reader that a conclusion is about to follow.

<em>Therefore…, In conclusion…, So…, Clearly…, Hence…, This shows that…, It can be concluded that…, Should…, Ought to…, Must…, As a result…, Thus…, Consequently…, As a consequence…, Accordingly…, It follows that…, For this reason…</em>

For example, consider the following conclusions that could be a part of Critical Reasoning questions:

> <strong><em>Therefore</em></strong><em>, the government of Verdantia should lower interest rates immediately.</em>

> <strong><em>In conclusion</em></strong><em>, plant-based diets are good for people and for the environment.</em>

> <strong><em>So</em></strong><em>, the city of Redville would be well advised to increase the number of miles of bicycle lanes found on city roadways. </em>

> <strong><em>Clearly</em></strong><em>, the new university study is flawed. </em>

> <strong><em>Hence</em></strong><em>, most people would benefit from increasing the amount of time they spend exercising each day. </em>

> <strong><em>This finding shows that</em></strong><em> Great White Sharks are not the solitary animals that scientists once suspected them to be. </em>

> <strong><em>It can be concluded that</em></strong><em> the conservation efforts are making a positive impact on the prosperity of local residents.</em>

> <strong><em>It is for this reason that</em></strong><em> the Rhino is one of the most respected animals in Africa. </em>

> <strong><em>As a result</em></strong><em>, the nurses’ union should demand higher wages for its constituents.</em>

> <strong><em>Consequently</em></strong><em>, the economist recommended that the economy reopen. </em>

> <strong><em>Accordingly</em></strong><em>, the bridge connecting the two islands should be immediately closed to all pedestrian traffic.</em>

> <strong><em>As a consequence</em></strong><em>, the wolves will soon be returning to their homes in Yellowstone. </em>

These keywords often signal that what follows will indeed be a conclusion.

Also, although quite often an argument’s conclusion is found at the end of the passage, the truth is that it can be found anywhere within the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Although quite often an argument’s conclusion is found at the end of the passage, the truth is that it can be found anywhere within the passage. So, don’t expect the conclusion to always be the last sentence of a passage.
{{< /alert >}}

To effectively and efficiently answer a Critical Reasoning question that is based on an argument, we must relentlessly seek out and understand the argument’s conclusion.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

To effectively and efficiently answer a Critical Reasoning question that is based on an argument, we must relentlessly seek out and understand the argument’s conclusion.
{{< /alert >}}
