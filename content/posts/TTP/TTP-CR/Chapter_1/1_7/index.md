## Lesson Content
Sometimes arguments contain background information that the author uses to frame the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

An author presents us with background information to “get us up to speed” on anything we may need to know in order to effectively understand the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

For example, in the following passage, the author uses the first sentence to convey background information to provide us with enough context to be able to completely understand the argument:

> <em>The Noodle House, which serves only noodles and has won the award for the best noodles in our state five years in a row, is opening a store in the Town of Redlands next month. The mayor of Redlands loves eating award-winning food. Therefore, the mayor of Redlands will love eating at the Noodle House. </em>

Notice that, without the background information about the Noodle House, the argument would not make much sense.

We’ll have a lot more to say about background information as the course progresses.
