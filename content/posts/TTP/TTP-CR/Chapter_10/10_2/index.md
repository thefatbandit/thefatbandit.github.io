## Lesson Content
As you already know, Critical Reasoning question stems are predictable, and the more quickly we can determine what we have to do in order to correctly answer the question we’re facing, the more time we’ll have for answering it.

The following are common ways in which Logical Flaw Questions are worded:

> <em>Which of the following best describes the error of reasoning contained in the argument above?</em>

> <em>Which of the following best describes a weakness present in the argument above?</em>

> <em>The argument is flawed because it fails to consider that</em>

> <em>The reasoning in the argument above is flawed because it presumes without justification that</em>

> <em>The argument above is most vulnerable to criticism on the grounds that it</em>

> <em>The author’s conclusion is based on the error in reasoning that</em>

> <em>Which of the following points to the most serious logical flaw in the argument?</em>

> <em>The CFO</em><em>’s response is flawed as a refutation of the COO's argument because it</em>

> <em>Of the following, the best criticism of the argument above is that it fails to account for</em>

> <em>A serious flaw in the reasoning of the argument is that</em>

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Become familiar with the language that commonly appears in Logical Flaw question stems and able to quickly recognize that a question is asking you to identify a logical flaw.
{{< /alert >}}

At this point, you are beginning to understand how Logical Flaw questions work.
