## Lesson Content
The more basic type of Logical Flaw question is a pure Logical Flaw question.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Some Logical Flaw Questions are pure Logical Flaw questions, meaning that they ask us to point out a flaw in the argument as it currently stands and without bringing in new information in the correct answer.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s look at an example stimulus of a pure Logical Flaw question:

> <em>In a recent medical study, all of the study participants who had in the past five years abused barbiturates, powerful prescription sedatives commonly used to treat anxiety, were found to be suffering with depression. Thus, it must be the case that abusing barbiturates causes depression. </em>

> <em>Which of the following best describes the error of reasoning contained in the argument above?</em>

> (A<em>) The argument overlooks the possibility that the observed correlation between barbiturate abuse and depression is not the result of the cause-and-effect relationship presented.</em>

This argument has a cause-and-effect conclusion, that abusing barbiturates causes depression, which is based on the observation that people who were known to abuse barbiturates were also found to be suffering from depression.

After all, does the author provide us with any strong evidence to support her conclusion? No – she does not.

Notice that the correct answer highlights a potential vulnerability in an assumption made by the author.

##  Example 2 

For the first four years of Technology Inc.’s being in business, the CEO handled all aspects of recruiting and hiring for the company, and, on average, revenue increased by close to 50 percent per year.

The argument is most vulnerable to criticism on the grounds that it

1. [ ] (A) assumes without sufficient warrant that the CEO’s strongest skills lie in recruiting and hiring.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) overlooks the possibility that, as the company has grown, the CEO has had to delegate more responsibilities in order for the company to operate efficiently.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) does not include consideration of the fact that human resources professionals are likely better versed in employment law than the CEO is.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) takes the change in the hiring process to be the cause of the change in revenue growth without considering other possible causes.
5. [ ] (E) fails to establish that the trend in Technology Inc.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The argument is based on the fact that, when human resource professionals took over the handling of most aspects of hiring and recruiting at the company, revenue growth slowed. Given that fact, the argument arrives at the following cause-and-effect conclusion:


<u>Conclusion</u>: The CEO is better at recruiting and hiring than are the human resources professionals at the company.


There is a gap in the argument, in that the evidence is a change in revenue growth, while the conclusion is about the difference between the CEO’s level of skill in hiring and recruiting and that of the human resources professionals. So, the argument depends on an assumption that fills that gap.


> <b>(A) assumes without sufficient warrant that the CEO’s strongest skills lie in recruiting and hiring.
</b>


 This choice describes something that does not actually go on in the argument. The argument neither states nor implies that <em>the CEO’s</em> <em>strongest skills</em> lie in recruiting and hiring. It indicates only that the CEO is more skilled in recruiting and hiring than are the human resource professionals.


> <b>(B) overlooks the possibility that, as the company has grown, the CEO has had to delegate more responsibilities in order for the company to operate efficiently.
</b>


This choice is incorrect because what it describes is not logically connected to the support for this argument’s conclusion. The conclusion is simply that the CEO is better at recruiting and hiring than are the human resources professionals. The reason why the CEO handed off responsibility for recruiting and hiring does not affect the support for that specific conclusion.


> <b>(C) does not include consideration of the fact that human resources professionals are likely better versed in employment law than the CEO is.
</b>


If the conclusion were that there are no benefits to having human resources professionals handle recruiting and hiring, this choice could bring up a flaw in the support for the conclusion. However, since the conclusion is simply that the CEO is better at recruiting and hiring than are the human resources professionals, the fact that the argument does not include consideration of the fact that the human resources professionals might have certain knowledge that could be valuable to the company is not a flaw in the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(D) takes the change in the hiring process to be the cause of the change in revenue growth without considering other possible causes.
</b>


In going from the evidence that revenue growth slowed when human resources professionals started handling recruiting and hiring to the conclusion that the revenue growth slowed <em>because</em> the human resources professionals starting handling recruiting and hiring, the argument fails to consider other potential reasons for the decline in revenue growth, reasons such as the fact that growing revenue by large percentages can be easier when a company is small than it is once the company has become much bigger.


> <b>(E) fails to establish that the trend in Technology Inc. ’s revenue growth is significantly different from the trends in revenue growth of the company’s peers.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, as one could perceive it as implying that the author has assumed that there is not some industry-wide factor that is causing multiple companies, including Technology Inc. and its peers, to experience similar declines in revenue growth. So, this choice could be perceived as indirectly saying that the author has assumed that the decline in revenue growth at Technology Inc. is not due to a factor other than the change in who handles recruiting and hiring.


The truth is, however, that that interpretation of this what this choice says is not supported. This choice simply says that the author has failed to establish that the trend in Technology Inc.’s revenue growth is significantly different from the trends in revenue growth of the company’s peers, and arriving at the argument’s conclusion does not require establishing that these trends are different. There are many reasons why the trends could be similar even if the conclusion of this argument is correct. As a matter of fact, it’s possible that, even if the trends are similar, the reason why the trends are the similar is that the CEOs of Technology Inc.’s peers are also better at recruiting and hiring than human resources professionals.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
