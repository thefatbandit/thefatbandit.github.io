## Lesson Content
Logical Flaw questions may seem to be rather similar to Weaken the Argument questions.

As we’ve discussed, almost always, in order to weaken the conclusion in a Weaken the Argument question, the correct answer option brings in new, outside information.

On the other hand, in answering a pure Logical Flaw question, we are not seeking to weaken the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a pure Logical Flaw question, we are not seeking to weaken the argument. Rather, our goal is to highlight an aspect of the argument that is flawed.
{{< /alert >}}

In other words, in answering questions of this type, our job is not to bring in outside, new information that weakens the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In a pure Logical Flaw question, a flaw in the argument must always exist within the argument, and without the interjection of outside information.
{{< /alert >}}

Okay, having reviewed the difference between pure Logical Flaw questions and Weaken questions, let’s now discuss the second type of Logical Flaw question, a question that is a hybrid of a Weaken the Argument question and a Logical Flaw question.
