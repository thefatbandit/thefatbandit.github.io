## Lesson Content
The second Logical Flaw question type contains questions that are hybrids of Weaken the Argument questions and pure Logical Flaw questions.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Some Logical Flaw Questions are hybrids of Weaken the Argument questions and pure Logical Flaw questions. These questions also ask us to point out a flaw in the author’s argument, but they bring in new information in the correct answer, in a way quite similar to the way in which regular Weaken the Argument questions do.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s look at a stimulus from a hybrid Logical Flaw/Weaken the Argument question.

> <em>In order to reduce crowding in the parking lots at Listria University, the university’s facilities director has proposed an increase in parking fees of one dollar per hour in all university lots. The facilities director believes that, once the fee for even one hour of parking exceeds the cost of a round-trip tram ticket to the university from nearby Studentville, most students will choose to take the tram rather than park their cars at the university.</em>

> <em>Which of the following statements, if true, provides the best evidence that the facilities director’s reasoning is flawed?</em>

In this question, a proposal is introduced: increase the university’s parking fees.

Is this proposal completely logical? Hmm – maybe not, right? As presented, there is no clear link allowing us to see that this proposal would change the situation for the students, in other words, that the increase in parking fees would provide a strong reason for a change in the students’ parking behavior.

Here’s a possible correct answer to the question:

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) The vast majority of Listria University students come from local towns and, therefore, do not reside in Studentville while studying at the university.</em>

The correct answer serves to point out that the author, in making her argument, has made an assumption that may not be valid, that using the tram to travel from Studentville to the university is a viable option for students.

Notice that the correct answer focused on the vulnerability of an assumption made by the author.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Be prepared to answer questions that are blends of Logical Flaw and Weaken the Argument questions.
{{< /alert >}}

Below are two examples of question stems that blur the distinction between Logical Flaw and Weaken the Argument question types.

> <em>Which of the following, if true, would point to a possible flaw in the plan described?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, points to the most serious logical flaw in the argument?</em>

Notice, while the correct answers to pure Logical Flaw questions are always true, since they describe characteristics of the arguments presented, the two stems above ask for answers that, “if true,” point to flaws.

Let’s practice answering questions that are blends of Logical Flaw and Weaken the Argument questions.

##  Example 3 

Analyst: Two years ago, Redcorp, a consumer products company, hired a new chief marketing officer.

Which of the following, if true, points to a possible flaw in the analyst’s argument?

1. [ ] (A) Over the last two years, the costs of the materials that Redcorp uses in producing the products it sells have not increased significantly.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Over the course of the past two years, the unemployment rate of the country in which Redcorp sells most of its products went from 25 percent to 5 percent.
3. [ ] (C) Redcorp’s sales revenue had been increasing by about 5 percent each year over the decade prior to the arrival of the new chief marketing officer.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) The new chief marketing officer was able to significantly increase the sales revenue at the company at which she worked prior to joining Redcorp.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) The new chief marketing officer raised the prices of the consumer products sold by Redcorp.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



<u>Premise</u>: Two years ago, Redcorp hired a new chief marketing officer.


<u>Premise</u>: Since that time, Redcorp’s sales revenue has increased by 50 percent each year.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Clearly, the new chief marketing officer is responsible for the increase in Redcorp’s sales revenue.


Notice that the author is making the cause-and-effect claim that the new chief marketing officer is responsible for the increase in Redcorp’s sales revenue. As always, we must be skeptical anytime we are presented with a cause-and-effect claim. In analyzing this argument, we must ask ourselves whether the new chief marketing officer is really responsible for the increase in Redcorp’s sales revenue. Might there have been another cause? What might the analyst have failed to consider?


> <b>(A) Over the last two years, the costs of the materials that Redcorp uses in producing the products it sells have not increased significantly.
</b>


 The argument’s conclusion is that the new chief marketing officer is responsible for the increase in Redcorp’s sales revenue.


What this answer choice says, that, over the last two years, the costs of the materials that Redcorp uses in producing the products it sells have not increased significantly, is not clearly connected to the increase in the company’s sales revenue.


Yes, the fact that costs of materials have not increased might be a reason why Redcorp would be a healthy or profitable company. However, the fact that the costs of materials have not increased would not serve as an alternative explanation for the increase in sales revenue, and, in general, this information about materials costs is not clearly connected to the increase in sales revenue.


All this choice really tells us is that a variable, costs of materials, has not increased. The fact that a variable related to Redcorp's business has not increased does not highlight any flaw in the reasoning via which the author arrived at the conclusion that the new chief marketing officer is responsible for the increase in Redcorp's sales revenue. Regardless of the fact that materials costs did not increase, the fact that sales revenue increased after the new chief marketing officer was hired supports the conclusion that the new chief marketing officer is responsible for the increase in sales revenue.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B) Over the course of the past two years, the unemployment rate of the country in which Redcorp sells most of its products went from 25 percent to 5 percent.
</b>


What this answer choice says points to a possible flaw in the analyst’s argument by highlighting that there could be an alternative explanation for why Redcorp’s sales revenue has increased by 50 percent each year since the arrival of the new chief marketing officer.


After all, if over the course of the past two years, the unemployment rate of the country in which Redcorp sells most of its products went from 25 percent to 5 percent, then way more people have jobs now than had jobs two years ago. If way more people have jobs now than had jobs two years ago, then many people have more money to spend than they had two years ago. Of course, it is common knowledge that, when people have more money to spend, they spend more on goods and services, and so, the huge decrease in the unemployment rate could explain why Redcorp’s sales volume increased.


Notice that this answer choice weakens the support for the conclusion by providing new information on a topic not discussed by the analyst who made the argument. Also, notice that this question is a hybrid between a Weaken the Argument question and a Logical Flaw question.


> <b>(C) Redcorp’s sales revenue had been increasing by about 5 percent each year over the decade prior to the arrival of the new chief marketing officer.
</b>


 If anything, the fact that Redcorp’s sales revenue had been increasing by about 5 percent each year prior to the arrival of the new chief marketing officer strengthens the case for the analyst’s conclusion by showing that, when the cause (the new chief marketing officer) was not at Redcorp, the effect (sales revenue increased by 50 percent each year) did not occur.


Of course, a fact that strengthens the case for the conclusion does not point to a flaw in the argument.


> <b>(D) The new chief marketing officer was able to significantly increase the sales revenue at the company at which she worked prior to joining Redcorp.
</b>


 If anything, what this is choices says is in line with the reasoning presented in the argument.


After all, if the new chief marketing officer was able to significantly increase the sales revenue at the company at which she worked prior to joining Redcorp, then we are a little bit more willing to accept that she may have also been responsible for the increase in Redcorp’s sales revenue.


Of course, in answering this question, we are not seeking an answer choice that is in line with the argument; we are seeking one that, if true, points to a possible flaw in the argument.


> <b>(E) The new chief marketing officer raised the prices of the consumer products sold by Redcorp.
</b>


 The argument’s conclusion is that the new chief marketing officer is responsible for the increase in Redcorp’s sales revenue.


What this answer choice says is in line with the argument because it provides a potential explanation for how she may have increased Redcorp’s sales revenue: she may have increased the revenue by raising prices.


Of course, in answering this question, we are not seeking an answer choice that is in line with the argument; we are seeking one that, if true, points to a possible flaw in the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

##  Example 4 

In Creos, even people employed full time at the previous minimum wage would not earn incomes above the official poverty level.

Which of the following, if true, would serve to call attention to a flaw in the above argument?

1. [ ] (A) Not all people in Creos whose incomes are below the official poverty level feel that they live in poverty.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) In Creos there has been an increase in the difference between the incomes of those earning the most and those earning the least.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Past increases in the minimum wage in Creos have not been sufficient to eradicate poverty in the country.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) Employers surveyed have indicated that further increases in the minimum wage would cause them to reduce staffing.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) Some people in Creos are experiencing chronic illnesses of severity such that the people experiencing the illnesses feel that they are unable to work.

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



<u>Conclusion</u>: There will not be anyone living in poverty in Creos.


The conclusion of the argument is based on the fact that someone working full time at the current minimum wage would have an income greater than the income officially considered a poverty level income. Is this one fact sufficient for arriving at the conclusion that absolutely nobody in Creos will be living in poverty? Not really. There could be other factors that would cause a person to live in poverty, even if working full time at minimum wage would theoretically result in a person’s not living in poverty. So, the argument depends on an unwarranted assumption, the assumption that there is only one factor, the level of the minimum wage, that can cause a person to live in poverty.


> <b>(A) Not all people in Creos whose incomes are below the official poverty level feel that they live in poverty.
</b>


This choice is worded to sound as if it makes a difference. By indicating that people earning below poverty level incomes don’t necessarily feel that they are living in poverty, this choice seems to disconnect the concept of poverty from the concept of income level and, thus, from the minimum wage. Notice, however, that, since what this choice says does not conflict with the idea that nobody in Creos will be living in poverty, it does not serve to call attention to a flaw in the argument.


> <b>(B) In Creos there has been an increase in the difference between the incomes of those earning the most and those earning the least.
</b>


Since an increase in the difference between the incomes of those earning the most and those earning the least does not change the fact that, at the current minimum wage, one can earn an above poverty level income, this choice does not highlight a flaw in the argument.


> <b>(C) Past increases in the minimum wage in Creos have not been sufficient to eradicate poverty in the country.
</b>


Regardless of whether past increases in the minimum wage have been sufficient to eradicate poverty in the country, this minimum wage, at which one can earn an above poverty level income, may be sufficient.


> <b>(D) Employers surveyed have indicated that further increases in the minimum wage would cause them to reduce staffing.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, because it points to a flaw in the support for a conclusion that is related to the conclusion of this argument. If the conclusion of the argument were that minimum wage increases <em>always</em> benefit workers, then this choice would highlight a possible flaw, by indicating that the fact that employers continued hiring after this minimum wage increase is no guarantee that they will continue hiring no matter how much the minimum wage increases. However, since the conclusion is simply that this particular minimum wage increase will cause an end to poverty in Creos, this choice does not point out a flaw in the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(E) Some people in Creos are experiencing chronic illnesses of severity such that the people experiencing the illnesses feel that they are unable to work.
</b>


By indicating that a person in Creos may not work because the person is experiencing an illness, this choice calls attention to an unwarranted assumption upon which the argument relies. This choice calls attention to the fact that, even if, by working at the minimum wage, one can earn an above poverty level income, people in Creos could still live in poverty for reasons other than the level of the minimum wage, such as illness. So, this choice calls attention to the fact that the conclusion of the argument relies on the unwarranted assumption that there are no factors other than the level of the minimum wage that could cause a person in Creos to live in poverty.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

##  Example 5 

In 2006 an outbreak of E.

Which of the following, if true, would point to a possible flaw in the farmers’ plan?

1. [x] (A) Plants and animals that live in natural habitats support crop yields by purifying water and preying on pests that damage crops.
2. [ ] (B) Many wild animals that harbor E.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) The 2006 E.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) It is possible to keep wildlife from entering farms by erecting fences, even if there are wildlife habitats near the farms.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) In many cases litigation related to food borne infections would be directed at wholesale buyers of produce rather than at farmers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



By clearing areas of wildlife habitat that are near their farms, the farmers hope to achieve the following outcome:


<u>Projected Outcome of the Plan</u>: Farmers will safeguard their profits.


The question stem asks us to find an answer choice that points to a possible flaw in the farmers’ plan. A choice that indicates that clearing wildlife habitat near the farms would not necessarily lead to safeguarding the farmers’ profits would point out a flaw in the plan.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(A) Plants and animals that live in natural habitats support crop yields by purifying water and preying on pests that damage crops.
</b>


This choice calls attention to a possibly incorrect assumption that the farmers have made in clearing the areas of natural habitat near their farms, the assumption that doing so will only serve to safeguard profitability. If the plants and animals living in those areas support the crop yields of the farms, then by clearing those areas, the farmers could be reducing the yields of their farms and thus reducing profitability. Thus, this choice calls attention to a possible flaw in the farmers’ plan.


> <b>(B) Many wild animals that harbor E. Coli are extremely unlikely to contaminate crops, even if the animals inhabit areas near farms.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, because it could be perceived as attacking the assumption that clearing the habitats near the farms will make a difference in the probability of crops’ becoming contaminated with E. Coli. However, even if many animals would not contaminate crops, some animals still might. So, even given what this choice says, it could make sense to conclude that, by clearing the areas, farmers could be safeguarding their profits.


> <b>(C) The 2006 E. Coli outbreak was linked only to bagged spinach and to no other types of produce.
</b>


This choice could be perceived as indicating that farmers who don’t farm spinach would be wasting their time by seeking to keep their produce from being contaminated. However, while that outbreak was linked to spinach only, the next could be linked to another type of produce. So, any produce farmer might be safeguarding profits by clearing areas of wildlife habitat near that farmer’s farm.


> <b>(D) It is possible to keep wildlife from entering farms by erecting fences, even if there are wildlife habitats near the farms.
</b>


This is a typical alternative plan answer choice, one that could seem to point out a weakness in a plan via indicating that the plan may not be necessary, as there is an alternative plan that would work. In this case, the alternative plan is the use of fences. The truth is, however, that, regardless of whether, via the use of fences, it may be possible to keep wildlife out of farms without clearing wildlife habitat near farms, clearing wildlife habitat could serve to safeguard farmers’ profits.


> <b>(E) In many cases litigation related to food borne infections would be directed at wholesale buyers of produce rather than at farmers.
</b>


This choice misses the point of the argument. The farmers are seeking to safeguard profits because wholesale buyers of produce are extremely wary of buying produce that may have been contaminated with E. Coli. So, regardless of whether litigation related to E. Coli infections would be directed at farmers, seeking to safeguard profits by taking measures to ensure that produce is not contaminated could make sense.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)

Let’s now discuss some common flaws in reasoning that appear in Logical Flaw questions.
