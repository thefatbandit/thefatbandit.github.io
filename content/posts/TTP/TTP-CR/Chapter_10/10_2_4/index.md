## Lesson Content
There are several mistakes (flaws) in reasoning that authors commonly make in their arguments.

> - Errors in Reasoning Involving Cause and Effect

> - Overgeneralizing and Making Sampling Errors

> - Confusion Related to Numbers, Percentages or Other Numerical Information

> - Conclusions that are not Supported by the Evidence

> - Confusing Necessary or Significant Information with Sufficient Information

The good news is that any of these concepts that are fair game in Weaken the Argument questions are also fair game in Logical Flaw questions.

But, in adhering to the deep learning mastery principles of Target Test Prep, we’re going to reinforce these concepts once again.

Let’s begin by discussing logical flaws in cause-and-effect reasoning.
