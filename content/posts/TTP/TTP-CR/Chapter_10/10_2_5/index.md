Here’s an error in cause-and-effect reasoning in the context of a Logical Flaw question:

> <em>The transportation secretary of the city of Niezque noticed that, in areas of the city in which there were many bicycle repair shops, there tended to be great numbers of people using bicycles. Therefore, she proposed that, in order to increase the use of bicycles in the city, the city offer tax breaks to encourage the opening of more bicycle repair shops.</em>

> <em>A flaw in transportation secretary’s reasoning is that she failed to consider the possibility that</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>the actual relationship between great numbers of cyclists and the presence of many bicycle repair shops is the reverse of the one upon which the success of the plan depends.</em>

The transportation secretary observed the correlation between the presence of many bicycle repair shops and great numbers of cyclists and arrived at a cause-and-effect conclusion, the conclusion that the presence of many bicycle repair shops causes there to be great numbers of cyclists.

##  Example 6 

The Dental Association maintains that adding fluoride to drinking water in order to prevent cavities is a practice that is safe for human health.

Which of the following is the best criticism of the argument above?

1. [ ] (A) It fails to consider the possibility that people may feel that avoiding experiencing cavities is worth an increased risk of experiencing attention and learning issues.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) It assumes without sufficient warrant that all people experience the same effects from consuming fluoridated drinking water.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) It overlooks the possibility that the regions in which water is fluoridated share other characteristics that underlie the observed attention and learning issues.
4. [ ] (D) It ignores the fact that dentists’ supporting fluoridation conflicts with some of their own interests, as the need for their services might increase were drinking water not fluoridated.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) It fails to establish that, before water fluoridation began, no children in the areas in which water is now fluoridated experienced attention and learning issues.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Conclusion</u>: Adding fluoride to drinking water is not safe for human health.


The conclusion is based on an observed correlation between fluoridation of drinking water in certain regions and relatively high incidences of children’s experiencing attention and learning issues in those regions. Without specifically saying so, the author is indicating that fluoridation is a cause of these issues. What could be wrong with the author’s reasoning?


> <b>(A) It fails to consider the possibility that people may feel that avoiding experiencing cavities is worth an increased risk of experiencing attention and learning issues.
</b>


What if it were true that people feel that avoiding experiencing cavities is worth an increased risk of experiencing attention and learning issues? Would the author’s reasoning be undermined? Not at all. It could still be the case that water fluoridation causes attention and learning issues, and is, therefore, not safe for human health.


> <b>(B) It assumes without sufficient warrant that all people experience the same effects from consuming fluoridated drinking water.
</b>


Does the author really assume what this choice says the author assumes? What if the author did not assume that all people experience the same effects from consuming fluoridated drinking water? Would the argument still work? Yes, it would. Even if we assume that people experience various different effects from drinking fluoridated water, drinking such water could be unsafe for human health.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) It overlooks the possibility that the regions in which water is fluoridated share other characteristics that underlie the observed attention and learning issues.
</b>


The author observes the correlation between water fluoridation in certain regions and relatively high incidences of attention and learning issues in those regions and assumes that the fluoridation must be causing the issues. In doing so, the author is overlooking the possibility that there is some other characteristic that these regions share that is the actual cause of the attention and learning issues. For instance, it could be that regions in which drinking water is fluoridated are also regions in which children experience more stress than is experienced by children in other regions. In such a case, stress, rather than fluoridation, could be the cause of the attention and learning issues, and, therefore, fluoridation may be safe for human health.


> <b>(D) It ignores the fact that dentists’ supporting fluoridation conflicts with some of their own interests, as the need for their services might increase were drinking water not fluoridated.
</b>


This answer could be tempting, because it is true that supporting fluoridation does, at least in a way, conflict with certain interests of dentists, and the existence of this conflict of interest could be perceived as supporting the idea that the opinion about fluoridation expressed by the dentists is correct, as in, “Why would dentists say something incorrect if saying it actually conflicts with their own interests?” However, even if one were not to ignore the existence of this apparent conflict of interest, one could still conclude from the evidence that fluoridation causes attention and learning issues, and that, as sincere as the dentists may be in expressing their opinion, their opinion is incorrect.


> <b>(E) It fails to establish that, before water fluoridation began, no children in the areas in which water is now fluoridated experienced attention and learning issues.
</b>


The conclusion of the argument is not that fluoridation is the sole cause of attention and learning issues. Therefore, there is no need for the author to establish that, before fluoridation began, there were no attention and learning issues. The evidence employed is that the incidences of attention and learning issues are <em>higher </em>where there is fluoridation, and this evidence could still be used to support the conclusion, even if, before fluoridation began, some children experienced attention and learning issues.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
