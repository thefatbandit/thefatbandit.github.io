## Lesson Content
The most obviously wrong answer choices in Logical Flaw questions are those that describe things that authors or arguments don’t actually do.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Unlike other major automobile companies, all of which produce mostly automobiles that are fossil fuel powered, The Blitz Automobile Company makes only electric automobiles. Because electric automobiles are easier to maintain, less costly to power, and more environmentally friendly, they are superior to fossil fuel powered vehicles. Thus, soon, the Blitz Automobile Company will put all of the other automobile companies out of business.</em>

> <em>Which of the following best describes the error of reasoning contained in the argument above?</em>

> <em>(A) It neglects to consider that the other automobiles companies may shift to producing electric automobiles.</em>

> <em>(B) It relies on an arbitrary distinction between products that does not reflect the realities of the automobile market.</em>

> <em>(C) It presents as evidence in support of its conclusion information that is in conflict with other evidence presented.</em>

We have already discussed the fact that choice (A) is correct because it brings up an unwarranted assumption upon which the argument relies.

> <em>(B) It relies on an arbitrary distinction between products that does not reflect the realities of the automobile market.</em>

Choice (B) is blatantly incorrect.

OK, now for choice (C).

> <em>(C) It presents as evidence in support of its conclusion information that is in conflict with other evidence presented.</em>

Let’s review the evidence presented:

> <u>Premise</u>: Other major automobile companies all produce mostly automobiles that are fossil fuel powered.

> <u>Premise</u>: The Blitz Automobile Company makes only electric automobiles.

> <u>Premise</u>: Because electric automobiles are easier to maintain, less costly to power, and more environmentally friendly, they are superior to fossil fuel powered vehicles.

With a quick review of the evidence, we can see that no part of it conflicts with any other part.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Some answer choices to Logical Flaw questions are blatantly incorrect because what they describe is not what the authors or arguments actually do.
{{< /alert >}}

Incorrect answer choices of this type are fairly easy to eliminate.

As you go through the following examples, you can develop your vision by noticing the characteristics of the blatantly incorrect choices, the characteristics of the more tempting but still incorrect choices, and the characteristics of the correct answer.

##  Example 12 

An independent consumer agency has concluded that, although it is overly complicated to install and run on computers, Smart Suite, a next generation computer anti-virus software package, provides computer users the most protection against infection by computer viruses provided by any anti-virus software.

The consumer agency’s reasoning is most vulnerable to criticism on which of the following grounds?

1. [ ] (A) It assumes without sufficient warrant that computer viruses that have infected computers cause problems for the users of those computers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) It presents a cause-and-effect conclusion without providing any evidence to support that cause and effect conclusion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) It overlooks the possibility that the computer users able to install and run overly complicated software would also be those most knowledgeable about avoiding computer viruses in the first place.
4. [ ] (D) It relies on an arbitrary distinction between the appearance of Smart Suite’s user interface and the appearances of user interfaces of other computer anti-virus software packages available to consumers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) It neglects to consider that computer anti-virus software packages sold by other companies can help computer users prevent computer viruses from infecting their computers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Breakdown of the argument:


<u>Conclusion</u>:An independent consumer agency has concluded that, although it is overly complicated to install and run on computers, Smart Suite, a next generation computer anti-virus software package, provides computer users the most protection against infection by computer viruses provided by any anti-virus software.


<u>Premise</u>: Numerous independent surveys show that the computers of users of Smart Suite have the lowest rate of infection with computer viruses of any group of computers.


Notice that the author is essentially making the cause-and-effect claim that the Smart Suite software package is the cause of “the lowest rate of infection with computer viruses. ”


As usual, we must be skeptical of this cause-and-effect claim. In analyzing this argument, we must ask, “Is it actually the Smart Suite program that is responsible for computers of users of Smart Suite having the lowest rate of infection with computer viruses of any group of computers?”


Might there have been another cause? Could there be alternative explanation for the observation made? What might the author have failed to consider?


> <b>(A) It assumes without sufficient warrant that computer viruses that have infected computers cause problems for the users of those computers.
</b>


 Notice that the consumer agency’s argument says absolutely nothing about computer viruses causing problems. The argument is entirely about how effective Smart Suite is in providing protection against <em>infection</em> by computer viruses.


The consumer agency does not claim that such protection is important or that it prevents any problem.


The agency’s conclusion is a specific one, that Smart Suite provides computer users the most protection against infection by computer viruses provided by any anti-virus software. In arriving at that conclusion, the agency does not have to assume that computer viruses that have infected computers cause problems for the users of those computers, because, regardless of whether the viruses cause problems, Smart Suite could still provide the most protection against infection by them.


So, what this choice describes is something that the argument simply does not do.


> <b>(B) It presents a cause-and-effect conclusion without providing any evidence to support that cause and effect conclusion.
</b>


 Notice that what this answer choice says conflicts with what the argument does.


After all, the argument <em>does</em> provide evidence for the cause-and-effect conclusion; the last sentence of the passage says, “The agency offers as evidence the fact that numerous independent surveys show that the computers of users of Smart Suite have the lowest rate of infection with computer viruses of any group of computers. ”


We must be careful not to choose as correct a choice that describes something that the argument simply does not do.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) It overlooks the possibility that the computer users able to install and run overly complicated software would also be those most knowledgeable about avoiding computer viruses in the first place.
</b>


 Notice that the passage tells us that Smart Suite is overly complicated to install and run on computers. Common sense logic would tell us that the users of computers most apt to use a software product that is “overly complicated to install and run on computers” would be those who have above average knowledge of and skills with computers and computer programs. After all, we would not expect those with novice or even intermediate knowledge of computers to use such a program.


What other characteristics would people with above average knowledge of and skills with computers and computer programs have? Logic tells us that such users might also be those most knowledgeable about avoiding computer viruses in the first place. Right?


So, this choice brings up an interesting point.


That point is that, in arriving at its conclusion, the argument has overlooked an important possibility, the possibility that the reason for the correlation between use of Smart Suite and “the lowest rate of infection with computer viruses” is not that Smart Suite offers the most protection against infection by computer viruses but, rather, that the people who use Smart Suite are those who would understand how to avoid computer viruses in the first place.


In other words, this answer choice brings up the fact that the argument is flawed, in that it has overlooked an alternative explanation for pattern present in the survey results.


> <b>(D) It relies on an arbitrary distinction between the appearance of Smart Suite’s user interface and the appearances of user interfaces of other computer anti-virus software packages available to consumers.
</b>


 Notice that the passage simply does not do what this choice describes. If you need to, read the passage again to prove to yourself that the passage does not rely on any distinction having to do with user interfaces.


In answering a Flaw question, we must be careful not to choose as correct a choice that mentions something that the argument simply does not do.


> <b>(E) It neglects to consider that computer anti-virus software packages sold by other companies can help computer users prevent computer viruses from infecting their computers.
</b>


 Notice that the argument simply does not do what this choice describes.


The conclusion is that Smart Suite provides the <em>most</em> protection against infection by computer viruses provided by any anti-virus software.


Of course, in concluding that Smart Suite provides the most protection, the argument does not have to be neglecting to consider that computer anti-virus software packages sold by other companies can help computer users prevent computer viruses from infecting their computers. In fact, in saying that Smart Suite provides “the most protection,” the argument seems to be making a comparison and implying that other packages provide <em>some protection</em>, just not as much protection as Smart Suite provides.


In answering a Flaw question, we must be careful not to choose as correct a choice that mentions something that the argument simply does not do.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Example 13 

People suffering with kidney stones can be treated in various ways, such as lithotripsy, which involves using shock waves to break up the stones, or drug therapy.

The argument is most vulnerable to criticism on which of the following grounds?

1. [ ] (A) Evidence it presents in support of its conclusion conflicts with other information it presents in support of the same conclusion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) It overlooks the possibility that drugs used to treat kidney stones cause reductions in blood pressure.
3. [ ] (C) It arrives at a conclusion about the negative side effects of lithotripsy without considering those of other treatment methods.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) It makes an unwarranted generalization about the overall population from a small sample composed only of people treated for kidney stones.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) It assumes without sufficient warrant that there is no way in which to perform lithotripsy without damaging the treated kidneys.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



The purpose of the argument is to support the following conclusion.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Lithotripsy causes damage to the kidneys that results in high blood pressure.


The support for the conclusion consists of one premise.


<u>Premise</u>: People whose kidney stones were treated via lithotripsy are more likely to experience excessively high blood pressure following treatment than are people who use drug therapy.


Notice that the premise is a simple correlation between treatment via lithotripsy rather than drug therapy and a greater incidence of excessively high blood pressure. Clearly, the reason for the existence of this correlation could be different from the reason mentioned, that lithotripsy causes high blood pressure.


> <b>(A) Evidence it presents in support of its conclusion conflicts with other information it presents in support of the same conclusion.
</b>


 This choice is blatantly incorrect. The argument presents only one piece of information as evidence. That information cannot conflict with itself. So, this choice describes something that the argument does not do.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B) It overlooks the possibility that drugs used to treat kidney stones cause reductions in blood pressure.
</b>


 The conclusion that lithotripsy “must” be the cause of the excessively high blood pressure is based on the correlation between higher blood pressure and use of lithotripsy rather than drug therapy. In arriving at this conclusion, the argument overlooks other possibilities, including the possibility that the incidence of excessively high blood pressure is lower in those who use drug therapy because drug therapy causes reductions in blood pressure.


> <b>(C) It arrives at a conclusion about the negative side effects of lithotripsy without considering those of other treatment methods.
</b>


 This choice is a little tricky, because it is true that the argument arrives at a conclusion about the negative side effects of lithotripsy without considering those of other treatment methods. However, since, in supporting its specific conclusion, which is simply that lithotripsy causes excessively high blood pressure, the argument does not have any reason to consider the side effects of other treatment methods, the fact that the argument does not consider the side effects of other treatment methods is not a flaw.


> <b>(D) It makes an unwarranted generalization about the overall population from a small sample composed only of people treated for kidney stones.
</b>


 This choice is blatantly incorrect, because the discussion in the argument is restricted to experiences of people treated for kidney stones, and, thus, no generalization about the overall population is made.


> <b>(E) It assumes without sufficient warrant that there is no way in which to perform lithotripsy without damaging the treated kidneys.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because the statements in the argument could be read as indicating that there is no way to perform lithotripsy without damaging the treated kidneys. The truth is, however, that the argument simply states that lithotripsy damages kidneys, without asserting that there is not some way in which lithotripsy could be refined or used differently so that it would not cause damage to the treated kidneys. So, what this choice describes is something that the argument does not actually do.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

Now let’s consider a type of answer choice that is less obviously incorrect.
