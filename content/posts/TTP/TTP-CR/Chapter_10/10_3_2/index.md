## Lesson Content
In order for a Logical Flaw answer choice to be correct, it has to have two characteristics.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In order for a Logical Flaw answer choice to be correct, it has to have two characteristics. It has to describe something that actually goes on in the argument, and what it describes has to be a flaw in the argument. Many Logical Flaw incorrect answer choices satisfy the first criterion, as they describe things that the authors or arguments actually do, but do not satisfy the second one, as what they describe are not flaws.
{{< /alert >}}

For instance, an answer choice could say that the author fails to establish that a certain idea is correct, and it could be the case that the author does not establish that that idea is correct.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Health insurance for those living in major cities costs significantly more than does health insurance for those living in small towns. However, empirical data shows that people who live in large cities are no less healthy than people who live in small towns. It follows that health insurance companies profit more from people who live in major cities than from those who live in small towns. </em>

> <em>Which of the following best describes the error of reasoning contained in the argument above?</em>

> <em>(A) The argument assumes without justification that the costs associated with providing health care are not significantly higher in major cities than in small towns.</em>

> <em>(B) The argument fails to establish whether people in cities have incomes higher than those of people who live in small towns.</em>

Notice that what choice (B) describes is a characteristic of the argument.

In contrast, as we discussed previously, if the assumption mentioned in choice (A) is not correct, then the argument falls apart.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A Logical Flaw question may include one or more incorrect answer choices that describe things that are characteristics of the argument but are not flaws.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 14 

Emotional freedom therapy, or EFT, is a method purported to heal via tapping on various parts of a person’s body.

The logic of the argument above is most vulnerable to the criticism that the argument

1. [ ] (A) concludes that EFT is not effective without considering the results of studies that show other healing methods to be effective.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) fails to explain why EFT has become increasingly popular even though no studies have generated solid evidence indicating that it has any healing effects.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) takes a lack of evidence indicating that EFT is effective as proof that it is not.
4. [ ] (D) fails to establish that no people who have used EFT have seen improvements in their health.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) provides no evidence indicating that the tapping associated with EFT has ever had any negative effects on any person’s health.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: medium



<u>Conclusion</u>: EFT is not effective in solving health problems.


The argument supports the conclusion with the following premise:


<u>Premise</u>: No study has generated solid evidence that EFT has any healing effects.


Notice that the premise doesn’t really say anything convincing. For all we know from what it says, there have been no studies of the effects of EFT at all.


To get the right answer to this question, we have to distinguish between choices that describe things that are characteristics of the passage but are not flaws and the correct answer, which brings up an actual flaw in the argument.


> <b>(A) concludes that EFT is not effective without considering the results of studies that show other healing methods to be effective.
</b>


 It is true that the argument does not include consideration of the results of studies that show other healing methods to be effective. All the same, while the results of studies of other healing methods may be somehow related to the efficacy of EFT, making the argument that EFT is not effective does not require considering the results of studies of other methods. All one really needs is solid evidence that EFT is not effective. So, while this choice does describe a characteristic of the argument, this choice does not describe a flaw.


> <b>(B) fails to explain why EFT has become increasingly popular even though no studies have generated solid evidence indicating that it has any healing effects.
</b>


 This choice is tempting because it is true that the argument does not include any such explanation, and also, one might wonder why EFT would be so popular if it is not effective. However, explaining why EFT is popular is not necessary for proving that EFT is not effective. So, even though it is true that the argument does not include such an explanation, that it does not is not a flaw.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) takes a lack of evidence indicating that EFT is effective as proof that it is not.
</b>


 The author concludes that EFT is not effective, but offers no actual proof to support this conclusion. The premise is simply that no studies have indicated that EFT is effective. It could be that the effectiveness of EFT has not been studied at all. Lack of study generated evidence that EFT has health benefits does not mean that EFT does not have health benefits.


> <b>(D) fails to establish that no people who have used EFT have seen improvements in their health.
</b>


 This choice is tricky. For one thing, it is true that the argument does not establish that no people who have used EFT have seen improvements in their health, and, meanwhile, the possibility that some people who have used EFT have seen improvements in their health could be perceived as undermining this argument. Thus, this choice could be perceived as pointing out a flaw. However, the truth is that, even if some people using EFT have seen improvements in their health, the method could still be totally useless, as it could be that those people healed for reasons other than the use of EFT. So, even though what this choice says is true of the argument, this choice does not describe a flaw in the argument.


> <b>(E) provides no evidence indicating that the tapping associated with EFT has ever had any negative effects on any person’s health.
</b>


 It is true that the argument provides no evidence indicating that the tapping associated with EFT has ever had any negative effects on any person’s health. However, the conclusion is not that EFT is harmful. So, the argument does not require evidence indicating that EFT has negative effects. So, while what this choice describes is a characteristic of the argument, it is not a flaw.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
