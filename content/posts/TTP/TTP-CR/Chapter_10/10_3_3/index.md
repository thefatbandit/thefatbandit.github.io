## Lesson Content
As we have seen in reviewing various types of Critical Reasoning questions, one way in which the creator of a question can create a tempting wrong answer choice is to have the choice address a conclusion that is closely related to the conclusion of the argument but is not actually the conclusion of the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

One way to create a tempting wrong answer choice is to have the choice address a conclusion that is closely related to the conclusion of the argument but is not actually the conclusion of the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

These types of choices appear in Logical Flaw questions as well, and the way they work is by bringing up something that would be a flaw in the argument if the argument were meant to support a conclusion different from the one that it actually supports.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Answer choices that center on the wrong conclusion appear in Logical Flaw questions, and the way they work is by bringing up something that would be a flaw in the argument if the argument were meant to support a conclusion different from the one that it actually supports.
{{< /alert >}}

For instance, if an argument’s conclusion is about the effects of a person’s following a certain diet, a related conclusion would be that a person will actually manage to stick with the diet.

So, in answering a Logical Flaw question, correctly and exactly identifying the conclusion of the argument is a key step.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Education Secretary: The results of a recent study show that, the more years of high school education a person has, the less likely it is that that person will experience drug addiction. Therefore, if we can get a greater percentage of students to complete all of high school, we will succeed in reducing the incidence of drug addiction in young people in our country.</em>

> <em>The argument is most vulnerable to which of the following criticisms?</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Describes a Flaw in the Support for the Wrong Conclusion</u>) It assumes without sufficient warrant that the Department of Education has strategies that would be effective in getting more students to complete all of high school.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) It overlooks the possibility that drug use is the cause of rather than the effect of students’ ceasing to attend high school before completing all of it.</em>

What’s the conclusion that the argument has to support?

> <u>Conclusion</u>: If we can get a greater percentage of students to complete all of high school, we will succeed in reducing the incidence of drug addiction in young people in our country.

So, in order to be effective, the argument has to support the conclusion that there will be a cause-and-effect relationship between getting students to complete high school and reducing drug addiction.

The answer that describes a flaw in the support for the wrong conclusion doesn’t find a flaw in the support for this cause-and-effect conclusion.

The correct answer, on the other hand, describes a key flaw in the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A Logical Flaw question may include one or more incorrect answer choices that describe flaws in support for conclusions that are related to the conclusion of the argument but are not actually the conclusion of the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

The good news is that by, correctly and exactly identifying the conclusions of the arguments in Logical Flaw questions, a move that is not particularly difficult to make, you can avoid choosing many of the trap answers that appear in these questions.

To develop your vision, as you consider the following example, notice the answer choices that describe flaws in the support for conclusions other than the conclusion of the argument presented.

##  Example 15 

One of the responsibilities of the Verdantville police force is to ensure that the streets of Verdantville are safe for travel.

Of the following, the best criticism of the argument above is that it overlooks the possibility that

1. [ ] (A) over the past few years, the Verdantville police force has had to reallocate resources to the criminal investigations department, in response to a rash of burglaries.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) many automobile collisions in Verdantville happen at times of day when traffic is so heavy that speeding is difficult.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) fines for speeding in Verdantville have increased, with the result that revenue from speeding fines has remained the same even as the number of citations has declined.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) all of the town’s departments have cut back in some areas in order for the town to maintain a balanced budget in times that are slow economically.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) the decline in the number of drivers cited for speeding in Verdantville is the result of the police force’s effectively handling its street travel related responsibilities.

##### Question Level: easy



In order to be sure that we find a choice that describes a flaw in the support for the conclusion of the argument presented, we have to identify that conclusion exactly.


<u>Conclusion</u>: The Verdantville police are neglecting their responsibility of ensuring that the streets of Verdantville are safe for travel.


Notice that there is a gap in this argument. The support for the conclusion is the number of citations issued, while the conclusion is about safety. Safety and citations issued may be related matters, but they are not exactly the same.


> <b>(A) over the past few years, the Verdantville police force has had to reallocate resources to the criminal investigations department, in response to a rash of burglaries.
</b>


 This choice describes a flaw in the support for a conclusion that is related to the conclusion of the argument presented. If the conclusion of the argument were that the police have cited fewer drivers for speeding because the police are neglecting their responsibilities in general, then what this choice says would describe a flaw in the argument. However, even if the police have a good reason for citing fewer drivers for speeding – they have been dealing with a rash of burglaries – they could be neglecting their street travel related responsibilities. So, this choice does not highlight a flaw in the argument.


> <b>(B) many automobile collisions in Verdantville happen at times of day when traffic is so heavy that speeding is difficult.
</b>


 This choice may seem to provide a valid reason for the decrease in the number of drivers cited for speeding and, thus, to highlight a flaw in the argument. However, the fact that many collisions occur when drivers are not speeding anyway is not an indication that, in citing fewer drivers for speeding, the police are not neglecting their responsibility of making the streets safe for travel.


If this choice were a little different and said that speeding had, over the past few years, become difficult in Verdantville, then perhaps it would highlight a flaw in the argument, but since it does not describe a change that has occurred over the past few years but rather a situation that seems to have been the same all along, it does not highlight a flaw in the support for the conclusion presented.


> <b>(C) fines for speeding in Verdantville have increased, with the result that revenue from speeding fines has remained the same even as the number of citations has declined.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because its topic, revenue from speeding fines, is related to the conclusion of the argument. However, since the conclusion is not that the police have been neglecting generating revenue, this choice’s pointing out the fact that revenue may not have decreased does not highlight a flaw in the argument presented in this question.


> <b>(D) all of the town’s departments have cut back in some areas in order for the town to maintain a balanced budget in times that are slow economically.
</b>


 This choice highlights a flaw in the support for a conclusion that is not the conclusion of the argument presented in this question. If the conclusion of the argument were that the police department is being neglectful because of poor management, this choice would highlight a flaw in the support for the conclusion. However, since, even if the police are cutting back services to stay within budget, the police could be doing so by choosing to cut back on street safety related activities, what this choice says doesn’t highlight a flaw in the support for the argument’s conclusion.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(E) the decline in the number of drivers cited for speeding in Verdantville is the result of the police force’s effectively handling its street travel related responsibilities.
</b>


 By bringing up the possibility that the number of citations issued has decreased as a result of the police department’s having very effectively handled the responsibility of making the streets safer, this choice highlights a flaw in the argument. It could be that the reason that the number of drivers cited for speeding has decreased over the past few years is that fewer drivers are speeding because the police have been doing a great job of making the streets safe for travel. In that case, the conclusion would be incorrect. Therefore, the argument’s overlooking this possibility is a flaw.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
