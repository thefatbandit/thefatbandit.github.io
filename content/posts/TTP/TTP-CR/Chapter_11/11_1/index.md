## Lesson Content
Complete the Passage questions are among the easiest Critical Reasoning questions to identify, principally because they end with a blank space.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Complete the Passage questions are among the easiest Critical Reasoning questions to identify, principally because they end with a blank space.
{{< /alert >}}

When we see a passage with a long, underlined segment at the end, “__________,” we know that we’re dealing with a Complete the Passage Question.

Complete the Passage questions generally begin with a question stem that is some variation of:

> <em>Which of the following most logically completes the passage</em>?

The question stem is followed by a passage, and the last sentence of the passage ends with a blank space, as described above.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The correct answer to a Complete the Passage question is the one that does the best job of filling the blank space and, thus, logically completing the passage.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 1 

Which of the following best completes the passage?

Paradoxical intervention is a healing technique that involves having a person engage in a behavior that the person wishes to discontinue.

1. [ ] (A) the person would prefer to get sufficient sleep rather than experience chronic insomnia
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) insomnia often develops as a result of behaviors that are only indirectly related to sleeping
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) a person experiencing chronic insomnia generally does not sleep that much anyway
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) without sufficient sleep, people generally experience physical health issues and cognitive problems
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) fear of failure, in this case, failure to sleep, is much of what has kept the person from succeeding

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The passage in this question describes a method, paradoxical intervention, that counselors use in order to get people experiencing chronic insomnia to sleep.


The method involves having a person experiencing chronic insomnia seek to stay awake rather than seek to sleep.


The last sentence of the passage says that the rationale for using the method is that, through using this method, the person will end up sleeping because of whatever is said by the part that fills the blank.


So, the correct answer, the one that fills the blank, will tell us why, through seeking to stay awake, the person will sleep.


> <b>(A) the person would prefer to get sufficient sleep rather than experience chronic insomnia
</b>


This choice adds almost nothing to the passage. From the fact that the person is working with a counselor to address the chronic insomnia, we can already reasonably conclude that the person would rather get sufficient sleep than experience chronic insomnia. Moreover, even if the fact that the person would rather get sufficient sleep were new information, that information would not explain why, through seeking to stay awake, the person would end up sleeping.


> <b>(B) insomnia often develops as a result of behaviors that are only indirectly related to sleeping
</b>


This choice merely provides some background on how insomnia develops, and that background does not explain why, through seeking to stay awake, a person experiencing chronic insomnia would end up sleeping.


> <b>(C) a person experiencing chronic insomnia generally does not sleep that much anyway
</b>


The fact that a person experiencing chronic insomnia doesn’t sleep much anyway might make staying awake seem to be fairly easy for a person experiencing chronic insomnia. However, that fact does not explain why, through seeking to stay awake, a person experiencing chronic insomnia would end up sleeping.


> <b>(D) without sufficient sleep, people generally experience physical health issues and cognitive problems
</b>


While preventing physical health issues and cognitive problems might be a good reason for a person to sleep, a person experiencing chronic insomnia has been having difficulty sleeping, regardless of the fact that the person would be better off sleeping. So, the fact that lack of sleep results in physical health issues and cognitive problems does not explain why, through seeking to stay awake, a person experiencing chronic insomnia would end up sleeping.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(E) fear of failure, in this case, failure to sleep, is much of what has kept the person from succeeding
</b>


If fear of failure has been keeping a person from succeeding at sleeping, changing the person’s goal from sleeping to staying awake could remove an impediment to the person’s falling asleep, because, if the person’s goal is to stay awake rather than to fall asleep, then, theoretically, the person would fear the failure to stay awake, rather than fear failure to sleep. At that point, without the fear of failure to sleep, which was keeping the person from sleeping, the person would sleep. Thus, this choice explains why, through seeking to stay awake, a person experiencing chronic insomnia would sleep.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
