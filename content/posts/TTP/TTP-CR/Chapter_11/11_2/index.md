## Lesson Content
The good news is that, while the format of a Complete the Passage question may seem new, in answering a Complete the Passage question, we won’t actually have to do anything particularly different from what other types of Critical Reasoning questions ask us to do.

For example, a Complete the Passage question could ask us to do any one of the following:

> · State an assumption made by the author

> · Strengthen the argument

> · Weaken the support for a claim presented in the argument

> · State the conclusion that the author has been leading up to

> · State an inference that can be made

> · Resolve a seeming paradox outlined in the passage

> · Evaluate a plan

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Complete the Passage questions won’t ask us to do anything particularly different from what other types of Critical Reasoning questions have already asked us to do.
{{< /alert >}}

This concept is easy to demonstrate with an example:

> <em>There are only two candidates for mayor in next month’s election: Thompson and McGuire. Since McGuire was recently arrested for tax evasion, Thompson will most likely win the election <strong>because</strong> __________.</em>

Let’s break down this argument.

> <u>Premise 1</u>: Thompson and McGuire are the only candidates for mayor

> <u>Premise 2</u>: McGuire was recently arrested

> <u>Conclusion</u>: Thompson will win

The blank line follows the conclusion and is connected to it with the word “because.” The word “because” signals that evidence is coming.

How could we strengthen this argument? An answer stating:

> <em>candidates who experience negative publicity in the period immediately before an election tend to lose that election</em>

would strengthen the argument because it would directly connect “tax evasion” in the premise with election results.

Similarly, an answer stating:

> <em>candidates currently facing criminal charges are removed from the ballot</em>

would also strengthen the argument because it explains how McGuire’s arrest would directly influence the result of the election.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Read the last sentence of the stimulus carefully. The cue words that appear before the blank are excellent indicators of the type of role that the answer that fills that blank performs in the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s now discuss a way to expose the trap choices in Complete the Passage questions.
