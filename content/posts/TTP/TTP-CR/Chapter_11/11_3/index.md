## Lesson Content
Much of what makes Complete the Passage questions tricky is the fact that the answer choice completes a sentence, and so, an incorrect choice that sounds as if it flows well with the sentence, even though it doesn’t effectively complete the passage, can be very tempting.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Which of the following most logically completes the set of facts presented below?</em>

> <em>In response to residents’ complaints about constant noise made by airplanes’ taking off and landing, the city council of Mergate is considering permanently closing Mergate Airport. Many businesses in Mergate depend on spending by vacationers. Nevertheless, the members of the city council feel that the profitability of these businesses would not be negatively impacted by the closing of the city’s airport, since __________.</em>

> <em>(A) these businesses represent a significant proportion of all businesses in Mergate</em>

> <em>(B) these businesses have tended to be some of the most profitable in the city</em>

> <em>(C) without the constant noise made by airplanes taking off and landing, Mergate would be a much more attractive destination for vacationers</em>

In the scenario described in the passage, the city council members are considering closing the city’s airport and feel that the profitability of businesses that depend on spending by vacationers would not be negatively affected by the closing of the airport.

If we fill the blank with choice (A), we get the following:

> <em>Nevertheless, the members of the city council feel that the profitability of these businesses would not be negatively impacted by the closing of the city’s airport, since these businesses represent a significant proportion of all businesses in Mergate.</em>

That sentence flows pretty nicely and almost seems to make sense.

Now, let’s fill the blank with choice (B):

> <em>Nevertheless, the members of the city council feel that the profitability of these businesses would not be negatively impacted by the closing of the city’s airport, since these businesses have tended to be some of the most profitable in the city.</em>

That sentence also flows well, and, if you weren’t paying close attention, you might not notice that, no matter how profitable these businesses have been, their profitability could be negatively impacted by the closing of the airport.

So, because, in merely plugging a choice into the end of a sentence, the wrongness of an incorrect choice can be camouflaged by the length and sound of the sentence, a choice that is not quite right can sound pretty good.

Now, notice what happens if we convert the sentence that ends with a blank into a question.

Here’s the sentence that ends with a blank converted into a question.

> <em>Why is it that the city council members feel that the profitability of businesses in Mergate that depend on spending by vacationers would not be negatively impacted by the closing of the city’s airport?</em>

Does choice (A) make sense as the answer to that question?

> <em>(A) these businesses represent a large proportion of businesses in Mergate</em>

As we discussed above, that answer clearly makes no sense, but now, by making the sentence that ends with a blank into a question, we have removed the camouflage that hid the wrongness of the choice when the choice was part of a long sentence.

Choice (B) also makes little to no sense as the answer to our question:

> <em>(B) these businesses have tended to be some of the most profitable in the city</em>

Now that choice (B) is an answer to a question rather than part of a long sentence, we can more easily see that it is incorrect.

Now, let’s answer our question with choice (C).

> <em>Why is it that the city council members feel that the profitability of businesses in Mergate that depend on spending by vacationers would not be negatively impacted by the closing of the city’s airport?</em>

> <em>(C) without the constant noise made by airplanes’ taking off and landing, Mergate would be a much more attractive destination for vacationers</em>

Positioned as an answer to a question, choice (C) is clearly correct.

When answering a Complete the Passage question, by converting the sentence with the blank into a question, you can make the differences between the wrong answers and the correct answer more obvious.

##  Example 2 

Which of the following most logically completes the passage?

Four blocks of a certain street in Trenditon are known as Restaurant Row, because, along those four blocks, most buildings have restaurants on their ground floors.

1. [x] (A) whether, to make space for trees, restaurants on Restaurant Row would have to significantly reduce the numbers of dining tables that they have on the sidewalk
2. [ ] (B) what types of trees would be the healthiest if they were planted along Restaurant Row
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) whether regulations in Trenditon allow for planting of trees in openings in public sidewalks
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) whether working with the town to change parking regulations would do more to enhance the profitability of restaurants on Restaurant Row than would planting trees
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) what proportion of owners of restaurants on Restaurant Row are in favor of planting trees in openings in the sidewalk there
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



If we convert the sentence ending with the blank into a question, we get the following:


What would the Chamber of Commerce determine in order to confirm that having trees growing in openings in the sidewalk along Restaurant Row would lead to the desired results?


Of course, in order to answer this question, we need to clearly identify what the “desired results” are. By looking through the passage we can find that the desired results are “make the street more attractive, thus resulting in greater profits for the restaurants.”


To get this question right, we have to find the answer choice that helps to confirm that the implementing the strategy will lead to those specific desired results.


(Notice, this question is essentially an Evaluate question in the form of a Complete the Passage question.)


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(A) whether, to make space for trees, restaurants on Restaurant Row would have to significantly reduce the numbers of dining tables that they have on the sidewalk
</b>


Since the desired results include “greater profits for the restaurants,” determining whether, to make space for trees, restaurants on Restaurant Row would have to significantly reduce the numbers of dining tables that they have on the sidewalk is important. If the restaurants would have to significantly reduce their numbers of tables, then planting trees might result in reduced profits rather than increased profits.


> <b>(B) what types of trees would be the healthiest if they were planted along Restaurant Row
</b>


While the success of the project would depend on planting trees that would actually grow in that area, confirming that planting trees would result in greater profits for the restaurants does not really require determining what types of trees would be healthiest if they were planted in that area. As long as some reasonably attractive trees would be reasonably healthy in that area, the plan could work.


> <b>(C) whether regulations in Trenditon allow for planting of trees in openings in public sidewalks
</b>


This answer could be tempting, as, if regulations prohibit planting trees in the sidewalks, then implementing the plan outlined may be virtually impossible. However, we have to remember what specifically has to be confirmed, which is not whether the plan can be implemented but, rather, whether, if implemented, the plan will result in greater profits for the restaurants.


> <b>(D) whether working with the town to change parking regulations would do more to enhance the profitability of restaurants on Restaurant Row than would planting trees
</b>


This is a typical “better plan” answer choice. The question at hand is not whether there is some other plan, such as working with the town to change parking regulations, that is better than planting trees. The question is whether implementing the plan of planting trees in the sidewalks will result in greater profits for the restaurants.


> <b>(E) what proportion of owners of restaurants on Restaurant Row are in favor of planting trees in openings in the sidewalk there
</b>


Since the proportion of restaurant owners in favor of planting trees along Restaurant Row is not clearly a factor that would affect whether doing so will result in greater profits for the restaurants, determining the proportion of owners in favor of planting trees is not necessary for confirming that planting the trees would result in greater profits for the restaurants.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)

So far, we have discussed examples of Complete the Passage questions such that, in answering them, it makes sense to convert the sentence ending with the blank into a question beginning with “Why.” Converting the sentence ending with the blank into a question beginning with “Why” makes sense if the word preceding the blank is a connecting word that functions similarly to “because,” in other words a word such as “because,” “since,” or “as.”

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a Complete the Passage question, converting the sentence with the blank into a question beginning with “Why” makes sense if the blank is preceded by “because,” “since,” or “as.”
{{< /alert >}}

In some Complete the Passage questions, however, the blank is preceded by something other than a connecting word that functions like “because.” In those cases, we can rephrase the sentence that ends with a blank as a question beginning with “What.”

Consider the following example:

> <em>It had long been believed that prehistoric cave paintings, which often depicted animals, were created by men for reasons related to hunting animals for food. However, many of these paintings include stenciled images of hands, and, by analyzing the images, researchers have determined that most of the hands used in creating the stenciled images were women’s hands. So, we can conclude that most prehistoric cave paintings were created by women, assuming that __________.</em>

> <em>(A)</em> <em>the people who created the cave paintings did not participate in hunting the types of animals depicted in the paintings.</em>

> <em>(B) the hands depicted in images in cave paintings were not the hands of people other than the artists who created the paintings.</em>

To answer this question, we can convert the sentence ending with a blank into the following question:

> <em>What has to be assumed in order to arrive at the conclusion that the cave paintings were created by women from the evidence that hands used in creating stenciled images included in the paintings were the hands of women?</em>

Let’s consider choice (A).

On the other hand, it is true that, in order to use the fact that the hands used in making the hand images were women’s hands as evidence that the cave paintings were created by women, we have to assume what choice (B) says, that the hands used to make the images were not the hands of people other than the artists who created the paintings.

So, choice (B) answers our “What has to be assumed … ?” question.

##  Example 3 

Which of the following most logically completes the passage?

Personalized learning involves allowing students great latitude to determine what topics to study and in what order.

1. [ ] (A) schools have found that effectively applying personalized learning methods can require hiring additional teaching staff members
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) learning can be difficult when the order in which topics are studied is inexpertly chosen, as understanding of one topic can be the basis for learning about another
3. [ ] (C) students in a group studying the same topics at the same time can motivate each other and help each other to learn
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) some schools that go from using from traditional methods to using personalized learning methods see student achievement gains, while others see reductions in achievement
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) some students are more excited than are others to take advantage of the benefits of personalized learning programs
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The passage describes personalized learning and explains why, in theory, students would learn more and learn faster when using personalized learning than in groups directed by teachers. It then brings up the issue that, in reality, students in personalized learning programs find their learning efforts frustrated and lose motivation.


The last sentence of the passage can be converted into the following question, the logical answer to which will correctly fill the blank:


Why is it that students in personalized learning programs can find their efforts frustrated and lose motivation?


> <b>(A) schools have found that effectively applying personalized learning methods can require hiring additional teaching staff members
</b>


That fact that schools have found that effectively applying personalized learning methods requires hiring additional staff members would not cause students to find their learning efforts frustrated and lose motivation.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B) learning can be difficult when the order in which topics are studied is inexpertly chosen, as understanding of one topic can be the basis for learning about another
</b>


 The fact that learning can be difficult when the order in which topics are studied is inexpertly chosen could explain why students in personalized learning programs can find their efforts frustrated and lose motivation. Because the students are likely not experts at choosing the order in which topics are studied, students in personalized learning programs may be doing poor jobs of choosing the orders in which they study topics, and may thus be making their learning difficult.


> <b>(C) students in a group studying the same topics at the same time can motivate each other and help each other to learn
</b>


This choice is a very tempting trap, but if you carefully consider what this choice says, you can see that the fact that students learning in groups can motivate each other does not explain why students in personalized learning programs can find their efforts frustrated and lose motivation. In other words, this choice explains why students in groups may be especially motivated rather than explains what is holding back students in personalized learning programs.


> <b>(D) some schools that go from using from traditional methods to using personalized learning methods see student achievement gains, while others see reductions in achievement
</b>


The fact that some schools that go from using from traditional methods to using personalized learning methods see student achievement gains, while other schools see reductions in achievement does not explain why students in personalized learning programs can find their efforts frustrated and lose motivation.


> <b>(E) some students are more excited than are others to take advantage of the benefits of personalized learning programs
</b>


The fact that some students are more excited than others to take advantage of the benefits of personalized learning programs does not explain why students in personalized learning programs can find their efforts frustrated and lose motivation.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
