## Lesson Content
Here is a 5-step strategy for handling Complete the Passage questions.

<u>Step 1 – Read the question stem and identify what it is asking you to do.</u>

In most Complete the Passage questions, the question stems precede the passages.

<u>Step 2 – Carefully read, break down, and completely understand the passage.</u>

The foundation of choosing the correct answer to a Complete the Passage question is understanding the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The foundation for choosing the correct answer to a Complete the Passage question is understanding the argument presented in the passage.
{{< /alert >}}

<u>Step 3 – Convert the sentence that ends with the blank into a question.</u>

Turn the sentence that ends with the blank into a “Why …” question or a “What …” question.

Trap answers can seem to fill the blank at the end of the passage, but often, once you turn the sentence ending with the blank into a question, that those traps are not correct answers becomes rather apparent.

<u>Step 4 – Eliminate incorrect answer choices</u><u>.</u>

As you know by now, eliminating incorrect answer choices is at least equally as useful as selecting the correct answer choice.

In this step, you’ll be using many of the skills you’ve previously learned.

<u>Step 5 – Test the final answer by reading it back into the passage</u><u>.</u>

Once we’ve picked the best answer, we fill in the blank with the answer choice and read the entire passage to make sure that, with the blank filled, the passage flows logically.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Always plug your chosen answer choice back into the passage to make sure that this choice makes the passage flow logically.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s apply our strategy to an example.

##  Example 4 

Which of the following best completes the passage below?

The technique known as hydraulic fracturing, or “fracking,” involves pumping pressurized liquid into an oil well.

1. [ ] (A) oil company profits are heavily influenced by factors such as the level of economic activity and seasonal variations in temperature
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) over time, the cost to revive a previously exhausted oil well through fracking has declined to almost half the cost to drill a new one
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) the supply of oil produced by fracking has far exceeded consumer demand, causing oil prices to collapse
4. [ ] (D) the technique known as fracking has been criticized by environmentalists for contaminating ground water sources
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) getting approval to construct additional pipelines for transporting oil has proven difficult
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



In order to answer this question, let’s go through our five-step process.


<u>Step 1 – Read the Question Stem and Identify What it is Asking You to Do</u><u>.</u>


The question stem that precedes the passage asks us to find the answer that best completes the passage, and the passage ends with a blank. So, to correctly answer this question, we have to find the choice that most logically completes the passage.


<u>Step 2 – Carefully read, break down, and completely understand the passage</u><u>.</u>


This stimulus presents a situation in which people expected that fracking would increase profits, while in fact profits have gone down. This sort of puzzling situation is a classic example of the type of scenario that we would see presented in a Resolve the Paradox question.


<u>Step 3</u><u> – Convert the sentence that ends with the blank into a question.</u>


In the sentence that ends with the blank, the blank is preceded by the word “because.” So, we can convert that sentence into a question beginning with “Why.”


Why did oil company profits fall in the past decade, after fracking became widespread, even though they were expected to increase?


<u>Step 4 – Eliminate incorrect answer choices.</u>


Let’s take a look at the answer choices:


> <b>(A) oil company profits are heavily influenced by factors such as the level of economic activity and seasonal variations in temperature
</b>


This choice is tempting, because it mentions two factors, economic activity and seasonal variations in temperature, that could affect oil company profits. Notice, however, the fact that these factors can affect oil company profits does not mean that they have caused this particular decrease in profits. In other words, this choice does not say that, for instance, economic activity has slowed and, that this slowdown has caused a decrease in oil profits. The choice merely indicates that a slowdown, if there were one, <em>could</em> affect oil company profits.


> <b>(B) over time, the cost to revive a previously exhausted oil well through fracking has declined to almost half the cost to drill a new one
</b>


Rather than explain why profits have decreased, this answer choice does the opposite. It provides a reason why profits would have increased: one of the oil companies’ costs, the cost of fracking, has decreased. So, the effect of this choice goes in the wrong direction.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) the supply of oil produced by fracking has far exceeded consumer demand, causing oil prices to collapse
</b>


Since a collapse in oil prices precipitated by the use of fracking could be what has caused oil company profits to decline, this choice answers the question of why oil company profits have declined as fracking has become widespread. So, this choice appears to be the correct answer.


> <b>(D) the technique known as fracking has been criticized by environmentalists for contaminating ground water sources
</b>


Since we have no clear reason to believe that criticism of fracking would result in a decrease in oil company profits, this choice does not answer the question of why oil company profits fell in the past decade, after fracking became widespread, even though they were expected to increase.


> <b>(E) getting approval to construct additional pipelines for transporting oil has proven difficult
</b>


This choice could be tempting, as difficulties related to constructing new pipelines could be a drag on oil companies’ ability to increase profits. However, since it does not make sense that difficulty in <em>increasing</em> the number of pipelines would result in a <em>decrease</em> in profits, this choice does not explain why oil company profits decreased.


<u>Step 5 – Double-check the Final Answer by Reading it Back into the Passage.</u>


Here’s the complete passage, with choice (C) filling the blank:


The technique known as hydraulic fracturing, or “fracking,”involves pumping pressurized liquid into an oil well. It was long believed that, through the use of fracking, oil companies could increase their profits by reviving previously exhausted wells. However, in the past decade, as fracking has entered widespread use, oil companies have seen their profits fall, because <u>the supply of oil produced by fracking has far exceeded consumer demand, causing oil prices to collapse</u>.


Since the passage with choice (C) filling the blank makes complete sense, choice (C) is the correct answer.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

Let’s now discuss some characteristics of correct answers to Complete the Passage questions.
