## Lesson Content
Because a Complete the Passage Question asks us to select an answer option that best completes the passage, it’s easy to focus too much on the last sentence of the stimulus (the one with the blank line in it).

Consider the following example:

> <em>Miracle Walnut Company sells two products – bags of walnuts in their shells, and bags of walnuts unshelled. The machinery that the company uses to remove the shells of walnuts has broken down, and will be replaced with new, more efficient machinery rather than repaired. Since delivery and installation of new machinery will take about a week, Miracle Walnut Company will not produce unshelled walnuts for a week while the replacement of the shell removing machinery is in progress. Nevertheless, it is expected that all of Miracle Walnut Company’s orders will be filled during that week, because __________.</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Could Work with the Last Sentence Only</u>) there is no reason to believe that the orders that the company receives during that week will not follow the usual pattern</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) the company has, in inventory, bags of unshelled walnuts sufficient to cover at least a week’s worth of orders</em>

Notice that if we were to read just the last sentence without considering the rest of the passage, the choice that could work with the last sentence only might seem correct.

> <em>Nevertheless, it is expected that all of Miracle Walnut Company’s orders will be filled during that week, because there is no reason to believe that the orders that the company receives during that week will not follow the usual pattern. </em>

The sentence above sounds pretty reasonable.

The correct answer, on the other hand, fills the blank in a way that addresses the entirety of what is presented in the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The answer to a Complete the Passage question must work with, and logically fit with, the passage as a whole. Any answer choice that fits only with the final sentence, and does not fit logically with the passage as a whole, will be incorrect.
{{< /alert >}}

The following example includes multiple answer choices that could be perceived as working with the last sentence of the passage but don’t work with the entire passage.

##  Example 5 

Which of the following best completes the passage?

In areas in which there are many people who are addicted to illicit drugs that they inject intravenously, people seeking to inject these drugs often seek out places that are secluded and have plenty of lighting that drug users can use to see their veins in order to inject drugs.

1. [ ] (A) drug users tend to prefer not to be visible to others when they are injecting drugs
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) these bulbs consume no more energy than is consumed by ordinary light bulbs
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) in blue light, intravenous drug users often have difficulty seeing their veins
4. [ ] (D) these bulbs provide just as much light as is provided by ordinary light bulbs
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) the bulbs provide light sufficient for safely walking through or engaging in most activities in these spaces
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: easy



In the passage above, in the sentence that ends with the blank, the blank is preceded by “because.” Therefore, our task is to find the answer choice that explains why the installation of the light bulbs is expected to be effective in achieving its purpose.


From the information provided in the passage, we can see that the bulbs to be installed emit blue light and that the purpose of the installation of the bulbs is to achieve a reduction in the use of certain spaces by drug users.


So, the correct answer will answer the following question:


Why will replacing ordinary light bulbs in certain spaces with bulbs that emit blue light result in a reduction in the use of those spaces by drug users?


In answering that question, we have to be careful to consider all of the information provided by the passage.


> <b>(A) drug users tend to prefer not to be visible to others when they are injecting drugs
</b>


If we were to consider this choice in the context of the last sentence only, this choice could be very tempting, as it could seem perfectly logical that installing light bulbs could deter drug users from using certain spaces because the drug users prefer not to be visible. However, if we consider everything said in the passage, we can see that the first sentence of the passage says that drug users actually choose spaces that have “plenty of lighting”. Since there is no reason to believe that drug users would be more visible in blue light than they were in spaces that already had “plenty of lighting,” this choice does not fit what is said in the passage.


> <b>(B) these bulbs consume no more energy than is consumed by ordinary light bulbs
</b>


While this choice could conceivably fit with the last sentence of the passage, since it does not fit with the entirety of the passage, and does not answer the question outlined in the introduction to this explanation that the correct answer has to answer, this choice is not correct.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) in blue light, intravenous drug users often have difficulty seeing their veins
</b>


This choice fits with the entirely of what is said in the passage and answers the question outlined in the introduction to this explanation. The passage says that intravenous drug users seek out spaces where there is plenty of light that they can use to see their veins. If replacing ordinary light bulbs in these spaces with blue light bulbs makes seeing veins difficult, then doing so should result in a reduction in the use of these spaces by intravenous drug users.


> <b>(D) these bulbs provide just as much light as is provided by ordinary light bulbs
</b>


This choice could be tempting to a test-taker seeking to find a choice that works only with the sentence that ends with the blank and not with the entire passage. However, since there is no reason to believe that providing in certain spaces the same amount of light that has been provided in those spaces all along would result in a reduction in use of those spaces by drug users, this choice is not correct.


> <b>(E) the bulbs provide light sufficient for safely walking through or engaging in most activities in these spaces
</b>


This choice might be tempting to a test-taker seeking to find a choice that works only with the sentence that ends with the blank and not with the entire passage. However, since there is no reason to believe that providing light sufficient for safely walking through or engaging in activities in spaces would result in a reduction in use of those spaces by drug users, this choice is not correct.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
