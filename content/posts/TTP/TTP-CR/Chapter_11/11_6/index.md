## Lesson Content
Trap answers to Complete the Passage questions may utilize words, phrases, and syntax from the passage.

We must be vigilant not to choose such wrong answers.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Since the introduction of e-books, many have speculated that traditional “hard copy” book publishing will become largely obsolete in the near future because the book-purchasing public will prefer the more convenient e-books to traditional books. However, this prediction may have been premature, as __________.</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice with Matching Wording</u>)</em> <em>the public’s</em> <em>purchases of traditional books have generated the majority of the revenue of local book stores</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>in recent years, physical book sales have actually increased</em>

To correctly answer this question, we need a choice that answers the following question:

Why is it that the prediction that “hard copy” book publishing will be largely obsolete in the near future may have been premature?

With what it says about “the public’s purchases of traditional books,” the choice with wording that matches that of the passage sounds like the passage and could seem to fit in the blank space.

The correct answer, on the other hand, uses the expression “physical books” rather than the wording used in the passage, “traditional books,” but does a much better job of completing the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

To correctly answer a Complete the Passage question, we must find an answer choice that properly completes the passage by fitting into the logic of the argument as a whole and that is not simply a statement that includes wording similar to that found in the passage.
{{< /alert >}}

Notice how the wording in incorrect answer choices in the following example matches the wording of the passage without actually expressing something that completes the logic of the passage, while the correct answer actually completes the logic of the passage.

##  Example 6 

Which of the following most logically completes the passage?

Since every megawatt of power generated via a sustainable, non-polluting method, such as wind or solar power generation, theoretically represents a megawatt of power that would otherwise have been generated via burning fossil fuels, which creates pollution, the creation of a certain quantity of pollution is, in theory, avoided each time a megawatt of power is generated via a sustainable method.

1. [ ] (A) government subsidies of sustainable power generation have resulted in a reduction in the end cost to the consumer of such power
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) technological advances have resulted in reductions in the amount of pollution created in generating a megawatt of power via burning fossil fuels
3. [ ] (C) the cost of generating a megawatt of power via burning fossil fuels has declined
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) the proportion of power generated via burning fossil fuels has been steadily declining
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) some countries are in the process of passing laws capping the total quantities of fossil fuel related pollution allowed to be created annually within their borders
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



To correctly answer this question, we need a choice that is a logical answer to the following question:


Why has the quantity of pollution avoided by generating a megawatt of power via a sustainable method rather than via the use of fossil fuels been declining?


Choices that merely resemble the wording of the passage but do not answer that question will not be correct.


> <b>(A) government subsidies of sustainable power generation have resulted in a reduction in the end cost to the consumer of such power
</b>


Since a reduction in cost to the consumer would not change how much pollution is avoided by generating power via sustainable methods, this choice does not answer the question.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B) technological advances have resulted in reductions in the amount of pollution created in generating a megawatt of power via burning fossil fuels
</b>


The passage says that, in burning fossil fuels to generate a megawatt of electricity, pollution is created and that the creation of that pollution can be avoided by generating that megawatt of power via a sustainable method instead.


If the amount of pollution created in generating a megawatt of power via burning fossil fuels has declined, as this choice says, then substituting a megawatt of power generated via a sustainable method for a megawatt of power generated via burning fossil fuels won’t prevent the creation of as much pollution as it would have in the past.


Thus, this choice answers our question and completes the passage, by explaining why the quantity of pollution avoided by generating a megawatt of power via a sustainable method, rather than via the use of fossil fuels, has been declining.


> <b>(C) the cost of generating a megawatt of power via burning fossil fuels has declined
</b>


There is no clear reason to connect the cost of generating power via burning fossil fuels to the amount of pollution created in the process of generating a megawatt of power via burning fossil fuels. Thus, although the wording of this choice, which includes “generating a megawatt of power via burning fossil fuels” and “declining,” sounds like the wording in the passage, this choice does not answer the question discussed in the introduction to this explanation or logically complete the passage.


> <b>(D) the proportion of power generated via burning fossil fuels has been steadily declining
</b>


This choice could be tempting, as, like the passage, it mentions “power generated via burning fossil fuels” and “declining.” However, the fact that a smaller proportion of the power generated is generated via the burning of fossil fuels does not explain why less pollution is avoided by generating a particular quantity of power via using sustainable methods rather than via burning fossil fuels.


> <b>(E) some countries are in the process of passing laws capping the total quantities of fossil fuel related pollution allowed to be created annually within their borders
</b>


This choice could be tempting, as one might be inclined to conclude that, if countries are capping the amount of power allowed to be generated via burning fossil fuels, then, using sustainable methods for generating power won’t make any difference in the amount of pollution being made, because fossil fuel use will just remain at its capped level regardless of whether sustainable methods are used, and that, therefore, this choice correctly completes the passage.


However, following that line of reasoning requires making multiple assumptions, such as that the caps have been reached. Further, even if that line of reasoning did not rely on such assumptions, what this choice says is that some countries are <em>in the process</em> of passing laws capping the total quantities of fossil fuel related pollution allowed to be created annually within their borders. In other words, the laws have not yet been passed. So, what this choice says would not explain why the quantity of pollution avoided by generating a megawatt of power via a sustainable method <em>has been declining</em>.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
