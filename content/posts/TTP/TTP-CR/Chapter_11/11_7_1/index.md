## Lesson Content
As you know very well at this point, many trap answers in Critical Reasoning questions sound good and appear to be correct, but, at the same time, they are incorrect.

In Complete the Passage questions, trap answers are often incorrect because, although they sound correct, they head in directions subtly different from the direction in which the passages were headed.

Notice the difference between the answers in the following example:

> <em>Which of the following best completes the passage?</em>

> <em>Overfishing in the waters around Seatown has begun to significantly deplete the populations of fish in them. The government of Seatown has proposed setting up in these waters fish farms for raising fish for human consumption, in hopes that focusing on farming fish rather than on catching wild fish will give wild fish populations a chance to recover. However, fish farms become breeding grounds for fish parasites. So, implementing this plan may not bring about the desired outcome, because __________.</em>

> <em>(A) medicated food fed to farmed fish in order to combat parasites can negatively affect some forms of marine life that inhabit the sea floor</em>

> <em>(B) parasites that proliferate in fish farms attack young wild fish passing by the fish farms, thus decimating wild fish populations</em>

To complete the sentence ending with the blank, we need a choice that explains why <em>“implementing the plan may not bring about the desired outcome.”</em> So, we have to make sure that we know what the “desired outcome” is.

Now, let’s consider the answer choices.

Notice the problem with choice (A).

Choice (B), on the other hand, effectively completes the passage.

##  Example 7 

Which of the following best completes the passage?

In many states, young drivers are given restricted driver’s licenses that permit legal driving only during certain times of day, such as after 5 am and before 9 pm.

1. [ ] (A) most restricted licenses permit legal driving at any time of day, if the license holder is driving straight from home to work or work to home
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) the fact that a person has attained an age at which one can get an unrestricted driver’s license does not necessarily indicate that the person is a skillful driver
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) in some states it is possible for a young driver to gain an unrestricted license by completing a driver education course
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) young drivers sometimes feel that the need to arrive at home before the time of day when driving becomes illegal for them justifies their taking risks while driving
5. [ ] (E) there are times of day when restricted licenses permit legal driving even though many car crashes occur during those times of day
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The passage discusses driver’s licenses that permit legal driving only during certain time of day. The conclusion presented is that “parents of these young drivers should be made aware that the presence of the restrictions can result in dangerous behavior.”


The blank in the passage follows the conclusion and is connected to the conclusion by the word “as.” Therefore, the answer choice that completes the passage will support a statement made, as the correct answer to a Strengthen question would.


We can convert the sentence that ends with a blank into the following question:


Why does the presence of restrictions on the times when a teenage driver can legally drive result in dangerous behavior?


Notice, the question is specifically about the fact that the presence of the restrictions can result in dangerous behavior. So, the correct answer will address this particular attribute of the restrictions, and not some other attribute of the restrictions or the restricted licenses that is subtly different from the fact that the presence of the restrictions can result in dangerous behavior.


> <b>(A) most restricted licenses permit legal driving at any time of day, if the license holder is driving straight from home to work or work to home
</b>


At first glance, this choice may seem to be correct. Notice, however, the subtle difference between this choice and what we need. This choice indicates that having a restricted license <em>may not prevent</em> a teenage driver’s being exposed to the dangers of driving during the times of day when driving is the most dangerous.


> <b>(B) the fact that a person has attained an age at which one can get an unrestricted driver’s license does not necessarily indicate that the person is a skillful driver
</b>


This choice goes in a subtly incorrect direction. It explains why licensing regulations may not prevent a person’s getting an unrestricted license without being skilled at driving. In contrast, the answer choice that we need is one that indicates that the restrictions cause dangerous behavior.


> <b>(C) in some states it is possible for a young driver to gain an unrestricted license by completing a driver education course
</b>


This choice indicates that a teenage driver can get an unrestricted license. In contrast, we need a choice that explains why the restrictions on driving by a person holding a restricted license can cause dangerous behavior.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(D) young drivers sometimes feel that the need to arrive at home before the time of day when driving becomes illegal for them justifies their taking risks while driving
</b>


This choice answers the question outlined in the introduction to this explanation and explains why the presence of restrictions on licenses can result in dangerous behavior, because it indicates that the presence of restrictions on licenses can result in teenage drivers’ taking risks while driving in order to arrive at home before the time of day when driving becomes illegal for them.


> <b>(E) there are times of day when restricted licenses permit legal driving even though many car crashes occur during those times of day
</b>


At first glance, this choice may seem to be correct. Notice, however, the subtle difference between what this choice does and what we need. This choice indicates that having a restricted license <em>may not completely prevent</em> a teenage driver’s driving during times of day when great numbers of crashes occur. In contrast, the answer choice that we need is one that indicates that the restrictions <em>cause </em>dangerous behavior.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
