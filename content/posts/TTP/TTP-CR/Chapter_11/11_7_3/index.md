## Lesson Content
When answering a Complete the Passage question that discusses a plan or a course of action, it’s important that we do not select as correct an answer choice that addresses a material issue that is not, however, the issue that is the focus of the passage.

In a situation, there can exist multiple issues.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Miracle Walnut Company sells two products – bags of walnuts in their shells, and bags of walnuts unshelled. The machinery that the company uses to remove the shells of walnuts has broken down, and will be replaced with new, more efficient machinery rather than repaired. Since delivery and installation of new machinery will take about a week, Miracle Walnut Company will not produce unshelled walnuts for a week while the replacement of the shell removing machinery is in progress. Nevertheless, during that week, it is expected that all of Miracle Walnut Company’s orders will be filled, because __________.</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Addresses the Wrong Issue</u>) the costs of the business disruption necessary for replacing the shell removing machinery will be significantly outweighed by the benefits of having new machinery</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) the company has, in inventory, bags of unshelled walnuts sufficient to cover at least a week’s worth of orders</em>

Notice that the choice that addresses the wrong issue could be tempting because, as a test-taker reads through the passage, the issue of the costs versus the benefits of replacing the machinery might come to mind.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When answering a Complete the Passage question that discusses a plan or a course of action, it’s important that we do not select an answer choice that addresses a material issue that is not, however, the issue that is the focus of the passage.
{{< /alert >}}

To avoid choosing a choice that addresses a material issue that is not, however, the issue that is the focus of the passage, make sure that, rather than consider the choices in light of what might matter in the situation presented, you consider the choices in the context of the exact focus of the passage.

##  Example 9 

Which of the following most logically completes the passage?

Increases in temperatures on Earth are expected to exacerbate the drought conditions that exist in areas that already experience little rainfall.

1. [ ] (A) the substances that would be used to block sunlight are not the same substances that when placed into the atmosphere cause rainfall
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) a country could decide on its own to spray sunlight reflecting substances into the atmosphere without the help of or approval of other countries
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) much of the atmospheric moisture that becomes rain enters the atmosphere through evaporation caused by sunlight hitting the surfaces of oceans
4. [ ] (D) blocking sunlight from reaching the planet attacks the symptoms of the problem of increased temperatures on Earth without addressing the underlying causes
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) many of the countries in which drought conditions are present do not have financial resources to fund spraying sun blocking substances into the atmosphere
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: easy



If we convert the sentence ending with the blank into a question, we get the following:


How could reducing temperatures on Earth by spraying into the atmosphere substances that would reflect sunlight that otherwise would have reached the planet’s surface exacerbate problems involving drought?


In order to correctly answer this Complete the Passage question, we need a choice that logically answers the specific question above. A choice that is about an issue that might arise from blocking sunlight but is not about exacerbation of problems involving drought will not be correct. So, for instance, a choice that says that spraying substances into the atmosphere would be harmful to birds, while relevant to the discussion, would not logically complete the passage.


> <b>(A) the substances that would be used to block sunlight are not the same substances that when placed into the atmosphere cause rainfall
</b>


The point of the passage is not that droughts <em>would not be solved</em> by spraying substances into the atmosphere. The point is that problems involving drought <em>could be exacerbated</em> by spraying substances into the atmosphere. So, this choice goes in the wrong direction and, thus, does not logically complete the passage.


> <b>(B) a country could decide on its own to spray sunlight reflecting substances into the atmosphere without the help of or approval of other countries
</b>


In mentioning that countries could spray sunlight blocking substances into the atmosphere without the help or approval of other countries, this choice brings up an issue other than the issue that is the focus of the passage, which is the exacerbation of problems involving drought.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) much of the atmospheric moisture that becomes rain enters the atmosphere through evaporation caused by sunlight hitting the surfaces of oceans
</b>


This choice explains how blocking sunlight from reaching Earth’s surface could exacerbate problems involving drought. Rain is in part the result of sunlight hitting the surfaces of the oceans. So, if sunlight is blocked from hitting the surface of Earth, there could be a reduction in rainfall, and, thus, an exacerbation of problems involving drought. Thus, this choice answers the question discussed in the introduction to this explanation and logically completes the passage.


> <b>(D) blocking sunlight from reaching the planet attacks the symptoms of the problem of increased temperatures on Earth without addressing the underlying causes
</b>


This choice could be tempting because it brings up an issue, addressing symptoms rather than underlying causes, that is relevant to what’s discussed in the passage. However, that issue is not the focus of the passage. The correct answer must complete the passage by explaining why problems involving drought could be exacerbated if sunlight is blocked from reaching Earth’s surface, and this choice does not do so.


> <b>(E) many of the countries in which drought conditions are present do not have financial resources to fund spraying sun blocking substances into the atmosphere
</b>


This choice could be tempting because it brings up an issue, the fact that many of the countries in which drought conditions are present do not have financial resources to fund spraying sun blocking substances into the atmosphere, that is relevant to what’s discussed in the passage. However, that issue is not the focus of the passage. The correct answer must complete the passage by explaining how problems involving drought could be exacerbated via the use of the sunlight blocking approach described, and this choice does not do so.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
