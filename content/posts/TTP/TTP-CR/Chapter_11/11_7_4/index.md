## Lesson Content
We’ll wrap up our discussion of incorrect answers in Complete the Passage questions by discussing a type of choice that can be surprisingly tempting, a choice that explains part of the passage rather than completes the passage.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Which of the following best completes the passage?</em>

> <em>Overfishing in the waters around Seatown has begun to significantly deplete the populations of fish in them. The government of Seatown has proposed setting up in these waters fish farms for raising fish for human consumption, in hopes that focusing on farming fish rather than on catching wild fish will give wild fish stocks a chance to recover. However, fish farms become breeding grounds for fish parasites. So, implementing this plan may not bring about the desired outcome, because __________.</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Explains but does not Complete the Passage</u>) fish parasites can quickly multiply in fish farms, because of the high density and low genetic diversity of the farmed fish</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) parasites that proliferate in fish farms attack young wild fish passing by the fish farms, thus decimating wild fish populations</em>

In order to correctly answer this question, we need a choice that answers the following:

> Why wouldn’t setting up fish farms in the waters near Seatown give wild fish stocks a chance to recover?

Notice what the choice that explains but does not complete the passage does.

However, the answer that explains could seem to answer our question and complete the passage, because it makes the fish farms seem pretty bad for fish health.

As we have discussed, the correct answer actually does answer our question and complete the passage, as it indicates that the parasites that breed in fish farms end up decimating wild fish populations.

> <em>Overfishing in the waters around Seatown has begun to significantly deplete the populations of fish in them. The government of Seatown has proposed setting up in these waters fish farms for raising fish for human consumption, in hopes that focusing on farming fish rather than on catching wild fish will give wild fish stocks a chance to recover. However, fish farms become breeding grounds for fish parasites. So, implementing this plan may not bring about the desired outcome, because <u>parasites that proliferate in fish farms attack young wild fish passing by the fish farms, thus decimating wild fish populations</u>.</em>

##  Example 10 

Which of the following best completes the passage below?

Claims by Hollywood studios that they lose millions of dollars annually in ticket sales when their movies are copied and sold by “pirates” are greatly exaggerated.

1. [ ] (A) some people who might not otherwise pay to see films purchase pirated films because they sell at prices lower than must be paid to view films legally
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) movie theaters can eject any ticket-holder who is illegally recording a film being screened and are under no obligation to return the ticket price to the ejected person
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) the primary cause of Hollywood’s financial difficulties is expensive union contracts that must be re-negotiated
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) movie pirates pay for tickets in order to film movies in theaters before studios release those movies for home viewing
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) potential revenue from the few purchasers of pirated films who truly represent “lost ticket sales” is minimal

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The passage expresses an argument that can be summarized as follows:


A person pirating a film pays for a ticket, and most people who purchase pirated copies of films would not otherwise have paid to see the films.


We come to the last sentence, and can convert it into the following question:


Why is the loss to Hollywood attributable to this type of movie piracy small?


The correct answer will answer the above question.


> <b>(A) some people who might not otherwise pay to see films purchase pirated films because they sell at prices lower than must be paid to view films legally
</b>


This choice could be tempting because what it says about people who purchase pirated films spending less than they would have had to spend to see the movies legally could be perceived as indicating that the studios are not losing much money by missing out on these people’s business.


The truth is, however, that all this choice really does is explain something mentioned in the passage, that most people who purchase pirated films would not otherwise pay to see the films. Explaining why people who purchase pirated films would not otherwise pay to see the films does not add information that answers the question of why the loss to Hollywood attributable to this type of movie piracy is small.


> <b>(B) movie theaters can eject any ticket-holder who is illegally recording a film being screened and are under no obligation to return the ticket price to the ejected person
</b>


 This answer could be tempting because, since it describes a way in which movie piracy could be prevented, it could be perceived as explaining why the losses to studios resulting from this type of piracy are small. However, since it’s not clear from this choice that movie piracy is in fact prevented in this way, this choice does not do a great job of completing the passage by explaining why the losses attributable to such piracy are small.


> <b>(C) the primary cause of Hollywood’s financial difficulties is expensive union contracts that must be re-negotiated
</b>


<em> </em>This answer goes in a direction clearly different from the direction of the passage. The fact that there are expensive union contracts that must be re-negotiated does absolutely nothing to explain why the losses resulting from this type of piracy are small.


> <b>(D) movie pirates pay for tickets in order to film movies in theaters before studios release those movies for home viewing
</b>


This choice explains why movie pirates pay for tickets, but we already know from the passage that movie pirates pay for tickets. So, this choice does not add information that explains why the losses to studios resulting from this type of piracy are small.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(E) potential revenue from the few purchasers of pirated films who truly represent “lost ticket sales” is minimal
</b>


<em> </em>This answer choice perfectly answers our question and fits logically into the passage. Since a person pirating a film pays for a ticket, and most of the people who purchase a pirated copy of the film would never have purchased a ticket even if the pirated copy had not been available, the loss to Hollywood attributable to this type of movie piracy is small, because, as this choice says, potential revenue from the few purchasers of pirated films who truly represent “lost ticket sales” is minimal.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
