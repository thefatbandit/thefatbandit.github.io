## Lesson Content
Method of Reasoning questions use predictable language, such as:

> <em>The argument proceeds by</em>

> <em>The father responds to his daughter’s criticism by doing which of the following?</em>

> <em>The exchange above supports the claim that the two parties disagree about</em>

> <em>Which one of the following most accurately describes the relationship between Jane’s argument and Mark’s argument?</em>

> <em>The Attorney General uses which of the following techniques in responding to the cheese importer?</em>

> <em>The method of the argument is to</em>

> <em>The author develops the argument above by</em>

> <em>The argument above employs which of the following reasoning techniques?</em>

> <em>Which of the following best describes the argument above?</em>

##  Example 2 

Burrito Express CEO: Burrito Express has been in business in Country X for over a decade.

The CEO proceeds by

1. [ ] (A) using the example of one company’s experience in Country X to make the case that Country X would benefit from making changes in its economic policies.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) describing a general economic situation and outlining its impact on a company’s global operations.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) evaluating the impact of current economic conditions on an industry and recommending how the industry should respond.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) assuming without providing justification that a course of action makes sense.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) describing certain consequences of current economic conditions and outlining a conclusion that is based on that information.

##### Question Level: medium



In answering Method of Reasoning questions, it’s especially important to focus on structure – or how the argument fits together. The structure of the argument is distinctly different from the content of the argument (its subject matter). Trap answers in Method of Reasoning questions may temptingly include content that is similar to the content of the passage and yet does not accurately reflect how the passage proceeds. Also, remember that a correct answer choice must be completely correct.


Let’s look at the structure of the stimulus:


A business needs certain items to make some of its products. Because of economic conditions, the business cannot get those items. Therefore, the business is shutting down, rather than scaling back its operations. That’s all. We need to find an answer choice that describes this basic structure.


> <b>(A) using the example of one company’s experience in Country X to make the case that Country X would benefit from making changes in its economic policies.
</b>


 This answer relies on content to tempt you. The stimulus discusses a company and economic conditions in Country X, and so does this answer. However, this answer doesn’t accurately describe the structure. While what the CEO describes could be taken as evidence that a change in Country X’s economic policy is warranted, the CEO is not making a case for a change in economic policy in Country X.


> <b>(B) describing a general economic situation and outlining its impact on a company’s global operations.
</b>


 This answer’s structure is closer to being correct, but it is still incorrect. For one thing, “describing a general economic situation” does not actually occur in the passage. Economic upheaval is mentioned, but the economic situation is not actually described. Similarly, the passage does not describe the impact that the situation in Country X has had on Burrito Express’s global operations. Remember, we can go back to the passage and ask “Does the argument do what the answer choice says it does?” If the answer to that question is “No,” then the answer can’t be correct.


> <b>(C) evaluating the impact of current economic conditions on an industry and recommending how the industry should respond.
</b>


 The passage does not describe the impact on an industry as a whole; it only describes the impact on Burrito Express. Likewise, the passage only states how Burrito Express is choosing to respond, and, unlike what this choice says, does not make a recommendation. Always go back to the stimulus and check!


> <b>(D) assuming without providing justification that a course of action makes sense.
</b>


 This answer could be tempting, as the CEO does mention a course of action that will be taken. However, since, in the passage, the CEO does provide some justification, the lack of ingredients, for the course of action, in saying that the CEO is “assuming without providing justification,” this choice does not accurately reflect what the CEO does.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(E) describing certain consequences of current economic conditions and outlining a conclusion that is based on that information.
</b>


 The CEO describes the consequences of current economic conditions (the inability to acquire ingredients for some items), and states a conclusion that is based on that information (shut down all operations rather than just eliminating those items). Notice that when we go back to the argument and check, we see that each part of this answer is correct.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
