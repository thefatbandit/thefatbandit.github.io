## Lesson Content
Many Method of Reasoning questions are based on dialogues between two speakers.

Each part of the dialogue is considered a separate argument, generally with its own evidence, assumption(s), and conclusion(s).

The first speaker’s argument may or may not be sound.

On the other hand, the second speaker may respond effectively to the first speaker.

The two speakers could believe that they agree (or disagree), when in fact they are talking about different things.

So, in answering a Method of Reasoning question that involves two speakers, our first job is read through the exchange, noting any conclusions made by each speaker and getting a sense of how the speakers support those conclusions.

##  Example 3 

Researcher: Our study of the Mediterranean diet shows that people who followed that diet were healthier than and lived longer than did people who followed other diets.

Editor: I talked with your assistant, and he told me that, to make his work easier, he had all the people in one area follow the Mediterranean diet and people in other areas follow other diets.

The editor responded to the researcher’s assertion by doing which of the following?

1. [ ] (A) Challenging the assumption that there are not other diets via which people would achieve results better than those achieved via following the Mediterranean diet.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Pointing out that aggregate results of people’s following a particular diet may not indicate that that diet is optimal for any particular individual.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Pointing out that the research team failed to adequately control for a variable other than the one that was the focus of the study.
4. [ ] (D) Questioning the ethics of having people in different areas follow diets that the researchers believe to be of varying levels of quality.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Asserting that the variations in health outcomes observed resulted from factors characteristic of areas in which people lived rather than from differences in diet.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: medium



The researcher recommends following the Mediterranean diet. The basis for this recommendation is a set of study results that show that people who followed the Mediterranean diet experienced better health outcomes than were experienced by people following other diets.


The editor responds by declining to publish the findings. The basis for the editor’s decision is a fact that the editor points out, the fact that, because of the way the study was set up – the people following different diets lived in different areas – there could have been a cause, characteristics of areas in which people lived, other than diet that was responsible for the observed pattern in health outcomes.


> <b>(A) Challenging the assumption that there are not other diets via which people would achieve results better than those achieved via following the Mediterranean diet.
</b>


While challenging the assumption that there are not other diets via which people would achieve results better than those achieved via following the Mediterranean diet might have been a valid way in which the editor could have responded, the editor did not challenge this assumption.


> <b>(B) Pointing out that aggregate results of people’s following a particular diet may not indicate that that diet is optimal for any particular individual.
</b>


While the point brought up by this answer choice seems valid, it is not the point that the editor brought up.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) Pointing out that the research team failed to adequately control for a variable other than the one that was the focus of the study.
</b>


By pointing out that characteristics of areas in which people lived, rather than diet, could have been responsible for the observed variation in health outcomes, the editor does essentially what this choice describes, points out that the research team did not adequately control for a variable, area in which people lived, other than the one that was the focus of the study, diet.


> <b>(D) Questioning the ethics of having people in different areas follow diets that the researchers believe to be of varying levels of quality.
</b>


While this choice brings up an interesting issue, the editor did not do what this choice describes.


> <b>(E) Asserting that the variations in health outcomes observed resulted from factors characteristic of areas in which people lived rather than from differences in diet.
</b>


This choice is a little tricky, because the editor did bring up the possibility that the variable area in which people lived, rather than diet, was responsible for the variation in health outcomes. However, unlike what this choice describes, the editor did not assert that factors related to areas in which people lived were in fact responsible for the variation in health outcomes.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
