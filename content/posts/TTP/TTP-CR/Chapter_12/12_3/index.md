## Lesson Content
To solve Method of Reasoning Questions, follow this simple strategy:

<u>Step 1 - Carefully Read, Break Down, and Completely Understand the Argument</u>

The foundation of choosing the right answer to a Method of Reasoning question is understanding the argument presented in the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Do whatever you must to ensure that you understand the argument presented in a Method of Reasoning question. Go slowly. Read carefully. Take short notes on what you read. Be an active, engaged reader. Force yourself to become interested in the information presented. Pretend that you are Sherlock Homes solving a mystery. Remember, your goal is to carefully consider the structure of the argument, not the merits of the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

<u>Remember to Identify the Conclusion and Premises.</u>

As you analyze the argument, ask yourself: What is the author’s conclusion? What specific evidence does he or she use to support that conclusion? If there are two arguments, do the authors agree or disagree?

Since these questions are based on arguments, it’s important to use the skills you’ve already developed in analyzing arguments; you must continue to seek out the argument’s conclusion and premises, and, in some cases, consider any assumptions that are made.

<u>Step 2 – Identify How to Answer the Question</u>

To identify how to answer the question, read the question stem to see what type of answer you have to find.

> <em>The Attorney General uses which of the following techniques in responding to the Chief Technology Officer?</em>

<u>Step 3 – Summarize the Method in Simple Terms.</u>

Since the question asks us to define the way in which the argument proceeds, it’s important that we have a clear sense of how the argument is structured before we look at the answer choices.

Describe the logical flow of the argument in your own words.

It’s important to keep in mind that we are unlikely to see our description exactly matched by an answer choice.

Remember, the GMAT rewards careful reading.

<u>Step 4 - Go Through the Answer Choices One By One, Seeking to Remove the Most Obviously Incorrect Options</u>

Your next step is to carefully go through the answer choices one by one, seeking to remove the most obviously incorrect options.

It’s worth writing “A, B, C, D, E” on your dry erase pad.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

To prevent your crossing out an answer choice only mistakenly to choose it as correct, write out “A, B, C, D, E” on your dry erase pad. As you eliminate the choices that are obviously wrong, physically cross them out.
{{< /alert >}}

Now – as you are already aware, one of the most common mistakes that people make is to cross off the correct answer in their initial assessment of the answer choices.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

One of the most common mistakes that people make is to cross off the correct answer in their initial assessment of the answer choices. So, even after you’ve crossed off an answer, you must stay open to the fact that you may have crossed off the correct answer. Always be willing to reassess the situation.
{{< /alert >}}

At this point, remember that you can go back to the argument and check on anything said in an answer choice! The passage is right in front of you.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

If even one part of an answer choice describes something untrue about the argument, that answer is incorrect.
{{< /alert >}}

Also, remember that the verbs used by the speakers can help us arrive at correct answers.

<u>Step 5 – Carefully Analyze the Final Answer Choices</u>

Of the answers remaining, the correct answer must do the best job of capturing the logical structure of the argument or arguments.

At this point, one fundamental error that many people make is simply to re-read the two final choices and rely on “gut instinct” as to which is correct.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When you are down to two answers, you must not become complacent and make your best guess. No way. Now is the time to shine, to turn the jets to overdrive, and to relentlessly seek out the correct answer.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following examples.

##  Example 5 

During the past ten years, the fastest growing segment of the video game market has been so-called “mobile gaming.” The graphics and game play of these video games, which are played on mobile devices such as tablet computers and smartphones, are becoming increasingly sophisticated and complicated.

The argument above proceeds in which of the following ways?

1. [ ] (A) The author considers two different viewpoints and, in light of a set of facts, chooses one of the viewpoints.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) The author uses an analogy between situations as the evidence upon which to base a prediction about a category of technology.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) The author mentions a commonly held view and then, using recent developments as evidence, demonstrates that that view is no longer correct.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) The author arrives at a cause-and-effect conclusion using as evidence characteristics of a limited sample.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) The author bases a prediction about certain technology upon recent trends.

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Step 1 - Carefully Read, Break Down, and Completely Understand the Argument</u>


The argument supports the following conclusion:


<u>Conclusion</u>: At some point in the near future, stand-alone video game consoles will seem as antiquated as pinball machines.


The premises are that mobile gaming is the “fastest growing” part of the video game market and that mobile games have graphics and game play that “are becoming increasingly sophisticated and complicated.”


<u>Step 2 – Identify How to Answer the Question</u>


The question stem asks which way the argument proceeds. So, this is a Method of Reasoning question, and we have to choose the choice that correctly describes how the argument works.


<u>Step 3 – Summarize the Method in Simple Terms.</u>


What does the author do? The author describes recent trends (“fastest growing”) and (“increasingly sophisticated and complicated” “graphics and game play”) before making a prediction about the future (“at some point in the near future”).


A correct answer to this Method of Reasoning question might be:


<em>The author brings up recent trends and uses those trends as the basis for making a prediction about video games.</em>


Obviously, there might be different ways to describe the author’s method, but this summarizes the basic idea fairly well.


<u>Step 4 - Go Through the Answer Choices One By One, Seeking to Remove the Most Obviously Incorrect Options</u>


> <b>(A) The author considers two different viewpoints and, in light of a set of facts, chooses one of the viewpoints.
</b>


This choice is fairly obviously incorrect, as the author does not consider two viewpoints.


(B) <strong>Keep. </strong>Since the author does make a prediction and does use an analogy, let’s keep this one for now.


> <b>(C) The author mentions a commonly held view and then, using recent developments as evidence, demonstrates that that view is no longer correct.
</b>


While this choice could be tempting, as the author does use recent developments as evidence, the author does not actually challenge any particular view.


> <b>(D) The author arrives at a cause-and-effect conclusion using as evidence characteristics of a limited sample.
</b>


This jumps out as the one of the most obviously incorrect. While in a sense the argument does mention a cause, developments in the video game market, and an effect, the obsolescence of video game consoles, the conclusion is not a cause-and-effect conclusion. It is simply a prediction.


(E) <strong>Keep. </strong>This choice sounds correct. The author does make a prediction about certain technology and bases that prediction on recent trends.


<u>Step 5 – Carefully Analyze the Final Answer Choices</u>


Choice (B) looked possibly correct at first, mostly because of its wording, but, upon careful review, we can see that, while the author does make a prediction, the author does not use the analogy as the basis for making that prediction. The author uses the analogy merely as a way of illustrating the predicted outcome.


Choice (E), on the other hand, accurately describes what the author does.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

##  Example 6 

Eleni: Most of the owners of hybrid cars whom I know are much healthier than the average person.

Lee: People who drive hybrid cars tend to be concerned about ecology.

Lee responds to Eleni’s argument by doing which of the following?

1. [ ] (A) Providing reason to question the premise that Eleni uses as support for her conclusion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Demonstrating that owning hybrid car is necessary for experiencing better than average health but likely not sufficient for doing so.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Pointing out that, because Eleni uses a limited sample that may not be representative of people in general, the support for her conclusion is weak.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Undermining the force of the evidence that Eleni uses by showing that a factor that Eleni did not mention may be causing the pattern she cites.
5. [ ] (E) Showing that the approach that Eleni is considering for achieving her goal is not the only way to achieve that goal.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



<u>Step 1 - Carefully Read, Break Down, and Completely Understand the Argument</u>


Eleni concludes that, in order to be healthy, she should get a hybrid car, rather than a typical internal combustion engine only car. Her basis for the conclusion is the fact that most of the owners of hybrid cars whom she knows are much healthier than the average person.


Lee brings up an alternative explanation for the correlation between hybrid car ownership and better than average health, the fact that people who own hybrid cars are generally concerned about ecology, and that, therefore, on average, they likely travel by bicycle more than the average person does and include in their diets more locally grown organic fruits and vegetables than the average person includes, lifestyle habits likely to result in better than average health. In doing so, he makes a case for doubting that Eleni’s choice of car will make any difference.


<u>Step 2 – Identify How to Answer the Question</u>


The question stem asks how Lee responds. So, this is a Method of Reasoning question, and we have to choose the choice that correctly describes the way in which Lee responds.


It is not necessary to determine whether Lee responds effectively to Eleni’s argument. We must only identify Lee’s method of reasoning.


<u>Step 3 – Summarize the Method in Simple Terms.</u>


What does Lee do? Lee brings up a characteristics of hybrid car owners, concern for ecology and the lifestyle habits that result from that concern, that constitute an alternative cause for the correlation that Eleni has observed.


He then states the conclusion that he doubts that her choice will make any difference.


A correct answer to this Method of Reasoning question might be:


<em>Lee brings up an alternative cause for the observed correlation upon which Eleni bases her conclusion and, on the basis of the likelihood that that alternative cause is what actually underlies the correlation, concludes that there is reason to doubt Eleni’s conclusion.</em>


Obviously, there are different ways to describe what Lee did, but the above summarizes the basic idea fairly well.


<u>Step 4 - Go Through the Answer Choices One By One, Seeking to Remove the Most Obviously Incorrect Options</u>


> <b>(A) Providing reason to question the premise that Eleni uses as support for her conclusion.
</b>


This choice is fairly obviously incorrect, as Lee does not provide a reason to question Eleni’s premise that most of the owners of hybrid cars whom she knows are much healthier than the average person.


> <b>(B) Demonstrating that owning hybrid car is necessary for experiencing better than average health but likely not sufficient for doing so.
</b>


Since Lee says that he doubts that Eleni’s choice of car will make any difference, it’s clear that he is not suggesting that owning a hybrid car is necessary for experiencing better than average health, because, if owning a hybrid car were necessary for experiencing better than average health, the choice to get a hybrid car would be important for experiencing health and, therefore, <em>would</em> make a difference. So, we can be rather confident that this choice is not the correct answer.


> <b>(C) Pointing out that, because Eleni uses a limited sample that may not be representative of people in general, the support for her conclusion is weak.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, since Eleni does use a limited sample that may not be representative of people in general. However, since Lee says nothing at all about the size or nature of the sample that Eleni uses, we can be rather confident that this choice is incorrect.


(D) <strong>Keep. </strong>Lee does bring up a factor that Eleni did not mention, concern about ecology and the lifestyle habits that result from that concern, that may be causing the correlation upon which Eleni bases her conclusion. In doing so, he undermines the force of the evidence that Eleni provides, because, what he says makes clear that, even though there is a correlation between owning a hybrid car and better than average health, it is likely that, concern about ecology, and lifestyle habits associated with that concern, rather than owning a hybrid car, is the cause of better than average health. So, this choice could be our answer.


(E) <strong>Keep. </strong>Lee does bring up ways other than owning a hybrid car that would likely result in people’s experiencing better than average health. So, let’s keep this choice in case it’s correct.


<u>Step 5 – Carefully Analyze the Final Answer Choices</u>


Choice (E) may seem possibly correct at first, but if we carefully consider what Lee says, we can see that he never asserts or implies that owning a hybrid car is one way, along with other ways, to experience better than average health. Rather he mentions lifestyle habits that are likely to result in better than average health and then indicates that he doubts that one’s choice of car, which could be a choice to get a hybrid, will make any difference. So, through careful examination of choice (E), we can see clearly that it does not accurately describe what Lee does.


Choice (D), on the other hand, accurately describes what Lee does.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

Let’s now discuss incorrect answers in method of reasoning questions.
