## Lesson Content
Remember, Method of Reasoning questions specifically ask us to analyze how a given argument is structured.

In other words, our analysis and identification will necessarily stick to the structure and wording of the argument presented.

For instance, in a question involving an argument that questions the effectiveness of a plan, an answer choice that might say something about questioning the motives of the planners.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Many people consider homeschooling inferior to traditional classroom education because they perceive homeschooling as failing to provide children with a means for learning to interact socially. However, while it is true that children who participate in traditional schooling often have more interaction with peers than do homeschooled children, traditional schooling is rather unlike the “real” world that comes after graduation. So, there is no reason to believe that children who participate in traditional schooling end up any better prepared to interact socially than homeschooled children.</em>

> <em>The author of the above argument proceeds by</em>

> <em>(A) basing its conclusion on the assumption that one type of educational experience is more similar to the “real” world than another.</em>

> <em>(B) conceding that there is evidence in favor of the conclusion he opposes and then providing an additional consideration that supports his conclusion</em>

In reading the argument above, one sees that the author compares the experience of traditional education to the “real” world.

The truth is, however, that choice (A) goes beyond what we can tell from what the passage says, as the passage does not indicate that the author assumes that the experience of homeschooling is “more similar” to the “real” world than is the experience of traditional schooling.

Choice (B), on the other hand, matches exactly how the author proceeds when he first concedes that children who participate in traditional schooling often have more interaction with peers than do homeschooled children, and then provides the additional consideration that traditional schooling is rather unlike the “real” world that comes after graduation.

💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Answering Method of reasoning questions involves considering the structure and wording of the arguments presented; therefore, an answer choice that presents new components cannot be correct.

##  Example 7 

City planners have traditionally sought ways to make cities more accessible to drivers by building parking garages and lots on valuable city real estate.

Which of the following statements made by a city planner best illustrates the reasoning that underlies the change in policy described in the argument above?

1. [ ] (A) “As our cities become denser, the prevailing attitude that it is practical to own a car in an urban area must be broken down.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) “This city has long done a disservice to its core taxpayer base in favor of attracting entertainment dollars from the surrounding suburbs.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) “New bicycle sharing systems reduce the parking lot space necessary to support commerce in this area, thereby opening up valuable real estate for use by restaurants and shops.
4. [ ] (D) “More young people can afford to buy in our city now than could in the recent past, but, without entertainment options, we won’t be able to interest them in moving here.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) “Parking lots are unattractive and waste valuable space that could be used by retailers and other business that will generate more tax revenue for the city.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: hard



In this Method of Reasoning question, we are asked to mimic the structure of the argument. If we break the argument down in simple terms, we see that


<u>Fact One</u>: City planners have traditionally sought ways to make cities more accessible to drivers by building parking garages and lots on valuable city real estate.


<u>Fact Two</u>: Now, city planners are ignoring the preferences of automobile drivers in favor of accommodating the preferences of urban residents who use alternate forms of transportation.


That’s all. We need to find a statement that reflects these ideas. Be careful not to select an answer that brings up any assumptions or additional ideas. Such components are not asked for in this case.


> <b>(A) “As our cities become denser, the prevailing attitude that it is practical to own a car in an urban area must be broken down. ”
</b>


 While this may be true in reality, this statement’s focus on density is unsupported as nowhere in the stimulus is density mentioned as a reason for the policy shift. Remember – in answering these questions, go back and check!


> <b>(B) “This city has long done a disservice to its core taxpayer base in favor of attracting entertainment dollars from the surrounding suburbs. ”
</b>


 Again, this statement may very well be true in reality. However, the stimulus never mentions drivers from the suburbs, nor does it imply that city dwellers have been “done a disservice.”


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) “New bicycle sharing systems reduce the parking lot space necessary to support commerce in this area, thereby opening up valuable real estate for use by restaurants and shops. ”
</b>


 This answer essentially repeats the stimulus. That is, of course, exactly what the question asks us to do. The city dwellers want things that they can walk or bike to, rather than parking lots.


> <b>(D) “More young people can afford to buy in our city now than could in the recent past, but, without entertainment options, we won’t be able to interest them in moving here. ”
</b>


 The stimulus never, of course, mentions affordability. Also, while the stimulus does discuss the lifestyle that urban dwellers prefer, the answer fails to mention changes in transportation.


> <b>(E) “Parking lots are unattractive and waste valuable space that could be used by retailers and other business that will generate more tax revenue for the city. ”
</b>


 Again, this may very well be true; however, the stimulus never mentions tax revenue.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
