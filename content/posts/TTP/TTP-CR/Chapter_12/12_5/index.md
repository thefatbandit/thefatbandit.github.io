## Lesson Content
Some Method of Reasoning Questions present us with stimuli that include one or two sections that are in boldface type.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Some Method of Reasoning Questions present us with stimuli that include one or two sections that are in boldface type. In answering such a question, our job is to identify the role that any section in boldfaced type plays in the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

Thus, in Boldface Method of Reasoning questions, the correct answer is the one that accurately describes the role, or roles, of any sections in boldface.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Boldface Method of Reasoning questions, when there are two boldfaced sections, each of the five answer choices provides a description of the role played by the first boldfaced section followed by a description of the role played by the second boldfaced section.
{{< /alert >}}

As in answering any Method of Reasoning question, in answering a Boldface question, we are far less concerned with the merits of the argument than we are with the structure of the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

As in answering any Method of Reasoning question, in answering a Boldface question, we are far less concerned with the merits of the argument than we are with the structure of the argument. In other words, in answering these questions, our goal is not to strengthen, weaken, or evaluate the argument. Instead, our goal is to assess how the argument is structured and determine what roles the bolded sections play in the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following example:

> <em>The use of body cameras by police was expected to result in a reduction in inappropriate use of force by police officers. However, <strong>as the use of body cameras has increased, the annual number of citizen complaints about use of force by police officers has remained the same.</strong> Clearly, the presence of body cameras does not affect police behavior.</em>

> <em>The portion in boldface plays which of the following roles in the argument above?</em>

> <em>(A) It serves as evidence supporting the argument’</em><em>s main conclusion.</em>

We can analyze the flow of this argument and see that it begins by mentioning an expectation, then provides information on what has actually occurred, and, finally, arrives at a conclusion based on that information.

Let’s consider another example:

> <em>People have perceived the markings on an ancient Mayan calendar as indicating that the Maya had predicted that the world would end in December of 2012. <strong>This perception is likely incorrect.</strong> For the Maya of the time of the creation of the calendar, time was considered infinite. Further, <strong>other Mayan inscriptions indicate that the ancient Mayas considered a date 4000 years after December 2012 a significant date in the future of the world.</strong></em>

> <em>In the argument given, the two boldfaced portions play which of the following roles?</em>

> <em>(A) The first is evidence supporting the author’s main conclusion; the second is that main conclusion.</em>

> <em>(B) The first is the author’s main conclusion; the second provides support for that conclusion.</em>

Now, we’re being asked to determine what roles two bolded portions play in an argument.

Since the first portion in boldface precedes the second, we might be tempted to go with choice (A), since, in most of the arguments that we have seen so far in GMAT Critical Reasoning, the evidence has preceded the conclusions.

However, if we carefully read what the bolded portions say and consider them in the context of the argument as a whole, it becomes apparent that, actually, the first portion in boldface states the author’s main conclusion and the second portion in boldface provides support for that conclusion.

As we will discuss further, the conclusion’s being placed in a position other than at the end of the passage is typical of Boldface questions.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The arguments in Boldface questions are usually structured in ways meant to make the roles of the statements in the passages non-obvious. So, correctly answering boldface questions requires careful reading.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 11 

Many people experiencing chronic back pain opt to undergo surgery, hoping to solve the pain issues.

In the passage above, the two portions in boldface play which of the following roles?

1. [ ] (A) The first is a conclusion opposed by the argument’s main conclusion; the second supports the main conclusion stated in the passage.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) The first is evidence in support of the main conclusion stated in the passage; the second is also used to support the main conclusion.
3. [ ] (C) The first is a consideration that supports the main conclusion stated in the passage; the second is that main conclusion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) The first is a consideration that conflicts with the author’s main conclusion; the second is evidence that the author uses to support the main conclusion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) The first is a consideration that weighs in favor of the author’s position; the second is a consideration that weighs against that position.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Let’s go through this passage to see what’s going on in it.


- It starts off by mentioning a choice that people commonly make, to undergo surgery to address back pain issues.


- Then the author states a position on this choice: “In many cases this choice is misguided.”


- Then, the next two sentences discuss issues with surgery for back pain.


The only statement that is supported by any other statements is the one that states the author’s position, which is supported by the last two sentences of the passage. Thus, that position is the main conclusion of the passage, and the two sentences that follow it are supporting evidence.


> <b>(A) The first is a conclusion opposed by the argument’s main conclusion; the second supports the main conclusion stated in the passage.
</b>


While the position of the author is in opposition to many people’s choosing to undergo back surgery, the choice that the author opposes is not mentioned in the first portion in boldface. So, the first part of this answer choice is incorrect.


Since the second bolded portion does support the main conclusion, the second part of this answer choice is correct. However, since the first part of this answer choice is incorrect, this choice cannot be the correct answer to the question.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B) The first is evidence in support of the main conclusion stated in the passage; the second is also used to support the main conclusion.
</b>


Since, as discussed in the introduction of this explanation, both the first portion in boldface and the second portion in boldface provide support for the author’s main conclusion, this choice is correct.


> <b>(C) The first is a consideration that supports the main conclusion stated in the passage; the second is that main conclusion.
</b>


The first part of this answer choice is correct, as the first portion in boldface does support the main conclusion stated in the passage. However, while the second boldface portion does appear at the end of the passage, it is not the conclusion of the argument. Thus, the second part of this choice is incorrect, and, therefore, this choice is not the correct answer.


> <b>(D) The first is a consideration that conflicts with the author’s main conclusion; the second is evidence that the author uses to support the main conclusion.
</b>


 The first portion in boldface does not conflict with the author’s main conclusion, but, rather, supports it. Therefore, even though the second part of this choice correctly describes the second bolded portion as evidence that supports the conclusion, this choice is not the correct answer.


> <b>(E) The first is a consideration that weighs in favor of the author’s position; the second is a consideration that weighs against that position.
</b>


As this choice says, the first bolded portion is indeed a consideration that weights in favor of the author’s position. However, unlike what this choice says, the second bolded portion supports rather than weighs against the author’s position. Therefore, this choice is not the correct answer.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
