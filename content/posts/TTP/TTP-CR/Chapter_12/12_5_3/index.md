## Lesson Content
As you now know, each of the five answer choices for a Boldface Method of Reasoning question either provides a description of the role played by one bolded section or provides a description of the role of the first of two bolded sections followed by a description of the role played by the second bolded section.

The good news is that these answer choices provide us with clear ways to eliminate incorrect answers.

Consider the following Boldface Method of Reasoning question answer choice:

> <em>(A) The first provides indisputable facts in support of a position; the second presents a consideration weighing against taking such a course of action.</em>

Notice that the answer choice describes, not necessarily correctly, the role that the first bolded sentence plays in the argument and then describes the role that the second bolded sentence plays in the argument.

Now – in the stimulus, it is either completely true that the first bolded sentence “<em>provides indisputable facts in support of a position” </em>and the second bolded sentence<em> “presents a consideration weighting against taking such a course of action,” </em>or it is not true! There is no middle ground.

When we return to the stimulus, we ask “Does the first bolded statement provide indisputable facts in support of a position?<em>” </em>If, upon returning to the stimulus, we find that it is not true that the first bolded sentence provides indisputable facts in support of a position, then that answer choice cannot be correct.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering Boldface Method of Reasoning questions, carefully compare the answer choices with the bolded sections of the passages to eliminate choices that cannot be correct.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 13 

While the appendix was long considered a useless part of the human body that somehow remained as evolution occurred, <strong>it is now apparent that the appendix is a useful piece of anatomy that plays a specific role.</strong> The previously accepted position on the appendix becomes suspect when one becomes aware of the discovery that at least thirty-two animal species have independently developed anatomy similar to the human appendix, as <strong>the likelihood that evolution made the same oversight in all of these cases is not great.</strong> Moreover, careful examination of the structure of the appendix reveals that it is well designed to play a unique role in the maintenance of gut flora.

In the argument above, the two portions in <strong>boldface</strong> play which of the following roles?

1. [x] (A) The first is the main conclusion of the argument; the second is a consideration in opposition to a conclusion that the main conclusion opposes.
2. [ ] (B) The first is a consideration in opposition to a conclusion that the main conclusion of the argument opposes; the second is the argument’s main conclusion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) The first is an intermediate conclusion that is used to support the argument’s main conclusion; the second is the argument’s main conclusion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) The first and second each provide evidence for the argument’s main conclusion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) The first is a position taken by the author; the second is a fact that author concedes conflicts with the position taken.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

##### Question Level: medium



Once we have read the entire passage, our next move is to locate the main conclusion of argument, if there is one.


The main conclusion of this argument is:


<u>Conclusion</u>: <strong>it is now apparent that the appendix is a useful piece of anatomy that plays a specific role.</strong>


That the statement above is the main conclusion becomes apparent if we notice the following.


The first clause, “the appendix has long been considered … as evolution occurred,” is just background information that neither supports nor is supported by any other statement in the passage.


The last two sentences, “The previously accepted position …<strong> is not great</strong>,” and “Moreover, careful examination … gut flora,” serve as support for the statement made in the second sentence, “<strong>it is now apparent that the appendix is a useful piece of anatomy that plays a specific role.</strong>”


So, it’s clear that the statement made in the second sentence is the main conclusion of the argument. Now, let’s check the answer choices.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(A) The first is the main conclusion of the argument; the second is a consideration in opposition to a conclusion that the main conclusion opposes.
</b>


As discussed above, the first is the main conclusion of the argument. The second is a consideration in opposition to the conclusion mentioned in the first statement made in the passage, the conclusion that the appendix is “a useless part of the human body that somehow remained as evolution occurred.”


> <b>(B) The first is a consideration in opposition to a conclusion that the main conclusion of the argument opposes; the second is the argument’s main conclusion.
</b>


While the first is in opposition to a position that the main conclusion opposes, the first is not a consideration but, rather, the main conclusion itself. The second is not the conclusion, but rather, evidence that supports the main conclusion.


> <b>(C) The first is an intermediate conclusion that is used to support the argument’s main conclusion; the second is the argument’s main conclusion.
</b>


The first is not an intermediate conclusion but, rather, the argument’s main conclusion. The second is not the main conclusion but rather a consideration that supports the main conclusion.


> <b>(D) The first and second each provide evidence for the argument’s main conclusion.
</b>


The first half of this answer is incorrect. As discussed above, the first bolded section is not evidence supporting the main conclusion but, rather, the main conclusion of the argument. The second half of this choice is correct, as the second bolded portion does support the main conclusion.


> <b>(E) The first is a position taken by the author; the second is a fact that author concedes conflicts with the position taken.
</b>


The first half of this choice is correct, as the first bolded portion is the main conclusion of the argument, and, as such, is a position taken by the author. The second half is not correct, as the second bolded portion supports rather than conflicts with the author’s position.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
