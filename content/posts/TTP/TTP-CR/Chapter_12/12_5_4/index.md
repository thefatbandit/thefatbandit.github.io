## Lesson Content
If the passages in Boldface questions were structured in the way in which the passages in typical Weaken the Argument questions are structured, with some background information, perhaps two premises, and a conclusion at the end, determining the roles of the sections of the arguments would be rather easy.

Here are some of the reasons why correctly identifying the roles of statements in and choosing the correct answers to Boldface questions can be tricky:

> - The conclusions are inconspicuous.

> - The bolded portions can be worded and positioned to look as if they play roles other than the roles that they play.

> - The passages often contain multiple conclusions, along with premises to support the conclusions.

> - Incorrect answer choices often say things that are partly correct or closely related to what is correct.

Let’s cover each of these.
