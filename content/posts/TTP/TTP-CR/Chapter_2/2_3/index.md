## Lesson Content
Most of the time, recognizing an Identify the Assumption question is easy, because these questions utilize predictable language to ask for answer choices that fill logical gaps in arguments.

> <em>Which of the following is an assumption upon which the argument depends?</em>

> <em>Which of the following is an assumption made in drawing the conclusion above?</em>

> <em>The argument depends on which of the following?</em>

> <em>The conclusion drawn above is based on the assumption that _______.</em>

> <em>The conclusion above would be more reasonably drawn if which of the following were inserted into the argument as an additional premise?</em>

> <em>The argument depends on assuming which of the following?</em>

> <em>The argument above logically depends on which of the following?</em>

> <em>In order for the conclusion to be true, which of the following must be true?</em>

While not all of the questions above include the word “assumption,” you can tell that the right answer to a question like any of them will be an assumption that fills a gap in the logic of an argument.

💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Learn to recognize the common and predictable language used by Identify the Assumption questions and understand what these questions are asking you to do.

##  Example 4 

Side by side tests of fruits and vegetables grown using conventional and organic methods have found no significant differences between the levels of key nutrients in the conventionally grown produce and the levels of those nutrients in the organically grown produce.

Which of the following is an assumption upon which the argument depends?

1. [ ] (A) Fruits and vegetables that are grown using organic methods do not taste different from those grown using conventional methods.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Plants take in nutrients from the soil as a necessary aspect of their growth processes.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Conventionally grown produce does not contain significant levels of health harming chemicals that organically grown produce does not contain.
4. [ ] (D) The differences between the prices of most organically grown produce and those of most conventionally grown produce are not justified.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) The methods used in farming organically grown produce tend to be more labor intensive than those used in farming conventionally grown produce.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: easy



From what’s being asked, we can clearly see that this is an Assumption question:


<em>Which of the following is an assumption upon which the argument depends?</em>


Breakdown of the Argument:


<u>Premise</u>: Side by side tests have found no significant differences between levels of certain nutrients in organically grown produce and the levels of those nutrients in conventionally grown produce.


<u>Conclusion</u>: There is no reason to believe that there is any health benefit to be derived from eating organically grown produce instead of conventionally grown produce.


The argument uses one specific fact about differences between organically grown produce and conventionally grown produce to support the general conclusion that there is no reason to believe that there is any health benefit to be derived from eating organically grown produce rather than conventionally grown produce.


Coming to that conclusion from that one fact requires assuming that the only way in which eating organically grown produce could be better for health than eating conventionally grown produce is through the organically grown produce’s having greater levels of certain nutrients.


What if there were some other way in which eating organically grown produce is better for one’s health? It would be naïve to think that there is only one way in which eating organically grown produce is better for one’s health.


> <b>(A) Fruits and vegetables that are grown using organic methods do not taste different from those grown using conventional methods.
</b>


The conclusion is specifically about the health benefits of eating organically grown produce rather than conventionally grown produce, and is not that there is no difference at all between the two types of produce.


Concluding that the health benefits of eating the two types of produce are the same does not require assuming that there is no difference between the tastes of the two types of produce.


> <b>(B) Plants take in nutrients from the soil as a necessary aspect of their growth processes.
</b>


This choice could be tempting because it mentions nutrients, and the support for the argument’s conclusion is that there are no significant differences between levels of certain nutrients in the two types of produce.


However, we have to remember that Critical Reasoning is not a word matching game. To arrive at correct answers consistently, we have to consider the logical implications of what choices say, not just look for choices that say things that sound like what the passages say.


The fact that there are no significant differences between levels of certain nutrients in the two types of produce supports the conclusion regardless of whether it is true that plants take in nutrients from the soil as a necessary aspect of their growth processes.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) Conventionally grown produce does not contain significant levels of health harming chemicals that organically grown produce does not contain.
</b>


 Concluding that, since there are no significant differences between levels of certain nutrients in the two types of produce, eating organic produce is not better for health requires assuming that eating conventionally grown produce is not somehow harmful to health in a way that eating organically grown produce is not.


After all, what if it were the case that conventionally grown produce contains significant levels of health harming chemicals that organically grown produce does not contain? The argument would fall apart, as, while the premise would be true, the conclusion that there is no reason to believe that there is any health benefit to be derived from eating organically grown produce rather than conventionally grown produce would not be true.


Thus, the information presented by this choice MUST BE TRUE in order for the logic of the argument to work.


> <b>(D) The differences between the prices of most organically grown produce and those of most conventionally grown produce are not justified.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, because what it says may seem to be supported by the statements that appear in the argument. However, we are not looking for something that can be concluded from what the argument says, but rather for an assumption integral to the argument.


> <b>(E) The methods used in farming organically grown produce tend to be more labor intensive than those used in farming conventionally grown produce.
</b>


 While what this choice says may be true in reality, we are looking not for a true statement but rather for an assumption necessary for arriving at the argument’s conclusion.


The evidence about the levels of nutrients in the two types of produce would support the conclusion about the health benefits of eating the two types of produce regardless of whether it is true that the methods used in farming organically grown produce tend to be more labor intensive than those used in farming conventionally grown produce.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
