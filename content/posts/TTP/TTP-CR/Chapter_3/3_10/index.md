## Lesson Content
Weaken the Argument questions will sometimes include the word “except” or “not” in their question stems to make the questions a bit more challenging.

> <em>Any of the following, if true, undermines support for the speaker’s claim EXCEPT:</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, does <u>not</u> weaken the argument above?</em>

When a Weaken question has the word “except” or “not” in the stem, four out of the five answer choices will weaken the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When a Weaken question has the word “except” or “not” in the stem, four out of the five answer choices will weaken the argument. These answer choices will be incorrect. The correct answer will be the one that does not weaken the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

Since an “except” or “not” question asks us to do the opposite of what we normally do in answering an Weaken question, in order to avoid becoming confused when answering such a question, we can mark each of the choices with a “Yes,” if the choice undermines the support for the argument (weakens the argument), or with a “No,” if the choice does not undermine the support for the argument (does not weaken the argument).

When we are done labeling, we will have labeled only one option “No” (assuming we did the labeling correctly), and that option will be the answer.

Consider the following complicated question stem:

> <em>Each of the following, if true, would counter the objection to the interpretation advanced above EXCEPT:</em>

When we encounter a stem like this, we should follow the below four steps:

> (1) Identify the “interpretation” advanced in the argument.

> (2) Identify the objection to this interpretation in the argument.

> (3) For every answer choice, ask yourself, “Does this answer choice counter (weaken the support for) the objection?” - YES or NO

> (4) The answer choice for which you have written NO is the correct answer.

Below is another complicated question stem that asks for a single statement that simultaneously weakens one hypothesis while strengthening another:

<em>Which of the following, if discovered in experiments, would support one of the two hypotheses and undermine the other?</em>

Though the words “except” and “not” do not appear in the above question stem, the question demands a similar organized process.

> (1) Identify the two hypotheses.

> (2) For every option, ask yourself “Does this option support hypothesis 1?” H1-YES or H1-NO

> (3) For every option, ask yourself “Does this option support hypothesis 2?” H2-YES or H2-NO

> (4) The answer choice with one YES and one NO is the correct one.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When a Weaken the Argument question contains the words “except” or “not,” follow a step-by-step approach in evaluating the options.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 32 

Caps and Crowns Dentistry plans to acquire new dental patients at its dental office by offering a free examination, dental cleaning, and set of x-rays for all new patients.

All of the following, if true, weaken the argument, EXCEPT:

1. [ ] (A) Generally, people who go to a dental office as a result of its offering free examination, dental cleaning, and x-rays do not return to that office for other dental services.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) The materials used by Caps and Crowns Dentistry in performing an examination and a dental cleaning and creating a set of x-rays cost the office $2,600.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Caps and Crowns Dentistry is staffed with some of the most qualified dentists practicing in any office within a 50-mile radius of Caps and Crowns.
4. [ ] (D) Because they take unusually good care of their dental health, the vast majority of patients whom the plan would attract spend very little on dental procedures during any two-year period.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) People who would patronize Caps and Crowns Dentistry are unlikely to spend money on dental procedures for at least three years because of a downturn in the local economy.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Breakdown of the argument:


<u>Premise</u>: Caps and Crowns Dentistry plans to acquire new dental patients at its dental office by offering a free examination, dental cleaning, and set of x-rays for all new patients.


<u>Premise</u>: Nationwide data indicates that, on average, each dental patient spends $2,500 on dental procedures during any two-year period.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Over the next two years, Caps and Crowns Dentistry will get from the new patients that it acquires by implementing the plan revenue that will exceed the cost of implementing the plan.


Caps and Crowns Dentistry has a plan. Any time someone presents a plan, he assumes that the plan will work. In other words, he assumes that there are not variables that will enter the equation that will spoil the plan. In the TTP Course, we refer to such variables as “malicious variables.” When an author presents a plan, he assumes that there are no malicious variables. What malicious variables might Caps and Crowns Dentistry have missed?


Since this question is a Weaken EXCEPT question, we have to go through the five choices asking ourselves in each case, “Does this choice present a factor that will weaken the conclusion?”


In four cases, the answer will be “Yes.” We will eliminate those choices.


In one case, the answer will be “No,” and that choice will be our answer.


> <b>(A) Generally, people who go to a dental office as a result of its offering free examination, dental cleaning, and x-rays do not return to that office for other dental services.
</b>


 What this choice says weakens the argument.


The fact that, generally, people who go to a dental office as a result of its offering free examination, dental cleaning, and x-rays do not return to that office for other dental services is a reason to question the conclusion that Caps and Crowns Dentistry will get from the new patients revenue that will exceed the cost of implementing the plan.


After all, if the new patients won’t return for other services, Cap and Crowns Dentistry is likely to generate no revenue at all by offering free services for new patients.


Weakens the argument? Yes.


> <b>(B) The materials used by Caps and Crowns Dentistry in performing an examination and a dental cleaning and creating a set of x-rays cost the office $2,600.
</b>


 What this choice says weakens the argument.


The passage tells us that, on average, each dental patient spends $2,500 on dental procedures during any two-year period. So, if the materials used by Caps and Crowns Dentistry in performing an examination and a dental cleaning and creating a set of x-rays cost the office $2,600, then Caps and Crowns Dentistry would not earn over the next two years revenue that will exceed the cost of implementing the plan.


Weakens the argument? Yes.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) Caps and Crowns Dentistry is staffed with some of the most qualified dentists practicing in any office within a 50-mile radius of Caps and Crowns.
</b>


 What this answer choice says goes in the wrong direction.


Rather than weaken the argument, this answer choice says gives us more faith in the conclusion.


After all, if Caps and Crowns Dentistry is staffed with some of the most qualified dentists practicing in any office within a 50-mile radius of Caps and Crowns, then we have more reason to think that new patients will stick around for long enough for Caps and Crowns Dentistry to earn its money back.


So, since this choice strengthens, rather than weakens, the support for the conclusion, this choice is the correct answer to this Weaken EXCEPT question.


Weakens the argument? No.


> <b>(D) Because they take unusually good care of their dental health, the vast majority of patients whom the plan would attract spend very little on dental procedures during any two-year period.
</b>


 What this answer choice says weakens the argument.


The fact that, because they take unusually good care of their dental health, the vast majority of patients whom the plan would attract spend very little on dental procedures during any two-year period is a reason to question the conclusion that Caps and Crowns Dentistry will earn over the next two years revenue that will exceed the cost of implementing the plan.


After all, if the new patients will spend very little, then Caps and Crowns Dentistry will earn very little revenue by acquiring them.


Weakens the argument? Yes.


> <b>(E) People who would patronize Caps and Crowns Dentistry are unlikely to spend money on dental procedures for at least three years because of a downturn in the local economy.
</b>


 What this answer choice says weakens the argument.


The fact that people who would patronize Caps and Crowns Dentistry are unlikely to spend money on dental procedures for at least three years because of a downturn in the local economy is a reason to question the conclusion that Caps and Crowns Dentistry will earn over the next two years revenue that will exceed the cost of implementing the plan.


How will Caps and Crowns Dentistry get revenue in the next two years from patients who won’t spend money for at least three years?


Weakens the argument? Yes.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Example 33 

Bans on smoking inside public establishments have been shown to be more effective at reducing per capita cigarette sales in colder climates than in warmer ones.

All of the following, if true, weaken the argument, EXCEPT:

1. [ ] (A) Boselton is known for its unique success in getting people to quit smoking through a program that has enabled all but the most diehard smokers living there to quit.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) A significant county-wide increase in taxes on cigarettes will go into effect simultaneously with the ban.
3. [ ] (C) In comparison with residents of other cities in the county, residents of Boselton spend a much smaller proportion of their time in public establishments.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) In Boselton, unlike in other cities in the county, it is generally the case that the people who smoke are not the same people who tend to frequent public establishments.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Unlike most public establishments located in other cities in the county, few in Boselton currently allow people to smoke inside.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



This argument is based on two premises:


<u>Premise</u>: Bans on smoking inside public establishments have been shown to be more effective at reducing per capita cigarette sales in colder climates than in warmer ones.


<u>Premise</u>: Boselton is much colder than other cities in the county.


Given these premises, it does seem logical to conclude the following:


<u>Conclusion</u>: Boselton will be the city in its county that experiences the greatest decrease in per capita cigarettes sales as the result of a ban on smoking in public establishments.


The truth is, however, that there could be, and probably are, factors other than climate that will affect the results of the ban.


Since this question asks for the one choice that does <em>not</em> weaken the argument, we have to go through the five choices, asking ourselves in each case, “Does this choice present a factor that will cast doubt on the conclusion about the ban affecting Boselton the most?” In four cases, the answer will be “Yes.” In one case, the answer will be “No,” and that choice will be our answer.


> <b>(A) Boselton is known for its unique success in getting people to quit smoking through a program that has enabled all but the most diehard smokers living there to quit.
</b>


 By mentioning that Boselton has had “unique success,” this choice differentiates Boselton from other cities. What’s different about Boselton? Most smokers there have quit, and only diehard smokers are left. Of course, there’s a good chance that a ban on smoking in public establishments will not cause diehard smokers to significantly reduce their smoking. So, this choice gives us reason to question the conclusion that Boselton will experience the greatest decrease in per capita cigarette sales as a result of the ban.


Furthermore, the conclusion is about a “per capita” decrease in cigarette sales. “Per capita” cigarette sales in a given town is defined as the following:


total cigarette sales in a given town / total population in that town


Because, in Boselton, most of the people have already quit smoking, per capita cigarette sales in Boselton would already be very low. In other words, the numerator of the fraction has already dropped significantly. Therefore, we would not expect to see another large drop in Boselton’s per capita cigarette sales.


Meanwhile, we know that Boselton is unique in this aspect. In other words, the other towns have not experienced already a large drop in the number of smokers, and so we’d expect per capita cigarette sales to be much higher in those towns than in Boselton. Thus, we’d expect the indoor smoking ban to have a larger impact on per capita cigarette sales in these towns than in Boselton.


Weakener? Yes.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B) A significant county-wide increase in taxes on cigarettes will go into effect simultaneously with the ban.
</b>


 Since this tax will affect all cities in the county equally, there’s no clear reason to believe that this tax will somehow offset the fact that Boselton is the coldest city and thus should experience the biggest effect from the ban.


Weakener? No.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) In comparison with residents of other cities in the county, residents of Boselton spend a much smaller proportion of their time in public establishments.
</b>


 The effect on per capita cigarette sales comes from people’s not smoking in public establishments. So, if people in Boselton don’t spend as much time in public establishments as people in other cities do, then they, and their purchases of cigarettes, won’t be affected by the ban as much as people in other cities will be. Thus, even though Boselton is colder, its per capita cigarette sales may not decrease as much as those of other cities will.


Weakener? Yes.


> <b>(D) In Boselton, unlike in other cities in the county, it is generally the case that the people who smoke are not the same people who tend to frequent public establishments.
</b>


 The effect on per capita cigarette sales comes from people’s not smoking in public establishments. If, unlike the smokers in other cities, the people in Boselton who smoke don’t even go to public establishment, then their smoking won’t be affected the way the smoking of people in other cities will. Thus, even though Boselton is colder, its per capita cigarette sales may not decrease as much as those of other cities will.


Weakener? Yes.


> <b>(E) Unlike most public establishments located in other cities in the county, few in Boselton currently allow people to smoke inside.
</b>


 If, unlike the public establishments in other cities in the county, most public establishments in Boselton have already banned smoking inside, then, in Boselton, unlike in the other cities, the ban won’t make much difference in people’s smoking in public establishments and, thus, won’t have much effect on per capita cigarette sales.


Weakener? Yes.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
