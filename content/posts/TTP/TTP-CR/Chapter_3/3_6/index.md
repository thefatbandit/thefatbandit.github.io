## Lesson Content
Students often get the impression that, in answering Weaken the Argument questions, they should eliminate any answer choice that seems to bring up a new topic.

The truth is, however, that all correct answers to Weaken the Argument questions will bring up new information, and it’s important to understand that information that does not resemble the information that makes up the argument is not the same as irrelevant information.

So, for sure don’t discount an answer choice simply because it brings up a new topic.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a Weaken the Argument Question, do not discount an answer choice just because it brings up a new topic.
{{< /alert >}}

Test-takers using that strategy often eliminate the correct answer first! The reason people often eliminate the correct answer first is that the correct answer to a Weaken question is often <em>the one that looks the most different from the information that makes up the argument</em>.

Therefore, our job is clear; our only job is to find the answer choice that most seriously weakens the argument provided in the question, regardless of whether that answer resembles the information that makes up the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a Weaken the Argument question, our only job is to find the answer choice that most seriously weakens the argument provided in the question, regardless of whether that answer resembles the information that makes up the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s practice noticing how new information can weaken an argument.

##  Example 8 

The island province of Mar de Isla will begin offering a 5 percent discount on the tax its residents must pay yearly on any real estate they own.

Which of the following, if true, most seriously weakens the above argument?

1. [ ] (A) Because most residents of the island know one another well, they are very willing to help each other out when needed.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) The rate of the tax residents of Mar de Isla must pay on real estate they own is slightly lower than that of similar taxes paid by residents of most nearby islands.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Over time, traditional passing of property from generation to generation has put almost all Mar de Isla real estate into the hands of less than 20 percent of its residents.
4. [ ] (D) Many residents of Mar de Isla could easily find 100 hours to volunteer over the course of a year.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Some Mar de Isla residents already donate some of their time to community endeavors, such as trash collection, dog walking, and drug counseling.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Background</u>:The island province of Mar de Isla will begin offering a 5 percent discount on the tax its residents must pay yearly on any real estate they own.


<u>Background</u>: To qualify, a resident seeking the discount must provide documentation indicating that he or she has completed at least 100 hours of community service during the tax year for which the discount is requested.


<u>Premise</u>: A 5 percent tax savings will be significant to Mar de Isla residents.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Most of the residents will choose to participate in the program.


The author has presented a plan. Anytime an author presents a plan, she assumes that the plan will work. In other words, she assumes that there are not variables that will enter into the equation that will spoil the plan. In the TTP Course, we refer to such variables as “malicious variables.” When an author presents a plan, she assumes that there are no malicious variables.


What might the author have failed to consider? What malicious variables might have gone unnoticed?


> <b>(A) Because most residents of the island know one another well, they are very willing to help each other out when needed.
</b>


 What this choice says is in line with what the argument says.


After all, if it is true that most residents of the island know one another well, so they are very willing to help each other out when needed, then we have more conviction that most of the residents will choose to participate in the program, performing at least 100 hours of community service.


Of course, we’re not seeking a choice that is in line with the argument’s conclusion. We’re seeking one that weakens the argument.


> <b>(B) The rate of the tax residents of Mar de Isla must pay on real estate they own is slightly lower than that of similar taxes paid by residents of most nearby islands.
</b>


 The passage states as fact that a 5 percent tax savings will be significant to Mar de Isla residents. So, that premise must be true even though the Mar de Isla tax rate is lower than tax rates of most nearby islands. Thus, the plan proposed by the argument could work.


After all, whatever the tax rate is, if a 5 percent savings is significant for residents, then most may very well be willing to do 100 hours of community service to get that 5 percent savings.


What this answer choice says does not weaken the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) Over time, traditional passing of property from generation to generation has put almost all Mar de Isla real estate into the hands of less than 20 percent of its residents.
</b>


 The conclusion of the argument is that <em>most </em>of the residents will choose to participate in the program. This conclusion is based on the fact that Mar de Isla will begin offering a 5 percent discount on the tax its residents must pay yearly on any real estate they own.


Of course, this 5 percent discount will be an incentive to complete 100 hours of community service only to people who own real estate. People who don’t own real estate do not have to pay any real estate tax. So, the discount would not affect them.


Now, if what this answer choice says is true, and traditional passing of property from generation to generation has put almost all Mar de Isla real estate into the hands of less than 20 percent of its residents, then MOST of the residents (more than 80 percent) do not own real estate.


Therefore, the 5 percent discount on the real estate tax would be an incentive to less than 20 percent of the residents, and less than 20 percent of the residents is clearly not “most of the residents.”


Given what this choice says, the fact that Mar de Isla will begin offering a 5 percent discount on the real estate tax to residents who complete at least 100 hours of community service gives us very little reason to believe that “most of the residents will choose to participate in the program,” as the conclusion states.


The information in this answer choice clearly weakens the argument.


Notice that what this answer choice says about traditional passing of property from generation to generation is new information that is quite different from any information presented in the passage. That’s OK. Correct answers that present new information are normal in Weaken the Argument questions.


> <b>(D) Many residents of Mar de Isla could easily find 100 hours to volunteer over the course of a year.
</b>


 What this choice says is in line with what the argument says, and perhaps even slightly strengthens the argument.


After all, if it is true that many residents of Mar de Isla could easily find 100 hours to volunteer over the course of a year, then we have more conviction that the plan will work.


Imagine if the residents could NOT easily find 100 hours to volunteer over the course of a year. Wouldn’t that be a serious impediment to the success of the plan? So, if what this answer choice says is true, and residents CAN easily find the time to volunteer, then this information provides some support for the argument’s conclusion.


Of course, we’re not seeking a choice that is in line with the argument’s conclusion. We’re seeking one that weakens the argument.


> <b>(E) Some Mar de Isla residents already donate some of their time to community endeavors, such as trash collection, dog walking, and drug counseling.
</b>


 We can’t be sure exactly how what this choice says affects the argument.


Does the fact that some Mar de Isla residents already donate some of their time to community endeavors indicate that these people are willing to donate their time and make us more convinced that the plan will work, or does it indicate that people are already doing all the volunteering they can handle and won’t be willing to do more? We just don’t know, right?


Be careful to not choose as correct an answer choice that could be interpreted in multiple ways when what you’re looking for is an answer that weakens the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Example 9 

Administrators at City Hospital are concerned that their doctors have less time than ever to work on patient charts, claiming that the doctors devote more shift hours to seeing patients than they did two years ago.

Which of the following, if true, most seriously weakens the argument?

1. [ ] (A) Two years ago, City Hospital implemented an electronic charts system that significantly improved the efficiency and effectiveness of the system used for recording patient information.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) City Hospital recently implemented a program that, using video diagnosis, outsources some aspects of handling certain patients’ cases to a satellite clinic.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) A hospital in a nearby county specializing in complex cases closed its doors two years ago, referring any future patients experiencing such situations to City Hospital.
4. [ ] (D) The hiring process at City Hospital has become notably more rigorous in the past two years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) City Hospital has long experienced difficulty in matching each patient case with the doctor likely to handle that case most efficiently.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



<u>Premise 1</u>: The number of patients has not changed.


<u>Premise 2</u>: Doctors’ shift-lengths have not changed.


<u>Premise 3</u>: The number of doctors on shift at any given time has not changed.


<u>Conclusion</u>: This claim (that doctors devote more shift hours to seeing patients) is likely unwarranted.


What is the assumption being made here? The assumption is that there are no variables other than the ones mentioned that can affect how much time doctors can spend with their patients. Is this a good assumption? Probably not, right?


> <b>(A) Two years ago, City Hospital implemented an electronic charts system that significantly improved the efficiency and effectiveness of the system used for recording patient information.
</b>


This introduces a topic that relates to something mentioned in the prompt: patient charts<em>.</em>


However, this choice is about the efficiency of and effectiveness of the system for recording patient information, while the argument is specifically about how much time doctors spend with patients.


Since the fact that there have been improvements to the chart system does not mean that doctors actually do spend more time with patients than they spent two years ago, this choice does not weaken the argument.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) City Hospital recently implemented a program that, using video diagnosis, outsources some aspects of handling certain patients’ cases to a satellite clinic.
</b>


By saying that some aspects of handling patients’ cases have been outsourced, this choice implies that the doctors have been relieved of some work and should be spending less time with patients, not more.


So, this choice is in line with what the argument says and, therefore, is not a weakener.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) A hospital in a nearby county specializing in complex cases closed its doors two years ago, referring any future patients experiencing such situations to City Hospital.
</b>


 This choice discusses a new topic and provides new information that weakens the argument.


Notice that, while this choice does not directly discuss patients, doctors, or shift length at City Hospital, what it says implies that, since the other hospital closed, City Hospital has been handling more cases that are complex.


Common sense tells us that complex cases could be particularly time consuming for the doctors at City Hospital. So, this choice provides reason to believe that it’s likely that the time spent per patient at City Hospital has increased.


Thus, the new information provided by this choice serves to cast doubt on the conclusion that City Hospital doctors are not spending more time with patients.


Another important thing to see is the shift in detail in the passage. Notice that the passage says that the administrators claim that “doctors <em>devote more shift hours to seeing patients</em> than they did two years ago.” However, the premise says, “<em>The number of patients, the length of each shift</em>, and <em>the number of doctors on shift </em>at City Hospital at any given time have not changed.”


Notice how the correct answer weakens the support for the conclusion by indicating that, even though what the premise says is true, it’s likely that the doctors do spend more shift hours seeing patients.


> <b>(D) The hiring process at City Hospital has become notably more rigorous in the past two years.
</b>


This choice introduces a new topic, the hiring process.


So, we have to assess whether what this choice says affects the argument.


That over the past two years the hospital has been using a more rigorous hiring process suggests that the hospital has hired better doctors, but it is unclear whether a “better” doctor would spend more or less time with a patient than a less skilled doctor would.


Therefore, there is no clear connection between a rigorous hiring process and the number of hours spent on each patient, and so this choice has no clear effect on the argument.


> <b>(E) City Hospital has long experienced difficulty in matching each patient case with the doctor likely to handle that case most efficiently.
</b>


This choice may appear, at first glance, to fall into our “weakener” category; inefficiency could cause doctors to spend more hours with patients.


Notice, however, that the argument is about a <em>change</em> that occurred two years ago. This choice, on the other hand, indicates that the inefficiency has been an <em>ongoing </em>problem. So, this choice, “City Hospital has long experienced…,” does not affect the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
