## Lesson Content
One way in which authors of arguments fail to consider other possibilities is by relying on false dichotomies in coming to conclusions.

The term “false dichotomy” may sound complex, but what it means is actually rather simple.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A false dichotomy occurs when an argument assumes that, just because one conclusion is impossible, another conclusion must be true. In other words, a false dichotomy occurs when an author incorrectly limits the number of possible conclusions to two.
{{< /alert >}}

In making a false dichotomy, the author assumes that there could not be other possibilities.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In making a false dichotomy, the author assumes that there could not be other possibilities. We can weaken such an argument by showing that that assumption is a poor one; we can weaken the argument by showing that there are, in fact, other possibilities.
{{< /alert >}}

For example, imagine if someone were to say, “Since you are hungry but don’t want to eat a hamburger for dinner, you must be a diehard vegan.” Would this person’s conclusion be valid? Do you necessarily have to be a diehard vegan to not want to eat a hamburger for dinner? Certainly not – you could be a carnivore who just doesn’t feel like eating hamburgers now, or you could have an aversion to ground meat.

The secret to handling an argument that is attempting to pull us into a false dichotomy is to be careful not to get stuck in an either/or mindset and to be cognizant of the other possibilities that exist.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Don’t get caught in an either/or mindset. If the conclusion in a weaken question is based on a choice between two alternatives, the correct answer to the question could weaken the argument by indicating that a third alternative exists.
{{< /alert >}}

For example:

> <em>Inspectors have informed the high school that three of the school’s buses need repairs that will take a week to complete. The school has just enough buses to cover all the routes via which students are picked up and brought to school. Since there is no practical way to temporarily change the routes so that they can be covered by a reduced number of buses, parents of students on three of the routes will have to drive those students to school for a week.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, weakens the argument?</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>Public buses on which high school students could safely travel run on routes almost exactly the same as the routes taken by some of the school’s buses.</em>

The argument concludes that, because one method of transporting students with a reduced number of school buses – changing the routes – is not available, it will be necessary to use one particular alternative method – parents drive the students – to transport students to the school.

Notice that the correct answer attacks the author’s assumption that there are no other ways to get the students to school.

Here’s an example of how a false dichotomy can work in a Weaken question.

##  Example 10 

In 1932, the painting <em>La Pose Enchantée</em>, which had been completed only five years before by painter René Magritte, disappeared.

Which of the following, if true, casts the most doubt on the above conclusion?

1. [x] (A) Examination of existing Magritte paintings has indicated that Magritte painted over some of his own paintings in the process of creating new paintings.
2. [ ] (B) Public awareness of the destruction of a well-known painting by a gallery’s personnel could do significant damage to that gallery’s reputation.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> is similar in some aspects to certain works of Picasso, an artist who was a contemporary of Magritte’s.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) There are no currently available records that clearly indicate the exact location where <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> was last seen.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Although <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> had been shown at multiple galleries, the painting had not yet been purchased from Magritte at the time of its disappearance.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

##### Question Level: medium



The conclusion of this argument, that <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> was inadvertently destroyed by gallery personnel, is based on a false dichotomy. The author arrives at this conclusion by simply eliminating another alternative, the painting’s being found.


Of course, the truth is that, in making the argument, the author has assumed that there are no possibilities other than the painting’s having been found or its having been destroyed by gallery personnel. In making a false dichotomy, the author assumes that there could not be other possibilities.


We can weaken the argument by showing that there are, in fact, other possibilities; we can weaken the argument by showing that her assumption was a poor one.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) Examination of existing Magritte paintings has indicated that Magritte painted over some of his own paintings in the process of creating new paintings.
</b>


 By saying that Magritte painted over some of his own paintings, this choice indicates that it could very well be that <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> disappeared because Magritte painted over <em>La Pose Enchantée</em>. By offering this third possibility, this choice weakens the argument, which is based on the idea that there are only two things that could have happened to the painting, its having been found or its having been destroyed by gallery personnel.


Notice that this answer choice weakens the argument by showing that the author’s assumption that there are only two possibilities is a poor one.


> <b>(B) Public awareness of the destruction of a well-known painting by a gallery’s personnel could do significant damage to that gallery’s reputation.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because it indicates that galleries at which <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> was displayed would have had a significant reason to make sure that it was not destroyed. However, the fact that galleries had a reason to make sure that the painting was not destroyed does not clearly indicate that the painting was not in fact destroyed at a gallery. It could be that <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> was destroyed at a gallery that subsequently hid the destruction of the painting from the public.


> <b>(C) <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> is similar in some aspects to certain works of Picasso, an artist who was a contemporary of Magritte’s.
</b>


While a creative test-taker might find a way to spin this choice into a reason to believe that <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> was not destroyed, the truth is that there is no clear reason to believe that, since the painting resembled another artist’s work, it was not destroyed.


So, this choice does not cast any doubt whatsoever on the conclusion that the painting was destroyed.


> <b>(D) There are no currently available records that clearly indicate the exact location where <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> was last seen.
</b>


The fact that the last location at which <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> was seen is not known does not indicate that the painting was not destroyed at that location. If the argument were that <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> was destroyed at a specific gallery, this choice might undermine support for the conclusion, but that conclusion is not the one that we are seeking to call into question.


> <b>(E) Although <em>La Pose Enchantée</em> had been shown at multiple galleries, the painting had not yet been purchased from Magritte at the time of its disappearance.
</b>


This choice tends to eliminate one explanation for the disappearance of <em>La Pose Enchantée</em>,<em> </em>that it was purchased and taken to an unknown location by the buyer. In eliminating an alternative explanation for the disappearance, this choice slightly strengthens, rather than weakens, the support for the argument’s conclusion.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
