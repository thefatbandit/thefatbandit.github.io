## Lesson Content
Remember, any time an author presents a plan, she assumes that the plan will work.

So, when an author presents a plan, she assumes that there are no malicious variables.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Any time an author presents a plan, she assumes that there are no variables that will enter into the equation that will spoil the plan. In Weaken the Argument questions, this assumption often turns out to be a poor one.
{{< /alert >}}

One pattern commonly seen in Weaken questions is a passage that outlines a plan or a course of action that an author expects will generate some desired or expected results.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When the argument in a Weaken question concludes that a plan will generate certain results, the correct answer will likely weaken the argument by indicating that the course of action will not generate those desired or expected results.
{{< /alert >}}

The answers indicating that plans will not achieve the desired or expected results tend to fall into the following categories:

> - The plan addresses one factor in a situation, but the correct answer indicates that there is another factor that the plan does not address.

> - The correct answer indicates that the method that the plan uses to solve a problem makes the problem worse in another way.

> - The argument concludes that the plan will work because a similar plan has worked before, but the correct answer shows that the plan will not work because the current situation is different from the situation existing when the plan was used previously.

> - The correct answer shows that the logic underlying the expectation that the plan will work misses a key aspect of how a situation works.

In order to be ready to recognize what’s going on in these types of questions, let’s look at examples with correct answers that fall into each of these categories.

Category:

> - The plan addresses one factor in a situation, but the correct answer indicates that there is another factor that the plan does not address.

Example:

> <em>Surveys of people in Sartania indicate that people living in rural areas of the state are overwhelmingly in favor of one of the candidates running for governor. However, many of the people living in rural areas of Sartania are not registered to vote and therefore would not be able to vote in the upcoming election. Therefore, this candidate’s campaign manager is sending volunteers to the rural areas of Sartania to register people for voting, believing that doing so will significantly increase the number of votes for this candidate.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, would indicate that the plan will not achieve the expected result?</em>

> <em>(</em><em><u>Correct Answer</u></em><em>) Since voters in rural Sartania must travel significant distances to get to polling places, few of the registered voters in those areas bother to vote.</em>

In this example, the campaign manager assumes that by removing one impediment to voting, lack of registration, the plan will result in significantly more votes from people living in rural areas.

The correct answer indicates that the course of action will not produce the expected result because there is another factor, travel distances, that will keep those people from voting, even though they are registered.

Category:

> - The correct answer indicates that the method that the plan uses to solve a problem makes the problem worse in another way.

Example:

> <em>Overfishing in the waters around Seatown has begun to significantly deplete the populations of fish in them. WildWater Fisheries Inc. has proposed setting up fish farms in these waters. By focusing on farming fish rather than on catching the wild fish that live in the waters near Seatown, we will stop the depletion of the wild fish stocks</em><em>.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if discovered to be true, would most undermine the support for the conclusion above? </em>

> <em>(</em><em><u>Correct Answer</u></em><em>) Crowding fish together in offshore farms can create conditions that support the multiplication of organisms that can infect and decimate wild fish populations.</em>

In this argument, which we discussed in the Assumption questions chapter, the author concludes that farming fish, rather than catching wild fish, will prevent the depletion of wild fish stocks.

Category:

> - The argument concludes that the plan will work because a similar plan has worked before, but the correct answer shows that the plan will not work because the current situation is different from the situation existing when the plan was used previously.

Example:

> <em>CEO of Steak Restaurant Chain: The key to our success has been to follow the hip young crowd wherever they go. Young people with high incomes and low expenses have been great customers for us and have tended to become customers for life. Even as we speak, our development team is considering new locations in areas where up and coming young people live and to which they travel. So, you can be sure that our company will continue to grow strongly.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides a reason to believe that the CEO’s prediction will prove to be incorrect?</em>

> <em>(</em><em><u>Correct Answer</u></em><em>) </em><em>Among young people, s</em><em>teak</em><em> is increasingly perceived as an unhealthy food consumed by unaware older people</em>.

In this example, the CEO assumes that what has worked in the past will work again.

Category:

> - The correct answer shows that the logic underlying the expectation that the plan will work misses a key aspect of how a situation works.

Example:

> <em>Owner of Stanton Toys: During the week, our location downtown is often full of children who come in after school, but most have little to no money with them, and, therefore, few make purchases. During the week, there is not much traffic at the location at the edge of town, but that location does a huge amount of business on weekends, when children accompanied by parents make purchases. Therefore, we would lose little business and could increase overall profitability by closing the downtown location.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most clearly indicates that, if the owner’s proposal were implemented, the results would not be as expected?</em>

> <em>(</em><em><u>Correct Answer</u></em><em>)</em> <em>Many children whose parents buy them toys from Stanton Toys on weekends have previously decided that they want those toys upon seeing them in the downtown store.</em>

In this example, the owner assumes that, just because few purchases are made at the downtown store, closing that location would not result in the loss of much business.

##  Example 19 

President: Some countries to which we could have been exporting wind turbines over the past few years have had unfair tariffs blocking their citizens from importing our wind turbines.

Which of the following, if true, provides reason to believe that the president’s actions will not have the results mentioned?

1. [ ] (A) Wind turbines produce energy only when winds are strong enough to cause the turbines to rotate.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) In the president’s country, the operations of wind turbine manufacturers are currently hampered by an ongoing shortage of copper, a key component of wind turbines.
3. [ ] (C) The removal of tariffs on solar panels, another source of renewable energy, is not expected to result from the trade deals that the president is negotiating.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) The price of oil, which is correlated with demand for wind turbines, is not expected to increase in the coming months.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Domestic demand for wind turbines in the president’s country is not expected to increase over the coming year.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Breakdown of the Argument:


<u>Premise</u>: Countries have had tariffs preventing their citizens from importing our wind turbines.


<u>Premise</u>: I am currently negotiating trade deals that will result in the removal of those tariffs.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Within months we will see increased production and increased employment in our wind turbine industry.


The president plans to negotiate a trade deal that will result in the removal of tariffs blocking companies in her country from doing business with people in other countries. She expects these actions to result in increased production of wind turbines in her country.


One key thing that we will see time and again is that, whenever anyone proposes a plan, he or she makes the assumption that the plan will work as it has been laid out, that there will not be other variables that enter the equation and derail the plan. Often, such an assumption is not so good.


> <b>(A) Wind turbines produce energy only when winds are strong enough to cause the turbines to rotate.
</b>


What this answer choice describes is a limitation of the usefulness of wind turbines, one that may affect overall demand for wind turbines. At the same time, even though wind turbines need wind in order to produce energy, people do purchase wind turbines. So, even though the usefulness of wind turbines may be limited by their need for wind in order to operate, it could still be the case that, once the tariffs are removed, the country’s wind turbine industry will do more business.


So, this choice does not weaken the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) In the president’s country, the operations of wind turbine manufacturers are currently hampered by an ongoing shortage of copper, a key component of wind turbines.
</b>


 This choice indicates that, in addition to the tariffs, there is another factor constraining the wind turbine industry, a copper shortage. As a result of this shortage, it seems likely that, even without the tariffs, the country’s wind turbine industry will not be able to increase production and hire more people, because the manufacturers need copper in order to produce wind turbines.


So, by indicating there is an additional factor hampering wind turbine production, a copper shortage, which the president’s actions will not address, this choice provides a reason to believe that the president’s actions will not have the expected results.


Notice that what this answer choice says weakens the argument by attacking a bad assumption made by the author.


> <b>(C) The removal of tariffs on solar panels, another source of renewable energy, is not expected to result from the trade deals that the president is negotiating.
</b>


Tariffs on solar panels should not negatively affect foreign demand for wind turbines. Rather, tariffs restricting trade in solar panels might cause people to buy wind turbines instead. So, if anything, this choice may strengthen, rather than weaken, the support for the conclusion.


> <b>(D) The price of oil, which is correlated with demand for wind turbines, is not expected to increase in the coming months.
</b>


Notice that the president has already provided support for the conclusion, the fact that the tariffs will be removed.


What this choice says indicates that another possible reason to believe that there will be increased production and increased employment in the country’s wind turbine industry – the expectation that oil prices will increase – does not exist.


OK, but so what? We don’t need more support for the conclusion. We already have a premise that supports the conclusion, and the fact that we don’t have a second premise does not weaken the support that we already have.


So, what this choice says has no effect on the support for the conclusion.


> <b>(E) Domestic demand for wind turbines in the president’s country is not expected to increase over the coming year.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, because it could be perceived as indicating that demand in general for the country’s wind turbines will not increase, even after the tariffs are lifted.


However, the conclusion is based on the expectation of an increase in international trade in wind turbines not on an expectation of an increase in domestic demand.


So, all this choice really indicates is that there will not be an increase in domestic demand in addition the expected increase in international trade.


Thus, this choice does not have any effect on the support for the argument’s conclusion.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

##  Example 20 

The city of Tennico has announced that it plans to start a program involving periodic gun buybacks.

Which of the following, if true, most calls into question the expected effectiveness of the program?

1. [ ] (A) By not questioning people selling guns to the city, officials may miss opportunities to gain information that could be used to prevent violence.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Sometimes a relative or friend of someone who owns a gun will sell the gun to the city in order to prevent the owner from using it to perpetrate violence.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) The money spent to buy back guns could be instead used to fund educational programs that would address the root causes of violence in the city.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Many people feel more comfortable purchasing new guns if they know that they can easily sell the guns should they no longer want them.
5. [ ] (E) Many of the guns sold to the city will come from people who would not have carried the guns had the guns remained in their possession.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



Breakdown of the Argument:


<u>Background</u>: The city of Tennico has announced that it plans to start a program involving periodic gun buybacks .


<u>Plan</u>: On the date of each announced gun buyback, a person in possession of a gun will be able to sell it to the city, no questions asked.


<u>Expected Outcome</u>: The gun buyback program will serve to reduce the number of guns carried by people on the streets of Tennico.


The reasoning underlying the guy buyback program – if the city buys guns from people possessing them, the number of guns carried by people will be reduced – seems to be rather straightforward. To weaken the argument, we need a statement that calls this reasoning into question, by indicating that it could very well be the case that the program will result in either no reduction in the number of guns on the streets or an increase in the number of guns on the streets.


Remember, whenever anyone proposes a plan, he or she makes the assumption that the plan will work as it has been laid out, that there will not be other variables that enter the equation and derail the plan. Often, such an assumption is not so good.


> <b>(A) By not questioning people selling guns to the city, officials may miss opportunities to gain information that could be used to prevent violence.
</b>


While this choice may bring up one disadvantage of the program, what this choice says does not go in the direction we need it to go in order for it to weaken the argument.


The point of the program is to reduce the number of guns on the streets of Tennico, not to gain information. So, this choice does not affect the argument.


> <b>(B) Sometimes a relative or friend of someone who owns a gun will sell the gun to the city in order to prevent the owner from using it to perpetrate violence.
</b>


In indicating that people may seek to prevent violence by selling guns to the city, what this choice says is in line with the reasoning of the argument, and therefore, this choice does not weaken the argument.


> <b>(C) The money spent to buy back guns could be instead used to fund educational programs that would address the root causes of violence in the city.
</b>


The argument is not about the relative merits of the gun buyback program or about whether the best way for the city to spend money is to buy back guns. The conclusion is simply that the program will serve to reduce the number of guns carried by people in Tennico.


So, as interesting or valid as what this choice says may be, it does not affect the argument.


Also notice that if we look carefully at what this answer choice says, we see that it mentions a different plan, a better plan. In general, unless an author claims that his plan is the only plan or the best plan, he need not assume that there are not other plans that could work or assume that there is not a better plan than his. Therefore, the mention of a different plan or a better plan will not weaken the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) Many people feel more comfortable purchasing new guns if they know that they can easily sell the guns should they no longer want them.
</b>


 While this choice says nothing about the program or about Tennico specifically, if we thoughtfully consider the implications of what it says, we can see the following. If people are more comfortable purchasing new guns if they know that they can readily sell the new guns that they purchase, then by providing people with a way to sell guns, Tennico may encourage more purchases of guns than there would have been had Tennico not had the buyback program. As a result, there could be more guns, and, therefore, more guns carried, in Tennico with the program in place than without.


So, while we don’t know for sure that what this choice says means that the buyback program will not result in a reduction in guns carried by people in Tennico, this information does call into question the conclusion that the program will have the desired effect.


> <b>(E) Many of the guns sold to the city will come from people who would not have carried the guns had the guns remained in their possession.
</b>


This answer choice could be tempting, since it seems to indicate that buying back guns will in many cases not make any difference in the number guns carried on the streets of Tennico.


However, even if many of the guns bought by the city would not have been carried anyway, if some of the guns bought by the city would have been carried, then the program could result in a reduction in the number of guns carried.


So, this choice does not fundamentally undermine the strength of the conclusion.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

##  Example 21 

In accordance with Home Squad real estate agency’s new plan for increasing sales, each month, the agency will hold a contest such that the agent who has generated the most sales for that month will get, in addition to standard sales commissions, a $10,000 bonus.

Which of the following, if true, most calls into question the above prediction?

1. [ ] (A) The least productive agents at the Home Squad will only rarely see their incomes affected by the bonus.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Much of the sales success of the Home Squad agency has resulted from agents’ cooperating to generate sales.
3. [ ] (C) $10,000 is more than some agents’ average monthly total commission from sales.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) The commission that an agent would receive for closing a particularly large sale would be much greater than the $10,000 bonus.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) The housing market in the area served by the Home Squad agency is not expected to be strong over the next few years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



The expected outcome:


<u>Conclusion</u>: Having the contest will have a positive effect on the sales of the agency.


How that expectation is supported:


<u>Background</u>: Each month, the Home Squad agency will hold a contest such that the agent who has generated the most sales for that month will get, in addition to standard sales commissions, a $10,000 bonus.


<u>Premise</u>: That bonus will constitute a significant incentive for even the most productive agents.


The reasoning of the argument is that, by offering a significant bonus to the agent who generates the most sales in any month, the agency will incentivize all of the agents to generate more sales for the agency. This argument could be weakened by information indicating that the author of the plan missed a key aspect of how the business works and that, therefore, having the contest could have unanticipated effects such that it would negatively rather than positively affect the sales of the agency.


Remember, whenever anyone proposes a plan, he or she makes the assumption that the plan will work as it has been laid out, that there will not be other variables that will enter the equation and derail the plan.


> <b>(A) The least productive agents at the Home Squad will only rarely see their incomes affected by the bonus.
</b>


While this choice indicates that it is possible that some of the of the agents will not be significantly affected by or incentivized by the contest, as long as some of the agents are incentivized by the contest, having the contest could have a positive effect on the sales of the agency.


So, this choice has no effect on the support for the conclusion.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) Much of the sales success of the Home Squad agency has resulted from agents’ cooperating to generate sales.
</b>


 By considering the implications of what this choice says in the context of what the passage says, we can see the following.


The contest provides an incentive for the agents to compete. Meanwhile, this choice says that much of the agency’s sales success comes from agents’ cooperation. By incentivizing the agents to compete with each other, the contest may sabotage some of the cooperation among agents that, according to this choice, has resulted in the agency’s sales success. Thus, by indicating that much of the agency’s success comes from cooperation, this choice illuminates an aspect of the situation that seems to have been missed by the author.


Given what this choice says, having the contest could actually negatively affect sales, and thus this choice serves to call into question the conclusion that having the contest will have a positive effect on sales.


Notice that what this answer choice says weakens the argument by attacking a bad assumption made by the author.


> <b>(C) $10,000 is more than some agents’ average monthly total commission from sales.
</b>


While perhaps we could come up with a complex convoluted story explaining why a monthly contest that involves awarding a bonus greater than the average monthly commissions of some of the agency’s agents would not positively affect sales, the truth is that there is no clear reason to believe that what this choice says affects the conclusion of the argument. If anything, what this choice says indicates that the bonus could be a significant incentive for some of the agents, and that, therefore, having the contest might cause them to generate more sales for the agency than they otherwise would have.


> <b>(D) The commission that an agent would receive for closing a particularly large sale would be much greater than the $10,000 bonus.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, as what it says tends to somewhat diminish the relative value of the bonus. All the same, the passage stated as fact that the bonus would constitute a significant incentive for even the most productive agents, and, meanwhile, the fact that a particular commission might be greater than the bonus does not mean that the bonus would not be valued by the agents.


So, what this choice says does not significantly change what we know about the scenario and does not weaken the argument.


> <b>(E) The housing market in the area served by the Home Squad agency is not expected to be strong over the next few years.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, because one could take what it says to mean that the agency will have difficulty generating sales over the next few years and decide that, therefore, the contest won’t have the expected effect.


However, the conclusion of the argument is not that the agency will increase sales or generate a lot of sales but rather that having the contest will have a positive effect on sales. In other words, the conclusion is that sales will be greater with the contest than they would have been without it.


Since, regardless of the housing market in general is strong, the contest could have a positive effect on sales, this choice does not affect the support for the conclusion.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

OK, now that we have covered questions in which arguments are weakened by answer choices that indicate that plans won’t work, let’s discuss questions that work in the opposite way, those in which arguments are weakened by answers indicating that plans will work.
