## Lesson Content
Plans are common topics in GMAT Critical Reasoning questions, and Weaken questions can involve conclusions that plans will not work or don’t make sense.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When authors claim that plans will not work or don’t make sense, support for such conclusions can be weakened by correct answers that provide new information that indicates that actually the plans will work or do make sense.
{{< /alert >}}

The correct answers to these questions generally weaken the arguments by providing new information that offsets or makes up for the information used to support the arguments’ conclusions that the plans will not work.

Notice how the correct answers to the following questions weaken the arguments by offsetting the premises that the arguments use to indicate that the plan will not work.

##  Example 22 

Travel Analyst: To entice new wealthy customers to book stays, the Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa recently began offering free international business class commercial airfare for two people with the booking of any beachfront suite for seven days or longer.

Which of the following, if true, most seriously weakens the argument above?

1. [ ] (A) The Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa is well-known for owning and operating a private airport one mile from the hotel, and that airport caters to hotel guests arriving on private planes.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Over the past three years, more Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa beachfront suites have been booked on the average night than were during previous years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Most customers who book Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa beachfront suites invite as nonpaying guests at least two people who live in countries far from where the clients live.
4. [ ] (D) Other luxury hotels near the Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa have begun offering customers who book luxury suites complimentary dinners and hot stone massages.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) The Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa recently underwent extensive renovations to both its interior and exterior and is now significantly more attractive than it was previously.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Background</u>: To entice new wealthy customers to book stays, the Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa recently began offering free international business class commercial airfare for two people with the booking of any beachfront suite for seven days or longer.


<u>Premise</u>: Almost all customers who could afford to pay for at least seven days in a beachfront suite at the Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa would certainly prefer to fly to the hotel in their own private planes, not in commercial airplanes.


<u>Conclusion</u>: By doing so (offering free international business class commercial airfare for two people), it is unlikely that the hotel will attract any new customers.


The hotel has a plan: begin offering free international business class commercial airfare for two people with the booking of any beachfront suite for seven days or longer. Why? To entice new wealthy customers to book stays.


Now, notice that the author claims that this plan will not work. Is the author correct? Might it be the case that the author has miscalculated, and that the plan will work after all? What information might come to light that would show that the author’s conclusion is incorrect, and the hotel’s plan will indeed work?


> <b>(A) The Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa is well-known for owning and operating a private airport one mile from the hotel, and that airport caters to hotel guests arriving on private planes.
</b>


 What this answer choice says does not weaken the argument.


The conclusion of the argument is that the hotel will not attract new customers by offering free international business class commercial airfare for two people with the booking of a beachfront suite. That conclusion is supported by the fact that almost all customers who could afford to pay for at least seven days in a beachfront suite at the hotel would prefer to fly to the hotel in their own private planes, not in commercial airplanes.


Certainly, the fact that the hotel is well-known for owning and operating a private airport that caters to hotel guests arriving on private planes does not weaken the argument by giving us more faith that the hotel’s plan will work. The fact that guests can arrive by private plane at a private airport isn’t a reason to believe that they will be enticed by complimentary commercial airfare.


> <b>(B) Over the past three years, more Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa beachfront suites have been booked on the average night than were during previous years.
</b>


 What this answer choice indicates is simply that, in recent years, the hotel has experienced in uptick in guests’ booking the beachfront suites. That fact alone does not weaken the support for the conclusion that the new plan will not attract more guests.


Regardless of how the hotel has been doing in general, it could still be the case that guests won’t be attracted by the offer of complimentary commercial airfare with the booking of a beachfront suite.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) Most customers who book Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa beachfront suites invite as nonpaying guests at least two people who live in countries far from where the clients live.
</b>


 The hotel is attempting to entice wealthy customers by offering free international business class commercial airfare for two people with the booking of any beachfront suite for seven days or longer.


The author claims that this plan will not work because almost all customers who could afford to pay for at least seven days in a beachfront suite at the hotel would prefer to fly there in their own private planes, not in commercial airplanes.


However, if it is true that most customers who book the beachfront suites invite as nonpaying guests at least two people who live in countries far from where the clients live, then the hotel’s plan may very well work.


After all, if the nonpaying guests who are invited by the paying customers live far away from the paying customers, it would be reasonable to think that these nonpaying guests could take advantage of the free international airfare, using it to fly to the hotel to meet the paying customers who invited them. Thus, the free international airfare could very well be a nice enticement to the paying customers because most invite as nonpaying guests at least two people who live in countries far from where the customers themselves live.


So, this choice provides a reason to believe that the plan will in fact work, and thus weakens the support for the conclusion that the plan will not work.


> <b>(D) Other luxury hotels near the Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa have begun offering customers who book luxury suites complimentary dinners and hot stone massages.
</b>


 The fact that other luxury hotels near the Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa have begun offering customers who book luxury suites complimentary dinners and hot stone massages does not weaken the argument.


If anything, the fact that other hotels are offering enticements to attract guests would strengthen the case for believing that the Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa won’t attract new customers by offering complimentary commercial airfare. If multiple hotels in the same area are offering enticements, maybe the enticements won’t have much effect.


> <b>(E) The Royal Sunset Hotel and Spa recently underwent extensive renovations to both its interior and exterior and is now significantly more attractive than it was previously.
</b>


 Notice that what this answer choice says is a general statement about the hotel. As wonderful as it may be that the hotel recently underwent extensive renovations that made it significantly more attractive, does that fact give us more faith that the hotel’s plan will work, thereby weakening the argument?


In order to avoid choosing this incorrect choice, we have to pay attention to what exactly the conclusion of the argument is. The conclusion is not that the hotel will not entice new customers <em>in general</em>. The conclusion is that the hotel will not entice new customers <em>by offering complimentary airfare to guests who book beachfront suites</em>.


However attractive the newly renovated hotel may be, it could still very well be the case that, since customers would rather fly in their own private jets, offering complimentary airfare will not entice any new customers to book stays at the hotel.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Example 23 

City Council Member: The administration proposes to improve the health of people living in the South Ward by getting one of the food store chains that sell healthy food to open a store in the South Ward.

Which of the following, if true, provides the most compelling reason to call into question the above conclusion?

1. [x] (A) People whose diets consist mostly of low-quality food generally spend significant portions of their incomes on dealing with health problems that result from living on such diets.
2. [ ] (B) Many residents of the South Ward receive from the government financial benefits that supplement their incomes.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) Many lower quality foods include ingredients chosen specifically for flavoring the foods in ways that cause people to want to continue to consume them.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) One food store chain that is considering opening a store in the South Ward offers in its stores a range of foods that includes both higher quality and lower quality foods.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) The administration has the ability to fast track permitting and take other measures to make opening a store that sells healthy food in the South Ward relatively easy.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The City Council Member has concluded the following about the administration’s plan to improve the health of people living in the South Ward:


<u>Conclusion</u>: Most residents of the South Ward would not be able to afford to shop at a store that sells only healthy foods.


The conclusion is supported by two premises:


<u>Premise</u>: Healthy food is more expensive than the food that residents of the South Ward are currently buying.


<u>Premise</u>: Most residents of the South Ward are already living at or close to the limits of their financial means.


So, what the City Council member is getting at is that most residents of the South Ward don’t have extra money that they could spend in order to upgrade the quality of their food.


Do they need extra money though? What if there is an offsetting factor that the City Council member is not taking into account in coming to the conclusion? Is he or she making one or more poor assumptions that a correct answer choice will target?


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) People whose diets consist mostly of low-quality food generally spend significant portions of their incomes on dealing with health problems that result from living on such diets.
</b>


 By considering the implications of this answer choice, we can see that it is exactly what we’re seeking.


If people who eat lower quality food spend large portions of their incomes on dealing with the resulting health problems, then people who eat higher quality food may be able to offset the extra expense of that food by not having to spend money to deal with health problems caused by the food that they eat.


So, this answer choice provides reason to believe that, actually, residents of the South Ward could afford to buy higher quality food, thus indicating that the administration’s plan could work and thus weakening the argument.


> <b>(B) Many residents of the South Ward receive from the government financial benefits that supplement their incomes.
</b>


 This choice is a tempting trap, because it could seem to indicate that the residents of the South Ward have more money than the City Council member believes them to have.


However, as we have discussed, correct answers to weaken questions can’t successfully attack premises of arguments, and the truth is that the City Council member didn’t say that the residents’ <em>incomes</em> are not sufficient for them to handle the expense of the healthy food. He said that the residents are <em>at the</em> <em>limits of their financial means</em>, which would include any financial benefits that they get from the government.


So, this choice actually changes nothing.


> <b>(C) Many lower quality foods include ingredients chosen specifically for flavoring the foods in ways that cause people to want to continue to consume them.
</b>


This choice is only tangentially related to the topic of the argument.


The flavor of lower quality food might be another reason, in addition to affordability, why people consume lower quality food. At the same time, the existence of this other reason to consume lower quality food does not affect the support for the conclusion.


Regardless of whether people consume lower quality food because of its flavor, the fact that residents of the South Ward are at living at or close to the limits of their financial means supports the conclusion that they would not be able to afford shop at a store that sells only healthy foods.


> <b>(D) One food store chain that is considering opening a store in the South Ward offers in its stores a range of foods that includes both higher quality and lower quality foods.
</b>


 This choice is a tempting trap that has no effect on the support for the conclusion.


Notice that the conclusion of the argument is not that most residents of the South Ward would not be able to afford to shop at any new store that opens in the South Ward. The conclusion is that they would not be able to afford to shop at a store that sells <em>only</em> healthy foods.


So, the fact that a store that sells both higher quality and lower quality foods may open in the South Ward makes no difference in the strength of the argument. Regardless of whether residents of the South Ward would be able to afford to shop at that store, the evidence still supports the conclusion that most residents would not be able to afford to shop at a store that sells only healthy foods.


> <b>(E) The administration has the ability to fast track permitting and take other measures to make opening a store that sells healthy food in the South Ward relatively easy.
</b>


 This choice affects the support for the wrong conclusion.


The argument’s conclusion is not that the administration has no ability to get a store chain to open a store that sells healthy foods in South Ward. The argument’s conclusion is that most residents of the South Ward would not be able to afford to shop at such a store.


Regardless of whether the administration can make opening a store in the South Ward relatively easy, the fact that most residents of the South Ward are living at or close to the limits of their financial means supports the argument’s conclusion.


So, this choice has no effect on the strength of the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
