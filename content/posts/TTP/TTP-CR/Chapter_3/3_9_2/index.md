## Lesson Content
In a Weaken question, along with the correct answer choice, which actually weakens the argument, there can be one or more pseudo-weakener answer choices, which may give the impression that they weaken the argument but don’t really get the job done.

Consider the following example:

> <em>The football coach at Verdantville high school wants the players on the team to gain weight. So, he is providing the players with all the chocolate bars that they can eat. Because the players have this tempting food available, the average weight of the players is sure to increase.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides the strongest reason to question the conclusion drawn?</em>

> <em>(<u>Pseudo-Weakener Choice</u>)</em> <em>Some of the players on the team who weigh the least don’t like the taste of chocolate.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>The bars that the coach is providing to the players contain large amounts of caffeine, which people seeking to lose weight often use to reduce their appetites.</em>

To see why the pseudo-weakener doesn’t weaken the argument, let’s look carefully at the conclusion of the argument.

> <u>Conclusion</u>: <em>The average weight of the players is sure to increase.</em>

As long as we can expect the average weight of the players to increase, the conclusion is supported.

What if the pseudo<em>-</em>weakener is true, and some of the players that weigh the least don’t like the taste of chocolate? It might seem that, in that case, the conclusion is called into question, because if some of the players that weight the least don’t like chocolate, then some won’t eat it, and those players won’t gain weight.

But wait.

Contrast the effect of the pseudo-weakener with the effect of the correct answer.

##  Example 27 

The SwiftBrew coffee company has been building its customer base by selling at relatively low prices the pods that go into its coffee makers.

Which of the following, if true, most seriously weakens the conclusion above?

1. [ ] (A) Some people who have bought SwiftBrew coffee makers have used them for a few months and then stopped because of the amount of waste created in using the pods.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Typically, some users of SwiftBrew pods stock up on enough SwiftBrew pods to last for months.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Sales of SwiftBrew’s coffee makers are not increasing as fast as they did during the first year of the company’s selling coffee makers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) MorningFresh, a company that makes coffee pods virtually identical to SwiftBrew’s coffee pods, plans to continue selling pods at the price at which SwiftBrew currently sells its pods.
5. [ ] (E) Some SwiftBrew customers reuse pods, making coffee more than once from a single pod.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

##### Question Level: medium



In the scenario presented in the passage, many people own coffee machines that use SwiftBrew pods and are fans of SwiftBrew pods. Now, the company plans to increase the price of its pods from its current relatively low level.


To determine which choice casts doubt on the conclusion, we have to note exactly what the conclusion is.


<u>Conclusion</u>: After SwiftBrew increases the price at which it sells its pods, its profits are sure to increase substantially.


So, to correctly answer the question, we need an answer choice that clearly calls into question the conclusion that the company’s profits will increase substantially after it increases the price at which it sells the pods.


As we go through the choices, let’s note which are pseudo-weakeners and which actually clearly weakens the argument.


> <b>(A) Some people who have bought SwiftBrew coffee makers have used them for a few months and then stopped because of the amount of waste created in using the pods.
</b>


This choice is a pseudo-weakener.


Yes, some people have stopped using the machines, but does that fact mean that the company’s profits won’t increase once the price of the pods is higher? Not really. The point is that, by selling pods at a higher price to people who <em>do</em> use the machines, the company will increase its profits.


> <b>(B) Typically, some users of SwiftBrew pods stock up on enough SwiftBrew pods to last for months.
</b>


This choice is a pseudo-weakener, because the fact that some people stock up on enough pods to last for months could be perceived as meaning that SwiftBrew won’t see a profit increase after increasing prices, because people who have stocked up on pods won’t have to buy any.


However, while the fact that some people stock up on enough pods to last for months means that, probably, some people won’t have to buy pods immediately after SwiftBrew increases the price of pods, those people would not have had to buy pods at that point in time anyway. What matters is that the people who do buy pods at that point in time will pay more than they would have before the price increase, and thus, SwiftBrew’s profits will increase.


Furthermore, even those who will have pods stocked up when SwiftBrew increases prices will eventually run out of pods and have to pay the new higher price if they want to buy more.


> <b>(C) Sales of SwiftBrew’s coffee makers are not increasing as fast as they did during the first year of the company’s selling coffee makers.
</b>


This choice is a pseudo-weakener. From what this choice says, one could get the impression that the company’s profits are not going to increase much, as coffee maker sales growth is slowing.


Notice, however, that what this choice says indicates that SwiftBrew’s coffee maker sales are still increasing, and meanwhile, the expected boost in profits is expected to result from the planned increase in the price of pods, which could cause profit growth even if there were no growth in sales of SwiftBrew’s coffee makers.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) MorningFresh, a company that makes coffee pods virtually identical to SwiftBrew’s coffee pods, plans to continue selling pods at the price at which SwiftBrew currently sells its pods.
</b>


 This choice truly weakens the argument, since, if another company plans to continue to sell pods that are virtually identical to SwiftBrew’s pods at the price at which SwiftBrew currently sells its pods, then it seems likely that, after SwiftBrew increases the price of its pods, many of its customers will start buying pods from the other company instead of from SwiftBrew. Since the customers’ switching could be a significant detriment to SwiftBrew’s profits and could even result in a decrease in SwiftBrew’s profits, this choice clearly serves to call the conclusion into question.


Remember, any time an author sets forth an argument, plan, proposal, or course of action, she assumes that there will be no offsetting factors that will somehow render that argument, plan, proposal, or course of action invalid. Notice that what this answer choice says highlights that that assumption was a poor one, that in fact there is an offsetting factor that could ruin the plan. This answer choice presents a malicious variable.


> <b>(E) Some SwiftBrew customers reuse pods, making coffee more than once from a single pod.
</b>


This choice is a pseudo-weakener.


We could perceive this choice as weakening the argument, because the fact that people reuse the pods may seem to be a factor that would keep SwiftBrew’s profits from increasing. After all, by reusing pods, people can make coffee without purchasing as many pods as they would have to purchase if they were using each pod just once.


Notice, however, that even a pod that is used multiple times must be purchased to be used. So, the fact that some people reuse the pods does not change the fact that people purchase the pods.


So, regardless of what this choice says, the fact that SwiftBrew plans to increase the price of the pods supports the conclusion of the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
