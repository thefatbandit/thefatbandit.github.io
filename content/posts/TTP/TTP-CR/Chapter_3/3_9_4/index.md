## Lesson Content
Some of the most tempting incorrect choices in Weaken questions are choices that mention things that one would likely consider in a real-world review of a situation.

At the same time, while these answer choices may say things worthy of consideration, they do not weaken the arguments that they follow.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

We must be careful not to be tempted into choosing an answer choice that says something that is valid and is pertinent to what the passage discusses but does not affect the argument. Answer choices can – and often do – appeal to our sensibilities, and yet not affect the argument presented.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following example:

> <em>Several commonly used agricultural pesticides have been linked to increased incidence of cancer in people exposed to them. By banning these pesticides and getting farmers to switch over to using organic farming methods, which do not include the use of such pesticides, we can make food safer to eat.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most seriously calls into question the conclusion drawn?</em>

> <em>(<u>Pertinent but Incorrect Choice</u>)</em> <em>Because organically farmed food is often more costly than food farmed via use of the pesticides in question, many people would prefer to be able to choose which to buy.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) Some forms of dangerous bacteria are more likely to be found on foods produced via the use of organic methods than on other foods.</em>

There are two key attributes of the pertinent but incorrect answer choice above.

The argument is about making food safer to eat.

Notice that the correct answer does in fact weaken the argument by providing a reason why banning these pesticides and getting farmers to switch over to using organic farming methods might not actually make food safer to eat.

Let’s look at another example:

> <em>Test results indicate that the new online learning system is a much more effective way for students to learn about many subjects than is a traditional classroom setting. Once the system is implemented in schools nationwide, students will learn more quickly, more effectively, and at their own paces.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, is a reason to question the likelihood that the above prediction will prove to be accurate?</em>

> <em>(<u>Pertinent but Incorrect Choice</u>) If an educational program is poorly designed, students may score high on tests while not learning the things that would be most useful to them.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) Effective use of the new online learning system requires types of support that very few members of school staffs know how to provide.</em>

You can see why the pertinent but incorrect choice could be very tempting.

However, the pertinent but incorrect choice does not affect the argument because that choice is not about what the conclusion of the argument is about.

The correct answer, on the other hand, does cast doubt on the conclusion of the argument by indicating that implementing the new online learning system may not produce the expected results because school staff members aren’t ready to support use of the system.

##  Example 29 

Politician: Our nation was once a major manufacturing power, but now companies in other countries are competing with ours by manufacturing at much lower costs.

Which of the following, if true, is most damaging to the politician’s argument?

1. [ ] (A) The gases and particulate matter put into the atmosphere via worldwide use of fossil fuels are harming air quality and destabilizing the environment.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) For the nation to which the politician is referring, focusing on developing service businesses may make more sense than seeking to compete in manufacturing.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Fuel costs constitute a substantial component of total operating expenses for many manufacturing companies.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) Investing in harnessing renewable resources would generate more long-term benefits than would be generated via investing in new fossil fuel extraction technology.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) When one nation stops importing fossil fuels, the resulting change in demand for fuel results in a decrease in worldwide fossil fuel prices.

##### Question Level: easy



The politician is arguing the following:


By using lower cost domestically produced fuel rather than imported fuel, the nation’s manufacturing companies will be able to become competitive once again.


What assumption is being made here? Any time someone proposes a plan, he or she assumes that the plan will work as it has be laid out and that there are not variables that have not been properly considered that will enter the equation and derail the plan. However, as we will see time and time again, this assumption is often a bad one.


For example, what if the country’s companies’ switching to using domestically produced fossils causes a decrease in demand for worldwide fossil fuel that in turn causes a decrease in the price of fossil fuels for companies in other countries?


To arrive at the correct answer, we have to carefully navigate through a set of choices that includes some that seem rather pertinent to the situation and yet have no bearing on the conclusion of the argument.


> <b>(A) The gases and particulate matter put into the atmosphere via worldwide use of fossil fuels are harming air quality and destabilizing the environment.
</b>


 This answer choice could be just what someone is thinking while reading an argument about the benefits of using lower cost fossil fuels. Don’t let this answer’s real-life pertinence to the situation tempt you into choosing it though. This choice does not affect the argument, because it does not have anything to do with costs and competitiveness.


Remember – do not fall for an answer choice that appeals to your sensibilities yet does not affect the argument.


> <b>(B) For the nation to which the politician is referring, focusing on developing service businesses may make more sense than seeking to compete in manufacturing.
</b>


 This is another interesting but incorrect choice. Yes, it might make more sense to focus on service businesses rather than manufacturing businesses. However, this choice changes nothing about an argument stating that, by using relatively low cost domestically produced fuel, industrial companies can compete effectively.


Also, notice that, if we look carefully at what this answer choice says, we see that it mentions a different plan, a better plan. In general, unless an author claims that his plan is the only plan or the best plan, he need not assume that there are not other plans that could work or assume that there is not a better plan than his. Therefore, the mention of a different plan or a better plan will not weaken the argument.


> <b>(C) Fuel costs constitute a substantial component of total operating expenses for many manufacturing companies.
</b>


 By confirming that fuel costs are significant for manufacturing businesses, this answer choice strengthens an argument that concludes that, by reducing their fuel costs, manufacturing companies can become more competitive. So, if anything, this answer choice is a strengthener rather than a weakener.


> <b>(D) Investing in harnessing renewable resources would generate more long-term benefits than would be generated via investing in new fossil fuel extraction technology.
</b>


 Whether doing something else would be more beneficial than investing in new fossil fuel extraction technology is certainly pertinent to the discussion at hand but does not affect the support for the conclusion of the argument.


Notice that the conclusion of the argument is simply that, by using lower cost fossil fuels, the nation’s manufacturing companies will be able to become more competitive. Thus, what this choice says about the benefits of investing in harnessing renewable resources has absolutely no effect on the argument’s conclusion, because what this choice says has no direct connection to the relationship between using lower cost fossil fuels and becoming more able to compete.


So, this choice is another one that is pertinent to the discussion but does not affect the argument.


Don’t fall for a better plan trap choice in a Weaken the Argument question. If we’re about to choose an answer choice that mentions a better plan, we better be super sure that the argument will be weakened by the introduction of a better plan.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) When one nation stops importing fossil fuels, the resulting change in demand for fuel results in a decrease in worldwide fossil fuel prices.
</b>


 This choice indicates that, if the nation’s industrial companies cease to use imported fuel, the reduction in demand for fuel will cause worldwide fuel prices to decrease. In other words, by using lower cost domestically produced fuel, the manufacturing companies will cause lower fuel prices for their competitors as well.


While it’s not clear whether the cost of fuel outside the nation will drop to the level of the cost of domestically produced fuel, that worldwide fuel prices will drop as a result of the nation’s manufacturing companies’ switching to domestically produced fuel calls into question the idea that the nation’s manufacturing companies will improve their competitive positions by using domestically produced fuel.


Notice that this answer choice presents a malicious variable that the politician did not consider.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
