## Lesson Content
In Strengthen questions, there can be choices that seem to strengthen arguments by providing information that seems to confirm the validity of premises.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Some incorrect answers to Strengthen questions are incorrect because their only function is to seem to confirm the validity of premises. Since the premises are already considered facts, these answer choices do not affect the strength of arguments.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following example:

> <em>Over the past decade, most people in Ostazia have significantly increased their consumption of fresh fruit. Over the same time period, the incidence of heart disease in Ostazia has decreased. Clearly then, consuming fresh fruit is an effective way to prevent heart disease.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, strengthens the argument?</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Seems to Confirm the Validity of a Premise</u>)</em> <em>Survey results indicate that over 80 percent of residents of Ostazia have significantly increased their consumption of fresh fruit.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>The incidence of heart disease in residents of areas of Ostazia where most people continue to follow a traditional diet that includes little fruit has not changed significantly over the past decade.</em>

Here’s the conclusion for which we need support:

> <u>Conclusion</u>: Consuming fresh fruit is an effective way to prevent heart disease.

Notice the effect of the choice that seems to confirm the validity of a premise.

One of the premises of the argument is “Most people in Ostazia have significantly increased their consumption of fresh fruit.” The choice that seems to confirm the validity of a premise provides survey results, “80 percent of residents of Ostazia have significantly increased their consumption of fresh fruit,” that seem to confirm that most people in Ostazia have increased their consumption of fresh fruit.

However, this choice does not strengthen the argument, because such confirmation makes no difference, as, even without this confirmation, we already consider the premise “most people in Ostazia have significantly increased their consumption of fresh fruit” a fact.

Now let’s consider the Correct Answer.

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>The incidence of heart disease in residents of areas of Ostazia where most people continue to follow a traditional diet that includes little fruit has not changed significantly over the past decade.</em>

This choice adds support to the conclusion by showing what happened without the presence of the variable increased consumption of fresh fruit: the incidence of heart disease did not decrease.

##  Example 13 

Approximately 65 million years ago, Earth experienced the largest mass extinction of plant and animal species in its history.

Which of the following, if true, most strongly supports the researchers’ hypothesis?

1. [ ] (A) Approximately 75 percent of Earth’s plant and animal species went extinct 65 million years ago.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Fossil records indicate that species that required very little sunlight to live did not go extinct at the time of the asteroid impact.
3. [ ] (C) Evidence suggests that the asteroid that impacted Earth approximately 65 million years ago could have been 81 kilometers wide, about as wide as the country of Luxembourg is long.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) Approximately 65 million years ago, massive volcanoes in what is now India began erupting, sending large quantities of soot and debris into Earth’s atmosphere.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Even relatively small amounts of soot or debris in the atmosphere can cause detectable changes in the amount of sunlight that reaches Earth’s surface.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



Breakdown of the argument:


<u>Background</u>: Approximately 65 million years ago, Earth experienced the largest mass extinction of plant and animal species in its history.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Researchers hypothesize that the cause of that mass extinction was the impact of an asteroid that occurred approximately 65 million years ago.


<u>Premise</u>: Because the asteroid was one of the most massive asteroids known to have impacted Earth, its impact sent enormous amounts of soot and debris into Earth’s atmosphere, causing the amount of sunlight reaching Earth to suddenly decrease.


> <b>(A) Approximately 75 percent of Earth’s plant and animal species went extinct 65 million years ago.
</b>


 This choice is a tempting trap, like a shiny object that we might see and want to pick up, because what it says is right in line with what the passage says.


However, in saying that approximately 75 percent of Earth’s plant and animal species went extinct 65 million years ago, this answer choice merely provides some further detail about what we already know.


In other words, the passage states as fact that, approximately 65 million years ago, Earth experienced<em> the largest mass extinction of plant and animal species in its history</em>. So, by saying that approximately 75 percent of Earth’s plant and animal species went extinct, all this choice does is clarify what exactly constitutes “the largest mass extinction.”


In answering Strengthen the Argument questions, be careful to not choose as correct a choice that says something that simply magnifies or clarifies a fact that the passage has already provided.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) Fossil records indicate that species that required very little sunlight to live did not go extinct at the time of the asteroid impact.
</b>


What this choice says strengthens the case for the researchers’ hypothesis.


The researchers’ hypothesis is, essentially, that the asteroid impact prompted the largest mass extinction of plant and animal species in Earth’s history by causing the amount of sunlight reaching Earth to suddenly decrease.


So, we can tell that many plants and animals went extinct at that time.


At the same time, this choice tells us that species that required very little sunlight to live did not go extinct at the time of the asteroid impact. Of course, this information indicates that what determined whether organisms went extinct or were able to survive was their ability to live without much sunlight, and what follows from that reasoning is that lack of sunlight is what caused the mass extinction.


While this information does not prove for sure that a sudden decrease in sunlight caused the mass extinction, it does tend to confirm the researchers’ hypothesis. Remember, we’re not looking for information that creates an airtight case; we simply need information that strengthens the case.


> <b>(C) Evidence suggests that the asteroid that impacted Earth approximately 65 million years ago could have been 81 kilometers wide, about as wide as the country of Luxembourg is long.
</b>


 This choice is very tempting because, by providing information on how massive the asteroid that impacted Earth may have been, it could seem to confirm that the asteroid impact caused the mass extinction.


Notice, however, that we already have the following facts:


- The asteroid was one of the most massive asteroids known to have impacted Earth.


- The impact sent enormous amounts of soot and debris into Earth’s atmosphere, causing the amount of sunlight reaching Earth to suddenly decrease.


So, the truth is that the additional information provided by this choice doesn’t really make any difference in the strength of the argument. This choice provides further detail about what the approximate size of the asteroid may have been, but we already knew that it was “one of the most massive asteroids known to have impacted Earth,” massive enough that its impact caused a sudden decrease in the amount of sunlight reaching Earth. The additional detail this choice provides about the asteroid’s massiveness does nothing to strengthen the argument that the decrease in sunlight is what caused the mass extinction.


Since a choice that magnifies a fact stated in the passage doesn’t affect the strength of an argument, this choice has no effect on the strength of the support for the researchers’ hypothesis.


> <b>(D) Approximately 65 million years ago, massive volcanoes in what is now India began erupting, sending large quantities of soot and debris into Earth’s atmosphere.
</b>


 What this choice says weakens the argument.


The researchers’ hypothesis is that it was the asteroid impact that caused the mass extinction of plants and animals.


What this answer choice says, that approximately 65 million years ago, massive volcanoes in what is now India began erupting, sending large quantities of soot and debris into Earth’s atmosphere, provides an alternative explanation for the mass extinction of plants and animals, because the soot and debris from the volcanoes could have blocked sunlight or caused other catastrophic issues that prompted the mass extinction.


By providing reason to believe that volcanic eruptions, instead of the asteroid impact, may have been responsible for the mass extinction, this choice serves to call into question the researchers’ hypothesis.


Of course, in answering this question, we’re seeking an answer choice that provides information that strengthens, not weakens, the argument.


> <b>(E) Even relatively small amounts of soot or debris in the atmosphere can cause detectable changes in the amount of sunlight that reaches Earth’s surface.
</b>


This choice is tempting because it seems to confirm the researcher’s hypothesis by confirming that soot and debris block sunlight.


Notice, however, that we don’t have to confirm that soot and debris block sunlight, because the passage states as fact that soot and debris from the asteroid impact caused the amount of sunlight reaching Earth to suddenly decrease.


An answer choice that confirms or magnifies a fact that has already been stated in the passage doesn’t affect the strength of the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

##  Example 14 

The ancient Mayan city of Tikal collapsed relatively suddenly after thriving for over 1000 years.

Which of the following adds further support to the researchers’ hypothesis?

1. [ ] (A) The ancient Mayans were not aware that clearing of forests could affect meteorological conditions in ways such that sustained extreme drought would result.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Clearing of forests to make way for city development was for Mayans a common practice during the time period extending from the founding to the collapse of Tikal.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Tikal was able to grow to an especially large size because of a sophisticated infrastructure that allowed for extensive use of water from rainfall.
4. [ ] (D) Archeological evidence suggests that, in order to sustain their lifestyles, residents of Tikal would have had to fell the majority of the trees in the Tikal area.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) El Zotz, a city located close to Tikal at the time of Tikal’s collapse, continued to thrive after that point in time.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



The passage states that researchers have hypothesized the following:


<u>Conclusion</u>: Tikal collapsed because of sustained extreme drought conditions resulting from the clearing of forests.


The conclusion is supported by one premise:


<u>Premise</u>: Residents of the Tikal had cleared approximately two-thirds of the forest in the area in which the city was located.


The passage also provides the following background information:


<u>Background Information</u>: The ancient Mayan city of Tikal collapsed relatively suddenly after thriving for over 1000 years.


In order to correctly answer the question, we need additional information that supports the hypothesis that drought caused by clearing of forests resulted in the collapse of the city.


In considering the choices, we have to be careful not to fall for trap choices that seem to add support to the argument by confirming the validity of the premise that appears in the passage. Since that premise is considered true, information that serves only to confirm the validity of that premise does not affect the strength of the argument.


> <b>(A) The ancient Mayans were not aware that clearing of forests could affect meteorological conditions in ways such that sustained extreme drought would result.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, because we could use it as the basis of a narrative about why the Mayans would clear forests even though doing so would result in drought, and then construe that narrative as somehow supporting the hypothesis that Tikal collapsed because the clearing of forests led to drought conditions.


However, the truth is that the fact that Mayans were not aware of the consequences of clearing forests does not add support to the hypothesis that Tikal did in fact collapse because of such consequences.


> <b>(B) Clearing of forests to make way for city development was for Mayans a common practice during the time period extending from the founding to the collapse of Tikal.
</b>


This choice could be tempting because it seems to confirm the premise that the Mayans cleared forests in the area in which Tikal was located.


However, since that premise is already considered factual, what this choice says has no effect on the strength of the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) Tikal was able to grow to an especially large size because of a sophisticated infrastructure that allowed for extensive use of water from rainfall.
</b>


In order to see why this choice is correct, we have to see that, while it does not indicate that clearing of forests led to drought, it does tend to indicate that drought would have led to the collapse of Tikal, as it tends to indicate that, without water from rainfall, the existence of Tikal was not sustainable.


In other words, by using common sense, we can tell that, if a city was able to grow to an especially large size only because of the extensive use of water from rainfall, it is likely that, in the absence of rainfall, that city would collapse.


So, what this choice says makes it seem likely that sustained drought would have resulted in the collapse of Tikal, and thus this choice provides additional support for the hypothesis that drought caused the collapse.


> <b>(D) Archeological evidence suggests that, in order to sustain their lifestyles, residents of Tikal would have had to fell the majority of the trees in the Tikal area.
</b>


This choice could seem to be correct because it could be perceived as strengthening the argument by confirming that residents of Tikal did in fact clear forests.


However, the truth is that, since the premise “the residents of the Tikal had cleared approximately two-thirds of the forest in the area in which the city was located” is considered factual, confirmation that the residents cleared forests has no effect on the validity of the premise or the strength of the argument.


> <b>(E) El Zotz, a city located close to Tikal at the time of Tikal’s collapse, continued to thrive after that point in time.
</b>


What this choice says could be spun into a convoluted story that would weaken or strengthen the support for the hypothesis. However, since El Zotz could have continued to thrive regardless of whether drought caused the collapse of Tikal, this choice neither weakens nor strengthens the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

Let’s now discuss answers that support conclusions, but the wrong conclusions.
