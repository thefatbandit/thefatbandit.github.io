## Lesson Content
As we know, to arrive at the correct answer to a Strengthen question, we have to clearly understand exactly what conclusion we are seeking to support.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

To arrive at the correct answer to a Strengthen question, we have to clearly understand exactly what conclusion we are seeking to support. If we don’t, we might fall for an answer choice that supports a conclusion that is similar to the conclusion we are seeking to support but is not that conclusion.
{{< /alert >}}

Like the blatantly wrong answer choices that we discussed in an earlier section, an answer choice that supports the wrong conclusion does not affect the argument.

We can have a tendency – if we are not careful – to selectively remember parts of or put our own spin on what we read.

For example, the argument’s conclusion could be about revenue, while an answer choice could support a conclusion about expenses.

In each of these cases, by presenting us with an answer choice that supports a conclusion related to the conclusion that we are seeking to support, the test makers set a mental trap for us to fall into.

So how do we avoid falling into such a trap and supporting the wrong conclusion? By remembering what conclusion we <em>do</em> need to support.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a Strengthen question, to avoid choosing an incorrect answer choice that supports the wrong conclusion, be careful to remember exactly what conclusion you are seeking to support.
{{< /alert >}}

To see this type of answer choice in action, let’s revisit our Golado tax increase example:

> <em>The government of Golado has recently increased the taxes imposed on the sale of all alcoholic beverages in Golado. While the tax increase is sure to result in a significant decrease in consumption of alcohol in Golado, government officials believe that increasing the taxes on sales of alcohol will result in a reduction in Golado’s government budget gap.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides evidence suggesting that the government officials’ belief is correct?</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Supports the Wrong Conclusion</u>) The budget gap represents a relatively small percentage of the total government budget.</em>

What this tricky choice that supports the wrong conclusion says is related to the argument, but it doesn’t support the argument’s conclusion.

This choice could support a different conclusion, such as that revenue from the tax on alcohol will be sufficient for completely closing Golado’s budget gap.

So, this choice does not provide evidence suggesting that the government of Golado will achieve its goal of reducing the gap.

Here’s another example:

> <em>A new type of roofing material reflects almost all of the sunlight that hits it. As a result, cooling systems of houses with roofs composed of this material generally do not have to run as much on hot days as do cooling systems of houses with roofs composed of other materials. Therefore, having a roof composed of this new material will help a household to save energy.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, adds the most support to the conclusion?</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Supports the Wrong Conclusion)</u></em> <em>A roof composed of the new material costs less to install than does a roof composed of other materials normally used in roof construction.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer)</u></em> <em>Because roofs composed of the new material hold heat in, in houses with such roofs, the heating systems run less on cold days than they would were the roofs composed of other materials.</em>

OK, what conclusion do we have to support in order to get this one correct?

<u>Conclusion</u>: Having a roof composed of this new material can help a household to save energy.

Now, what conclusion does the choice that supports the wrong Conclusion support? It supports multiple possible conclusions.

> - Households using the new material will save money.

> - Because roofs composed of the new material are less expensive to install, they will be popular.

Of course, neither of these conclusions is the one that we need to support.

Now, let’s consider the correct answer.

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>Because roofs composed of the new material hold heat in, in houses with such roofs, the heating systems run less on cold days than they would were the roofs composed of other materials.</em>

This choice adds support for the argument’s conclusion, because, if the heating systems of houses with roofs composed of the new material run less than they would were the roofs composed of other materials, the heating system of such a house will use less energy than it would were the new material not used.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When answering a Strengthen question, we must be careful to choose an answer choice that supports the conclusion of the argument, not a choice that supports some other conclusion.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 15 

While, ordinarily, software companies hire customer service specialists to handle inbound calls made by people having trouble using the companies’ software, JustDoIt, a tax preparation software company, has the people who develop its software handle such calls.

Which of the following, if true, provides the strongest confirmation that JustDoIt’s strategy will work as predicted?

1. [ ] (A) JustDoIt receives fewer support calls per user of its software than other software companies typically receive per user of their software.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) JustDoIt’s software developers can help customers resolve software use issues as quickly as can customer service specialists.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Surveys indicate that JustDoIt’s customers are more satisfied with the support that JustDoIt provides than other software companies’ customers are with the support that those companies provide.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Software developers who handle calls from people having trouble using software gain insights they can use to make future versions of that software easy for virtually anyone to use.
5. [ ] (E) JustDoIt employs enough software developers that their taking time to handle support calls does not make impossible the timely development of software products.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

##### Question Level: medium



In the scenario presented, JustDoIt is having relatively highly paid software developers handle customer support calls rather than having customer service specialists who are paid less per hour than software developers handle the calls. In doing so, JustDoIt expects to achieve the following outcome.


<u>Conclusion</u>: JustDoIt expects to save money in the long run by having its software developers handle the calls.


In order to get this question correct, we have to remember what conclusion we are seeking to support, since some of the incorrect choices are trap choices that support related conclusions.


It’s important to notice that the author of this argument provides no evidence to support JustDoIt’s conclusion. Why does JustDoIt expect to save money in the long run?


So, to correctly answer the question, we have to find an answer choice that indicates that, by using the strategy, JustDoIt will save money in the long run.


> <b>(A) JustDoIt receives fewer support calls per user of its software than other software companies typically receive per user of their software.
</b>


 This choice supports the wrong conclusion. It supports a conclusion along the lines of that JustDoIt will not spend more money on customer support than other companies typically spend even though JustDoIt pays the people handling calls more per hour than the other companies pay. Why? Because, even though JustDoIt pays more per hour, it gets fewer calls per user.


On the other hand, this choice does not support the conclusion that JustDoIt will <em>save money</em> by having software developers handle calls.


After all, regardless of how few calls per user JustDoIt receives, by having software developers handle the calls, it is spending more money than it would if people who get paid less per hour were handling the calls.


So, the fact that JustDoIt receives fewer support calls per user than other companies receive does not indicate that JustDoIt will save money in the long run by having software developers handle the calls.


> <b>(B) JustDoIt’s software developers can help customers resolve software use issues as quickly as can customer service specialists.
</b>


 This choice supports the wrong conclusion. The conclusion is not that the software developers can handle calls just as well as or just as quickly as customer service specialists.


The fact that software developers can handle calls as quickly as customer service specialists can does not indicate that JustDoIt will save money by having software developers take the calls. After all, if it costs JustDoIt more per hour to have software developers handle the calls, and if the software developers handle the calls as quickly as customer service specialists do, then JustDoIt is still spending more money than it would spend by hiring customer service specialists, not saving money.


> <b>(C) Surveys indicate that JustDoIt’s customers are more satisfied with the support that JustDoIt provides than other software companies’ customers are with the support that those companies provide.
</b>


 This choice supports the wrong conclusion. The conclusion is not that JustDoIt can satisfy its customers by having software developers take support calls.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) Software developers who handle calls from people having trouble using software gain insights they can use to make future versions of that software easy for virtually anyone to use.
</b>


 If, in the process of taking support calls, JustDoIt’s software developers gain insights that they can use to make future versions of JustDoIt’s software easy for virtually anyone to use, then it’s likely that, in the future, JustDoIt’s software will be so easy to use that JustDoIt will receive few support calls.


Thus, given what this choice says, it seems likely that, by having product development professionals take support calls now, JustDoIt can reduce the amount of money it will have to spend on taking support calls in the future.


Notice that, with what this answer choice says, the author actually has support for her argument.


> <b>(E) JustDoIt employs enough software developers that their taking time to handle support calls does not make impossible the timely development of software products.
</b>


 This choice supports the wrong conclusion. The conclusion is not that JustDoIt can develop products in a timely manner even though its software developers handle support calls.


Notice that the fact that JustDoIt employs “enough software developers” does not indicate that JustDoIt will save money. On the contrary, in order to employ “enough software developers,” JustDoIt has to spend enough money.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

Let’s now discuss answer choices that explain rather than add support.
