## Lesson Content
We must always remember that the premises in Critical Reasoning arguments are considered factual.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The premises in Critical Reasoning arguments are considered factual. In other words, the premises are facts. Since there is no way to strengthen a fact, there is no way to strengthen a premise.
{{< /alert >}}

Therefore, it’s important to know that answer choices that explain part of a passage do not strengthen an argument because, as we just said, premises are facts, and even if we were to provide a 100-page essay explaining a fact, we couldn’t strengthen it.

So we need to be careful not to be tricked into choosing as correct a choice that says something that explains part of the passage instead of providing information that actually strengthens the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When answering a Strengthen question, we must make sure that we don’t get tricked into choosing a choice that explains part of the passage rather than supports the conclusion of the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s continue with our roofing material example:

> <em>A new type of roofing material reflects almost all of the sunlight that hits it. As a result, cooling systems of houses with roofs composed of this material generally do not have to run as much on hot days as do cooling systems of houses with roofs composed of other materials. Therefore, having a roof composed of this new material will help a household to save energy.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, adds the most support for the conclusion?</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Explains Part of the Passage</u>)</em> <em>Sunlight not reflected by a house’s roof can heat the house, an effect that the house’s cooling system must work to counteract.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>Because roofs composed of the new material hold heat in, in houses with such roofs, the heating systems run less on cold days than they would were the roofs composed of other materials.</em>

The choice that explains part of the stimulus seems to support the argument.

Because, we ALREADY KNOW that, by reflecting sunlight, the new material makes it easier for a cooling system to cool a house, and all this choice does is <em>explain</em> how that process works.

So, this choice doesn’t add support for the conclusion.

The correct answer, on the other hand, provides entirely new support, by indicating that, not only do roofs composed of the new material make cooling houses easier but also, they help to save energy on cold days when houses have to be heated.

Now, let’s see how a choice that explains part of the passage could work with the Super Soap scenario:

> <em>The Endless Summer cruise line washes its ships regularly, with the result that a large amount of soap goes into the ocean. Endless Summer’s management has decided to switch soaps, from the soap that the company currently uses to Super Soap, which, unlike the soap that the company currently uses, has essentially no negative environmental effects in the concentrations that result from washing ships. Management feels that, by making this change, Endless Summer will be taking a step toward reducing the negative environmental effects of the company’s operations.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, serves to confirm that management’s plan will have the desired effect?</em>

> <em>(<u>Choice that Explains Part of the Passage</u>) All of the ingredients in Super Soap are substances that exist naturally in the ocean.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) The process of manufacturing Super Soap has fewer negative effects on the environment than does the process of manufacturing the soap currently used by Endless Summer.</em>

Once again, the choice that explains part of the passage seems to support the conclusion.

However, the issue with this choice is that we already know from the passage that Super Soap “has essentially no negative environmental effects.” All the choice that explains part of the passage does is provide information that may <em>explain why</em> Super Soap has essentially no negative environmental effects.

As we discussed previously, the correct answer, on the other hand, does add new support for the conclusion, by showing an additional way in which Super Soap is less harmful to the environment than the soap that the company has been using.

##  Example 16 

Over the last forty years, the crime rate in the nation of Zundinia has steadily decreased.

Which of the following, if true, provides the best support for the hypothesis?

1. [ ] (A) Lead in water can be absorbed into a person’s body through the person’s digestive system if the person drinks water that contains lead.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) While, in Zundinia, paint that contains lead is no longer used, there is still paint that contains lead on the walls of some older buildings.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Forty years ago, a major initiative to increase quality of education in Zundinia was the start of a trend of improvement in education there that has continued to this day.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) The adding of lead to gasoline meant for use in automobiles in Zundinia has been almost completely phased out over the past four decades.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) In Zundinian cities in which most water is still transported via pipes containing lead, the crime rates are little changed from what they were 40 years ago.

##### Question Level: medium



We are looking for support for the following hypothesis:


<u>Conclusion</u>: It is likely that the decrease in the crime rate in Zundinia can be at least partly attributed to the simultaneous decrease that has occurred in the amount of lead in the air and water in Zundinia.


There is already some support for the hypothesis:


<u>Premise</u>: People exposed to air or water that contains lead absorb some lead into their bodies, where it has negative impacts on their cognitive functioning.


We must keep in mind that this premise must be considered a fact, and any explanation of this fact (how it came to be, what’s causing it, the physiology of lead poisoning, etc.) will not strengthen the argument.


At the same time, the above premise is a general principle, rather than information that clearly connects the decrease in lead in the air and water in Zundinia with the decrease in the crime rate. Thus, information that indicates more clearly that a reduction in lead exposure is indeed what underlies the decrease in the crime rate in Zundinia would strengthen the support for the hypothesis.


Notice that the author is making the cause-and-effect claim that the steady decrease in the crime rate has been caused by the decrease that has occurred in the amount of lead in the air and water. In answering this question, our job is to find an answer choice that strengthens the case for that cause-and-effect claim, but in answering question, we have to be careful not to choose a choice that simply explains an aspect of the scenario described in the passage.


> <b>(A) Lead in water can be absorbed into a person’s body through the person’s digestive system if the person drinks water that contains lead.
</b>


 This choice does not add support for the hypothesis but, rather, explains how lead from water gets absorbed into people’s bodies. The passage has already stated as fact that lead in water can be absorbed into people’s bodies. Explaining how it is absorbed does not strengthen the support for the hypothesis.


> <b>(B) While, in Zundinia, paint that contains lead is no longer used, there is still paint that contains lead on the walls of some older buildings.
</b>


 This detail does not add support for the conclusion, as the fact that there is still lead to which people may be exposed certainly does not help to support the conclusion that a reduction in lead exposure is the reason for a decrease in the crime rate. At the same time, this choice does not affect the validity of the premise, which is the stated fact that the amount of lead in the air and water has decreased.


> <b>(C) Forty years ago, a major initiative to increase quality of education in Zundinia was the start of a trend of improvement in education there that has continued to this day.
</b>


 This choice presents another change that has occurred over the past 40 years and that could underlie the decrease in the crime rate, an improvement in education. So, given what this choice says, it may be that better education rather than a reduction in environmental lead underlies the decrease in the crime rate. Thus, by presenting a plausible alternative hypothesis, this choice weakens the support for the hypothesis presented in the passage.


> <b>(D) The adding of lead to gasoline meant for use in automobiles in Zundinia has been almost completely phased out over the past four decades.
</b>


 This choice explains, at least partly, why the amount of lead in the air and water in Zundinia has decreased: it has decreased because automobiles are no longer burning leaded gasoline and thus spreading lead everywhere.


At the same time, we already know that the amount of lead in the air and water has decreased over the past 40 years.


Explaining why it has decreased does not strengthen the support for the hypothesis.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) In Zundinian cities in which most water is still transported via pipes containing lead, the crime rates are little changed from what they were 40 years ago.
</b>


 This choice strengthens the support for the hypothesis by showing what happens when we eliminate a variable, the decrease in the presence of lead.


It seems likely that, in cities in which most water is still transported via pipes containing lead, the water still contains lead. Thus, those cities likely do not have the variable “decrease of the amount of lead in the water.” It is also the case that in those cities the crime rates didn’t decrease.


Thus, without the variable “decrease in the amount of lead in the water,” we also don’t have the result “decrease in the crime rate.” Therefore, what this choice says tends to confirm that crime rates decreased because of decreases in the amount of lead in the water and, thus, strengthens the support for the hypothesis.


Remember, when we have a cause-and-effect conclusion, if we can show that, when the cause is eliminated, the effect is also, then we can strengthen the support for the conclusion.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
