## Lesson Content
A typical argument in a Strengthen question is based on a scenario that involves, for example, a particular place, company, phenomenon, or point in time.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a Strengthen question, we must take care not to select as correct choices that aren’t well-connected to the scenario in the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

So, what does it mean to be “not well-connected?” The truth of the matter is that what the GMAT considers not well-connected might be different from what we’d expect (sorry – don’t kill the messenger).

For instance, an argument could be about the expected effects of increasing education spending in one large city, while the answer choice could be about the effects of increasing education spending in a small town.

While, in the real world, we might consider the effects of actions taken in one city or town when considering what the effects of similar actions taken in a different city or town might be, in GMAT Critical Reasoning, the point of view generally taken is that such comparisons do not provide reliable evidence for conclusions.

So, in this example, the GMAT would take the point of view that, since conditions in the two places could be different, the fact that increasing education spending in the city had a certain effect is not strong support for the conclusion that increasing education spending in the small town will have the same effect.

Here are some more examples of information that the GMAT would not consider strong support for a conclusion:

> - The fact that a storm that occurred five years ago was followed by an increase in the price of tomatoes probably would not be considered strong support for the argument that, because there was a storm last month, the price of tomatoes will soon increase.

> - The fact that one large company was able to increase profitability by shutting down half its factories would probably not be considered the correct answer supporting a conclusion that a relatively small company should shut down factories.

> - The fact that a discount store's profits did not decrease when a recession occurred would not provide good support for the conclusion that a high-end department store's profits will not decrease during a recession.

> - The fact that a trucking company was able to use truck driving simulators to help its drivers reduce their yearly number of driving accidents does not provide good support that an airline would be able to use flight simulators to reduce the yearly number of accidents experienced by its airplane pilots.

While it would not be accurate to say that the GMAT never uses evidence from one situation to support an argument about another situation, it is accurate to say that comparisons like those in the examples above will rarely constitute the basis for a Strengthen correct answer.

Here’s an example of how these not well-connected choices work in context:

> <em>In response to residents’ complaints about constant noise, the city council of Mergate has decided to permanently close large public Mergate Airport. Even though many businesses in Mergate depend on spending by vacationers, the members of the city council believe that closing the airport will result in increased rather than decreased profitability for such businesses in Mergate.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides the strongest support for the city council members’ prediction?</em>

> <em>(<u>Not Well Connected Choice</u>)</em> <em>When a small private airport was shut down in Boselton, some businesses in Boselton experienced increases in revenue.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) Without the constant noise made by airplanes taking off and landing at Mergate Airport, Mergate will be a much more attractive destination for vacationers.</em>

To get this one correct, we would have to find the choice that supports the following conclusion:

> <u>Conclusion</u>: Closing Mergate Airport will result in increased rather than decreased profitability for many businesses in Mergate that depend on spending by vacationers.

Does the not well-connected choice provide solid support for this conclusion? Not really.

The correct answer, on the other hand, provides strong support for the conclusion that closing Mergate Airport will result in increased profitability for businesses in Mergate that depend on spending by vacationers, since it provides a reason to believe that, as a result of the closing of the airport, Mergate will become a much more attractive destination, and more vacationers will go there and spend money.

When answering a Strengthen question, look for a choice that provides information that is clearly connected to the scenario presented in the argument.

##  Example 17 

CSim Labs creates simulators that can be used to train people to use heavy construction equipment before they actually operate the equipment.

Which of the following, if true, provides the most additional support to the conclusion above?

1. [ ] (A) The use of simulators that pilots can use to simulate the operation of aircraft has contributed significantly to the high level of safety currently existing in civil aviation.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) A construction equipment operator in training using a simulator can learn to safely handle types of challenging situations that an operator might otherwise not be prepared to handle.
3. [ ] (C) When operators train by using actual construction equipment, the companies training the operators can incur significant expenses for fuel and equipment maintenance.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) Classroom training for young drivers has been shown to reduce the probability of drivers’ getting into crashes during their first few years of driving.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Simulators for training operators of construction equipment can be tailored for training operators in the use of a wide variety of types of construction equipment.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

##### Question Level: easy



The passage presents the following conclusion:


<u>Conclusion</u>: Companies that use CSim Lab’s simulators to train operators to operate heavy construction equipment will avoid many mishaps.


The passage supports that conclusion via the following premises:


<u>Premise</u>: Simulators can be used to train people to use heavy construction equipment before they actually operate the equipment.


<u>Premise</u>: Many operating mishaps occur when operators are first learning how to operate heavy construction equipment.


So, the reasoning is that, if operators can learn to use the equipment before they actually operate it, mishaps that would have occurred as the operators were learning will be avoided.


The correct answer will provide additional support for the same conclusion. In considering the choices, we must take into account how well connected the information presented in each choice is to the conclusion of the argument.


> <b>(A) The use of simulators that pilots can use to simulate the operation of aircraft has contributed significantly to the high level of safety currently existing in civil aviation.
</b>


 While the use of simulators in training pilots seems similar to using simulators in training construction equipment operators, the GMAT would not consider the fact that use of simulators in aviation contributed to a high level of safety strong evidence that, by using simulators in a different venue, construction, companies would avoid mishaps that would otherwise occur when operators are first learning how to use heavy construction equipment. This choice is not well enough connected to the scenario presented in the passage to be considered support for the conclusion.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B) A construction equipment operator in training using a simulator can learn to safely handle types of challenging situations that an operator might otherwise not be prepared to handle.
</b>


 If, through using a simulator, a construction equipment operator can learn to safely handle challenging situations that an operator might not otherwise be prepared to handle, then that operator might avoid experiencing mishaps in those types of situations. So, this choice adds support for the conclusion.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) When operators train by using actual construction equipment, the companies training the operators can incur significant expenses for fuel and equipment maintenance.
</b>


 This choice supports the wrong conclusion, the conclusion that companies can reduce expenses by using simulators, rather than the conclusion that companies can avoid mishaps by using simulators.


> <b>(D) Classroom training for young drivers has been shown to reduce the probability of drivers’ getting into crashes during their first few years of driving.
</b>


 This choice discusses a situation in which training helps with avoiding mishaps. However, the use of classroom training, rather than simulators, in the training of young drivers, rather than equipment operators, is not sufficiently connected to the scenario presented in the passage to be considered strong support for the conclusion.


> <b>(E) Simulators for training operators of construction equipment can be tailored for training operators in the use of a wide variety of types of construction equipment.
</b>


 This choice supports the wrong conclusion. We need something that supports the conclusion that training via the use of simulators will have certain effects. This choice supports the conclusion that simulators can be widely used.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

Let’s now talk about another type of incorrect choice, choices that are connected to the scenarios presented and seem to support the conclusions but don’t actually get the job done.
