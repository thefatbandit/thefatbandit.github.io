## Lesson Content
If the goal in answering a Strengthen question is to find an answer choice that strengthens the argument, then clearly a choice that weakens the argument must be incorrect.

However, when a test-taker is under the pressure of the test, answers that weaken rather than strengthen the arguments in Strengthen questions can be tempting because they are closely related to the given information and, thus, can seem to provide just the type of information needed to answer the question.

It’s easier than you might think to lose track and choose a weakening answer choice when we need a strengthener, because an answer of either type can be relevant to the argument.

So, when answering a Strengthen question, be on the lookout for answer choices that actually weaken the author’s argument, and eliminate them, by remembering what type of answer you need and paying attention to the effects of answer choices you are considering.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When you are answering a Strengthen question, be careful not to get tempted by relevance or wording into choosing an answer that weakens rather than strengthens the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s apply this awareness in the context of our Golado example:

> <em>The government of Golado has recently increased the taxes imposed on the sale of all alcoholic beverages in Golado. While the tax increase is sure to result in a significant decrease in consumption of alcohol in Golado, government officials believe that increasing the taxes on sales of alcohol will result in a reduction in Golado’s government budget gap.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides evidence suggesting that the government officials’ belief is correct?</em>

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Choice that Weakens</u>) A reduction in alcohol consumption resulting from the tax increase is expected to result in a reduction in the fees the government of Golado collects from importers of alcohol.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>In Golado, all of the organizations involved in dealing with health problems resulting from the consumption of alcohol are funded by the government.</em>

To correctly answer the question, we need an answer that supports the conclusion that increasing the taxes on sales of alcohol will result in a reduction in Golado’s government budget gap.

However, under the pressure of the test, a test-taker might lose track and choose an answer like the incorrect choice that weakens, because what it says is clearly relevant to a discussion of the budget of Golado.

Of course, as we have discussed, the correct answer, which at first glance may seem less relevant to the discussion of the budget than the incorrect choice that weakens, does support the conclusion.

##  Example 20 

65 million years ago, a mass extinction of dinosaurs occurred.

Which of the following, if true, does the most to confirm the validity of the explanation given?

1. [ ] (A) A group of volcanoes located in Asia were unusually active around the time of the dinosaur mass extinction, ejecting large amounts of matter into the atmosphere.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Fossil evidence indicates that, 65 million years ago, the number of species of dinosaurs inhabiting Earth rapidly decreased.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) The chemical composition of the layer of sediment deposited worldwide 65 million years ago resembles that of material found in the area where the asteroid landed in Yucatan.
4. [ ] (D) Near the Yucatan crater, material created by the asteroid impact was found underneath deposits of material that was created thousands of years before the dinosaur mass extinction.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) A dust cloud could affect the environment by reflecting much of the sunlight that would otherwise reach the planet.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: hard



To correctly answer this question, we need additional support for the following explanation:


<u>Conclusion</u>: It is likely that the dinosaur mass extinction was caused by the impact of an asteroid in the Yucatan area.


Notice that the author is making a cause-and-effect claim. Has the author presented sufficient evidence to support this cause and-effect-claim?


Not exactly. The passage has supported the explanation with evidence indicating that an asteroid impacted Earth and that a layer of sediment deposited at the time of the mass extinction came from a dust cloud that could have been created by an asteroid impact and that severely altered Earth’s environment.


Ok, great. But what’s missing? Something important. It’s not clear that the asteroid actually caused the dust cloud. Remember, the fact that one event precedes a second event does not allow us to conclude that the first event caused the second. The argument could be strengthened by information more clearly connecting the asteroid with the dust cloud.


> <b>(A) A group of volcanoes located in Asia were unusually active around the time of the dinosaur mass extinction, ejecting large amounts of matter into the atmosphere.
</b>


 This choice weakens rather than strengthens the argument. If volcanoes were erupting around the time of the mass extinction and ejecting large amounts of matter into the atmosphere, it could have been the volcanoes, rather than an asteroid impact, that caused the mass extinction.


> <b>(B) Fossil evidence indicates that, 65 million years ago, the number of species of dinosaurs inhabiting Earth rapidly decreased.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting because the effect of this choice is that it tends to confirm something stated in the passage, that a mass extinction of dinosaurs occurred 65 million years ago. After all, a rapid decrease in the number of dinosaur species and a mass extinction of dinosaurs are essentially the same thing. So, this choice could appear to make the argument more solid.


However, we don’t need to confirm that a mass extinction occurred, since the passage has already stated as fact that, 65 million years ago, a mass extinction of dinosaurs occurred, and as we have discussed, we can’t strengthen an argument by confirming premises or other facts stated in the passage.


So, actually, this choice has no material effect on what we know about the events described and, thus, has no effect on the strength of the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) The chemical composition of the layer of sediment deposited worldwide 65 million years ago resembles that of material found in the area where the asteroid landed in Yucatan.
</b>


 By saying that the chemical composition of the layer of sediment deposited worldwide 65 million years ago resembles that of material found in the area where the asteroid landed in Yucatan, this choice connects the asteroid impact to the sediment layer and to the dust cloud. Thus, it gives us reason to believe that the dust cloud was created by material from the asteroid or material from the Yucatan area that entered the atmosphere when the asteroid landed in Yucatan.


So, this choice provides evidence that supports the explanation given.


Notice that what this answer choice say gives us more reason to believe that the author’s conclusion is accurate.


> <b>(D) Near the Yucatan crater, material created by the asteroid impact was found underneath deposits of material that was created thousands of years before the dinosaur mass extinction.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting because it mentions material from the asteroid impact and, thus, seems relevant to the argument.


However, this choice weakens the argument.


By saying that material created by the asteroid impact was found underneath deposits of material created of thousands of years before the dinosaur mass extinction, this choice tends to indicate that the asteroid impacted Earth well before the dinosaur mass extinction, and thus would not have caused the mass extinction.


We have to be careful not to be tempted into choosing a relevant but incorrect choice like this one.


> <b>(E) A dust cloud could affect the environment by reflecting much of the sunlight that would otherwise reach the planet.
</b>


 This choice may seem to add information that supports the conclusion, but actually, it only <em>explains</em> how a dust cloud could have severely altered the environment.


The argument has already stated that the layer of sediment indicates that there was a dust cloud that severely altered the environment. Adding information on how such an alteration of the environment could occur does nothing to add support to the conclusion.


Remember, the premises provided in the passage are facts, and explaining some aspect of those facts does not provide additional support for the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
