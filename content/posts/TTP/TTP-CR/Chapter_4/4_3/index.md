## Lesson Content
Most of the time, recognizing a Strengthen the Argument question is easy because these questions utilize predictable language.

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most strongly supports _____?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides the strongest grounds for _____?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most helps to justify _____?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides the best reason for _____?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, would strengthen _____?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides the most support for _____?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, does the most to strengthen the argument?</em>

> <em>Any of the following statements, if true, helps to justify _____ EXCEPT:</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, is further evidence that _____?</em>

It’s important that we be ready to recognize these common prompts.

##  Example 2 

A major multinational company has instituted a program in which women who work in the factories that produce the garments that the company sells receive training in life skills and problem solving over a period of approximately three months.

Which of the following, if true, provides the most significant support for the company’s expectation?

1. [ ] (A) The number of women who have indicated that they are interested in participating in the program is sufficient for filling every available slot.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) Many of the women who work at the company’s factories have not previously had many opportunities to develop good life skills.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Even some of the women employed by the company who have more life experience than most others feel that they can gain from participating in the program.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) There is some question among experts regarding whether adults can develop their life skills significantly over a period of a few months.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) Lack of life or problem solving skills can underlie the development of ongoing issues that can interfere with people’s ability to work or remain employed.

##### Question Level: easy



In order to answer the question, we need an answer choice that provides support for the following expectation:


<u>Conclusion</u>: The program will pay for itself by resulting in greater productivity at the factories.


It’s important to notice that this argument provides no evidence to support the conclusion. After all, a conclusion is just an author’s opinion; it is his or her interpretation of some information. What information does the passage provide? The passage says that the company “has instituted a program in which women who work in the factories that produce the garments that the company sells receive training in life skills and problem solving over a period of approximately three months.” Does that fact necessarily allow the author to conclude that the program will pay for itself by resulting in greater productivity at the factories? No, it does not. After all, from the same fact the author could have just as well concluded that the program WILL NOT pay for itself by resulting in greater productivity at the factories. Right? In either case, there would be no support presented in the passage.


We’ll often see in a Strengthen the Argument question a situation in which a fact is presented, followed by an author’s conclusion, yet there is no support provided to justify that conclusion. Of course, without support for the conclusion, the argument is weak. Our job then is to seek an answer choice that provides actual support for the conclusion.


> <b>(A) The number of women who have indicated that they are interested in participating in the program is sufficient for filling every available slot.
</b>


 While, in order for the program to work, women have to sign up for it, the fact that every slot can be filled does not clearly support the conclusion that the program will result in greater productivity at the factories.


> <b>(B) Many of the women who work at the company’s factories have not previously had many opportunities to develop good life skills.
</b>


 This choice supports the wrong conclusion. It supports the conclusion that the program could benefit the women who sign up for it. However, that’s not the conclusion that we have to support.


> <b>(C) Even some of the women employed by the company who have more life experience than most others feel that they can gain from participating in the program.
</b>


 While the women may be correct in thinking that they will gain from participating in the program, the conclusion that we have to support is not that the women will gain from participating in the program.


> <b>(D) There is some question among experts regarding whether adults can develop their life skills significantly over a period of a few months.
</b>


 If anything, the fact that there is some question among experts regarding whether adults can develop their life skills significantly over a period of a few months weakens rather than strengthens the support for the conclusion, because it provides reason to question whether the program will have much of an effect.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) Lack of life or problem solving skills can underlie the development of ongoing issues that can interfere with people’s ability to work or remain employed.
</b>


 If, as this choice says, lack of life or problem solving skills can underlie the development of ongoing issues that can interfere with people’s ability to work or remain employed, then by helping the women to develop life skills, the program could prevent the development of issues detrimental to women’s productivity at the factories. Thus, this choice provides solid support for the expectation that offering the program will result in increased productivity at the company’s factories and that, thus, the program could pay for itself.


Notice that, if we now break this argument down, it’s considerable stronger with this answer choice added as a premise than the argument was as presented to us in the stem:


<u>Background</u>: A major multinational company has instituted a program in which women who work in the factories that produce the garments that the company sells receive training in life skills and problem solving over a period of approximately three months.


<u>Premise</u>: Lack of life or problem solving skills can underlie the development of ongoing issues that can interfere with people’s ability to work or remain employed.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Even though the skills that the women develop via the training are not directly related to the production of garments, the company expects the program to pay for itself by resulting in greater productivity at the factories.


We see that the addition of the premise has greatly strengthened the argument, giving us reason to believe that the author’s conclusion is valid.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

Let’s now discuss a systematic strategy that you can use when answering Strengthen the Argument questions.
