## Lesson Content
To find the correct answer to a Strengthen question, follow this 6-step strategy.

<u>Step 1 </u><u>–</u><u> Carefully read and understand the passage.</u>

<strong>The foundation of arriving at the correct answer to a Strengthen question is understanding the passage.</strong>

The importance of understanding the passage cannot be overemphasized.

So, your first job in answering a Strengthen question is to carefully read the passage, developing understanding of what the passage says and noting key details of the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

{{< /alert >}}

<u>Step 2 </u><u>–</u><u> Read the question stem and identify what it is asking you to do.</u>

Sometimes people confuse Strengthen the Argument questions with Weaken the Argument questions.

<u>Step 3 – Go back to the passage and determine exactly what the conclusion of the argument is.</u>

At this point, you understand the passage and know that you have to strengthen the argument.

Of course, the correct answer will somehow strengthen the support for the conclusion.

So, the next step is to go back to the passage and identify exactly what the conclusion is.

<strong>Identifying the exact conclusion of the argument is possibly the single most crucial step you will take in answering a Strengthen question.</strong>

If you have only a vague idea of what the conclusion is, you can easily fall for trap choices that strengthen the support for the wrong conclusion.

So, identify exactly what the conclusion is.

<u>Step 4 – Identify how the conclusion is supported.</u>

In some Strengthen questions, there are no premises in the passages.

In other Strengthen questions, there are in the passages supporting premises that we have to consider in answering the questions.

<u>Step 5 </u><u>–</u><u> Remove “weakeners” and </u><u>the choices that most obviously do not affect the argument</u><u>.</u>

Just as in a Weaken the Argument question, in a Strengthen the Argument question, there are usually only two serious contenders for the “best” answer among the five answer choices.

Although we’ll often find that three of the answer choices will be fairly easily classified as “weakeners” or choices that have no impact, we must read all five of the answer choices carefully before we start eliminating.

Now – and this is a major point – often people quickly eliminate the correct answers to Strengthen questions because they don’t see the connections between the correct answers and the arguments.

In fact, often the correct answer to a Strengthen question can look irrelevant at first, especially if it discusses something that was not mentioned in the argument.

So, in your first pass through the answer choices in a Strengthen the Argument question, it may make sense to keep some choices that you are not really sure about, even if your doing so means that you are left with more than two choices to work with.

<u>Step 6 </u><u>–</u><u> Choose the answer choice that most clearly strengthens the argument.</u>

Once we’ve narrowed the answer choices down to the final two or three, we must ask ourselves the following: Which of these <em>most clearly</em> strengthens the support for the conclusion?

One choice will have a clear strengthening impact on the support for the conclusion.

As the chapter progresses, we’ll continue to talk about the importance of precision in reading, and we know not to underestimate the impact that a single word can have on an answer choice.

Also, keep in mind that, in Strengthen the Argument questions, the correct answer need not discuss only information similar to what was discussed in the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Strengthen the Argument questions, as in Weaken the Argument questions, the correct answer need not discuss information similar to what was discussed in the passage. In other words, Strengthen the Argument correct answers are famous for presenting new information.
{{< /alert >}}

In fact, a hallmark of a more difficult Strengthen the Argument question is that the correct answer may well appear to be “out of scope,” although it does in fact strengthen the argument.
