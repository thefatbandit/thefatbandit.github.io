## Lesson Content
Often, in a scenario described in a Strengthen the Argument passage, there is room for issues that could undermine the conclusion.

For example, imagine if the CEO of an electric car company made the claim that his electric cars are much more environmentally friendly than their gas-powered counterparts because, while gas-powered cars emit greenhouse gases, electric cars do not.

In making that argument, he makes many assumptions, one of which is that there is no part of the electric car production process that could cause enough harm to the environment that his argument would be unsound.

For example, he’s assuming that the gas-powered heavy equipment (trucks, diggers, earth movers, cranes, backhoes, etc.) used to mine the metals used in the production of his car batteries don’t emit as much, or more, greenhouse gas as the gas-powered cars emit over their lifetimes.

To see how this type of answer choice works in a Strengthen the Argument question, consider the following mini-example:

> <em>The Endless Summer cruise line washes its ships regularly, with the result that a large amount of soap goes into the ocean. Endless Summer’s management has decided to switch soaps, from the soap that the company currently uses to Super Soap, which, unlike the soap that the company currently uses, has essentially no negative environmental effects in the concentrations that result from washing ships. Management feels that, by making this change, Endless Summer will be taking a step toward reducing the negative environmental effects of the company’s operations.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, serves to confirm that management’s plan will have the desired effect?</em>

In the scenario outlined above, Endless Summer’s management feels that changing soaps will result in reduced negative effects on the environment.

Think about what assumption is being made.

So, the following could be a strengthening answer choice:

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) The process of manufacturing Super Soap has fewer negative effects on the environment than does the process of manufacturing the soap currently used by Endless Summer.</em>

That answer choice addresses the issue of the effects of manufacturing Super Soap and provides some confirmation that using Super Soap is indeed better for the environment than using the soap that Endless Summer currently uses.

💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

An argument can be strengthened via addressing an issue that could affect the validity of the argument’s conclusion.

##  Example 5 

Because birds eat grapes, vineyards often use methods that are expensive or harmful to birds to keep birds from consuming the vineyards’ grapes before the grapes can be harvested.

Which of the following, if true, is most supportive of the idea that the plan will have the desired outcome?

1. [ ] (A) For many types of birds that live in the area where the Grape Estate vineyard is located, grapes are not preferred food.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Netting that can be used to cover grapevines and keep birds from eating the grapes not only is expensive but also must be replaced every few years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) If dogs in a vineyard are relatively inactive, some birds may feel that it is safe to eat grapes in the vineyard.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Generally, dogs that roam in vineyards do not either chew on grapevines or eat grapes.
5. [ ] (E) Most of the birds that would consume grapes from the vines at Grape Estates are migratory birds that pass through the area just twice a year.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



In the scenario presented, the vineyard plans to use dogs to keep birds from eating the grapes and thus to preserve the grapes for harvesting.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Using this low-cost approach that does not harm birds will result in most grapes remaining on the vines unharmed until they are ready for harvest.


In proposing the use of dogs, is there an assumption being made? Definitely, right? A major assumption is that the dogs themselves won’t somehow damage the grapes. We could weaken this argument by attacking that assumption. But here our job is to bolster the argument, and we can do so by showing that this assumption is a good one and that, in fact, the dogs will not cause harm to the grapes.


Always remember that, whenever anyone proposes a plan, he or she makes the assumption that the plan will work as it has be laid out, that there are not unaddressed issues that will derail the plan. Our job in answering a question like this one is to show that that assumption is a good one.


> <b>(A) For many types of birds that live in the area where the Grape Estate vineyard is located, grapes are not preferred food.
</b>


This choice could be tempting, because, since it indicates that many birds do not prefer to eat grapes, it could be perceived as providing additional support for the conclusion that the grapes will remain on the vines.


However, the passage has made clear that birds, at least some of them, do eat grapes, and what this choice says does not negate that stated fact.


So, this choice does not add information that changes the scenario or strengthens the argument, which is about keeping the birds that are interested in eating grapes from eating grapes.


> <b>(B) Netting that can be used to cover grapevines and keep birds from eating the grapes not only is expensive but also must be replaced every few years.
</b>


This answer is a tempting trap because, by mentioning some disadvantages of using netting to keep birds from eating grapes, this answer choice presents some reasons for using dogs to keep birds away from the grapes and makes the dogs approach look good.


However, it is not the correct answer because it does not provide additional support for the specific conclusion for which we are seeking support, which is not that the dogs approach is good in general but, rather, that the dogs approach will work.


> <b>(C) If dogs in a vineyard are relatively inactive, some birds may feel that it is safe to eat grapes in the vineyard.
</b>


What this choice says does not support the conclusion that the plan will work. If anything, it provides a reason to believe that the plan may not work. So, this choice is not a strengthener.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) Generally, dogs that roam in vineyards do not either chew on grapevines or eat grapes.
</b>


 This choice addresses a possible issue.


While dogs may scare away birds that would eat the grapes, it’s possible that the dogs themselves would damage the vines or eat the grapes. By addressing this issue by indicating that the dogs will not chew the vines or eat the grapes, this choice supports the idea that the plan for preserving the grapes will work.


Notice that this answer choice strengthens the argument by addressing an issue that could affect the validity of the conclusion.


> <b>(E) Most of the birds that would consume grapes from the vines at Grape Estates are migratory birds that pass through the area just twice a year.
</b>


This answer choice is one that gives us room to make up a convoluted story supporting the idea that the plan will work.


A test-taker might think, for instance, “Birds that pass through the area only twice a year will be more likely to be scared by the dogs than would be birds that have time to get accustomed to the dogs. So, this choice confirms that the plan will work.”


We really don’t have any reason to believe such a story though, and the truth is that this choice does not actually say anything that clearly supports the idea that the plan will work.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

##  Example 6 

The Roasted Coffee Company (RCC) is widely known for both its expensive coffees and its colorful ads featuring happy employees.

Which of the following, if true, provides the most effective support for the claim made by RCC?

1. [ ] (A) Field workers employed by RCC work 4 to 5 hours less per week than do field workers doing the same basic work for other coffee manufacturers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) RCC’s profit margins are considerably higher than those of the coffee manufacturers that dominate the market.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) RCC has promised to consider field workers when hiring people for management positions.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) RCC field workers who were asked to rate their level of job satisfaction knew that they could do so anonymously.
5. [ ] (E) Field workers employed by RCC tend to voluntarily leave the company after fewer years than are typically spent at one coffee manufacturer by workers employed by other coffee manufacturers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



RCC makes the following claim:


<u>Conclusion</u>: The field workers whom the company employs have a work environment that is <em>relatively relaxed</em> in comparison with the work environments of similar companies.


This claim is based on the evidence that RCC workers’ reported job satisfaction is higher than that of other coffee workers.


The Company’s claim rests on survey evidence, which is statistical in nature. Therefore, the credibility of the Company’s claim is tied to the validity of the statistics cited, and there is room for an issue that could affect the validity of the conclusion. In other words, remember that whenever an author cites statistical data – such as the results of a survey – she assumes that that data is actually effective as evidence in her argument. Therefore, any answer choice that provides additional evidence that underscores that that assumption is a good one will strengthen the argument.


> <b>(A) Field workers employed by RCC work 4 to 5 hours less per week than do field workers doing the same basic work for other coffee manufacturers.
</b>


 Be careful here. This evidence supports the wrong conclusion.


While the workers at RCC may be less stressed than workers at other companies because the workers at RCC work for fewer hours per week, working for fewer hours is not the same as working in a more relaxed environment. After all, workers could be working fewer hours per week in a high-pressure, stress-filled environment.


> <b>(B) RCC’s profit margins are considerably higher than those of the coffee manufacturers that dominate the market.
</b>


 This information is not clearly connected to the conclusion.


While profit margins and work environment could somehow be connected, in this case, we are given no information that indicates that the fact that the company has higher profit margins means that the company has a more relaxed work environment.


Don’t do the GMAT test-makers any favors by making up some suppose-suppose-suppose chain of logic in your own mind, one that connects profit margins to work environment, or the like. That type of reasoning is too hypothetical. Don’t shoehorn in an answer. Remember, as soon as you say, “Well, if I look at it <em>that </em>way…” likely you’re looking at the wrong answer.


> <b>(C) RCC has promised to consider field workers when hiring people for management positions.
</b>


Promotions and the degree to which the work environment is relaxed are two different subjects. So, while this choice might support a different conclusion, such as that RCC is a great place to work, it does not support the specific conclusion that we have to support, which is that RCC has a relatively relaxed work environment.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) RCC field workers who were asked to rate their level of job satisfaction knew that they could do so anonymously.
</b>


 By addressing an issue related to how the statistics were generated, this fact lends credibility to the conclusion.


Anonymous worker surveys may be considered more reliable than worker surveys in which the respondents are not anonymous, because survey respondents may answer more honestly when they remain anonymous, feeling that any negative answers can’t be held against them by their supervisors or by the company in general. Thus, a conclusion based on the results of an anonymous survey may be better supported than one based on those of a non-anonymous survey.


Notice that this answer choice strengthens the support for the conclusion by addressing an issue that could affect the validity of the conclusion.


Notice also that, in general, what this answer choice says underscores that the author’s assumption that the results of the survey are actually effective as evidence in her argument is a good one.


> <b>(E) Field workers employed by RCC tend to voluntarily leave the company after fewer years than are typically spent at one coffee manufacturer by workers employed by other coffee manufacturers.
</b>


 This answer choice actually weakens the argument. It doesn’t make much sense to say that employees experience high job satisfaction, yet they also have a high turnover rate. Common sense indicates that, if a person is happy with his or her job, that person stays with the job. This choice does <em>not</em> support the Company’s claim.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

Let’s now discuss how we can strengthen an argument by ruling out an alternative cause of an effect.
