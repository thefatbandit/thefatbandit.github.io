## Lesson Content
A Resolve the Paradox question is based on a passage that presents an apparent paradox, which, in GMAT CR, is a situation in which there exist two facts that seemingly contradict each other or that seemingly cannot both be true at the same time.

In other words, a paradox is composed of two seemingly incompatible statements of fact; the facts, at least without more information, don’t seem to make sense together.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In GMAT CR, a paradox is a situation in which there exist two facts that seemingly contradict each other or that seemingly cannot both be true at the same time.
{{< /alert >}}

However, in these questions there is no actual paradox, and the correct answer to a Resolve the Paradox question will present more information that reconciles the two seemingly incompatible statements.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The correct answer to a Resolve the Paradox question will present additional information that reconciles the two seemingly incompatible statements, showing how they can exist together harmoniously.
{{< /alert >}}

Thus, in answering Paradox questions, our job is to find an answer choice that reconciles the apparently contradictory statements, facts, or ideas presented in the stimulus.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a Resolve the Paradox question, our job is to choose the answer choice that best explains how both facts presented in the passage could simultaneously be true.
{{< /alert >}}

For instance, consider the following two facts, which create a paradox:

> Fact 1: A country’s natural resources can be used to generate wealth.

> Fact 2: It tends to be the case that countries with abundances of natural resources tend to have less economic growth than countries with fewer natural resources have.

Since the fact that natural resources can be used to generate wealth and the fact that countries with more resources tend to have less economic growth than countries with fewer resources have seem to contradict each other, the situation described by those two statements is paradoxical; it appears that the two statements cannot or would not be true simultaneously.

How could we resolve that paradox? One way is the following:

> Countries with abundances of natural resources are significantly more likely to be engaged in internal or external conflicts, which stifle economic growth, than are countries with fewer natural resources.

Notice that the above statement presents us with new information that allows us to see how the seemingly contradictory statements can in fact harmoniously coexist.

Here’s another example of a paradox.

> Fact 1: Patients with insomnia, which is the inability to fall asleep or stay asleep, risk falling ill because of the harmful effects caused by lack of sleep.

> Fact 2: Most doctors who treat insomnia patients do not instruct their patients to sleep more.

Notice the paradox presented to us.

One way to resolve this paradox is the following:

> It is well known that telling insomnia patients that they must sleep more causes them to experience increased worry about not sleeping, leading to even less sleep.

Notice that the above statement presents us with new information that allows us to see how the seemingly contradictory statements can in fact harmoniously coexist.

Now that we understand the basics of how Paradox questions work, let’s consider an example.

##  Example 1 

Critics of the current mayor of East Beach say that, while he has been mayor, little has changed in East Beach.

Which of the following statements, if true, would best resolve the apparent contradiction described above?

1. [ ] (A) In East Beach, mayoral elections are not held in the same years in which national elections are held.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) The most effective leaders of organizations are often unpopular among the organization’s members.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Not all residents of East Beach bother to vote in mayoral elections.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) The current mayor’s first and second terms were not consecutive but, rather, were separated by a term during which someone else was mayor.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) Most residents of East Beach are happy with the city the way it is.

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



What is the paradox here?


On one hand, critics of the current mayor of East Beach say that nothing has changed while he has been mayor. On the other hand, he keeps getting reelected.


How could both of these facts be true? The correct answer will provide a piece of information that explains how these two facts coexist.


> <b>(A) In East Beach, mayoral elections are not held in the same years in which national elections are held.
</b>


While this choice could be spun into a story about how people in East Beach don’t pay much attention to mayoral elections because they are not held when national elections are held, that story is not really supported by the given information.


Also, even if that story were true, that story would not explain why this person, and not some other person, has been elected. So, this choice does not resolve the paradox.


> <b>(B) The most effective leaders of organizations are often unpopular among the organization’s members.
</b>


This choice is tempting because it could be perceived as suggesting that the reason for the reelection of this person is that this person is effective. Notice, however, that this choice simply presents us with a generality about leaders, that the most effective leaders of organizations are often unpopular.


On the other hand, the passage does not provide reason to believe that the mayor is either effective or unpopular. Rather, in saying that little has changed, the critics of the current mayor of East Beach seem to be suggesting that he has not been effective.


So, what this choice says is not clearly connected to the scenario presented and, thus, doesn’t resolve the paradox.


> <b>(C) Not all residents of East Beach bother to vote in mayoral elections.
</b>


Notice that, regardless of what this choice says, we still don’t have an explanation for why whoever does vote votes for the current mayor, even though nothing has changed while he has been mayor.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) The current mayor’s first and second terms were not consecutive but, rather, were separated by a term during which someone else was mayor.
</b>


 This option could be tempting, because what it says could be perceived as somehow contradicting what the passage says or changing the scenario, since, while the passage says that this person has just been reelected for a third term, this choice indicates that this person’s time as mayor has been broken up by a term during which someone else was mayor.


However, the truth is that what this choice says doesn’t really change anything. Regardless of whether this person’s terms as mayor have been consecutive, this person has still been elected mayor for a third time, and the point made by the critics, that little has changed <em>while this person has been mayor</em>, is unaffected by what this choice says.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(E) Most residents of East Beach are happy with the city the way it is.
</b>


The paradox stands on the assumption that what the critics say, that little has changed in East Beach while this person has been mayor, constitutes a reason why this person would not be reelected.


What this choice says attacks that assumption, because, if most residents of East Beach are happy with the city the way it is, the fact that little has changed while this person has been mayor constitutes a reason <em>to </em>reelect this person; he’ll do exactly what most people want – change little. So, this choice resolves the apparent contradiction.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

{{< /alert >}}
</details></h5>


Notice how answer choice (E) in the mayor of East Beach example resolves the paradox and allows us to say, “Ah – that’s why the mayor keeps getting reelected even though critics say that little has changed.”
