## Lesson Content
The faster we can recognize what a Critical Reasoning question is asking us to find, the faster we can answer it.

Here are some of the most common question stems you’ll see:

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most helps to <strong>resolve</strong> the apparent discrepancy in the passage above?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, <strong>would explain</strong> the discrepancy described above?</em>

> <em>Which one of the following, if true, does most to <strong>justify</strong> this apparently paradoxical belief?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, best <strong>reconciles</strong> the seeming discrepancy described above?</em>

> <em>Which one of the following, if true, does the most to <strong>resolve </strong>the apparent paradox?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most helps to <strong>resolve</strong> the apparent paradox?</em>

> <em>Which of the following hypotheses best <strong>explains</strong> the contrast described above?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, <strong>most helps to account for</strong> …?</em>

> <em>Which of the following would, if true, best serve</em><em> as an <strong>explanation</strong> of the results of …?</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most helps to <strong>explain </strong>the apparent inconsistency?</em>

As we can observe, answering Paradox questions involves explaining how seemingly contradictory facts can harmoniously coexist.

##  Example 2 

To help curtail air pollution caused by emissions from gasoline engines, five years ago, the country of Rantala began providing citizens with large tax breaks on the purchase of any zero-emission electric vehicle, with the result that the number of such electric vehicles on Rantalan roadways has increased, while the number of gasoline-powered vehicles on the roadways has decreased.

Which one of the following, if true, does the most to resolve the apparent paradox?

1. [ ] (A) The country of Rantala has always used exemplary methods to measure air pollution caused by emissions from gasoline powered vehicles.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) When countries neighboring Rantala provided tax breaks on the purchase of zero-emission electric vehicles, those countries experienced significant declines in air pollution caused by emissions from gasoline engines.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Many of the citizens of Rantala have always chosen to use emission-free forms of transportation, including walking, riding bicycles, and using the electric subway system.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) In Rantala, five years ago, great numbers of massive gasoline-powered machines began working around the clock to extract from the ground metals necessary for manufacturing batteries of electric vehicles.
5. [ ] (E) In addition to providing citizens with large tax breaks on the purchase of any zero-emission electric vehicle, Rantala also imposed clean air taxes on any gasoline-powered vehicle owner.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

##### Question Level: easy



What is the paradox here?


The passage says that the number of zero-emission electric vehicles on Rantalan roadways has increased, while the number of gasoline-powered vehicles on the roadways has decreased.


At the same time, the passage says also that the amount of air pollution caused by emissions from gasoline engines has increased in Rantala.


How could both of these facts be true? The correct answer will explain how it could be that, even though the first fact is true, the second fact is also true.


> <b>(A) The country of Rantala has always used exemplary methods to measure air pollution caused by emissions from gasoline powered vehicles.
</b>


 Since this is a Paradox question, not a Strengthen question, we don’t have to provide a reason to believe anything stated by the passage. Our job is to take the statements as true and explain how the facts presented could be true at the same time.


So, while what this choice says about Rantala using exemplary methods to measure pollution could seem to provide some confirmation that what the passage says about pollution increasing is true, since we don’t need any such confirmation, this choice is not the correct answer.


> <b>(B) When countries neighboring Rantala provided tax breaks on the purchase of zero-emission electric vehicles, those countries experienced significant declines in air pollution caused by emissions from gasoline engines.
</b>


This choice does not explain how the two facts could be true at the same time. In fact, what this choice says makes us even more confused.


After all, if other countries experienced declines in air pollution when they provided tax breaks on the purchase of zero-emission electric vehicles, why did Rantala experience an increase in pollution?


> <b>(C) Many of the citizens of Rantala have always chosen to use emission-free forms of transportation, including walking, riding bicycles, and using the electric subway system.
</b>


 This choice does not affect the paradox at all.


OK, so, given what this choice says, many citizens of Rantala were already getting around in emission-free ways even before Rantala began providing tax breaks on the purchase of zero-emission electric vehicles.


All the same, according to the passage, since Rantala began providing those tax breaks, the number of zero-emission electric vehicles on Rantalan roadways has increased, while the number of gasoline-powered vehicles on the roadways has decreased.


So, why then has the amount of air pollution caused by emissions from gasoline engines increased in Rantala?


This choice does not answer that question.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) In Rantala, five years ago, great numbers of massive gasoline-powered machines began working around the clock to extract from the ground metals necessary for manufacturing batteries of electric vehicles.
</b>


 This choice explains why the amount of air pollution caused by emissions from gasoline engines has increased in Rantala.


While it’s true that, on the roadways, the number of electric vehicles has increased, and the number of gasoline-powered vehicles has decreased, another change has occurred as well. In order for that increase in the number of electric vehicles to occur, the manufacture of batteries has been necessary, and in order for those batteries to be manufactured, great numbers of massive gasoline-powered machines have been working around the clock to extract metals from the ground.


So, while there are fewer gasoline-powered vehicles on the roads, there are also more gasoline-powered machines working, and the operation of those machines is resulting in the increase in pollution.


So, this choice gives us that “Aha!” moment, resolving the paradox.


> <b>(E) In addition to providing citizens with large tax breaks on the purchase of any zero-emission electric vehicle, Rantala also imposed clean air taxes on any gasoline-powered vehicle owner.
</b>


 This choice explains the wrong thing.


This choice could help to explain why the number of gasoline-powered vehicles on Rantala’s roadways has decreased: people reduced their use of gasoline-powered vehicles to avoid paying the clear air tax.


Of course, we don’t have to explain why the number of gasoline-powered vehicles on Rantala’s roadways has decreased; we have to explain why pollution increased, and since there’s no reason to believe that a clean air tax imposed on gasoline-powered vehicle owners would cause an increase in pollution, this choice does not explain the increase in pollution and is not the correct answer.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
