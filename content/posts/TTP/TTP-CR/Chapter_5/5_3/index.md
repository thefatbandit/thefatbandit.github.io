## Lesson Content
In Resolve the Paradox Questions, the two seemingly contradictory statements will generally be connected by a keyword that indicates a contrast.

> yet, but, paradoxically, however, despite, unlike, even though, though, although, instead, rather, surprisingly, unexpectedly

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Learn to recognize the keywords that indicate a contrast between the two seemingly contradictory facts.
{{< /alert >}}

In Paradox questions, contrast keywords often appear between the two paradoxical facts.

Notice the use of the contrast word in the example below.

##  Example 3 

There are in Argentium three bridges across the river that flows through the city, a main bridge and two smaller bridges.

Which of the following, if true, best explains the surprising development?

1. [ ] (A) There are bridges just outside of Argentium city limits that motorists traveling to destinations within Argentium can use to cross the river without necessarily making long detours.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) The two smaller bridges located in Argentium were designed to handle much more vehicular traffic than usually passes over them, even during rush hour.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Many residents of Argentium ride bicycles, using special bike lanes, and thus do not contribute to vehicular traffic congestion.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) When aware in advance of issues that may cause traffic congestion, many people in Argentium choose to use public transportation rather than private automobiles.
5. [ ] (E) During the time period when the roadway on the main bridge was closed for maintenance, the pedestrian walkway on the main bridge remained open.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



We are presented with an apparent paradox outlined by two statements connected by the contrast word <strong>“however.”</strong> The facts presented are the following:


Fact 1: Normally, when a traffic incident causes a closure of the main bridge during rush hour, the two smaller bridges become congested with vehicular traffic.


Fact 2: When the roadway on the main bridge was closed for two months for maintenance, the amount of vehicular traffic on the two smaller bridges during rush hour was no greater than usual.


<u>What has to be explained</u>: So, picture it: when a traffic incident causes the main bridge to be closed during rush hour, the two smaller bridges become congested with vehicular traffic. However, when the roadway on the main bridge was closed for two months for maintenance, the amount of vehicular traffic on the two smaller bridges during rush hour was no greater than usual. That’s definitely a paradox, right? What could explain this surprising phenomenon?


> <b>(A) There are bridges just outside of Argentium city limits that motorists traveling to destinations within Argentium can use to cross the river without necessarily making long detours.
</b>


 The fact that there are bridges just outside of Argentium city limits that motorists traveling to destinations within Argentium can use to cross the river without necessarily making long detours does not help to resolve the paradox, because these bridges were always there. These other bridges were there when the rush hour crashes occurred, and these other bridges were there during the planned maintenance.


Now, the story would have been different if these new bridges had been constructed just prior to the planned maintenance period. Of course, in that situation, the presence of additional bridges would have helped to explain the surprising development, but, because the bridges were always there, the fact of their presence doesn’t help to resolve the paradox.


> <b>(B) The two smaller bridges located in Argentium were designed to handle much more vehicular traffic than usually passes over them, even during rush hour.
</b>


 The fact that the two smaller bridges located in Argentium were designed to handle much more vehicular traffic than usually passes over them, even during rush hour, does not help us to resolve the paradox. Regardless of how much vehicular traffic these two smaller bridges were designed to handle, we still wonder why, when the roadway on the main bridge was closed for two months for maintenance, the amount of vehicular traffic on the two smaller bridges during rush hour did not become greater than usual, given the fact that, when a traffic incident causes a closure of the main bridge during rush hour, the two smaller bridges become congested with vehicular traffic.


> <b>(C) Many residents of Argentium ride bicycles, using special bike lanes, and thus do not contribute to vehicular traffic congestion.
</b>


Regardless of how many residents of Argentium ride bicycles, using special bike lanes, and thus do not contribute to vehicular traffic congestion, the paradox, which involves vehicular traffic congestion, remains unresolved, because the use of bicycles is a constant that existed when the main bridged was closed because of a traffic incident and when the construction was ongoing. So, the fact that residents of Argentium ride bicycles does not explain the difference between the two situations.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) When aware in advance of issues that may cause traffic congestion, many people in Argentium choose to use public transportation rather than private automobiles.
</b>


Let’s think about this. Drivers generally cannot foresee, or plan for, traffic incidents. So, during rush hour, people head to the roads, operating under the assumption that they will get to where they are going without delay. So, when traffic incidents occur, the bridges become backed up. However, when the roadway on the main bridge was closed for two months for maintenance, people could have been, and likely were, aware of the closure in advance of traveling. If they were, they would have used public transportation, with the result that, even though the main bridge was closed, the two smaller bridges did not become congested. So, this choice explains why two different outcomes result from two different types of closures of the main bridge.


> <b>(E) During the time period when the roadway on the main bridge was closed for maintenance, the pedestrian walkway on the main bridge remained open.
</b>


 The fact that, during the time period when the roadway on the main bridge was closed for maintenance, the pedestrian walkway on the main bridge remained open does not resolve the paradox. Regardless of whether the pedestrian walkway was open, if the roadway was closed, presumably the bridge was closed to vehicular traffic, a state of affairs that normally results in traffic congestion on the two smaller bridges.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
