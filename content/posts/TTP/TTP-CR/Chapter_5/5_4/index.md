## Lesson Content
To answer Resolve the Paradox questions correctly, follow this 5-step strategy.

<u>Step 1</u><u> –</u><u> Carefully read and completely understand the passage.</u>

As is the case in answering any other type of Critical Reasoning question, the foundation of choosing the right answer to a Paradox question is understanding the passage.

<u>Step 2 – Read the question stem and identify what it is asking you to do.</u>

The nature of the information presented in the passage, along with the question stem, should make it easy to tell that you’re faced with a Paradox question.

<u>Step 3 – Bullet point the paradoxical facts.</u>

There might be more than two pieces of information in the passage, but there will be only two contradictory facts that need to be reconciled.

<u>Step 4 – Rephrase the paradox as a question.</u>

Rephrase the paradox as a question:

> <em>How can it be the case that <strong>Fact 1</strong>, even though <strong>Fact 2</strong>?</em>

For example:

> <em>How can it be the case that, <strong>on average,</strong> <strong>lifestyles did not improve</strong>, even though <strong>the percentage of people with college educations increased</strong>? </em>

In rephrasing the paradox as a question, what you are doing is making clear what you are seeking to explain.

In answering Critical Reasoning questions of some types, identifying the conclusions is key.

<u>Step 5 – Analyze the answer choices.</u>

For each choice, ask yourself: does this choice explain how it can be the case that Fact 1 is true even though Fact 2 is true? For only one choice will the answer to that question be “Yes.” Remember, if a choice explains anything other than how those facts can be true at the same time, it will not be the correct answer.

<u>Summary of the Strategy for Resolve the Paradox Questions</u>

To summarize, here is the 5-step strategy that you can use to answer Resolve the Paradox questions correctly:

##  Example 4 

Many of a company’s employees were using the company health plan to pay for services of holistic health practitioners.

Which of the following, if true, would best explain why the policy produced the reverse of its expected effect?

1. [ ] (A) Many holistic health practitioners are just as good as licensed physicians at identifying the reasons why a person feels ill.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) The company’s employees who use the services of holistic health practitioners believe that the methods used by holistic health practitioners are effective in solving health problems.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) The process of determining whether to formally diagnose a person with an illness often costs many times what a visit to a holistic health practitioner costs.
4. [ ] (D) The company could reduce its health plan-related costs by ceasing to require employees to get formal diagnoses from licensed physicians before using the services of holistic health practitioners.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) In many cases licensed physicians charge about what holistic health practitioners charge for similar services.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Step 1 – Carefully read and completely understand the passage.</u>


We see that, in the scenario presented, a company began requiring its employees to get formal diagnoses from licensed physicians before using the company health plan to pay for the services of holistic health practitioners, and that, after the company instituted this policy, its health plan-related costs unexpectedly increased.


<u>Step 2 – Read the question stem and identify what it is asking you to do.</u>


In reading the passage, we saw that it outlined a paradoxical situation by using two statements connected by the contrast word “however.”


We next see that the question stem is asking us to find the choice that explains why the policy produced the reverse of its expected effect.


Thus, we can tell that we are dealing with a Paradox question and that the correct answer will resolve the apparent paradox or explain what’s going on in the scenario.


<u>3. Bullet point the paradoxical facts.</u>


Fact 1: To ensure that employees would not use the services of holistic health practitioners unnecessarily, a company began requiring its employees to get formal diagnoses from licensed physicians before using the health plan to pay for such services.


Fact 2: After the company instituted this policy, its health plan-related costs increased rather than decreased.


<u>Step 4 – Rephrase the paradox as a question.</u>


<u>What has to be explained</u>: Why would it be that, after a company began requiring its employees to get formal diagnoses from licensed physicians before using the company health plan to pay for the services of holistic health practitioners, the company’s health plan-related costs <em>increased</em>?


<u>Step 5 – Analyze the answer choices.</u>


> <b>(A) Many holistic health practitioners are just as good as licensed physicians at identifying the reasons why a person feels ill.
</b>


 This choice merely presents information related to the situation that has no effect on the paradox. The fact that holistic health practitioners are just as good as licensed physicians at identifying the reasons why a person feels ill does not tell us anything about why the company’s costs increased.


> <b>(B) The company’s employees who use the services of holistic health practitioners believe that the methods used by holistic health practitioners are effective in solving health problems.
</b>


 This choice explains the wrong thing.


The fact that employees believe that the methods used by holistic health practitioners are effective explains why the employees use the services of holistic health practitioners.


Of course, that’s not what the correct answer has to explain, and this choice does not explain why the company’s costs increased. So, it’s not the correct answer.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) The process of determining whether to formally diagnose a person with an illness often costs many times what a visit to a holistic health practitioner costs.
</b>


 This choice explains exactly what we have to explain.


After all, if the process of determining whether to formally diagnose a person with an illness often costs many times what a visit to a holistic health practitioner costs, then by requiring employees to get formal diagnoses from licensed physicians, the company is likely requiring employees to spend more than they would have spent had they simply used the services of holistic health practitioners.


So, this choice explains why the company’s health plan-related costs increased and resolves the apparent paradox.


> <b>(D) The company could reduce its health plan-related costs by ceasing to require employees to get formal diagnoses from licensed physicians before using the services of holistic health practitioners.
</b>


 This choice does not have the effect we need at all.


Notice that the relationship between this choice and the statements in the passage is not that this choice explains the statements in the passage. Rather, this choice states a conclusion that follows from the statements in the passage.


After all, what this choice says certainly follows from the fact that, after the company began requiring its employees to get formal diagnoses from licensed physicians before using the health plan to pay for the services of holistic health practitioners, it’s health plan-related costs increased.


> <b>(E) In many cases licensed physicians charge about what holistic health practitioners charge for similar services.
</b>


 Notice that, what we have to explain is a difference, in terms of the company’s costs, between employees’ simply using the services of holistic health practitioners and employees’ using the services of physicians before they use the services of holistic health practitioners.


Meanwhile this choice tells us how physicians and holistic health practitioners are similar: what they charge for similar services is, in many cases, about the same.


The fact that physicians and holistic health practitioners are similar in this way does not explain a difference in the company’s costs.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

Let’s now discuss an important, make-or-break component of answering a Resolve the Paradox question.
