## Lesson Content
Precision in reading is one of the most important Critical Reasoning skills to master.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Resolve the Paradox questions, every word matters.
{{< /alert >}}

Accordingly, when test-takers get Paradox questions incorrect, they often do so because, when reading the passage of a Paradox question, they do not develop a precise understanding of what the paradox is that they need to resolve.

In answering a Paradox question, we must have a precise understanding of exactly what the paradox is that we must resolve.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a Paradox question, we must have a precise understanding of exactly what the paradox is that we must resolve. Without such an understanding, we’ll have little chance of correctly answering the question.
{{< /alert >}}

Now, you may be thinking that it’s obvious that, to correctly answer a Paradox question, we have to understand exactly what the paradox is that we must resolve, but here’s why we’re focusing on this component of answering a Paradox question.

To see why giving this component our full attention is so important, let’s consider an example:

> <em>For years, the town of Pondville had been overrun with ducks. So, the government of Pondville decided to offer a reward to any citizen who showed proof of having taken a duck to a neighboring town that lacked ducks and released it in the neighboring town. However, two years after the government began offering the reward, Pondville was even more overrun with ducks than it was before the reward was offered.</em>

<em>Which of the following best explains the surprising outcome described above?</em>

If we read the passage carefully, we see that what we have to explain is the following:

> Why, after the government of Pondville began offering a reward to any citizen who showed proof of having taken a duck to a neighboring town, did Pondville become even more overrun with ducks?

Here’s the issue though.

For instance, in the case of this question, we might decide that we have to explain the following, which is a vague, inexact version of what we have to explain:

> Why didn’t the plan work?

Or, we may decide that we have to explain the following:

> Why didn’t the number of ducks in Pondville decrease?

Notice that, while each of these questions resembles what we have to explain, neither is exactly what we have to explain.

> <em>(<u>Trap Choice</u>) Most citizens of Pondville were too busy to take ducks to another town to get a reward.</em>

Notice that this choice would explain <em>why the plan didn’t work</em>: citizens of Pondville were too busy to take ducks to another town to get the reward.

However, this choice does not explain what we have to explain.

Here’s another trap choice that we could fall for if we didn’t correctly identify what we have to explain:

> <em>(<u>Trap Choice</u>) Most ducks released in the neighboring town did not like the neighboring town and found their way back to Pondville.</em>

This choice could help to explain why <em>the number of ducks in Pondville didn’t decrease</em>: most ducks released in the neighboring town returned to Pondville.

Once again, however, this choice does not explain what we actually have to explain.

All the same, we can see how we could end up choosing one of the trap choices if we weren’t careful to identify exactly what we have to explain.

OK, so, what would explain what we have to explain?

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) In order to easily get the reward multiple times, some people in Pondville began breeding ducks in their back yards, from which many ducks escaped.</em>

We can see that this correct answer does explain what we have to explain.

At the same time, the key thing to notice here is that <strong>the correct answer would not look any better than the trap choices if we hadn’t identified exactly what we have to explain</strong>.

So, we can see that a very important step in answering a Paradox question is identifying exactly what the paradox is that we must resolve.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A very important step in answering a Paradox question is identifying exactly what the paradox is that we must resolve.
{{< /alert >}}

Now, often, to correctly identify what the correct answer to a Paradox question has to explain, we have to notice the <strong>direction</strong> of what the correct answer has to explain.

For instance, in the case of our example about the town overrun with ducks, what the correct answer has to explain is, basically, why there was an increase.

In other questions, the correct answers may have to explain why something decreased, why something did not increase, or why something remained the same.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Often, to correctly answer a Paradox question, we have to notice the direction of what the correct answer has to explain.
{{< /alert >}}

Alternatively, we may have to explain why two things that seem the same have different effects or why one variable has a surprising effect on another variable.

In each case, identifying exactly what we have to explain is going to be a key aspect of arriving at the correct answer.

Let’s now apply what we have just discussed in answering an example question.

##  Example 5 

In the small town of Oakville, the proprietors of the three locally owned coffee shops located there were dismayed to see that a major coffee shop chain with a big marketing budget had decided to open a shop in town.

Which of the following does the most to explain the surprising outcome?

1. [ ] (A) Most of the people who regularly drink coffee in Oakville prefer the taste of the locally owned shops’ coffee over that of the major chain’s coffee.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) During peak hours, the length of time customers spend in line at the major chain’s shop is often longer than some people are willing to wait.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) The proprietors of the locally owned coffee shops have run the shops for many years and are familiar to residents of Oakville.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) The marketing messages that the major chain’s shop puts out to potential customers have convinced many that drinking coffee is enjoyable and life enhancing.
5. [ ] (E) The major chain’s shop serves not only coffee but also gourmet sandwiches and desserts.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

##### Question Level: medium



What is the apparent paradox presented here?


Fact 1: A major coffee shop chain with a big marketing budget opened a shop in Oakville.


Fact 2: After the major coffee chain’s shop opened, three locally owned shops experienced significant increases in business.


Rephrased as a question, the apparent paradox is the following:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: Why would it be that, after a major coffee chain with a big marketing budget opened a shop in Oakville, three locally owned shops experienced significant increases in business?


Now, the key thing we have to note in this case is that only an answer choice that explains why the three locally owned shops <em>experienced increases in business</em> will be correct. A choice that does not explain increases in business will not be correct.


So, if we look for a choice that explains something vaguely similar to what we have to explain, such as why the locally owned coffee shops <em>have not experienced decreases in business</em> or why the local coffee shops <em>are still doing OK</em>, we could easily miss this question.


> <b>(A) Most of the people who regularly drink coffee in Oakville prefer the taste of the locally owned shops’ coffee over that of the major chain’s coffee.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because it describes a reason why locally owned coffee shops could successfully compete with the major chain’s shop.


Notice, however, that the fact that people prefer the locally owned shops’ coffee does not explain why the shops would experience <em>increases</em> in business. After all, people’s liking the locally owned shops’ coffee is not a new thing. Presumably they have liked these shops’ coffee all along. So, while this choice would explain why the locally owned shops <em>have not experienced decreases in business</em>, there is no reason why people’s preferring these shops’ coffee would result in increases in business.


So, this choice does not explain what we have to explain.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) During peak hours, the length of time customers spend in line at the major chain’s shop is often longer than some people are willing to wait.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because it could be taken as indicating that, at times, in order to avoid the line at the major chain’s shop, some people might decide to go to locally owned coffee shops.


Notice, however, that what the fact that there are long lines at the major chain’s shop really indicates is that the major chain’s shop has many customers. That fact that major chain’s shop has many customers would not explain why the locally owned shops experienced increases in business.


Also, for all we know, the locally owned shops also have long lines during peak hours.


So, this choice does not explain what the correct answer has to explain.


> <b>(C) The proprietors of the locally owned coffee shops have run the shops for many years and are familiar to residents of Oakville.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because it describes a reason why locally owned coffee shops could successfully compete with the major chain’s shop.


Notice, however, that the fact that people are familiar with the locally owned shops’ owners does not explain why the shops would experience <em>increases</em> in business. People’s knowing the locally owned shops’ owners is not a new thing. So, there is no reason why it would cause increases, in other words, something new.


Thus, this choice does not explain what we have to explain.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(D) The marketing messages that the major chain’s shop puts out to potential customers have convinced many that drinking coffee is enjoyable and life enhancing.
</b>


This choice could explain why the locally owned shops experienced increases in business.


The marketing done by the major chain’s shop convinced people to drink coffee. So, people in Oakville increased the amount of coffee that they drink, and all of the shops, including the locally owned shops, experienced increases in business.


Since this choice explains exactly what we have to explain, it is the correct answer.


> <b>(E) The major chain’s shop serves not only coffee but also gourmet sandwiches and desserts.
</b>


 There is no clear reason to believe that the major chain’s shop’s serving gourmet sandwiches and desserts would result in an increase in business at the other shops. So, this choice does not explain what we have to explain.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
