## Lesson Content
As we answer a Paradox question, there is no need to look for a conclusion either in the stimulus or in the correct answer, because the author is not making an argument in the way in which he or she does in a Strengthen or Weaken question.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

We don’t need to find a conclusion to correctly answer a Resolve the Paradox question. So, don’t look for one!
{{< /alert >}}

In the stimulus of a Resolve the Paradox question, the author is presenting us with a paradox; the stimulus is simply an observation.

Let’s look at a simple example:

> <em>Antibiotics are ineffective against viruses. Yet, doctors routinely prescribe a course of antibiotics to patients infected with certain viruses that weaken the immune system.</em>

Let’s look at the paradox here.

> Fact 1: Antibiotics are ineffective against viruses.

> Fact 2: Doctors prescribe antibiotics to patients infected with viruses that weaken the immune system.

If you’re struggling to find a conclusion in the above passage, the reason you are is because there isn’t one in the passage.

One way out of this paradox is to point out the following:

> <em>Patients who have compromised immune systems (such as those infected with these viruses) are likely to acquire secondary bacterial infections that antibiotics are effective against.</em>

Meanwhile, just as there is no point in looking for a conclusion in a Paradox question stimulus, we don’t need to look for a conclusion among the answer choices.

In fact, in some cases, incorrect choices that appear in a Resolve the Paradox question are simply conclusions that follow from the information in the stimulus.

Here’s an example of this type of incorrect answer choice in action:

> <em>In Verdantia, there are websites that allow consumers to compare prices at which goods are sold by various retailers. Even though the presence of such sites fosters competition between retailers, as the number of price comparison sites in Verdantia has increased to a point such that there are a very large number of such sites, the prices of goods that appear on them also have increased.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, would best explain this unexpected result of the proliferation of price comparison sites?</em>

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Choice that States a Conclusion</u>)</em> <em>Consumers are best off when there are some, but not a very large number of, price comparison sites.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>When there are many price comparison sites, retailers, which pay to appear on the sites, incur significant expenses, which they pass on to consumers in the form of higher prices.</em>

Notice how tempting the incorrect choice that states a conclusion could be.

The correct answer, on the other hand, has the effect that we are looking for, since it explains how it could be the case that a proliferation of price comparison sites could result in price increases even though such sites engender competition.

##  Example 6 

Online dating apps have made it easy for people to get in touch with many potential partners.

Which of the following, if true, provides the best explanation for these paradoxical results?

1. [ ] (A) For many people, using more traditional methods of meeting potential partners might work better than using online dating apps.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) A person using an online dating app can get in touch with likeminded people whom the person probably would not have met without using such an app.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) A person faced with even slight imperfections in a potential partner may be tempted to continue looking if many other potential partners seem to be available.
4. [ ] (D) Having many options does not always make choosing among options easier than it is when fewer options are available.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Whether a person is using dating apps or using more traditional methods of meeting people, in order for a relationship to work, the person has to communicate well.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: easy



What is the apparent paradox presented here?


Fact 1: Online dating apps have made it easy for people to get in touch with many potential partners.


Fact 2: In many cases, the use of such apps has resulted in people’s remaining single for much longer than they would have had they not used such apps.


Notice that the passage does not include a conclusion. That it doesn’t is not a problem, as we don’t need a conclusion. What we do need to see clearly is a paradox.


Rephrased as a question, the apparent paradox looks something like this:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: How could it be that using an app that makes getting in touch with potential partners easy results in people’s remaining single for much longer than they otherwise would have?


> <b>(A) For many people, using more traditional methods of meeting potential partners might work better than using online dating apps.
</b>


 This choice states a conclusion that follows from the statements in the stimulus.


If, even though online dating apps make getting in touch with potential partners easy, many people using them stay single for longer than they would have were they not using such apps, then it follows that, for many people, using more traditional methods of meeting potential partners might work better than using online dating apps.


All the same, we don’t need a conclusion. We need an explanation, and this choice does not explain how the two facts can be true simultaneously.


> <b>(B) A person using an online dating app can get in touch with likeminded people whom the person probably would not have met without using such an app.
</b>


 This choice simply extends what we already know, that people using online dating apps can get in touch with people easily. It doesn’t explain how using the apps can result in people’s staying single for longer than they otherwise would have.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) A person faced with even slight imperfections in a potential partner may be tempted to continue looking if many other potential partners seem to be available.
</b>


 This choice explains how using an app that makes getting in touch with potential partners easy could result in a person’s staying single for much longer than that person otherwise would have. Feeling that a huge number of choices are easily available, a person using such an app might become so picky that the person doesn’t end up in a relationship with anyone.


> <b>(D) Having many options does not always make choosing among options easier than it is when fewer options are available.
</b>


This choice might seem to state a conclusion that follows from the information presented in the stimulus.


Given the fact that using apps that make getting in touch with many people easy results in people staying single longer than they otherwise would have, one might conclude that having more options from which to choose does not always make choosing easier.


All the same, we don’t need a conclusion. We need an explanation that reconciles the two facts, and this choice does not explain why apps that make it easy to get in touch with many people results in people staying single for longer than they otherwise would have.


> <b>(E) Whether a person is using dating apps or using more traditional methods of meeting people, in order for a relationship to work, the person has to communicate well.
</b>


 This choice does not explain the right thing.


While this choice might explain why someone using online dating apps would stay single for just as long as a person using other methods would, it does not explain why a person using such apps would stay single for even longer than that person would without using such apps.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
