## Lesson Content
For any Resolve the Paradox question, there will likely be many possible ways in which we could resolve the paradox.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

For any Resolve the Paradox question, there will likely be many possible ways in which we could resolve the paradox. Our job is just to find one of them.
{{< /alert >}}

We’re not trying to find the one answer that must be true.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering a Resolve the Paradox question, we’re looking for an answer that could be true. We’re not seeking an answer that must be true.
{{< /alert >}}

For example, let’s say we were given the following stimulus:

<em>The South County region has received virtually no rainfall for several years. However, regional water managers have decided not to impose restrictions on residential water use.</em>

What’s the paradox here? Well, on the one hand, there’s no rain.

Here are some possible explanations:

<u>Explanation 1</u>:<em> </em><em>South County imports most of its water from North County, which has received above average rain</em>.

> <u>Reason</u>: If South County gets its water from North County, which has lots of water, then the lack of rain may not result in water shortfall and thus may not be grounds for restrictions on residential water use.

<u>Explanation 2</u>:<em> South County has received unusually high amounts of snow during the last few winters.</em>

> <u>Reason</u>: If South County has received lots of snow, then it could meet its water needs even though there hasn’t been rain.

<u>Explanation 3</u>:<em> South County recently opened a desalination plant that converts seawater into drinkable fresh water.</em>

> <u>Reason</u>: If South County is making fresh water from seawater, then it doesn’t need rain to meet its water needs.

<u>Explanation 4</u>:<em> South County regional water managers have imposed severe water restrictions on industrial and agricultural water use.</em>

> <u>Reason</u>: If the water managers are restricting other kinds of water uses, then they might not need to restrict residential water use.

<u>Explanation 5</u>:<em> South County has water reserves sufficient for meeting its needs for the next decade.</em>

> <u>Reason</u>: If South County has lots of extra water in reserve, then a period without rain isn’t necessarily a problem that would require restrictions on water use.

We should notice that none of these statements has to be true.

##  Example 7 

Estimates of life expectancy made via use of mortality tables are, on average, found to be fairly accurate, and many companies successfully utilize such tables for business purposes.

Which of the following, if true, best explains the discrepancy between the common experience of the predictive utility of the tables and the experiences financial planners have had with using the tables?

1. [x] (A) People who hire financial planning consultants tend to take unusually good care of all aspects of their lives, including their health.
2. [ ] (B) A financial planning consultant who makes recommendations on the basis of life expectancy predictions based entirely on standard mortality tables could cause clients to experience significant financial difficulties.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) In addition to using mortality tables, many financial planners use interactive online tools to predict client lifespans.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Financial planners who base predictions of clients’ lifespans entirely on data from mortality tables are more likely to underestimate than overestimate clients’ lifespans.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Most financial planning consultants are aware that using mortality tables can result in underestimating life expectancy, and therefore they adjust their predictions accordingly.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



What is the apparent paradox presented here?


Fact 1: Estimates of life expectancy made via use of mortality tables are, on average, found to be fairly accurate.


Fact 2: Financial planning consultants who have used mortality tables in determining the life expectancies of clients have found that, on average, their clients significantly outlive the projections that have been made via use of the tables.


Rephrased as a question, the apparent paradox looks something like this:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: Why would it be that, even though estimates of life expectancy made via use of mortality tables are, on average, found to be fairly accurate, financial planning consultants find that, on average, their clients significantly outlive such estimates?


Notice, the answer does not have to be true given what is said in the stimulus. Rather, the answer will have to show why the clients tend to outlive the estimates.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) People who hire financial planning consultants tend to take unusually good care of all aspects of their lives, including their health.
</b>


 This choice shows that there is something about the clients of financial planning consultants that makes the clients unusual and that makes them likely to live longer than most people do. They tend to take unusually good care of their health.


This fact would explain why, even though life expectancies predicted via use of mortality tables are generally fairly accurate, clients of financial planning consultants would, on average, outlive such life expectancies.


> <b>(B) A financial planning consultant who makes recommendations on the basis of life expectancy predictions based entirely on standard mortality tables could cause clients to experience significant financial difficulties.
</b>


 It must be true that a financial planning consultant who makes recommendations on the basis of life expectancy predictions based entirely on data from mortality tables could cause clients to experience significant financial difficulties, because clients of financial planning consultants tend to outlive such projected life expectancies, and financial planning consultants help people to plan for retirement.


However, in order to correctly answer the question, we don’t need an answer that must be true, but rather one that explains why the clients tend to outlive such predictions, and this choice does not help to explain why they do.


> <b>(C) In addition to using mortality tables, many financial planners use interactive online tools to predict client lifespans.
</b>


 Given that lifespan predictions based on mortality tables often turn out to be inaccurate, it’s not surprising that financial planning consultants would use tools other than mortality tables for predicting lifespans.


However, the fact that financial planning consultants use other tools does not explain why their clients tend to outlive lifespans predicted via the use of mortality tables.


> <b>(D) Financial planners who base predictions of clients’ lifespans entirely on data from mortality tables are more likely to underestimate than overestimate clients’ lifespans.
</b>


It must be true that financial planners who base predictions of clients’ lifespans entirely on data from mortality tables are more likely to underestimate than overestimate clients’ lifespans, because, on average, financial planning consultant’s clients outlive projected lifespans based on data from mortality tables.


However, we don’t need a choice that expresses something that must be true, but rather a choice that explains why the clients tend to outlive such predictions.


> <b>(E) Most financial planning consultants are aware that using mortality tables can result in underestimating life expectancy, and therefore they adjust their predictions accordingly.
</b>


 This choice does not explain the right thing.


While this choice helps to explain how financial planning consultants avoid underestimating their clients’ lifespans, it doesn’t explain why their clients tend to outlive projected lifespans based on data from mortality tables.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
