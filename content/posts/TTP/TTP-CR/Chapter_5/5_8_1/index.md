## Lesson Content
As we have discussed, our job in answering a Resolve the Paradox question is to find a choice that explains how the facts that make up the apparent paradox can coexist.

Incorrect choices that explain the wrong thing, in other words, choices that explain something other than how the facts can coexist, are common in Paradox questions.

Such a choice can be rather tempting because, even though it doesn’t explain what the correct answer has to explain, it does explain something related to the information presented by the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Resolve the Paradox questions, trap choices often explain the wrong thing.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s look at an example:

> <em>In South America, many fruit crops have traditionally been pollinated by native bumblebees. In the late 1990’s European bumblebees were introduced into areas of South America, to assist with crop pollination. However, in some cases, following the introduction of European bumblebees, crop yields decreased rather than increased.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, does the most to explain why this unexpected result occured?</em>

So, what’s the paradox here?

> Fact 1: European bumblebees were introduced into areas of South America, to assist with crop pollination.

> Fact 2: Following the introduction of European bumblebees, yields of some crops decreased rather than increased.

The correct answer to this Paradox question will explain how both facts could be true even though they seem to be in conflict.

For instance, a choice that explains something related to only one side of the paradox will be incorrect.

> <em>(<u>Choice that Explains the Wrong Thing</u>) Since European bumblebees are known to successfully pollinate crops in Europe, it was expected that they would be helpful in pollinating crops in South America.</em>

The above choice explains why European bumblebees were introduced into areas of South America.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Resolve the Paradox questions, an option that explains something related to only one side of the paradox will be incorrect.
{{< /alert >}}

A choice that explains something related to the entire stimulus but does not explain how the two facts can coexist will also be incorrect.

> <em>(<u>Choice that Explains the Wrong Thing</u>) Pollination of most crop plants by bees is necessary, because, without such pollination, these plants will not bear fruit.</em>

The above statement explains something related to the entire stimulus: why pollination by bees is necessary in order for crop plants to yield fruit.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Resolve the Paradox questions, an option that explains something related to the entire stimulus but does not explain how the two facts can coexist will be incorrect.
{{< /alert >}}

The following choice has the effect that we are looking for:

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) European bumblebees carry bee killing parasites to which bumblebees native to South America have little resistance.</em>

Finally, we have a statement that explains why crop yields decreased: while European bumblebees were assisting with pollination, the native bees that had been pollinating crops before the arrival of European bees were being killed by parasites carried by the European bees.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Beware of choices that explain things other than how the two facts that make up the paradox can coexist.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 8 

Dolphins have been observed swimming at speeds in excess of 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water.

Which of the following, if true, resolves the apparent paradox described above?

1. [x] (A) Dolphins can ride waves, utilizing the waves’ energies to propel themselves through water.
2. [ ] (B) When dolphins swim at their maximum speeds, they can readily catch fast moving fish and squid, which are staple foods in dolphins’ diets.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) Scientists have determined that sharks of some species generate enough energy to swim at speeds in excess of 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Dolphins have been observed swimming at speeds far lower than 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Dolphins navigate through ocean water using echolocation, a process involving emitting and receiving sound waves, allowing them to accurately map their surroundings.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Fact 1: Dolphins have been observed swimming at speeds in excess of 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water.


Fact 2: However, when biologists analyze the muscles of dolphins, all agree that dolphins do not have sufficient muscle mass to generate enough energy to propel their bodies at 50 kilometers per hour through water.


Think about it. Dolphins have been observed swimming at speeds in excess of the speeds one would expect them to be able to achieve given their muscle mass.


What could explain this peculiar situation? What information would resolve this apparent paradox?


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) Dolphins can ride waves, utilizing the waves’ energies to propel themselves through water.
</b>


What this answer choice says resolves the apparent paradox.


If dolphins can ride waves, utilizing the waves’ energies to propel themselves through water, then this fact would explain why dolphins have been observed swimming at speeds in excess of 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water, even though their own bodies can’t produce enough power for them to reach those speeds.


Imagine, for example, that a dolphin is swimming at, let’s say, 25 kilometers per hour and riding a wave moving at 25 kilometers per hour. In this case, the dolphin could be moving through the ocean at 50 kilometers per hour.


We see that there is no paradox after all. Dolphins ride waves to make use of power that they cannot produce on their own.


> <b>(B) When dolphins swim at their maximum speeds, they can readily catch fast moving fish and squid, which are staple foods in dolphins’ diets.
</b>


 Notice that this choice explains the wrong thing.


This choice may explain why dolphins swim so fast: maybe they swim fast to catch fast moving fish and squid.


Of course, in answering this question, we’re not seeking to explain <em>why</em> they swim that fast. Rather, we’re seeking to explain <em>how</em> they swim that fast.


> <b>(C) Scientists have determined that sharks of some species generate enough energy to swim at speeds in excess of 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water.
</b>


 We’re seeking to explain how dolphins can travel more than 50 kilometers per hour through the ocean despite the fact their bodies can’t produce energy sufficient for them to reach such speeds.


What this answer choice says, that scientists have determined that sharks of some species generate enough energy to swim at speeds in excess of 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water, can’t help us resolve the paradox because it doesn’t change what we know about dolphins.


Given that this answer choice says, we’re still wondering how dolphins reach speeds in excess of 50 kilometers per hour in the ocean.


> <b>(D) Dolphins have been observed swimming at speeds far lower than 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water.
</b>


 The fact that dolphins have been observed swimming at speeds far lower than 50 kilometers per hour in ocean water does not help us explain how dolphins have been observed moving at speeds in excess of 50 kilometers per hour in the ocean.


OK, so they don’t always swim faster than 50 kilometers per hour. That fact does not change the fact that we have to explain, that sometimes dolphins do swim at speeds greater than 50 kilometers per hour.


We can’t resolve a paradox by showing that not all situations are characterized by the paradox that we have to explain.


> <b>(E) Dolphins navigate through ocean water using echolocation, a process involving emitting and receiving sound waves, allowing them to accurately map their surroundings.
</b>


 What this choice says explains the wrong thing.


What it says may explain how dolphins navigate effectively while moving at high speeds: maybe they use echolocation to navigate effectively at high speeds.


However, it does not explain how dolphins move at speeds in excess of 50 mph in the ocean.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)

##  Example 9 

Email scams work by convincing people that they will benefit from engaging in business transactions with people who have contacted them by email.

Which of the following best helps to explain this surprising pattern?

1. [ ] (A) For many people who perpetrate email scams, the language that they use in composing emails pitching sham transactions is not their first language.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) A single person can efficiently send thousands of emails pitching sham transactions by using bulk email management software.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Many of the introductory emails sent by people perpetrating email scams are based on patterns that have been used many times.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) The people who do not notice the low quality of an email are those least likely to realize that a transaction is a scam in the middle of the process of completing it.
5. [ ] (E) Many of the people who write emails pitching scams have much experience in perpetrating scams and thus know which methods are most effective.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



What is the apparent paradox presented here?


Fact 1: The introductory emails meant to get people to consider engaging in scam transactions are often written in ways such that recognizing that the senders are pitching scams is not difficult.


Fact 2: The low quality of the emails can result in scammers being more rather than less productive.


Rephrased as a question, the apparent paradox looks something like this:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: How could it be that the fact that it is not difficult to recognize that emails are pitching scam transactions results in scammers’ being more productive?


Only a choice that answers the above question will work. A choice that explains anything else will not work.


> <b>(A) For many people who perpetrate email scams, the language that they use in composing emails pitching sham transactions is not their first language.
</b>


 This choice explains the wrong thing.


Rather than explain how the low quality of the emails results in scammers’ being more productive, it seems to explain why the wording of the emails is of such poor quality: the writers are composing emails in languages other than the writers’ first languages.


> <b>(B) A single person can efficiently send thousands of emails pitching sham transactions by using bulk email management software.
</b>


 This choice explains the wrong thing.


Rather than explain how the low quality of the emails results in scammers’ being more productive, it explains their productivity in another way: they use software to send emails in bulk.


> <b>(C) Many of the introductory emails sent by people perpetrating email scams are based on patterns that have been used many times.
</b>


 This choice does not explain the right thing.


This choice could perhaps help to explain why scammers often use poorly worded email messages that discuss transactions that sound unlikely to be legitimate: the scammers are sending messages based on patterns that have been used many times, and perhaps those patterns aren’t very good.


However, this choice does not explain why sending poorly worded messages about transactions that sound unlikely to be legitimate results in relatively high productivity. After all, the fact that the patterns have been used many times does not explain why use of messages based on the patterns results in relatively high productivity. In other words, simply using a pattern many times does not cause it to work well.


In fact, what this choice says leaves us with a new thing to explain: Why have email scammers been basing their messages on these patterns if doing so results in poorly worded messages about transactions that sound unlikely to be legitimate?


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) The people who do not notice the low quality of an email are those least likely to realize that a transaction is a scam in the middle of the process of completing it.
</b>


This choice explains why sending low quality messages results in relatively high productivity: people who would use scammers’ time by starting to engage in transactions and then stopping when they realized that the transactions are fraudulent won’t even start to engage in the transactions, because the poor quality of the messages tips them off from the outset.


Thus, as a result of the low quality of the messages, the scammers end up dealing with the people least likely to realize that the transactions are scams and who are, thus, most likely to complete the transactions.


In other words, as a result of the low quality of the messages, scammers spend less time dealing with people unlikely to complete the scam transactions and more time dealing with people likely to complete the transactions and, thus, are more productive than they would be if the messages were of higher quality.


> <b>(E) Many of the people who write emails pitching scams have much experience in perpetrating scams and thus know which methods are most effective.
</b>


 This choice could be very tempting, since it almost seems to explain why sending low quality emails results in relatively high productivity. However, it explains the wrong thing.


Rather than explain how the low quality of the emails results in scammers’ being more productive, this choice seems to explain why scammers use low quality emails: the scammers have learned that using low quality emails results in relatively high productivity via their experiences with various methods.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

{{< /alert >}}
</details></h5>


So, an answer choice in a Paradox question will be correct only if it explains how the two facts can be true simultaneously.
