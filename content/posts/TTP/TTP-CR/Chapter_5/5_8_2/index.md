## Lesson Content
We can never use a similarity between two things to explain a paradoxical difference between them.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

We can never use a similarity between two things to explain a paradoxical difference between them.
{{< /alert >}}

A stimulus may discuss things that are similar in some ways but are different in a way that seems paradoxical.

Now, one good strategy for approaching a question involving such a scenario is to look for a choice that explains how the two seemingly similar things are actually different.

On the other hand, any answer choice that shows how the two are similar will not resolve the paradox.

Let’s look at an example:

> <em>Many of the most profitable movies in recent years have been based on well-known television shows. Last year, Global Pictures released two such movies: <u>Star Command</u> and <u>Space Explorers</u>. Even though both were based on well-known television shows, <u>Star Command</u> was a commercial success, while <u>Space Explorers</u> was a commercial failure.</em>

<em>Which of the following best explains the surprising difference between the two outcomes?</em>

So, what’s the paradox here?

> Fact 1: Both <u>Star Command</u> and <u>Space Explorers</u> were based on well-known television shows.

> Fact 2: <u>Star Command</u> was a success and <u>Space Explorers</u> was not.

The correct answer has to point out some relevant difference between the two movies that explains why one succeeded and the other failed.

A choice that focused on how the two movies were similar would be incorrect.

For example:

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Choice that Shows How the Two Things are Similar</u>) Critics reviewing the movies had expected both of them to be commercial successes.</em>

The incorrect choice that shows how the two things are similar fits with the fact that the two movies were created in similar ways, but we aren’t looking for a choice that simply fits well with the fact that the movies were created in similar ways.

In other words, we can’t resolve a paradox that involves a difference between two things by saying, “Well, actually, they aren’t so different.”

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A paradox involving a difference between two things cannot be resolved by simply showing that the things are actually somehow similar.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following examples.

##  Example 10 

Although cardiovascular exercises, such as running, tend to burn significantly more calories per hour than strength training exercises, such as weight lifting, a recent study has shown that people who engage in only strength training exercises tend to lose more body fat than people who engage in only cardiovascular exercises lose.

Which of the following, if true, resolves the apparent paradox described above?

1. [ ] (A) Both cardiovascular exercises and strength training exercises have been shown to increase the endurance of those who engage in them.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) Both cardiovascular exercises and strength training exercises have been shown to help people live longer.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Most people who engage in cardiovascular exercises do so for significantly longer time intervals than people who engage in strength training exercises do so for.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) Both cardiovascular exercises and strength training exercises provide valuable benefits for heart health, blood sugar control, and joint stability.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) People who burn relatively high numbers of calories per hour while exercising are triggered to consume more post-exercise calories than people who burn fewer calories per hour while exercising consume.

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



What is the apparent paradox presented here?


Fact 1: Cardiovascular exercises tend to burn significantly more calories per hour than strength training exercises.


Fact 2: A recent study has shown that people who engage in only strength training exercises tend to lose more body fat than people who engage in only cardiovascular exercises lose.


Rephrased as a question, the apparent paradox looks something like this:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: Why would it be that, even though cardiovascular exercises tend to burn significantly more calories per hour than strength training exercises, a study has shown that people who engage in only strength training exercises tend to lose more body fat than people who engage in only cardiovascular exercises lose?


Notice that the correct answer has to explain a difference between the results of engaging in strength training exercises and the results of engaging in cardiovascular exercises: people who engage in the first lose more body fat than people who engage in the second.


So, a choice that explains how the two forms of exercise are similar will not be correct, because we can’t explain a difference by showing how two things are similar.


> <b>(A) Both cardiovascular exercises and strength training exercises have been shown to increase the endurance of those who engage in them.
</b>


 Notice that this choice explains how the two forms of exercise are similar: both increase the endurance of those who engage in them.


A choice that explains how the two are similar will not explain the difference in results.


> <b>(B) Both cardiovascular exercises and strength training exercises have been shown to help people live longer.
</b>


 Notice that this choice explains how the two forms of exercise are similar: both have been shown to help people live longer.


A choice that explains how the two are similar will not explain the difference in results.


> <b>(C) Most people who engage in cardiovascular exercises do so for significantly longer time intervals than people who engage in strength training exercises do so for.
</b>


 The effect of this choice is the opposite of the effect the correct answer must have. This choice makes the scenario even more paradoxical.


After all, the information that we already have makes it surprising that a study has shown that people who engage in only strength training exercises tend to lose more body fat than people who engage in only cardiovascular exercises lose. Now, the information provided by this choice makes those study results even more surprising.


After all, if most people who engage in cardiovascular exercises do so for significantly longer time intervals than people who engage in strength training exercises do so for, then we have even more reason to expect that the people who engage in cardiovascular exercises would be the ones to lose more body fat.


But they aren’t the ones. Why?


> <b>(D) Both cardiovascular exercises and strength training exercises provide valuable benefits for heart health, blood sugar control, and joint stability.
</b>


 Notice that this choice explains how the two forms of exercise are similar: both provide valuable benefits for heart health, blood sugar control, and joint stability.


A choice that explains how the two are similar will not explain the difference in results.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) People who burn relatively high numbers of calories per hour while exercising are triggered to consume more post-exercise calories than people who burn fewer calories per hour while exercising consume.
</b>


 This choice perfectly explains what we have to explain.


After all, if what this choice says is true, then we can see why a study has shown that people who engage in only strength training exercises tend to lose more body fat than people who engage in only cardiovascular exercises lose: the people who engage in only cardiovascular exercises burn relatively high numbers of calories per hour and, therefore, are triggered to consume relatively high numbers of post-exercise calories. So, even though they burn more calories while exercising, they make up for burning those calories by consuming more calories after exercising.


Thus, by showing how the two forms of exercise are different, this choice explains the difference in results and resolves the apparent paradox.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

##  Example 11 

The primary ingredient in both dark chocolate and milk chocolate is cocoa.

Which of the following, if true, best explains the difference in health benefits?

1. [ ] (A) Dark chocolate and milk chocolate both contain sugar, which, when consumed in sufficient quantities, can cause health problems.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Unlike dark chocolate, milk chocolate often contains large quantities of milk, cream, butter, and certain oils, each of which is well-documented to cause significant damage to the lining of arteries.
3. [ ] (C) Both dark chocolate and milk chocolate have been found to contain pesticide residues, which have been shown to damage the lining of arteries when present in large amounts.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) A large percentage of the calories found in dark chocolate come from saturated fat, heavy consumption of which has been linked to artery dysfunction.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Because both dark chocolate and milk chocolate contain a relatively large number of calories per ounce, both can cause weight gain in those who consume them.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



What is the apparent paradox presented here?


Fact 1: The primary ingredient in both dark chocolate and milk chocolate is cocoa, which contains compounds called flavonoids, which have been demonstrated to improve the health of the lining of arteries.


Fact 2: Research shows that, while the consumption of dark chocolate offers health benefits to arteries, the consumption of milk chocolate does not offer such benefits.


Rephrased as a question, the apparent paradox looks something like this:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: Why does research show that, while the consumption of dark chocolate offers health benefits to arteries, the consumption of milk chocolate does not offer such benefits, even though the primary ingredient in both dark chocolate and milk chocolate contains flavonoids, which have been demonstrated to improve the health of the lining of arteries?


Notice that the correct answer has to explain the difference between the health benefits of the consumption of dark chocolate and those of the consumption of milk chocolate. So, a choice that explains how the two forms of chocolate are similar will not be correct, because we can’t explain a difference by showing how two things are similar.


> <b>(A) Dark chocolate and milk chocolate both contain sugar, which, when consumed in sufficient quantities, can cause health problems.
</b>


 Notice that this choice explains how the two forms of chocolate are similar: both contain sugar, which can cause health problems.


A choice that explains how the two are similar will not explain the difference in health benefits.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) Unlike dark chocolate, milk chocolate often contains large quantities of milk, cream, butter, and certain oils, each of which is well-documented to cause significant damage to the lining of arteries.
</b>


This choice perfectly explains what we have to explain.


After all, if what this choice says is true, then we can see why research shows that, while the consumption of dark chocolate offers health benefits to arteries, the consumption of milk chocolate does not offer such benefits: milk chocolate is “unlike” dark chocolate, in that milk contains ingredients not contained in dark chocolate that are well-documented to cause significant damage to the lining of arteries.


Thus, by showing how the two forms of chocolate differ, this choice explains the difference in health benefits and resolves the apparent paradox.


> <b>(C) Both dark chocolate and milk chocolate have been found to contain pesticide residues, which have been shown to damage the lining of arteries when present in large amounts.
</b>


 Notice that this choice explains how the two forms of chocolate are similar: both have been found to contain pesticide residues.


A choice that explains how the two are similar will not explain the difference in health benefits.


> <b>(D) A large percentage of the calories found in dark chocolate come from saturated fat, heavy consumption of which has been linked to artery dysfunction.
</b>


 The key issue with this choice is that it provides information on dark chocolate only and does not tell us whether it is also the case that a large percentage of the calories found in milk chocolate come from saturated fat.


We can’t explain the difference between the health benefits of consumption of dark chocolate and the health benefits of consumption of milk chocolate by using information on dark chocolate without corresponding information on milk chocolate.


Since we don’t have information on whether this characteristic of dark chocolate makes it similar to or different from milk chocolate, what this choice says about dark chocolate does not help us to explain the difference in health benefits.


Of course, there’s also another issue: this choice seems to explain why dark chocolate, which contains saturated fat, could be detrimental, rather than beneficial to artery health.


So, this choice does not explain the difference in health benefits.


> <b>(E) Because both dark chocolate and milk chocolate contain a relatively large number of calories per ounce, both can cause weight gain in those who consume them.
</b>


 Notice that this choice explains how the two forms of chocolate are similar: both contain a relatively large number of calories per ounce.


A choice that explains how the two are similar will not explain the difference in health benefits.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

Remember, choices that explain how things are similar will not resolve paradoxes involving differences between those things.
