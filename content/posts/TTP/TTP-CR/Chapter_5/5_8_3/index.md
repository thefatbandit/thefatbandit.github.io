## Lesson Content
Be on the lookout for choices that actually make the situation described even more paradoxical.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Beware of choices that actually make the situation described even more paradoxical, that deepen the paradox. A situation can be made more paradoxical by information making the two facts appear to be more contradictory or by the addition of what seems to be more conflict to the scenario presented.
{{< /alert >}}

Remember, our job is to resolve the paradox, not to add to it.

Let’s look at an example:

> <em>Currently, the Beach City Gazette, a local newspaper, has more subscribers to its printed version than it has had at any other point in its 80-year history. However, the paper reported significant operating losses and this week had to lay off 30 percent of its staff.</em>

<em>Which of the following, if true, would explain what underlies this paradoxical situation?</em>

So, what’s the paradox here?

> Fact 1: More subscribers to its printed version than ever before.

> Fact 2: Operating losses and layoffs.

The correct answer has to provide information that could be used to explain why, even though the paper has a record number of subscribers to its printed version, it is experiencing operating losses.

Meanwhile, a choice that adds to the paradoxical nature of the situation is incorrect.

> <em>In addition to having a record number of subscribers, the Gazette has been selling a record number of copies at newsstands.</em>

> <em>The cost of the paper on which the Beach City Gazette is printed has decreased.</em>

> <em>Advertising rates received by the Beach City Gazette are the highest they have ever been.</em>

Notice the effects of these incorrect choices.

Could a choice that, like the three above, does the opposite of what the correct answer has to do ever really be tempting? Yes, and here’s why.

Consider this statement:

> <em>The online version of the Beach City Gazette has become even more popular than the printed version.</em>

The above statement adds to the paradoxical nature of the scenario, by mentioning another way in which the Beach City Gazette is succeeding.

The moral of this story is that, to get Paradox questions right, we have to pay close attention to the effects of what answer choices say.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When answering a Paradox question, be careful not to choose an answer that actually augments, rather than resolves, the paradox.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following examples.

##  Example 12 

Five years ago, after determining that few cyclists wore helmets while riding bikes in Rockport, the Town of Rockport passed a new law making the use of helmets mandatory for all cyclists riding in Rockport.

Which of the following, if true, provides the best explanation for these paradoxical results?

1. [x] (A) Wearing a helmet while cycling increases the likelihood that a cyclist will ride more recklessly than he or she would if he or she were not wearing a helmet.
2. [ ] (B) As of five years ago, all automobiles registered in the Town of Rockport have been required to be equipped with advanced collision avoidance safety systems.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) The number of people who cycle in the Town of Rockport has decreased over the last five years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Since the passage of the new helmet law, the Town of Rockport has increased its number of bicycle-only lanes, in which neither cars nor pedestrians are permitted.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Manchester, a town close to Rockport, saw a decrease in cycling-related injuries after passing a similar law, which required the use of helmets while cycling.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Why would the passage of a law mandating the use of helmets, which one might reasonably expect to cause a decrease in the number of injuries to cyclists, be followed by an increase in the number of such injuries?


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) Wearing a helmet while cycling increases the likelihood that a cyclist will ride more recklessly than he or she would if he or she were not wearing a helmet.
</b>


We need to explain why the number of injuries sustained by cyclists in Rockport each year increased after the passage of the helmet law, which would have been expected to lead to a decrease in injuries sustained by cyclists.


If it is true that wearing a helmet while cycling increases the likelihood that a cyclist will ride more recklessly than he or she would if he or she were not wearing a helmet, then we have a very good explanation for the paradox presented in the passage. If cyclists began to ride more recklessly while wearing helmets, then that reckless riding would certainly explain why the number of injuries sustained by cyclists increased despite the helmet law.


> <b>(B) As of five years ago, all automobiles registered in the Town of Rockport have been required to be equipped with advanced collision avoidance safety systems.
</b>


 What this answer choice says deepens the paradox, leaving us more confused.


If, as of five years ago, all automobiles registered in the Town of Rockport have been required to be equipped with advanced collision avoidance safety systems, then why did the number of injuries sustained by cyclists increase?


We would expect advanced collision avoidance safety systems to cause a <em>decrease</em> in the number of injuries sustained by cyclists, or at the very least, we certainly would not expect that the safety systems would lead to an increase in the number of injuries sustained by cyclists.


We must always be careful not to choose as correct a choice that deepens the paradox.


> <b>(C) The number of people who cycle in the Town of Rockport has decreased over the last five years.
</b>


What this answer choice says deepens the paradox, leaving us more confused.


If it is true that the number of people who cycle in the Town of Rockport has decreased over the last five years, then we would expect to see a decrease in the number of injuries sustained by cyclists, because a decrease in the number of cyclists on the road should result in a decrease in injuries.


Instead, the number of injuries increased.


We must always be careful not to choose as correct a choice that deepens the paradox.


> <b>(D) Since the passage of the new helmet law, the Town of Rockport has increased its number of bicycle-only lanes, in which neither cars nor pedestrians are permitted.
</b>


What this choice says deepens the paradox, leaving us more confused.


If it is true that, since the passage of the new helmet law, the Town of Rockport has increased its number of bicycle-only lanes, in which neither cars nor pedestrians are permitted, then we would expect to see a decrease in the number of cycling injuries, because it is common knowledge that bicycle-only lanes make riding safer, not more dangerous, for cyclists.


If you, for some reason, didn’t agree that it is common knowledge that bike lanes should make cycling safer, fine. In any case, an increase in bicycle-only lanes would not explain the increase in the number of cycling injuries.


> <b>(E) Manchester, a town close to Rockport, saw a decrease in cycling-related injuries after passing a similar law, which required the use of helmets while cycling.
</b>


Although the Town of Manchester might be materially different from the Town of Rockport, what this answer choice says goes in the direction of deepening the paradox.


After all, if Manchester, a town close to Rockport, saw a decrease in cycling-related injuries after passing a similar law, which required the use of helmets while cycling, then we have some information that seems to indicate that helmet laws make cycling safer.


So then, why did the number of injuries sustained by cyclists in Rockport each year increase? The results of the helmet law in Manchester give us even further reason to believe that the number of cycling injuries in Rockport should have <em>decreased</em> rather than increased.


We must always be careful not to choose as correct a choice that deepens the paradox.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)

##  Example 13 

In an effort to increase the amount of tax revenue it collects each year, three years ago, the City of Newland Falls doubled the rate of the property tax that its residents must pay on the ownership of real estate within city limits from 5 percent to 10 percent.

Which of the following statements, if true, would best explain the paradoxical outcome described above?

1. [x] (A) Because of the high property tax rate, residents of Newland Falls have been abandoning their houses and moving to other towns, leaving the houses unoccupied and without owners.
2. [ ] (B) Newland Falls used much of the property tax revenue it collected to pay for marketing campaigns in nearby states designed to entice people to move to, and purchase new homes in, Newland Falls.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) Over the past decade, the average annual income of residents of Newland Falls has consistently been nearly three times as high as that of residents of all adjacent cities.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Census surveys administered by Newland Falls show that the average age of all residents of Newland Falls has increased over the past three years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Over the past three years, the amount of sales tax revenue collected by Newland Falls each year has increased by more than 20 percent.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Three years ago, the City of Newland Falls doubled the rate of the property tax that its residents must pay on the ownership of real estate within city limits from 5 percent to 10 percent. Of course, we would expect such a change to result in an increase in the tax revenue collected by the city. However, paradoxically, since that time, the total tax revenue collected by the city has decreased by 15 percent each year.


Our job is to find an answer choice that helps us to explain why the tax revenue collected by the city has decreased in the face of an increase in the real estate tax.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) Because of the high property tax rate, residents of Newland Falls have been abandoning their houses and moving to other towns, leaving the houses unoccupied and without owners.
</b>


 A key detail of the passage to notice is, “the City of Newland Falls doubled the rate of the property tax that its residents must pay on <em>the ownership of real estate</em> within city limits from 5 percent to 10 percent.” In other words, if people do not own real estate, they do not pay the real estate tax.


Thus, if it is true that, because of the high property tax rate, residents of Newland Falls have been “abandoning their houses and moving to other towns, leaving the houses unoccupied and without owners,” then this fact would certainly resolve the paradox. After all, if residents are abandoning their houses and moving to other towns, leaving the houses without owners, then, probably, there are fewer owners of real estate paying real estate tax in Newland Falls than there were before.


Notice that this answer choice gives us that “Aha!” feeling. We think, “Right. That is why the total tax revenue collected by the city has decreased by 15 percent each year even though the city increased the property tax rate.”


> <b>(B) Newland Falls used much of the property tax revenue it collected to pay for marketing campaigns in nearby states designed to entice people to move to, and purchase new homes in, Newland Falls.
</b>


 What this choice says deepens the paradox.


After all, if Newland Falls used much of the property tax revenue it collected to pay for marketing campaigns designed to entice people to move to, and purchase new homes in, Newland Falls, then we would expect to see more people purchasing homes in Newland Falls. As more people purchased homes in Newland Falls, they would be subject to the increased tax rate. Thus, we would expect to see the city collect more, not less, tax revenue. So, then, why did the tax revenue decline?


We are left more puzzled by what this choice says.


> <b>(C) Over the past decade, the average annual income of residents of Newland Falls has consistently been nearly three times as high as that of residents of all adjacent cities.
</b>


 This choice is not clearly connected to the paradoxical decrease in tax revenue in Newland Falls. While some government entities do collect taxes on income, we have no clear reason to believe that the level of income of Newland Falls residents is directly connected to the amount of tax revenue collected by the City of Newland Falls each year.


Furthermore, even if there exists some connection between the level of Newland Falls residents’ income and the amount of tax revenue collected by the city, this choice does not say that the level of income of Newland Falls residents changed. Rather, it indicates that the ratio of the average income of Newland Falls residents to the average income of residents of other towns has remained the same for a decade.


The fact that this ratio <em>has not changed</em> would not explain why the amount of tax revenue collected by the City of Newland Falls <em>has changed</em>.


> <b>(D) Census surveys administered by Newland Falls show that the average age of all residents of Newland Falls has increased over the past three years.
</b>


 We’re seeking a choice that helps us to explain why the tax revenue collected by the city has decreased in the face of an increase in the real estate tax.


The fact that the average age of all residents of Newland Falls has increased over the past three years does not help us to explain that paradoxical outcome. How can an increase in the average age of all residents explain that outcome? Without more information, we don’t know how average age affects the tax collected by the city.


If you choose this answer choice as correct, you likely made up an unsupported story about how average age affects the real estate tax collected by the city. However, no such link is provided by the passage or by this choice. As always, we must avoid concocting unsubstantiated stories.


> <b>(E) Over the past three years, the amount of sales tax revenue collected by Newland Falls each year has increased by more than 20 percent.
</b>


 What this choice says deepens the paradox.


It was already paradoxical that, since the City of Newland Falls doubled its property tax rate from 5 percent to 10 percent, the total tax revenue collected by the city has decreased by 15 percent each year.


Now, this choice says that the amount of sales tax revenue collected by Newland Falls each year has increased significantly over the past three years, a fact that makes it even more surprising that the total tax revenue collected by the city has decreased by 15 percent each year.


This choice leaves us more confused.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)

##  Example 14 

A certain Midwestern American town had only two hardware stores.

Which of the following, if true, best explains why the result was counter to the result that might have been reasonably expected?

1. [ ] (A) Over the past year, the town invested in the district in which the remaining hardware store is located, making the district more attractive to shoppers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) The remaining hardware store is run by a national corporation with no close ties to the community in which the store is located.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) In a town near the town in which the hardware store is located, a store that had been selling some hardware type items in one department closed that department.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) After the other hardware store closed, the store manager at the remaining store did not make any significant changes to that store’s operations.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) The store that closed had been offering free classes via which people could learn how to undertake projects requiring tools and supplies available at hardware stores.

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



Generally, demand for goods sold in a store is inversely related to competition in that store’s market. In a situation in which the number of stores in a market decreases, demand for a remaining store’s goods would be expected to increase.


So, the scenario presented in this question seems paradoxical, in that, when one hardware store of only two in a town closed, demand for goods from the remaining store did not increase but, rather, decreased.


Rephrased as a question, the apparent paradox would look something like:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: Why would it be that, after one of only two hardware stores in a town closed, demand for goods sold in the remaining store decreased?


> <b>(A) Over the past year, the town invested in the district in which the remaining hardware store is located, making the district more attractive to shoppers.
</b>


 This choice adds to the paradoxical nature of the situation, because the district’s having become more attractive to shoppers would be expected to cause demand for the hardware store’s goods to increase rather than decrease. After all, a more attractive shopping district should attract more shoppers.


So, what this choice says makes the fact the demand decreased even more surprising.


(B) <strong>Incorrect</strong>. This choice could be tempting, because it could be perceived as providing a reason why demand for the remaining store’s goods would be relatively low: people in the community don’t feel that the store is connected to the community.


Remember, however, that what we need to explain is not why demand for the remaining store’s goods is relatively low, if it even is low, but rather why demand <em>decreased</em> after the other store closed, and this choice does not explain why this <em>change </em>in demand occurred.


After all, presumably the remaining store was run by a national corporation all along. Something that was the case all along would not have suddenly caused a decrease in demand after the other store closed.


> <b>(C) In a town near the town in which the hardware store is located, a store that had been selling some hardware type items in one department closed that department.
</b>


 This answer choice adds to the paradoxical nature of the situation.


The fact that a nearby store that sold hardware type items has ceased selling such items means that competition in the hardware market has decreased. Since this reduction in competition would be expected to cause demand for the remaining hardware store’s goods to increase, what this choice says make the decrease in demand for the remaining hardware store’s goods even more surprising.


> <b>(D) After the other hardware store closed, the store manager at the remaining store did not make any significant changes to that store’s operations.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, as the fact that the manager made no changes could be perceived as indicating that the manager at the remaining store has been somehow remiss in his management of the store and has thus driven down demand for the store’s goods.


However, the truth is that, by confirming that nothing has changed at the remaining store, this choice adds to the paradoxical nature of the scenario, because it eliminates what could have been one possible reason for the decrease in demand for goods sold at the remaining store, some kind of change in the way in which the store has been operating.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) The store that closed had been offering free classes via which people could learn how to undertake projects requiring tools and supplies available at hardware stores.
</b>


 While, at first glance, this choice may seem unrelated to demand at the remaining hardware store, if we consider the following, we can see how this choice explains why demand at the remaining store decreased.


By offering classes via which people could learn to undertake projects requiring items available at hardware stores, the store that closed was generating demand for goods sold at all hardware stores in the town in which it was located. Then, when it closed, and, thus, ceased to generate demand for goods sold at hardware stores, demand fell for the goods sold by the hardware store that remained.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

{{< /alert >}}
</details></h5>


Remember, our job is to explain the paradox, not enhance it.
