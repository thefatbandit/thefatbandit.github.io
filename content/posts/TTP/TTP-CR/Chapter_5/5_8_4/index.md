## Lesson Content
In answering Resolve the Paradox questions, our job is not to call into question the credibility of any statement, nor is it to reinforce the credibility of any statement.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering Resolve the Paradox questions, we accept the statements in the passages as true, even though they apparently conflict.
{{< /alert >}}

Accordingly, we never resolve the paradox in a Paradox question by challenging one of the facts stated in the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

We never resolve the paradox in a Paradox question by challenging one of the facts stated in the passage. Our job is to find a choice that explains how the two seemingly contradictory facts can be true at the same time.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider this example:

> <em>The moon is only 4.5 billion years old. Nevertheless, Apollo astronauts found on the moon rocks that are over 5 billion years old. </em>

The above situation is paradoxical since we wouldn’t expect rocks on the moon to be older than the moon itself.

We cannot resolve the above paradox by saying/indicating any of the following:

> - Apollo astronauts did not find any rocks on the moon.

> - The rocks that were found on the moon are not 5 billion years old.

> - The moon is older than 4.5 billion years.

The reason each of the above statements does not resolve the paradox is that we accept the statements in the passage to be true, and thus we don’t challenge them.

Of course, since a Paradox question is based on the idea that the statements in the passage are true, we won’t actually see choices that directly contradict the statements in the passage.

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Choice that Could be Perceived as Contradicting</u>) Before advanced methods for calculating the moon’s age became available, some scientists thought it to be much older than other scientists did.</em>

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Choice that Could be Perceived as Contradicting)</u> Some rocks brought back from the moon by astronauts were lost before their ages could be accurately determined.</em>

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Choice that Could be Perceived as Contradicting)</u> Evidence indicates that it is possible for a moon much older than 4.5 billion years to exist.</em>

Notice, none of those choices truly contradicts the original statements about the ages of the moon and the rocks.

So, what could resolve this paradox?

A choice that says:

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>The rocks found by the astronauts had been flying through space until they crashed into the moon after it was formed.</em>

Such a choice would resolve the paradox, as it explains how both of the facts could be true: some rocks could have formed before the formation of the moon, and later crashed into moon.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

An answer that seems to contradict one of the two facts presented in a Paradox question will be incorrect.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 15 

It is common knowledge that a diet that includes many high calorie foods can be a cause of weight gain.

Which of the following, if true, resolves the apparent paradox described above?

1. [ ] (A) A recent study found that some people who strictly adhered to a ketogenic diet gained rather than lost weight.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) Nutritionists who recommend ketogenic diets base their recommendations partly on information that is not common knowledge.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) A ketogenic diet is not made up exclusively of foods high in fats and oils but, rather, also includes vegetables.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) Most experts agree that following an extreme diet such as a ketogenic diet is not as effective for maintaining an ideal weight as simply consistently eating in healthy ways.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) Generally, when a person’s diet is composed mostly of fatty foods, the person develops a heightened ability to burn fat.

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The stimulus presents the paradox that consumption of foods that would normally be expected to cause weight gain results in weight loss. Rephrased as a question, this paradox would look something like:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: How could it be that, even though consuming foods high in calories is known to cause weight gain, people following diets made up mostly of foods that are high in calories would, generally, be successful in losing weight?


> <b>(A) A recent study found that some people who strictly adhered to a ketogenic diet gained rather than lost weight.
</b>


 This choice might seem to contradict the fact that people who adhere to ketogenic diets tend to lose weight, as it indicates that some people who followed a ketogenic diet did not lose weight.


However, even though some people gain weight when following ketogenic diets, we know from the passage that generally people who follow such diets are successful in losing weight. Thus, the paradox stands as is.


> <b>(B) Nutritionists who recommend ketogenic diets base their recommendations partly on information that is not common knowledge.
</b>


 The fact that nutritionists who recommend ketogenic diets base their recommendations partly on information that is not common knowledge does not contradict the common knowledge that diets that include many high calorie foods tend to cause weight gain and does not explain why ketogenic diets work. To resolve the paradox, we need some information like the information that the nutritionists have!


> <b>(C) A ketogenic diet is not made up exclusively of foods high in fats and oils but, rather, also includes vegetables.
</b>


 This choice could be perceived as somehow contradicting the fact that a ketogenic diet is made up mostly of fats and oils.


Notice, however, that what this choice actually says is that a ketogenic diet involves eating vegetables <em>in addition to</em> fats and oils. Of course, that fact does not contradict the fact that a ketogenic diet is made up <em>mostly</em> of fats and oils.


If a person were to replace fatty foods with vegetables, then, given common knowledge about calories and weight, we might expect the person to lose weight. However, if a person eats both vegetables and fat and oils, we don’t expect the person to lose weight.


Thus, the paradox stands as is.


> <b>(D) Most experts agree that following an extreme diet such as a ketogenic diet is not as effective for maintaining an ideal weight as simply consistently eating in healthy ways.
</b>


This choice could be perceived as somehow contradicting the fact that people adhering to ketogenic diets lost weight.


However, the truth is that, even if following a ketogenic diet is not the most effective way to maintain an ideal weight, people still lost weight while following ketogenic diets. So, this choice has no effect on the paradox.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) Generally, when a person’s diet is composed mostly of fatty foods, the person develops a heightened ability to burn fat.
</b>


 This choice explains why, even though high calorie foods are known to cause weight gain, people following diets made up mostly of fats and oils, which are high calorie foods, generally lose weight. By following such diets, the people develop heightened abilities to burn fat and, thus, burn fat and lose weight.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

</details></h5>

##  Example 16 

To solve a rental housing affordability problem, the city of Coastal West passed a rent control law, making it illegal for landlords in Coastal West to increase the rent they charge on any rental housing occupied by a tenant.

Which of the following, if true, provides the best explanation for these paradoxical results?

1. [ ] (A) Rental housing is more affordable in Coastal West than it is in many other cities that have the same population as Coastal West.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Some property owners responded to Coastal West’s rent control law by converting rental housing to commercial space, thus significantly reducing the supply of rental housing in Coastal West.
3. [ ] (C) For many decades, the city of Coastal West did not have in place any law that prevented landlords from increasing the rent that they charged on rental housing occupied by tenants.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) From time to time, the city of Coastal West passes laws that have effects opposite to those that the city hopes will result.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Soon after passing the rent control law, the city of Coastal West passed a historic stimulus package that provides all renters with a $2,000 check on the first day of each new year.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The passage presents a paradoxical situation:


Fact 1: The city of Coastal West passed a rent control law, making it illegal for landlords in Coastal West to increase the rent they charge on any rental housing occupied by a tenant.


Fact 2: After the passage of the law, affordability of rental housing in Coastal West quickly declined to its lowest point in the city’s history.


In answering this question, we have to keep in mind that the correct answer will not be correct because it somehow contradicts one of the above two facts. We have to take the facts as true and find a choice that explains how it can be that both are true.


> <b>(A) Rental housing is more affordable in Coastal West than it is in many other cities that have the same population as Coastal West.
</b>


 This choice could seem to contradict the fact that affordability of rental housing in Coastal West quickly declined to its lowest point in the city’s history. After all, if what this choice says is true, then maybe the affordability of rental housing in Coastal West isn’t too terrible.


Notice, however, that the fact that rental housing is more affordable in Coastal West than it is in other cities does not actually contradict the fact that affordability of rental housing in Coastal West declined. After all, even if rental housing is more affordable in Coastal West than it is in other cities, affordability of rental housing in Coastal West could have declined.


So, this choice does not really affect the paradox at all.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) Some property owners responded to Coastal West’s rent control law by converting rental housing to commercial space, thus significantly reducing the supply of rental housing in Coastal West.
</b>


This choice perfectly explains what we have to explain.


After all, if what this choice says is true, then we can see why, after the passage of the law, affordability of rental housing in Coastal West quickly declined: property owners responded to the rent control law by converting rental housing to commercial space, thus significantly reducing the supply of rental housing. Of course, a significant reduction in supply would likely cause an increase in rent levels, even if landlords could not increase the rent they charge on any rental housing occupied by a tenant. After all, landlords could still increase the rent they charge new tenants.


Thus, by showing why the affordability of rental housing declined, this choice resolves the apparent paradox.


> <b>(C) For many decades, the city of Coastal West did not have in place any law that prevented landlords from increasing the rent that they charged on rental housing occupied by tenants.
</b>


 This choice could seem to somehow negate the fact that the city of Coastal West passed a rent control law. We might be tempted to think something along the lines of, “Coastal West passed a rent control law, but it didn’t have one before. So, maybe affordability declined because Coastal West didn’t have a rent control law before.”


However, the truth is that, regardless of whether Coastal West did not have any rent control law for decades, it did pass such a law. So, regardless of what this choice says, the fact that affordability declined is still paradoxical.


So, this choice does not really affect the paradox at all.


> <b>(D) From time to time, the city of Coastal West passes laws that have effects opposite to those that the city hopes will result.
</b>


 The relationship between this choice and the statements in the passage is not that this choice explains how the statements in the passage could be true. Rather, this choice states a conclusion that follows from the statements in the passage.


After all, what this choice says follows from the fact that, after the city of Coastal West passed a rent control law to fix a rental housing affordability problem, affordability of rental housing in Coastal West quickly declined to its lowest point in the city’s history.


Since this choice follows from rather than explains the facts, it does not have the effect we seek.


> <b>(E) Soon after passing the rent control law, the city of Coastal West passed a historic stimulus package that provides all renters with a $2,000 check on the first day of each new year.
</b>


 If anything, what this choice says deepens the paradox, leaving us more confused.


After all, if what this choice says is true, then soon after the rent control law was passed, renters in Coastal West should have been better able to afford rental housing, because the city was giving them $2000 more to spend on rent each year than they had previously. So, given what this choice says, we would have expected affordability to improve, not decline.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
