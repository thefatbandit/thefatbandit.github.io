## Lesson Content
Perhaps the trickiest type of incorrect choice that can appear in a Resolve the Paradox question is one that discusses the facts or aspects of the facts that make up the apparent paradox but does not resolve the paradox.

Consider the following example:

> <em>In order to spur scientific research, the government of Golado instituted a new program that involved giving significant financial awards and recognition to researchers who made breakthrough discoveries. However, in the years that followed the institution of the program, the pace of scientific discovery in Golado significantly decreased.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, would do the most to explain the surprising outcome?</em>

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Choice that Discusses Without Explaining</u>)</em> <em>The government did not make an effort to inform the scientific community that the purpose of the program was to increase the pace of scientific discovery.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>Researchers given strong incentives to compete with each other often respond by reducing the degree to which they share information that could lead to breakthrough discoveries.</em>

So what’s the paradox here?

> Fact 1: The government of Golado instituted a new program that involved giving significant financial awards and recognition to researchers who made breakthrough discoveries.

> Fact 2: After the program was instituted the pace of scientific discovery decreased.

> <u>What has to be explained</u>: Why would it be the case that, after the government started giving more awards and recognition to researchers who made breakthrough discoveries, the pace of scientific discovery decreased?

To make sure that we get the correct answer to a question like this one, we have to keep in mind that we need a choice that explains a specific thing: why the pace of scientific discovery <em>decreased</em>.

The incorrect choice that discusses without explaining does discuss both the program and the pace of scientific discovery.

Now, let’s contrast the incorrect choice that discusses without explaining with the correct answer.

So, as we are answering Paradox questions, we must be sure to:

> - Identify <em>exactly</em> what the correct answer has to explain.

> - Read the answer choices carefully.

> - Make sure that the answer choice that we choose doesn’t just mention the facts discussed in the passage but, rather, clearly serves to explain what has to be explained.

##  Example 18 

In experiments, when trees, which use carbon dioxide when they grow, are exposed to air in which carbon dioxide is at a higher than normal level, they grow faster than they normally do.

Which of the following, if true, provides the best explanation for why the most common result is different from what might have been expected?

1. [ ] (A) Increased atmospheric carbon dioxide is correlated with increased rates of tree growth in tropical forests more than it is with increased rates of tree growth in forests located in temperate areas.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Rising temperatures caused by increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide have resulted in a reduction in the amounts of water available to trees in temperate areas.
3. [ ] (C) Plants eventually adjust to an increased level of atmospheric carbon dioxide, their growth rates returning to what they were originally.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) In areas in which levels of nitrogen in the soil are low, trees have difficulty growing.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) The growth rates of trees of some species accelerate more than do those of trees of other species when the trees are exposed to air with higher than normal levels of carbon dioxide.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

##### Question Level: easy



What is the paradox presented here?


Fact 1: In experiments, when trees are exposed to air in which carbon dioxide is at a higher than normal level, they grow faster than they normally do.


Fact 2: In forests located in temperate areas, rates of tree growth have slowed from what had been normal as the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has increased.


Rephrased as a question, the paradox presented in the passage looks something like this:


<u>What has to be explained</u>: How could it be that, even though, in experiments, trees exposed to air containing extra carbon dioxide grow faster than they normally do, the growth of trees in temperate forests has slowed to below normal as the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has increased?


Only a choice that answers the above question will work. A choice that discusses increased atmospheric carbon dioxide and decreasing tree growth rates without explaining the how the two facts can coexist will not work.


> <b>(A) Increased atmospheric carbon dioxide is correlated with increased rates of tree growth in tropical forests more than it is with increased rates of tree growth in forests located in temperate areas.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because it discusses both increased atmospheric carbon dioxide and rates of tree growth and because it indicates that the growth rates of trees in tropical areas are more positively correlated with levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide than are the growth rates of trees in temperate areas.


Notice, however, this choice discusses only <em>increases</em> in growth rates. So, nothing in this choice explains why the rates of growth of trees in temperate areas have <em>slowed</em> as the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has increased.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) Rising temperatures caused by increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide have resulted in a reduction in the amounts of water available to trees in temperate areas.
</b>


 This choice explains why the rates of growth of trees in temperate areas have slowed as the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has increased: as the level of carbon dioxide has increased, the amounts of water available to the trees have decreased.


> <b>(C) Plants eventually adjust to an increased level of atmospheric carbon dioxide, their growth rates returning to what they were originally.
</b>


 This choice is a tricky trap, as it could seem to explain why the tree growth rates have decreased. Perhaps they had already increased and have more recently been decreasing in the process of returning to normal.


However, notice what we have to explain. The passage says that “rates of tree growth have slowed from what had been normal.” So, whereas the phenomenon described in this choice involves growth rates returning to normal, the passage indicates, not that rates are returning to normal, but rather that rates are slowing to <em>below normal</em>. Thus, this choice cannot be correct.


> <b>(D) In areas in which levels of nitrogen in the soil are low, trees have difficulty growing.
</b>


This choice could seem to explain why growth rates of trees in temperate areas are decreasing. Perhaps, the soil in those areas is low in nitrogen.


However, since we are not provided with a reason to believe that the levels of nitrogen in the soil in temperate areas <em>have changed</em> as the level of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere has increased, this choice does not explain why the growth rates of trees in temperate areas have decreased, i.e., <em>changed</em>.


> <b>(E) The growth rates of trees of some species accelerate more than do those of trees of other species when the trees are exposed to air with higher than normal levels of carbon dioxide.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting because it discusses both tree growth rates and carbon dioxide levels.


Notice, however, that we can easily eliminate this choice, as it mentions only differences in <em>acceleration</em> of growth rates and says nothing that would explain <em>decreasing</em> growth rates.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
