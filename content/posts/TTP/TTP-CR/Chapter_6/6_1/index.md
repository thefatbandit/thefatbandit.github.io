## Lesson Content
A cause-and-effect relationship is a relationship in which one event, X, causes another event, Y,<i> </i>to occur.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A cause-and-effect relationship is one in which one event causes another event to occur.
{{< /alert >}}

Many arguments found in Critical Reasoning questions involve cause-and-effect relationships, or relationships that appear to be cause-and-effect relationships.

In fact, you’ve already encountered arguments of this type several times in the preceding chapters.

Let’s begin this chapter by reviewing, and elaborating on, the assumptions that are inherent to any cause-and-effect argument.
