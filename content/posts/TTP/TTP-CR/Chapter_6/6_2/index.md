## Lesson Content
As we learned in prior chapters, when an author makes a cause-and-effect claim, she assumes that her cause-and-effect claim is actually correct; she assumes that there could not be an alternative explanation for the observed events.

In other words, when an author makes a cause-and-effect claim, she posits that the cause she has put forth is the ONLY possible cause.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When an author makes a cause-and-effect claim, she assumes that her cause-and-effect claim is actually correct; she assumes that there could not be an alternative explanation for the observed events.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following example:

> <em>Psychologists who studied the smartphone usage of 5,000 children determined that, as the daily number of hours a child spent using a smartphone increased, so too did the likelihood of the child experiencing attention and learning issues. On this basis, the psychologists concluded that smartphone use causes attention and learning issues in children. ‘</em>

> <em>Which of the following is an assumption made by the psychologists in arriving at the above conclusion?</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) Children experiencing attention and learning issues are not likely to spend much of the day using smartphones as a means of coping with these issues. </em>

In this example, the psychologists are concluding that smartphone usage causes attention and learning issues in children.

Notice that the correct answer highlights a piece of information that must be true in order for the logic of the argument to work.

Often the key assumption stated by the correct answer to an Assumption question involving a cause-and-effect argument will be, essentially, that there is not something other than the cause mentioned in the argument that could be the cause of the effect mentioned in the argument.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Ever since new owners took over the beachfront building and changed it from a restaurant with outdoor dining to a dance club with outdoor dancing to loud music, the birds that used to flock around the building have disappeared. It must be that the loud music disturbs the birds.</em>

> <em>Which of the following is an assumption made in arriving at the above conclusion?</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>Birds are not less attracted to the building than they were previously because there is much less food at the club than there was at the restaurant.</em>

In this example, the conclusion that loud music caused the disappearance of the birds is based on the premise that the birds stopped flocking around the building once the building became the location of a club at which loud music is played.

The correct answer expresses such an assumption, the assumption that the birds have not disappeared because of a reduction in the amount of food at the building.

What’s being assumed in the following example?

##  Example 1 

Administrators in the Red Valley School District have decided that a new math curriculum being used for teaching math to elementary school students in the district is not as good as the curriculum that was used previously.

Which of the following must the administrators be assuming?

1. [ ] (A) Math test scores of high school students in the district have not increased following a switch to a curriculum similar to the new curriculum given to the elementary school students.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) The training that the teachers received to prepare them to apply the new math curriculum was not inadequate for preparing them to apply the curriculum effectively.
3. [ ] (C) The math tests currently given to the students are substantially different from tests that they took when they were using the older math curriculum.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) Red Valley School District elementary school students’ English test scores did not decrease when the school switched to using a new curriculum for teaching English.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) The reviews of the new math curriculum that were read by Red Valley School District faculty members before they chose the new curriculum were not accurate.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The administrators have concluded the following:


<u>Conclusion</u>: The new math curriculum being used for teaching math to elementary school students is not as good as the curriculum that was used previously.


Their conclusion is based on the fact that the district’s elementary school students’ math test scores have decreased following the implementation of the new curriculum.


In arriving at their conclusion, the administrators are assuming that the use of the new curriculum has caused the decrease in test scores and that there is not some other factor that has caused the decrease in the test scores.


> <b>(A) Math test scores of high school students in the district have not increased following a switch to a curriculum similar to the new curriculum given to the elementary school students.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting since, if math tests scores of high school students using a similar curriculum increased, then it would make sense to wonder whether the change to the new curriculum was truly the cause of the decrease in elementary school test scores.


At the same time, in arriving at their conclusion, the administrators don’t <em>have to</em> be assuming that test scores of high school students did not increase following a switch to using a similar curriculum. The switch could have been a downgrade for the elementary school students even if a switch to a similar curriculum was an upgrade for the high school students.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) The training that the teachers received to prepare them to apply the new math curriculum was not inadequate for preparing them to apply the curriculum effectively.
</b>


 In deciding that the new curriculum is not as good as the one that was used previously, the administrators must be assuming that there was not some other reason for the decrease in test scores.


For instance, they must be assuming that the training that the elementary school teachers received to prepare them to apply the new curriculum was not inadequate for preparing them to use it effectively. After all, if the training was inadequate, then inadequacy of the training, rather than the quality of the curriculum, could explain the decrease in test scores. In other words, if the training was inadequate then the argument would fall apart.


So, the administrators must be assuming what this choice says.


> <b>(C) The math tests currently given to the students are substantially different from tests that they took when they were using the older math curriculum.
</b>


 This choice is tricky, because it is the opposite of what we are looking for. So, if we are not careful, we can be tempted into picking a choice like this one, particularly because, as we are going through the choices, we might already be thinking that something changed about the tests.


Notice that, the administrators must be assuming that the tests given currently are not <em>different from</em> but rather <em>similar to</em>, at least in terms of difficulty, the tests that students took when they were using the old curriculum.


The administrators certainly aren’t assuming that the tests are substantially different. After all, if the tests were different, that difference, rather than the change in curriculum, could be responsible for the decrease in test scores.


> <b>(D) Red Valley School District elementary school students’ English test scores did not decrease when the school switched to using a new curriculum for teaching English.
</b>


 The administrators are not comparing the new math curriculum with the new English curriculum but, rather, the new math curriculum with the previous math curriculum. So, they aren’t assuming that the switch to a new English curriculum produced results better than those resulting from the switch to the new math curriculum.


> <b>(E) The reviews of the new math curriculum that were read by Red Valley School District faculty members before they chose the new curriculum were not accurate.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting because, in our minds, we could add to this choice things that it does not say, such as the following:


- The reviews of the new math curriculum said that it is better than any other.


- The reviews of the new math curriculum said that it is better than the previous math curriculum.


At the same time, the truth is that this choice does not say what the reviews actually said. So, there is no reason for us to believe that the reviews said anything that might be considered not accurate by the administrators.


Furthermore, even if the reviews did include comparisons of the new curriculum with the previous curriculum, the administrators don’t have to assume anything about reviews in order to go from the test scores to the conclusion about the curriculum. The administrators don’t even have to be aware that such reviews exist.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

##  Example 2 

In a recent medical study, all of the study participants who had abused alcohol in the past five years were found to be suffering with depression.

Each of the following is an assumption upon which the argument depends EXCEPT:

1. [x] (A) Both alcohol abuse issues and depression could be solved via the use of therapy specially designed for the treatment of alcohol abuse issues.
2. [ ] (B) The depression and alcohol abuse of the study participants were not both results of heavy use of illicit drugs.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) The study participants were not found to be suffering with depression before they started abusing alcohol.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) The study was not set up in a way such that people who were not suffering with depression would be very unlikely to participate.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) The concurrence of alcohol abuse and depression seen in the people studied was not merely a coincidence.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The argument has a cause-and-effect conclusion.


<u>Conclusion</u>: It must be the case that abusing alcohol causes depression.


The conclusion is based on the fact that all of the people studied who had abused alcohol were found to be depressed.


Going from that evidence to that cause-and-effect conclusion requires assuming that the observed pattern was not the result of something other than alcohol abuse causing depression. In other words, the author must be assuming that the above conclusion is the only possible explanation for the fact that all of the people studied who had abused alcohol were found to be depressed.


Now, in this case, because the question stem says, “Each of the following is an assumption … EXCEPT,” we have to look for the one answer that is NOT an assumption upon which the argument depends. The four choices that ARE assumptions upon which the argument depends are incorrect.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) Both alcohol abuse issues and depression could be solved via the use of therapy specially designed for the treatment of alcohol abuse issues.
</b>


 This choice could seem to be an assumption that the author must be making, because it could seem necessary to assume that therapy designed for treatment for alcohol abuse issues could solve both alcohol abuse issues and depression in order to conclude that abusing alcohol causes depression.


However, the truth is that this choice is not an <em>assumption </em>necessary for going from the evidence to the conclusion. Rather, this choice is <em>a conclusion that follows from</em> the author’s conclusion. After all, if abusing alcohol causes depression, then it makes sense to conclude that therapy designed to treat alcohol abuse issues could solve depression as well.


At the same time, concluding that alcohol abuse causes depression does not require assuming anything about therapy designed for treating alcohol abuse issues. After all, one could arrive at the argument’s conclusion even if such therapy did not even exist.


Assumption upon which the argument depends? No.


> <b>(B) The depression and alcohol abuse of the study participants were not both results of heavy use of illicit drugs.
</b>


 Going from the evidence that the participants in the study both abused alcohol and were found to be depressed to the conclusion that alcohol abuse causes depression requires assuming that a third factor, use of illicit drugs, is not the actual cause of both the alcohol abuse and the depression.


Assumption upon which the argument depends? Yes.


> <b>(C) The study participants were not found to be suffering with depression before they started abusing alcohol.
</b>


 The conclusion that alcohol abuse causes depression depends on the assumption that the depression did not precede the alcohol abuse. After all, if the participants were found to be suffering with depression before they started abusing alcohol, then clearly abusing alcohol did not cause their depression.


Assumption upon which the argument depends? Yes.


> <b>(D) The study was not set up in a way such that people who were not suffering with depression would be very unlikely to participate.
</b>


 If the study had been set up in a way such that people who were not depressed would have been very unlikely to participate, then it could very well be that the reason that all the participants who abused alcohol were depressed was simply that everyone who participated in the study was depressed. So, in order to connect the pattern of alcohol abuse and depression among study participants to the conclusion that alcohol abuse causes depression, we need the assumption that the study was not set up in such a way that people who were not depressed would not have been very unlikely to participate.


Assumption upon which the argument depends? Yes.


> <b>(E) The concurrence of alcohol abuse and depression seen in the people studied was not merely a coincidence.
</b>


 Going from the fact that all study participants who abused alcohol were found to be depressed to the conclusion that alcohol abuse causes depression requires the assumption that it was not mere coincidence that the study participants who abused alcohol were also depressed.


Assumption upon which the argument depends? Yes.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
