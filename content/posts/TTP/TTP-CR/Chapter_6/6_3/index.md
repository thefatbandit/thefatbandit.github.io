## Lesson Content
Humans tend to seek to see patterns and relationships in situations.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Humans often make incorrect assumptions about cause-and-effect relationships.
{{< /alert >}}

For example, there’s a story of an old woman who lived in a tiny village.

The old woman made a classic mistake in cause-and-effect reasoning.

Causes and effects are very difficult to assign.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Causes and effects are very difficult to assign. So, we must be very careful any time we encounter a question dealing with cause and effect.
{{< /alert >}}

Of course, you’d never make a reasoning error as silly as the one that the old woman made, would you? Let’s see.

Let’s now discuss some ways in which one can arrive at erroneous conclusions about causes and effects.
