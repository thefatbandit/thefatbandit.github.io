## Lesson Content
Let’s say that you were to express a wish to a friend that you want to attain a v-shaped physique.

One way to weaken the case for your friend’s conclusion would be to show that perhaps the cause and the effect are in fact reversed.

In other words, what if we had information that indicates that it may be that only people who are somehow “naturally” built with v-shaped physiques thrive as professional swimmers? In such a case, it could be that professional swimmers have v-shaped physiques not because swimming helps in building v-shaped physiques but because only people with v-shaped physiques can do that well in swimming.

Regardless of the degree to which either of the foregoing cause-and-effect relationships has any basis in reality, the point to understand here is that the original conclusion about the cause and effect was drawn entirely on the basis of the co-occurrence of the two variables.

Remember that, when an author concludes that one thing causes another thing, she is assuming that there can be no other explanation for the observed pattern.

So then, one way to weaken a cause-and-effect argument is to show that perhaps the cause and effect are actually reversed.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

It is possible to weaken a cause-and-effect argument by showing that the cause-and-effect relation could be reversed. That is, if the argument assumes or concludes that that A caused B, showing that B may have caused A would successfully weaken the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

As another example, some researchers have long hypothesized that marijuana use causes depression in adolescents.

Consider the following examples.

##  Example 9 

A biology professor noticed that, among his students, those who drink the most coffee also appear to be the most anxious.

Which of the following, if true, most weakens the professor’s conclusion?

1. [x] (A) People often attempt to deal with anxiety by drinking hot beverages such as coffee.
2. [ ] (B) Coffee does not contain substances other than caffeine that may lead to increased anxiety levels.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) The students who drank decaffeinated coffee experienced lower levels of anxiety.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Biology students tend to drink more coffee than is drunk by students in other disciplines.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Biology students tend to report experiencing levels of anxiety higher than those experienced by students in other disciplines.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The professor concludes that the high level of caffeine in coffee causes an increase in the anxiety levels of people. The conclusion is based on the co-occurrence of higher amounts of coffee and higher levels of anxiety.


One way to weaken the argument is by showing that the causal relationship might actually be in the opposite direction. In other words, greater anxiety may be causing greater consumption of coffee.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) People often attempt to deal with anxiety by drinking hot beverages such as coffee.
</b>


 If people routinely drink hot beverages to deal with anxiety, then the cause-and-effect relationship may be the reverse of what the professor has concluded it to be. In this case, the higher levels of anxiety and the greater consumption of coffee co-occur not because coffee leads to anxiety but because anxiety leads people to drink more coffee.


Notice that this choice casts doubt on the conclusion by showing that perhaps the cause and effect are actually reversed.


> <b>(B) Coffee does not contain substances other than caffeine that may lead to increased anxiety levels.
</b>


 This choice strengthens the professor’s argument.


The opposite of this statement – coffee contains other substances that may lead to increased anxiety levels – would cast doubt on the professor’s conclusion that the caffeine is leading to increased anxiety. After all, in that case, those other substances, rather than caffeine, could be leading to higher anxiety levels.


This choice strengthens the argument by serving to rule out these alternative causes.


Remember, whenever we can eliminate a potential weakener, we strengthen the argument.


> <b>(C) The students who drank decaffeinated coffee experienced lower levels of anxiety.
</b>


 This choice strengthens the argument by showing that, when you remove caffeine from coffee, the anxiety levels also go down. In general, a valid way of strengthening a causal relation “X led to Y” is saying that, when X doesn’t happen, Y also doesn’t happen.


> <b>(D) Biology students tend to drink more coffee than is drunk by students in other disciplines.
</b>


 Because the conclusion is that anxiety is caused by coffee, in order for this choice to have any impact on the argument, we’d need to know not only what this choice says but also whether biology students are more anxious than students from other disciplines. Given that we do not have this information, this choice does not have any impact on the argument.


> <b>(E) Biology students tend to report experiencing levels of anxiety higher than those experienced by students in other disciplines.
</b>


 Because the conclusion is that anxiety is caused by coffee, in order for this choice to have any impact on the argument, we’d need to know not only what this choice says but also the coffee consumption of biology students relative to the coffee consumption of students in other disciplines. After all, the information that this choice provides does not indicate whether caffeine is the cause of biology students’ relatively high levels of anxiety.


Since we don’t have information on the coffee consumption of biology students, this choice does not have any impact on the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)

##  Example 10 

While studying traffic patterns in a major city, an urban planner observed that areas that had the most traffic congestion also had the highest numbers of vehicular crashes.

Which of the following, if true, would cast the most doubt that implementation of the urban planner’s recommendation would achieve its intended objective?

1. [ ] (A) Many crashes occur because the drivers involved are distracted.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Reducing traffic congestion in major cities is expensive.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Many of the worst vehicular crashes occur late at night.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Traffic congestion tends to be preceded by vehicular crashes.
5. [ ] (E) When another city expanded the city’s roads to reduce traffic congestion, the city experienced fewer crashes.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The recommendation that the city reduce traffic congestion to decrease the number of crashes is based on the co-occurrence of traffic congestion and vehicular crashes. The assumption underlying the recommendation is that the co-occurrence is actually the result of a causal relationship in which traffic congestion led to crashes.


One way in which the argument can be weakened is by showing that the causal relationship may in fact be the other way around: crashes led to traffic congestion – a very plausible scenario. In other words, one way to weaken this cause-and-effect argument is to find an alternative explanation.


> <b>(A) Many crashes occur because the drivers involved are distracted.</b>


 Regardless of whether distraction causes many crashes, there is a co-occurrence of congestion and crashes. So, it could be that, while many crashes are caused by distraction, many others are caused by congestion, or it could be that distraction causes crashes when there is congestion. In either scenario, congestion still seems to be a factor causing crashes. So, this choice does not really weaken the argument


> <b>(B) Reducing traffic congestion in major cities is expensive.</b>


 We are concerned not with the costs of reducing congestion but, rather, with the effects of reducing congestion. The cost of reducing congestion does not affect the effects of reducing congestion.


> <b>(C) Many of the worst vehicular crashes occur late at night.</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because what it says about the worst crashes occurring late at night, when, in theory, there would be less congestion, could be perceived as contradicting the premise that congestion and vehicular crashes co-occur and, thus, as weakening the argument.


However, as we are well aware, premises in Critical Reasoning questions are to be taken as fact. So, choices that seem to weaken arguments by attacking premises cannot be correct.


In this case, the fact that many of the worst crashes occur at night does not change the fact that crashes and congestion co-occur and, therefore, does not weaken the support for the recommendation.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) Traffic congestion tends to be preceded by vehicular crashes.</b>


 If traffic congestion tends to be preceded by traffic crashes, then traffic congestion is not generally the cause of crashes, as the crashes happen before the traffic congestion does.


In such a case, it seems likely that crashes lead to congestion, and thus, the co-occurrence of crashes and congestion may be the result of this reverse causality. If so, the whole basis of the recommendation is challenged.


Thus, this choice casts doubt on the idea that reducing congestion will achieve the desired outcome.


> <b>(E) When another city expanded the city’s roads to reduce traffic congestion, the city experienced fewer crashes.</b>


 This choice can only strengthen the urban planner’s recommendation.


If another city experienced fewer crashes by reducing congestion, then it seems likely that the city in the argument will be able to achieve the same. Even though there could be differences in the cities because of which reducing congestion may not have the same impact in both cities, this choice still provides some support for the urban planner’s recommendation.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
