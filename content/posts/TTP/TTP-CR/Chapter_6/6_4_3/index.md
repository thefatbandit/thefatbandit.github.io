## Lesson Content
Another way to weaken a cause-and-effect argument is to find a third variable that causes both of the observed events.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

We can weaken a cause-and-effect argument by showing that there is a common cause for the two variables in question, rather than a direct cause-and-effect link between the two variables in question.
{{< /alert >}}

For example, let’s say that a statistician notices that, during the winter months, hospital admissions for frostbite increase, as does the number of people wearing coats.

Of course not.

Of course, the third variable here is the temperature.

While few people would make the mistake of assuming that wearing coats causes frostbite, here’s a more interesting example, one about the handwriting of children.

On an average, children with larger shoe sizes tend to have better handwriting.

Let’s see how this type of answer works in context:

> <em>In recent years in Pyortau, air pollution has increased and, simultaneously, the average level of physical fitness of the residents of the city has decreased. Some people noticing these two trends have declared that air pollution is clearly causing people to become less fit.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, provides the best reason to question the validity of the conclusion drawn?</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>In recent years, the use of automobiles has supplanted bicycling as the main way in which people get around Pyortau.</em>

In the scenario presented, people have observed trends in two inversely correlated variables, air pollution and physical fitness, and concluded that an increase in one, air pollution, must be causing the decrease in the other, physical fitness.

Consider the following example, in which there is a common cause for both the observed outcomes.

##  Example 13 

Epidemiologist: Most people would agree that smoking causes lung cancer.

Which of the following, if true, most serves to call into question the epidemiologist’s conclusion?

1. [ ] (A) Substances called flavonoids, which are found in red wine, a popular alcoholic beverage, have been found to prevent cancer.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) The incidence of lung cancer in people who consume alcohol moderately is lower than the incidence in people who consume alcohol more heavily.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Studies of people who are occasional consumers of alcohol show no difference between the incidence of lung cancer in such people and that in the general population.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) While inhaled smoke does readily visible damage to lung tissue with which it comes into contact, no similarly visible lung tissue damage results from alcohol consumption.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) People often smoke or consume alcohol in order to cope with the types of emotional stress that are believed to be factors contributing to the development of lung cancer.

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Premise</u>: Our studies show the rate of lung cancer in people who regularly consume alcohol in significant quantities to be 50 percent higher than the rate in the general population.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Our research has led to the discovery of another habit that can cause lung cancer, alcohol consumption.


(Notice, in this argument, the conclusion is stated before the evidence is presented.)


In making the argument, the epidemiologist uses an observed correlation between regular consumption of significant quantities of alcohol and increased incidence of lung cancer as evidence that such alcohol consumption causes lung cancer. The argument could be weakened by a choice that provides information that indicates that the relationship between alcohol consumption and lung cancer may not be causative, and that instead another factor underlies both the alcohol consumption and lung cancer.


> <b>(A) Substances called flavonoids, which are found in red wine, a popular alcoholic beverage, have been found to prevent cancer.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because it could be taken as weakening the argument, in that it seems to indicate that alcohol consumption could prevent rather than cause lung cancer, at least as long as the alcohol is in red wine.


However, the fact that one substance in one type of alcoholic beverage, red wine, may prevent cancer does not either negate the fact that the study results show a strong correlation between consumption of alcohol and lung cancer or provide a clear reason to come to a conclusion different from the one arrived at by the epidemiologist.


In other words, while one substance in red wine may prevent cancer, the evidence provided still seems to support the conclusion that consumption of alcohol causes lung cancer.


> <b>(B) The incidence of lung cancer in people who consume alcohol moderately is lower than the incidence in people who consume alcohol more heavily.
</b>


 By indicating that greater alcohol consumption is correlated with a higher incidence of lung cancer, this choice provides additional support for the conclusion that regular alcohol consumption causes lung cancer.


> <b>(C) Studies of people who are occasional consumers of alcohol show no difference between the incidence of lung cancer in such people and that in the general population.
</b>


 The fact that <em>occasional</em> consumption of alcohol is not correlated with increased incidence of lung cancer does not cast doubt on the conclusion of this argument, which is, essentially, that <em>regular</em> consumption of alcohol causes lung cancer.


> <b>(D) While inhaled smoke does readily visible damage to lung tissue with which it comes into contact, no similarly visible lung tissue damage results from alcohol consumption.
</b>


 While what this choice says indicates that alcohol consumption does not affect lung tissue in the way that smoking does, the fact that alcohol consumption does not have the same effect that smoking has does not mean that alcohol consumption does not cause lung cancer. So, what this choice says doesn’t really cast doubt on the conclusion of the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) People often smoke or consume alcohol in order to cope with the types of emotional stress that are believed to be factors contributing to the development of lung cancer.
</b>


 At first glance, what this choice says may seem to have no way to weaken the argument, since it is about reasons for smoking and alcohol consumption rather than effects of smoking and alcohol consumption.


However, in saying that people consume alcohol in order to cope with the types of emotional stress believed to be factors underlying the development of lung cancer, this choice provides an alternative reason for the correlation between alcohol consumption and lung cancer: the same factor, emotional stress, underlies both the alcohol consumption and the development of lung cancer.


By presenting a third variable that could be causing both alcohol consumption and lung cancer, this choice serves to call into question the conclusion that alcohol consumption causes lung cancer.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

##  Example 14 

It has been consistently found that children who attend preschool are more likely to graduate from high school than children who don’t attend preschool.

Which of the following, if true, would most seriously weaken the argument above?

1. [ ] (A) Some forms of early childhood enrichment activities are not associated with higher high school graduation rates.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) High school graduation rates are not the only measures of academic performance.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Parents who are actively involved in their children’s academic progress are much more likely to send their children to preschool than parents who are not.
4. [ ] (D) Children who do not attend preschool are more likely to score below grade level on state assessments.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Many school districts currently do not have the financial resources to implement universal preschool.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The argument concludes that making preschool universal would increase high school graduation rates. The conclusion is supported by the first sentence, which indicates that there is a correlation between attending preschool and high school graduation.


One way to weaken the argument is to show that a third variable may be causing both preschool attendance and the greater high school graduation rates of children who have attended preschool.


> <b>(A) Some forms of early childhood enrichment activities are not associated with higher high school graduation rates.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting because it presents information that seems to indicate that at least some early childhood enrichment activities, such as preschool, do not contribute to greater academic success.


However, the argument has already made clear that attending preschool is associated with a higher rate of high school graduation, and what this choice says does not somehow negate that premise. It could simply be that, whereas some other early childhood enrichment activities are not associated with higher high school graduation rates, preschool does contribute to academic success.


So, this choice neither negates the premise upon which the argument is based nor gives us any other reason to doubt the argument’s conclusion.


> <b>(B) High school graduation rates are not the only measures of academic performance.
</b>


 Who cares? While there could be other measures of academic performance in addition to high school graduation rates, the argument is specifically about the effect of attending preschool on high school graduation rates. Thus, this choice does not affect the argument.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) Parents who are actively involved in their children’s academic progress are much more likely to send their children to preschool than parents who are not.
</b>


 If parents who are actively involved in the academic performance of their children are much more likely to send their children to preschool, then it is possible that parental involvement results in both children’s going to preschool and the relatively high rate of high school graduation of children who went to preschool.


By presenting information indicating that parental involvement may cause both preschool attendance and greater high school graduation rates, this choice weakens the support for the conclusion that preschool attendance results in greater rates of high school graduation. In other words, this choice weakens the argument by presenting an alternative explanation for the correlation between preschool attendance and greater rates of high school graduation.


> <b>(D) Children who do not attend preschool are more likely to score below grade level on state assessments.
</b>


 This choice tends to strengthen the argument by showing that children who do not go to preschool are more likely to underperform in academics.


> <b>(E) Many school districts currently do not have the financial resources to implement universal preschool.
</b>


 This choice weakens the support for the wrong conclusion.


It weakens the support for the conclusion that making preschool universal is practical or the support for the conclusion that preschool will be made universal.


The conclusion of this argument is specifically that making preschool universal would lead to a greater rate of high school graduation.


The fact that some school districts do not have the resources to implement universal preschool does not have any impact on the cause-and-effect relationship that is the basis of the argument. The argument can be weakened only by attacking the link between making preschool universal and increasing the high school graduation rate.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
