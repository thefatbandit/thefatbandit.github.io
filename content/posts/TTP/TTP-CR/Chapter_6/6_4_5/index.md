## Lesson Content
Just as we can weaken the support for a conclusion about a cause-and-effect relationship by showing that the supposed cause happens without the effect’s happening, we can also weaken the support for such a conclusion by showing that the effect happens even when the supposed cause does not.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

We can weaken the support for a cause-and-effect conclusion by showing that the effect happens even when the supposed cause does not.
{{< /alert >}}

Of course, it is possible that something is not the only cause of an effect.

At the same time, there are situations in which cause-and-effect conclusions can be called into question by the fact that the effect happens when the supposed cause does not.

To see how reasoning of this type could be involved in a Weaken question, consider the following example:

> <em>Researchers studying a rare metabolic disorder found a family in which thirty-one members were experiencing or had experienced that rare disorder. Genetic testing of many members of that family revealed that the vast majority of them carried the same mutated version of a gene related to metabolism. Therefore, the scientists concluded that the disorder is caused by the mutated gene.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, would most seriously weaken the support for the scientists’ conclusion?</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>Further tests revealed that a third of the family members who were experiencing the disorder did not carry the mutated version of the gene.</em>

Since many members of one family are experiencing what is usually a rare metabolic disorder, and since the vast majority of them carry a mutated version of a gene related to metabolism, concluding that the mutated gene is the cause of the disorder may seem to make sense.

However, what the correct answer says seriously weakens the argument since, once we find out that a third of the family members who are experiencing the disease don’t carry the gene, the conclusion that the gene is the cause no longer seems to make sense.

Here’s an example question in which the argument is weakened in this way.

##  Example 18 

Since, in 1989, rates of violent crime across Verdantia had been increasing for over two decades, the administrations of some cities in Verdantia decided to get tough on crime by taking actions such as increasing the strictness with which police handled even minor crimes and pushing prosecutors to seek lengthy sentences for all people convicted of crimes.

Which of the following, if true, does the most to undermine the force of the evidence cited by the administrations?

1. [ ] (A) Many of the crimes of which people were convicted as a result of the get tough strategies were non-violent crimes such as small-scale drug dealing and shoplifting.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) Some of the cities employing get tough on crime strategies were unprepared for the increases in arrests that ensued, with the result that their court systems became clogged.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) After the use of get tough strategies began, not just the rates of violent crime, but the rates of crime in general decreased in many cities in Verdantia.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) It has been found that education and community renewal can be more effective in causing reductions in violent crime and less costly than get tough on crime strategies.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) In cities in Verdantia in which get tough on crime strategies were not employed, rates of violent crime declined precipitously starting around 1990.

##### Question Level: easy



The administrations of the cities in which get tough on crime strategies were used concluded the following:


<u>Conclusion</u>: The get tough strategies have been successful.


In other words, since, when get tough strategies were used, rates of violent crime decreased, the administrations arrived at the cause-and-effect conclusion that get tough strategies caused decreases in rates of violent crime.


That conclusion could be called into question by information indicating that the same effect occurred even when the supposed cause was not present.


> <b>(A) Many of the crimes of which people were convicted as a result of the get tough strategies were non-violent crimes such as small-scale drug dealing and shoplifting.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, as one could perceive it as indicating that the strategies were somehow affecting non-violent crime rather than having the desired effect, reduced rates of violent crime.


However, the truth is that the passage states as fact that rates of violent crime did decrease in the cities using the get tough strategies. So, what this choice says indicates that the strategies may have had effects in addition to the desired effects, not that the desired effects did not occur or did not result from the use of the strategies.


> <b>(B) Some of the cities employing get tough on crime strategies were unprepared for the increases in arrests that ensued, with the result that their court systems became clogged.
</b>


 The fact that the cities ran into problems in the process of implementing get tough strategies does not mean that using those strategies did not result in a reduction in violent crime.


> <b>(C) After the use of get tough strategies began, not just the rates of violent crime, but the rates of crime in general decreased in many cities in Verdantia.
</b>


 While the purpose of using get tough strategies was to reduce violent crime, the fact that the rates of other types of crime decreased as well does not mean that the strategies did not work. It could simply indicate that the strategies resulting in reductions in crime of all types.


> <b>(D) It has been found that education and community renewal can be more effective in causing reductions in violent crime and less costly than get tough on crime strategies.
</b>


 This is a typical “better plan” trap answer. The fact that there might be a way to reduce violent crime (education and community renewal) that is better than the way that was used (get-tough strategies) does not mean that the use of get tough strategies didn’t cause the reduction in violent crime.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) In cities in Verdantia in which get tough on crime strategies were not employed, rates of violent crime declined precipitously starting around 1990.
</b>


 What this choice says is a reason to question the conclusion that the get tough strategies were the reason rates of violent crime decreased.


After all, if the effect (rates of violent crime declined) happened even without the supposed cause (get tough strategies), then maybe the supposed cause was not what caused the effect. For instance, it could be that something else, such as a general shift in mindset in Verdantia, resulted in the reduction in rates of violent crime, and that the use of get tough strategies made no difference.


So, this choice undermines the support that the administrations’ premise provides for their conclusion.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
