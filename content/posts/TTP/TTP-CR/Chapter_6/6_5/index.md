## Lesson Content
As we saw in the Strengthen the Argument chapter, there are many ways to strengthen an argument.

> 1) Ruling out alternative causes.

> 2) Showing that when the cause is not present the effect does not occur.

> 3) Showing that the relationship cannot be reversed.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Three ways that are commonly used to strengthen the support for cause-and-effect conclusions are the following: 1) Ruling out alternative causes, 2) Showing that when the cause is not present the effect does not occur, 3) Showing that the relationship cannot be reversed.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s start with the first way: ruling out alternative causes.
