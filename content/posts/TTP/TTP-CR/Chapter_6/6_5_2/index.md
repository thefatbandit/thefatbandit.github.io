## Lesson Content
The major goal of an experiment is to isolate a single variable and measure its effect on another variable.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The major goal of an experiment is to isolate a single variable and measure its effect on another variable.
{{< /alert >}}

In scientific studies, the method most commonly used for ruling out alternative causes is to have a control group alongside the true test group, which is also referred to as the experimental group.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

To rule out alternative causes, in an experiment, a control group is used alongside an experimental group. In a perfect experiment, the only difference between the control group and the experimental group would be the variable of interest.
{{< /alert >}}

For example, if we were interested in how a certain multivitamin affects people’s vitamin D levels, we’d have two groups of people.

Meanwhile, the experiment would be designed so that, except for the multivitamin, the two groups would be as identical as possible.

Now, if, at the end of the experiment, the control group and the experimental group were to exhibit a similar change in vitamin D levels, the results would be strong evidence that the change in vitamin D levels was not caused by the multivitamin.

Be on the lookout for questions that utilize the concept of the control group and the scientific experiment.

##  Example 21 

The Timas are a tribe of relatively closely related people with similar lifestyles who have for centuries occupied an area now located partly within the nation of Gredaria and partly within the nation of Lexica.

Which of the following, if true, would provide further evidence supporting the above conclusion?

1. [ ] (A) Among the 62 percent of Timas living in Gredaria who are not experiencing diabetes are many who consume large amounts of heavily processed foods.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) When asked, Timas who eat heavily processed foods and are experiencing diabetes generally indicate that they are not aware that there could be a connection between diet and diabetes.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Technological advances have made treating the symptoms of diabetes relatively simple.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) In the Timas who live in Lexica, who consume a traditional diet that includes no heavily processed foods, the rate of diabetes is 7 percent.
5. [ ] (E) The Timas who maintain healthy body weights experience diabetes only rarely.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

##### Question Level: easy



The argument arrives at the following conclusion:


<u>Conclusion</u>: The high rate of diabetes in the Timas who live in Gredaria results from their dietary choices.


The conclusion is supported by the fact that the Timas in Gredaria consume large amounts of heavily processed foods.


Notice that the author makes a cause-and-effect claim. Whenever an author makes a cause-and-effect claim, she makes the assumption that her cause-and-effect claim is correct and that there cannot be an alternative explanation for her observation she seeks to explain. In this question, our job is to confirm the validity of that assumption.


An experiment set up to isolate the variable in question, diet, and eliminate other variables would help to confirm that the dietary choices cause the high rate of diabetes.


> <b>(A) Among the 62 percent of Timas living in Gredaria who are not experiencing diabetes are many who consume large amounts of heavily processed foods.
</b>


 Since this choice indicates that many Timas who consume large amounts of heavily processed foods do not experience diabetes, it actually weakens the link between consuming heavily processed foods and experiencing diabetes, and therefore weakens rather than strengthens the argument. We’re not seeking an answer choice that weakens the argument.


> <b>(B) When asked, Timas who eat heavily processed foods and are experiencing diabetes generally indicate that they are not aware that there could be a connection between diet and diabetes.
</b>


 While a person may be more likely to choose an unhealthy diet if that person is not aware that that diet causes health problems, the fact that a person is not aware of the effects of his diet does not somehow make it more likely that that diet is causing diabetes. Therefore, what this choice says has no effect on the conclusion.


> <b>(C) Technological advances have made treating the symptoms of diabetes relatively simple.
</b>


 The fact that treating the symptoms of diabetes has become relatively simple does not strengthen the connection between the heavily processed food laden diet of Timas in Gredaria and the higher than normal incidence of diabetes in those people.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) In the Timas who live in Lexica, who consume a traditional diet that includes no heavily processed foods, the rate of diabetes is 7 percent.
</b>


 This choice provides us with information that is like the information provided by a controlled experiment.


From the passage, we know the following about the Timas in Gredaria and the Timas in Lexica:


- They are closely related.


- Their lifestyles are similar.


So, in considering the differences in health of the two groups of Timas, we can rule out, at least to a degree, genetics and lifestyle.


Thus, to a degree, we have isolated one variable: diet.


So, we have our experiment. Other variables have been eliminated, and we can look for the effects of diet.


From the passage and this choice, we know the following:


- An abnormally high percentage of the Timas who eat heavily processed foods (the Timas in Gredaria) experience diabetes.


- A much lower percentage of the Timas who don’t eat heavily processed foods (the Timas in Lexica) experience diabetes.


By providing us with information that is like the results of a scientific experiment, this choice adds further support to the conclusion that the high rate of diabetes in the Timas who live in Gredaria results from their dietary choices, i.e., from their eating heavily processed foods.


> <b>(E) The Timas who maintain healthy body weights experience diabetes only rarely.
</b>


 This choice is a possible trap, as we could see that it mentions body weight and perceive it as indicating something about diet.


However, this choice does not give us any specifics about diet, and it does not indicate whether the Timas who maintain healthy body weights do or do not eat lots of heavily processed foods.


Therefore, since the argument is about the effects of diets that include lots of heavily processed foods, this choice has no clear effect on the strength of the argument.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

Let’s now discuss how to strengthen an argument by showing that, when the variable that the argument presents as the cause in a cause-and-effect relationship is not present, the effect does not occur.
