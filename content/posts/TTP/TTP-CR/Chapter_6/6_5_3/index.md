## Lesson Content
In the question above that involved the diets of the two groups of Timas, the central way in which the correct answer strengthened the argument was to show what happens when the diet of heavily processed foods is not present.

Showing that, when the cause is not present, the effect does not occur is one of the ways in which correct answers to Strengthen questions commonly strengthen support for conclusions about cause-and-effect relationships.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

An argument with a cause-and-effect conclusion can be strengthened by an answer choice that shows that, when the cause is not present, the effect does not occur.
{{< /alert >}}

Here’s another example in which the argument is strengthened by an answer choice that shows that, when the cause is not present, the effect does not occur.

##  Example 22 

Recently, bee populations have been declining worldwide.

Which of the following, if true, provides the most support for the hypothesis stated above?

1. [x] (A) In areas in which bees are able to gather nectar and pollen mainly from wildflowers, bee populations are thriving.
2. [ ] (B) The current decline in bee populations involves a greater percentage of bees than were involved in similar declines in bee populations that occurred in the past.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) When neonicotinoid pesticides first came into use, it was believed that they would not have significant negative effects on bees.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Study results indicate that bees that get pollen from a variety of plant species may have immune systems healthier than those of bees that get pollen from a single species.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Neonicotinoid pesticides get into the pollen and nectar of crop plants by traveling through the plants after being applied to the seeds from which the plants are grown.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Conclusion</u>: It is the use of neonicotinoid pesticides that underlies the decline in bee populations.


The argument supports the conclusion by linking the use of the pesticides to the decline in bee populations by using the fact that the use of the pesticides coincides chronologically with the decline and the fact that the pesticides have been found in nectar and pollen of crop plants, which bees use for food.


The conclusion could be further supported by information indicating that, when the cause, pesticides, is not present, the effect, bee population decline, does not occur.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) In areas in which bees are able to gather nectar and pollen mainly from wildflowers, bee populations are thriving.
</b>


 The argument indicates that the pesticides are present in the nectar and pollen of crop plants as a result of the pesticides’ having been used on crops. So, it is reasonable to presume that the pesticides are not found in the nectar and pollen of non-crop plants, such as wildflowers.


Thus, this choice indicates that populations of bees are thriving when the pesticides, which are used on crops but not on wildflowers, are not present in the bees’ food sources.


So, by indicating that, when the cause, the pesticides, is not present, the effect, bee populations declining, does not occur, this choice strengthens the support for the conclusion.


> <b>(B) The current decline in bee populations involves a greater percentage of bees than were involved in similar declines in bee populations that occurred in the past.
</b>


 This choice could be a tempting trap, as the argument indicates that this bee population decline and the use of neonicotinoid pesticides have occurred simultaneously. So, we could be tempted to decide that the fact that the current decline is more pronounced than other declines in bee populations somehow indicates that the pesticides that are currently in use are the cause.


However, the fact that the current decline is particularly pronounced does not clearly indicate that there is a link between this decline and the pesticides. After all, no matter how pronounced this decline is, some factor other than the pesticides could be the cause of this decline.


> <b>(C) When neonicotinoid pesticides first came into use, it was believed that they would not have significant negative effects on bees.
</b>


 The fact that it was believed that the pesticides would not have significant negative effects on bees clearly does not indicate that the pesticides do have significant negative effects on bees.


> <b>(D) Study results indicate that bees that get pollen from a variety of plant species may have immune systems healthier than those of bees that get pollen from a single species.
</b>


 Be careful not to spin a choice like this into a convoluted story that seems to connect a cause to an effect. Yes, this choice mentions pollen and bee health, but what it says about these topics does not link bee health or a bee population decline to the use of certain pesticides.


> <b>(E) Neonicotinoid pesticides get into the pollen and nectar of crop plants by traveling through the plants after being applied to the seeds from which the plants are grown.
</b>


 This choice could be a tempting trap, because it seems to provide confirmation that the pesticides get into the nectar and pollen that the bees make into food.


However, we don’t need such confirmation, because the argument has already stated that the pesticides have been found in crop plant nectar and pollen.


All this choice does is <em>explain how</em> the pesticides get into nectar and pollen. We have to watch out for choices that explain rather than strengthen.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
