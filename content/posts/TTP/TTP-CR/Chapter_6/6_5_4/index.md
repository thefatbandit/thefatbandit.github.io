## Lesson Content
Remember that, when an argument claims that A is the cause of B, we can weaken that argument by showing that it’s likely that B causes A.

Therefore, another way in which we can strengthen the conclusion in a cause-and-effect argument is to show that the relationship cannot be reversed.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

One way to strengthen a cause-and-effect conclusion is to show that the relationship between the cause and the effect cannot be reversed.
{{< /alert >}}

For example, having an underactive thyroid gland, or hypothyroidism, is associated with tiredness and weight gain.

Here’s an example in context:

> <em>In the Middle Ages it was observed that people who were feverish had fewer lice on their bodies than people who were not feverish had on their bodies. It must be the case that fever causes lice to leave a person’s body.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most strengthens the argument?</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>People in the Middle Ages who removed all lice from their bodies were no more likely to become feverish than were people who allowed lice to remain on them.</em>

In this example, the conclusion of the argument is that fever causes the absence of lice.

Interestingly, in the Middle Ages, some people observed the relatively low numbers of lice on feverish people and came to the opposite conclusion, the conclusion that lack of lice causes people to become feverish.

The correct answer supports the argument’s conclusion over the conclusion arrived at by some people in the Middle Ages by indicating that the cause-and-effect relationship presented in the argument’s conclusion cannot be reversed; the removal of lice does not result in a person’s becoming feverish.

Consider this example in which the argument is strengthened by a choice indicating that the cause-and-effect relationship cannot be reversed.

##  Example 23 

A sociological study of adults aged 24 years to 45 years shows that, the lower a person’s socioeconomic status, the greater is the likelihood of the person’s suffering from a chronic illness.

Which of the following, if true, would most strengthen the argument?

1. [ ] (A) The study included just as many participants whose ages were at the lower end of the age range as participants whose ages were at the higher end of the age range.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) A person’s socioeconomic status does not usually decline after a person develops a chronic illness.
3. [ ] (C) Some chronic illnesses, including post-viral fatigue syndrome, have a higher rate of incidence amongst high earners.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) A person’s income and profession usually determine her or his socioeconomic status.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) People with low socioeconomic status are more likely to suffer from psychological chronic illnesses than to suffer from physical chronic illnesses.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



In this argument, a correlation between relatively low socioeconomic status and relatively high incidence of chronic illness is used to support the conclusion that low socioeconomic status leads to chronic illness.


The argument can be strengthened by information showing that the reverse relationship does not hold; that is, chronic illnesses do not lead to low socioeconomic status.


> <b>(A) The study included just as many participants whose ages were at the lower end of the age range as participants whose ages were at the higher end of the age range.
</b>


 This information on the distribution of the ages of the participants does not affect the argument at all.


After all, regardless of the distribution of the ages of the participants, the fact that, the lower a person’s socioeconomic status, the greater is the likelihood of the person’s suffering from a chronic illness supports the conclusion that a person’s having a relatively low socioeconomic status results in that person’s being susceptible to chronic illness.


Thus, information on the ages of the participants neither weakens nor strengthens the support that the premise provides for the conclusion.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) A person’s socioeconomic status does not usually decline after a person develops a chronic illness.
</b>


 This answer shows that the reverse cause-and-effect relationship does not hold: developing a chronic illness does not lead to a drop in socio-economic status.


By indicating that the explanation for the correlation between low socioeconomic status and chronic illness is not that developing a chronic illness leads to a drop in socio-economic status, this choice eliminates an alternative explanation for the observed correlation between socioeconomic status and chronic illness and, thus, strengthens the argument.


> <b>(C) Some chronic illnesses, including post-viral fatigue syndrome, have a higher rate of incidence amongst high earners.
</b>


 While the fact that some forms of chronic illness are more common in high earners doesn’t negate the premise upon which the conclusion is based, this fact is somewhat counter to the premise that chronic illness is more common in people of low socioeconomic status.


Thus, while this choice doesn’t really weaken the support for the conclusion, it certainly doesn’t strengthen it.


> <b>(D) A person’s income and profession usually determine her or his socioeconomic status.
</b>


 Since the fact that income and profession are related to socioeconomic status is not clearly connected to the relationship between socioeconomic status and chronic illness, this choice has no impact on the argument.


> <b>(E) People with low socioeconomic status are more likely to suffer from psychological chronic illnesses than to suffer from physical chronic illnesses.
</b>


 We may be able to somehow spin the comparison outlined by this choice into a convoluted story that seems to strengthen the support for the conclusion.


However, the truth is that neither the passage nor this answer choice provides information indicating that the types of chronic illness people experience indicate anything about whether people’s experiencing chronic illness is related to people’s socioeconomic status.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
