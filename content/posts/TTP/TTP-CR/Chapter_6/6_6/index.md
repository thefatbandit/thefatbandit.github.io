## Lesson Content
When a cause-and-effect scenario appears in a Paradox question, we won’t have to find a choice that affects the support for a cause-and-effect conclusion, since Paradox questions don’t focus on support for conclusions.

Consider the following example:

> <em>In Australia, a fence was built to keep dingoes out of areas in which ranchers have livestock. It was expected that small marsupials, upon which dingoes prey, would thrive in the areas without dingoes, but instead the numbers of small marsupials in areas without dingoes are much lower than the numbers of such marsupials in areas where there are still dingoes.</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, most helps to explain why the results have been the opposite of those expected?</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>When dingoes are not present in an area in Australia, foxes, which prey heavily on small marsupials, become much more common.</em>

In this question, the stimulus presents us with an unexpected effect.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Resolve the Paradox questions involving cause-and-effect scenarios, the correct answers often explain effects mentioned in the passages.
{{< /alert >}}

Notice how the correct answer in the following question explains an effect.

##  Example 24 

In the practice of snake charming, the charmer plays a wind instrument, called a pungi, and moves in time to the music.

Which one of the following, if true, does the most to explain this surprising phenomenon?

1. [ ] (A) Since, because of the use of circular breathing, there are no pauses when the pungi is played, the sound created via the playing of the pungi is trance like.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) A cobra in close proximity to a person will take a defensive posture in which it stands up and follows the person’s movements to protect itself.
3. [ ] (C) Snake charmers are experienced in working with cobras, and use a variety of proven methods, some of them cruel, to get the results that the snake charmers want.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) The cobra is the type of snake most commonly used by modern snake charmers.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) The hissing sounds that cobras make must be for the purpose of chasing off other animals, not for communicating with other cobras.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

##### Question Level: easy



The unexpected situation presented is the following:


Fact 1: Cobras do not have ears or eardrums.


Fact 2: A cobra sitting in front of a snake charmer stands up and sways in time with the music, appearing to follow the rhythm.


If cobras don’t have ears or eardrums, and thus presumably cannot hear music, then how can the cobra’s actions be explained?


> <b>(A) Since, because of the use of circular breathing, there are no pauses when the pungi is played, the sound created via the playing of the pungi is trance like.
</b>


 This choice could seem to explain why the cobra moves in time to the music, if we were to lose track of the fact that the cobra has no way to hear the music and take the fact that the sound created by the playing of the pungi is “trance like” as indicating that the reason that the cobra moves in time to the music is that the cobra is entranced.


However, since the cobra cannot hear the music, the fact that the music is trance like makes no difference and does not explain why the cobra moves in time to it.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) A cobra in close proximity to a person will take a defensive posture in which it stands up and follows the person’s movements to protect itself.
</b>


This fact provides a link between the cobra’s standing up and swaying and the pungi music. Seeing this link requires noticing a key detail of the passage: the passage says that the charmer … moves in time to the music. So, this choice indicates that what is likely going on is that the cobra appears to detect the music, but really just sees the charmer’s movements and moves along with them in the process of defending itself.


Thus, by providing a cause, the cobra’s feeling that it needs to defend itself, for the observed effect, the movement of the cobra, this choice provides the explanation that we need.


> <b>(C) Snake charmers are experienced in working with cobras, and use a variety of proven methods, some of them cruel, to get the results that the snake charmers want.
</b>


This choice could seem to explain why the cobra moves in time to the music: snake charmers know how to make cobras move in time to music.


However, it does not explain exactly what we are seeking to explain, which is how, even if they are experienced, snake charmers get cobras to move in time to music that the cobras do not hear.


> <b>(D) The cobra is the type of snake most commonly used by modern snake charmers.
</b>


 While it could be that cobras are the snakes that are most commonly used by snake charmers because they are the ones most likely to move in time to the music, the fact that they are the most commonly used does not explain why they move in time to the music even though they don’t have ears with which to hear the music.


> <b>(E) The hissing sounds that cobras make must be for the purpose of chasing off other animals, not for communicating with other cobras.
</b>


This choice tempts the test-taker to choose it by providing a conclusion supported by what the passage says.


What the passage says, that cobras do not have ears, supports what this choice says, that the hissing sounds that cobras make must be for the purpose of chasing off other animals, not for communicating with other cobras. After all, if cobras cannot hear, then they would not hear the hissing of other cobras.


Of course, since we are looking for an explanation for the phenomenon, rather than a conclusion that is supported by the passage, this choice does not answer the question.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
