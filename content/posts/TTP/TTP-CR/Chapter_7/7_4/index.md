## Lesson Content
<u>Step 1 </u><u>– </u><u>Carefully Read and Completely Understand the Passage</u>

As is the case in answering any other type of Critical Reasoning question, the foundation of choosing the right answer to an Inference question is understanding the passage.

Also, remember that, when answering an Inference question, <strong>misreading a</strong> <strong>single word</strong> in the stimulus can significantly change the implications of what is said.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When answering an Inference question, misreading a single word in the stimulus can significantly change the implications of what is said. Thus, we MUST pay extremely close attention as we read so as not to misinterpret what is being expressed.
{{< /alert >}}

<u>Step 2 – Identify the Question Type (What type of information is the question asking for?)</u>

Identifying the question type is relatively easy in the case of an Inference question.

<u>Step 3 – Carefully Analyze the Facts</u>

Stimuli that accompany Inference questions won’t be arguments so much as lists of facts.

The point is to internalize the given information and to express the meanings and the effects of the statements in your own words and/or symbols, so that you can fully understand the information in the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Inference question stimuli typically don’t contain conclusions. Don’t spend your time searching for them.
{{< /alert >}}

To get a sense of the types of things you might take away from an Inference question stimulus, consider the following example of the type of passage you might see:

> <em>From the end of 2007 to the end of 2017, the number of actors working at Magic Movie Studios increased by 20 percent. Over the same period, the number of set designers working at Magic Movie Studios decreased by 10 percent. Nevertheless, over the same period, the total number of people working at the studios remained unchanged.</em>

Here’s an analysis of the statements in the passage:

> <u>Effect of First Statement</u>: Defines the change in the number of actors working at Magic Movie Studios over the time period.

> <u>Effect of Second Statement</u>: Defines the change in the number of set designers working at Magic Movie Studios over the time period.

> <u>Effect of Third Statement</u>: Makes clear that the total number of people working at the studios did not change over the period.

Notice, the statements tell us only about direction of change and degree of change in the numbers of three groups of people over a particular period.

In order to correctly answer Inference questions, we have to be clear about what information the statements provide and what information the statements do not provide.

<u>Step 4 –</u><u> Eliminate </u><u>“Sometimes True,” “Could Be True,” and “Definitely Not True” Options</u>

Some answers may make sense or seem reasonable.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Remember, something that is possibly true (but likewise possibly false) cannot be the correct answer to an Inference question.
{{< /alert >}}

Also, the fact that an answer choice does not conflict with the information in the passage does not make it correct.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The fact that an answer choice does not conflict with the information in the passage does not make it correct.
{{< /alert >}}

Always read all five answer choices first and then go through them more carefully on the second pass, eliminating the options that are more obviously wrong.

Let’s consider our example some more:

> <em>From the end of 2007 to the end of 2017, the number of actors working at Magic Movie Studios increased by 20 percent. Over the same period, the number of set designers working at Magic Movie Studios decreased by 10 percent. Nevertheless, over the same period, the total number of people working at the studios remained unchanged.</em>

> <em>Which of the following can be properly inferred from the statements above?</em>

Here’s an example of an answer choice that is plausible but not necessarily true:

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Answer</u>) At the end of 2017, there were more actors than set designers working at Magic Movie Studios.</em>

Is it possible that more actors than set designers were working at the studios? Yes.

Does the passage clearly indicate that it is the case that more actors than set designers were working at the studios? No.

As we outlined above, all the passage does is define degrees of change in numbers.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering an Inference question, never select as correct an answer choice that could be true but doesn’t have to be true. Remember, an inference must be true.
{{< /alert >}}

<u>Step 5 – Select the Choice that MUST be True</u>

We now pick an answer option that must be true.

Negate the statement: if that statement were false, would it contradict information in the paragraph? The answer that conflicts the most with the statements in the passage when negated is most likely the choice that “must” be true.

If a statement or condition, when negated, contradicts basic information contained in the original passage, then this statement must be important.

Remember, we are looking for an answer that <em>must be true</em>.

Let’s go back to our example:

> <em>From the end of 2007 to the end of 2017, the number of actors working at Magic Movie Studios increased by 20 percent. Over the same period, the number of set designers working at Magic Movie Studios decreased by 10 percent. Nevertheless, over the same period, the total number of people working at the studios remained unchanged.</em>

> <em>Which of the following can be properly inferred from the statements above?</em>

Here’s an example of an answer choice that must be true:

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) From the end of 2007 to the end of 2017, the ratio of actors to set designers at Magic Movie Studios increased.</em>

Given what the passage says, there is no way to deny what this choice says.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Practice with the Inference Question Strategy.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 4 

Whenever demand by travelers for hotel rooms in Mennerton decreases, the rates that hotels charge decrease.

Which of the following can be properly inferred regarding the past three years from the statement above?

1. [ ] (A) Mennerton hotel operators’ fears that allowing people to rent out their apartments for short stays would hurt the hotel business were unfounded.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) There have been more empty hotel rooms in Mennerton than there were in previous years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Overall traveler demand for lodging in Mennerton has increased.
4. [ ] (D) People renting out their apartments for short term stays in Mennerton have been charging rates similar to those charged by hotels.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) The number of hotels operating in Mennerton has decreased over the past three years.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: medium



<u>Step 1 – Carefully Read and Completely Understand the Passage</u>


At this point, we have carefully read the passage, making sure that we have correctly interpreted every word and each sentence.


<u>Step 2 – Identify the Question Type (What type of information is the question asking for?)</u>


Since the question asks “Which of the following can be properly inferred … ?” we know that we have to find an inference based on the information in the passage.


<u>Step 3 – Carefully Analyze the Facts</u>


Information Given:


<u>Fact 1</u>: Whenever traveler demand for hotel rooms in Mennerton decreases, rates charged by hotels decrease.


<u>Fact 2</u>: Over the past three years, the availability of places for travelers to stay in Mennerton has increased.


<u>Fact 3</u>: Over the past three years, people renting out their apartments for short stays have increasingly provided hotel type lodging to many of the travelers to Mennerton who otherwise would have stayed in hotels.


<u>Fact 4</u>: Over the same period of time, rates charged by hotels have not changed.


The correct answer will be one that is fully supported by what the passage says, even if it would not be considered the main conclusion of the passage. Given the facts stated in the passage, we know:


- how demand for hotel rooms in Mennerton affects rates: when demand decreases, rates do too.


- that the availability of places for travelers to stay in Mennerton has increased.


- that people in Mennerton have been increasing renting out apartments for short stays.


- what has happened with rates charged by hotels: no change.


<u>Step 4 –</u><u> Eliminate </u><u>“Sometimes True,” “Could Be True,” and “Definitely Not True” Options</u>


(A) What this choice says sounds as if it could be a main conclusion supported by what the passage says. The fact that the rates charged by hotels did not decrease even though the availability of places for travelers to stay in Mennerton has increased since people were renting out their apartments for short stays could be taken as evidence the hotel operators’ fears related to people’s renting out their apartments were unfounded. However, it does not have to be the case that hotel operators’ fears were unfounded. For one thing, the fact that rates have not changed over this time period does not necessarily indicate that the hotel business has not been hurt. For all we know, if people hadn’t been renting out their apartments for short stays over that time period, hotel rates would have increased substantially. Thus, while what this choice says could be true, it does not have to be true.


<strong>We can eliminate this choice.</strong>


(B) All we know from what the passage says is that people some people who would have stayed in hotels stayed in people’s apartments instead. That they did so does not mean that the number of empty hotel rooms increased. For all we know, there were many empty hotel rooms during the year four years ago, and, since then, hotels have seen an increase in business, even though people have been renting out their apartments to travelers.


<strong>We can eliminate this choice.</strong>


(C) Given the fact that hotel rates in Mennerton always decrease when demand for hotel rooms decreases, we can conclude that, even though the availability of places for travelers to stay in Mennerton has increased since people are renting out their apartments to travelers for short stays, demand for hotel rooms in Mennerton has not decreased. If demand for hotel rooms has not decreased even though people with apartments are taking business from hotels, it seems to make sense that overall traveler demand for lodging in Mennerton must have increased.


<strong>This choice could be our answer.</strong>


(D) While what this choice says seems plausible. If hotel rates have not changed even though, by renting out their apartments, people are providing lodging similar to that provided by hotels, it might make sense to conclude that the people who are renting out their apartments must be charging rates similar to those charged by hotels.


<strong>Keep for now.</strong>


(E) Given what the passage says about people’s staying in private apartments and hotel rates’ remaining at the same level, what this choice says seems to make sense. However, since the passage does not provide information that clearly supports any conclusion about the number of hotels in Mennerton, this choice cannot be the correct answer.


<strong>We can eliminate this choice.</strong>


<u>Step 5 – Select the Choice that MUST be True</u>


Although choice (D) seems to make sense, it does not have to be true that people renting out their apartments are charging rates similar to those charged by hotels. For all we know, people would rather stay in apartments than in hotels, and so people renting out apartments charge rates higher than those charged by hotels. On the other hand, it could be that people prefer to stay in hotels, and so the rates paid for apartments are lower than hotel rates.


Choice (C), on the other hand, must be true. If rates for hotel rooms have not decreased even though the availability of places for travelers to stay in Mennerton has increased since people are taking business from hotels by renting out their apartments, then overall traveler demand for lodging in Mennerton must have increased.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
