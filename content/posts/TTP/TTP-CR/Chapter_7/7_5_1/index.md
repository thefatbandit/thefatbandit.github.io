## Lesson Content
Remember that an Inference question requires us to find an answer that must be true.

If we select an answer that only could be true or is only likely to be true, we are by definition selecting an incorrect answer.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering an Inference question, if we select an answer that only could be true or is only likely to be true, we are by definition selecting an incorrect answer.
{{< /alert >}}

For the purposes of GMAT Inference questions, there are three categories of truth: absolute truth (whatever must be true); mixed truth (whatever could be true, but might possibly be false); and un-truth (whatever is always false).

Let’s revisit our earlier example and consider into what categories of truth we can put each of five answer choices:

> <em>From the end of 2007 to the end of 2017, the number of actors working at Magic Movie Studios increased by 20 percent. Over the same period, the number of set designers working at Magic Movie Studios decreased by 10 percent. Nevertheless, over the same period, the total number of people working at the studios remained unchanged.</em>

> <em>Which of the following can be properly inferred from the statements above?</em>

> <em>(A) The ratio of set designers to actors at Magic Movie Studios was greater at the end of 2017 than it was at the end of 2007.</em>

What choice (A) says is clearly false.

> <em>(B) From the end of 2017 to the end of 2027, the number of actors working at Magic Movie Studios will increase by 20 percent.</em>

While what choice (B) says does not conflict with what the passage says, it is almost completely unsubstantiated, as the passage gives us no reason to believe that the increase in actors that occurred from 2007 to 2017 will be repeated from 2017 to 2027.

> <em>(C) At the end of 2017, there were more actors than set designers working at Magic Movie Studios.</em>

Notice the difference between choice (C), which we saw earlier, and choices (A) and (B).

> <em>(D) In 2017 Magic Movie Studios needed fewer set designers than it needed in 2007.</em>

Choice (D) is even more dangerous than choice (C), as the fact that fewer set designers were working at the studios at the end of 2017 than were working there at the end of 2007 could be taken as a reasonably clear indication that fewer set designers were needed at the studios in 2017 than were needed in 2007.

> <em>(E) From the end of 2007 to the end of 2017, the ratio of actors to set designers at Magic Movie Studios increased.</em>

(E) is our <strong>correct</strong> answer.

When we are answering Inference questions, the type of solid support that there is for choice (E) is the type of support to look for in determining which answer is correct.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Inference questions, answers that only could be true or are only likely to be true are incorrect; an inference must be true 100 percent of the time. When seeking out the correct inference, look for an answer choice that is fully supported by the information presented in the stimulus.
{{< /alert >}}

In going through the following example, pay particular attention to why each of the incorrect answers <em>could be true</em>, but does not have to be true.

##  Example 5 

City X has always provided a certain number of subsidized housing units in the downtown area.

Given the information above, which of the following statements must be true?

1. [ ] (A) Some subsidized housing residents will have to move because the rent will not be affordable.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Residents of subsidized housing will have to take on second jobs in order to pay the additional rent.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) The increase in the supply of subsidized housing units will result in a decrease in cost of rent for such units.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) The portion of the rent for subsidized housing paid for by the tenants of such housing will increase.
5. [ ] (E) Local non-profits will make more funds available to assist low-income families in paying their rent.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Fact 1</u>: City X has been paying 1/2 of the rent paid for subsidized housing units.


<u>Fact 2</u>: City X will increase the number of subsidized housing units.


<u>Fact 3</u>: City X will pay only 1/3 of the rent.


What must be true?


The facts provided mention only what the city plans to do in terms of the number of subsidized housing units and the proportion of the rent for such units that the city will pay. There are many possible results of the changes that the city plans to make, results such as some residents of subsidized housing moving out of their units because of their not being able to afford to pay the rent without the city’s paying half, and some of these possible results are likely to occur. However, we are looking for a choice that describes something that must be true, not something that just may occur. We need a choice that states something clearly supported by the facts in the stimulus.


> <b>(A) Some subsidized housing residents will have to move because the rent will not be affordable.
</b>


While some residents may have to move out, the passage never described the financial situations of the residents of subsidized housing. Could the residents easily afford to pay more for rent? Will the landlords lower the rents because they conclude that having occupied units with lower rents is better than having vacant units with higher rents? Since any of these alternate outcomes are quite possible, what this choice says is not <em>necessarily true.</em>


> <b>(B) Residents of subsidized housing will have to take on second jobs in order to pay the additional rent.
</b>


This option is similar to (A). We don’t know what the residents can and cannot afford. Furthermore, maybe the residents currently don’t have jobs. In such a case they wouldn’t be taking second jobs; they may be taking first jobs.


> <b>(C) The increase in the supply of subsidized housing units will result in a decrease in cost of rent for such units.
</b>


This is in fact very possible. Since the supply of such units will increase, the average cost to rent such a unit may decrease. However, we don’t know that this will definitely happen. Maybe, demand for such units is expected to increase, and so, adding more will only keep the rent levels stable. Alternatively, maybe the landlords will raise rents and seek to attract higher income renters. Again, the passage doesn’t give us information via the use of which these possibilities can be ruled out. So, what this says is not necessarily true.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) The portion of the rent for subsidized housing paid for by the tenants of such housing will increase.
</b>


Given that the city will pay a smaller fraction of the rent, what this choice says must be true in every case. For example, even if landlords decide to charge lower rents, the tenants will still end up paying a larger fraction of this decreased rent. In other words, regardless of what happens with rents, because the city will end up paying a smaller fraction of the rent, the tenants will pay a greater portion of the rent. Notice that this fact must be true.


> <b>(E) Local non-profits will make more funds available to assist low-income families in paying their rent.
</b>


This is especially tricky because, in real life, what this choice describes has often occurred, and so, this option may easily appeal to our sensibilities. It most definitely could be true! However, there is nothing in the passage that suggests that what this choice describes will necessarily happen. Maybe these residents will just be forced to move out. Maybe some other group, such as the state or federal government, might step in and cover the portion that the city is no longer paying for. Either way, we don’t know for sure what will occur. So, what this choice says does not have to be true.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

Let’s look at another common type of trap answer: an answer that presents information from the stimulus in a distorted fashion.
