## Lesson Content
Unless we are paying very close attention when answering a Critical Reasoning question, it’s easy to forget and or twist some of what we read.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Unless we are paying very close attention when answering a Critical Reasoning question, it’s easy to forget and or twist some of what we read. Thus, we may create a distorted picture of what we read. Such a distorted picture makes it a lot easier for a test-taker to fall for trap answers that include unsupported information.
{{< /alert >}}

This kind of trap answer – one that presents information from the stimulus in a distorted fashion – is attractive because it sounds very similar to information presented.

The information in the answer will be distorted just enough to make the answer incorrect.

When we go through the answers, we must carefully and directly compare them to the information in the original stimulus (rather than simply relying upon our memory).

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Look out for answers that present information from the original argument in a distorted fashion. It only takes a small change to render incorrect an otherwise correct answer. Remember, “almost correct” is “completely wrong.” It is incredibly easy to fall for trap answers that use language taken directly from the passage.
{{< /alert >}}

Here’s an inside tip: the correct answer will sometimes use <em>different </em>words to discuss the <em>same </em>ideas that are in the passage.

Let’s review the following stimulus and answer choices, which present information from the stimulus in a distorted fashion.

> <em>Limiting animal agriculture and switching from using fossil fuel to using hydroelectric power are two ways in which Vertland could reduce its national carbon emissions. Limiting animal agriculture would serve to reduce carbon emissions more than would switching to using hydroelectric power.</em>

> <em>Which of the following must be true if all of the statements above are true?</em>

> <em>(A) </em><em>For Vertland, switching to hydroelectric power would not be a cost effective method of reducing carbon emissions.</em>

Choice (A) distorts the information provided in the stimulus.

Let’s consider another choice.

> <em>(B) </em><em>For Vertland, limiting animal agriculture is the method that would be most effective in reducing the nation’s carbon emissions. </em>

This answer distorts the information provided in the stimulus, in that it takes what the passage says about the effect of limiting animal agriculture versus the effect of switching to using hydroelectric power and distorts it into the conclusion that limiting animal agriculture is the method that would be <em>most effective</em> of all possible methods.

##  Example 6 

When soil is low in calcium, crops can have difficulty growing in it.

Which of the following is information provided by the statements above?

1. [x] (A) A farmer who adds too much lime to a calcium deficient field may initially see improvements in crop growth and then see declines in crop growth.
2. [ ] (B) Crop growth in soil in which calcium levels are too high will generally be stronger than crop growth in soil in which calcium levels are too low.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) Foods grown in soil with relatively high calcium levels are more nutritious than foods grown in soil with relatively low calcium levels.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Three years after lime has been added to the soil in a field, it becomes necessary to reapply lime to maintain calcium levels sufficient for strong crop growth.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Of all the nutrients that may be present in soil, calcium is the nutrient that is most important for strong crop growth.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The passage provides four related pieces of information.


<u>Fact 1</u>: When soil is low in calcium, crops can have difficulty growing in it.


<u>Fact 2</u>: To remedy a soil calcium deficiency, a farmer can add lime to the soil.


<u>Fact 3</u>: Once lime has been applied, soil calcium levels increase gradually.


<u>Fact 4</u>: Overly high soil calcium levels also can inhibit crop growth.


In order to get this question right, we have to be careful to stick to the facts and not to get sucked in by trap answers that present conclusions that are arrived at by distorting or going beyond what the passage says.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) A farmer who adds too much lime to a calcium deficient field may initially see improvements in crop growth and then see declines in crop growth.
</b>


 Since, once lime has been applied, soil calcium levels increase gradually, and since overly high soil calcium levels can inhibit crop growth, a farmer who adds too much lime to a calcium deficient field may initially see improvements in crop growth as the calcium levels begin to increase and then see declines in crop growth as the calcium levels continue to increase to levels higher than are optimal for crop growth. Thus, this choice follows exactly from what the passage says.


> <b>(B) Crop growth in soil in which calcium levels are too high will generally be stronger than crop growth in soil in which calcium levels are too low.
</b>


 This choice distorts what the passage says, as, while the passage does say that an increase in calcium levels can help crops to grow, it does not follow from that fact that overly high calcium levels are better for crop growth than are calcium levels that are too low.


> <b>(C) Foods grown in soil with relatively high calcium levels are more nutritious than foods grown in soil with relatively low calcium levels.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because what it says is plausible. However, the passage discusses only the effects of soil calcium on crop growth and says nothing that we can use to arrive at a firm conclusion about the effects of soil calcium values on the nutritional value of crops grown in the soil.


> <b>(D) Three years after lime has been added to the soil in a field, it becomes necessary to reapply lime to maintain calcium levels sufficient for strong crop growth.
</b>


 This choice expresses a conclusion arrived at by distorting a fact presented in the stimulus. While the stimulus does say that, after the application of lime, the full effects of adding the lime can take three years to appear, it does not indicate that, once those effects appear, lime has to be added to the soil to maintain calcium levels sufficient for strong crop growth. It could be that, once the effects appear, the calcium levels will remain optimal for years.


> <b>(E) Of all the nutrients that may be present in soil, calcium is the nutrient that is most important for strong crop growth.
</b>


 What this choice says goes beyond what the passage says. While the facts in the passage do indicate that calcium is important for crop growth, they don’t provide information that we can use to conclude that calcium is the nutrient <em>most important</em> for crop growth.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)

Let’s take a look at another common type of trap answer: an answer that is out of proportion to the information presented in the stimulus.
