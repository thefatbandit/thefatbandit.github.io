## Lesson Content
An “out of proportion” answer is one that exaggerates information presented in the stimulus.

For example, if a passage were to suggest that “some people in Group A like basketball,” while an answer choice implies that “all people in Group A are basketball fans,” the choice would be out of proportion and incorrect.

Let’s say there are ten people in Group A, then “some” could be three people, four people, or even ten people.

We must be very careful to not select answers that are out of proportion with the information presented in the stimulus.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

We must be very careful to not select answers that are out of proportion with the information presented in the stimulus.
{{< /alert >}}

Some key words that frequently go together are:

> - some/many/most/all

> - never/seldom/rarely/sometimes/often

> - good/better/best

> - bad/worse/worst

In the following sentences, consider how the information presented in the stimulus is different from the out of proportion answer.

> <u>Stimulus</u>: <strong><em>Some</em></strong><em> of the senators who attended the meeting had salad for lunch.</em>

> <u>Out of Proportion</u>: <strong><em>Most</em></strong><em> of the senators who attended the meeting had salad for lunch.</em>

Notice that the words “some” and “most” mean very different things.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

“Some” means “one or more,” while “most” means more than half.
{{< /alert >}}

> <u>Stimulus</u>: <em>After the passage of the Stokes-Miller act, that there will be new tariffs on goods from China is <strong>not guaranteed</strong>. </em>

> <u>Out of Proportion</u>: <em>After the passage of the Stokes-Miller act, that there will be new tariffs on goods from China is <strong>unlikely</strong>.</em>

Similarly, notice that the words “not guaranteed” and “unlikely” have very different meanings.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

“Not guaranteed” means “a probability of less than 100 percent,” while “unlikely” means “a probability of less than 50 percent.”
{{< /alert >}}

> <u>Stimulus</u>: <em>A three percent decrease in the sales tax would be </em><strong><em>sufficient</em></strong><em> to boost revenue by twenty percent.</em>

> <u>Out of Proportion</u>: <em>A three percent decrease in the sales tax is <strong>necessary</strong> to boost revenue by twenty percent.</em>

It is easy to confuse “sufficient” with “necessary,” even though these two words have quite different meanings.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Remember, “sufficient” means “enough,” while “necessary” means “required.”
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 7 

Scientists studying populations of monarch butterflies and domestic honeybees between 1976 and 2014 noticed that a decline in the population of butterflies was always followed by a decline in the population of honeybees within two weeks.

Which of the following conclusions can be properly drawn from information above?

1. [ ] (A) A decrease in the population of butterflies will always be followed by a decrease in the population of honeybees.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Factors that cause declines in the honeybee population do not necessarily cause declines in the butterfly population.
3. [ ] (C) The size of the honeybee population is more closely connected to the size of the monarch butterfly population than to the size of the population of any other insect.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) If monarch butterflies become extinct, domestic honeybees are likely to become extinct as well.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) The size of the honeybee population does not impact the size of the butterfly population.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



<u>Fact 1</u>: Butterfly population decline was always followed by honeybee decline.


<u>Fact 2</u>: Honeybee population decline was not always followed by butterfly decline.


What must be true?


Whenever the population of butterflies declined, the honeybee population also declined. Therefore, it is likely that there is some kind of relationship between the two species. Either the factor – such as weather, pollution, or disease - that caused the butterfly population declines also caused the honeybee population declines, or the butterfly population declines caused the honeybee population declines.


However, since the converse did not hold true (honeybee population declines were not always followed by butterfly population declines) we can’t merely assume that, for example, pollution kills both butterflies and honeybees or that weather kills both butterflies and honeybees. The information in the passage suggests that the order matters; that is, if the honeybee population declines first, then the butterfly population will not always decline.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) A decrease in the population of butterflies will always be followed by a decrease in the population of honeybees.
</b>


 While what this choice says might seem reasonable given the fact that, in the past, declines in the butterfly population were always been followed by declines in the honeybee population, the truth is that, since the passage does not provide information on why the pattern manifested in the past, we don’t have a clear reason to believe that the pattern will hold in the future. In other words, since the passage mentions only a past pattern, while this choice is about the future, this choice is out of proportion with the information that is presented in the passage.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B) Factors that cause declines in the honeybee population do not necessarily cause declines in the butterfly population.
</b>


This choice is fully supported by the information presented in the passage. Since honeybee population declines are not always followed by butterfly population declines, whatever causes honeybee population declines doesn’t necessarily cause butterfly population declines.


> <b>(C) The size of the honeybee population is more closely connected to the size of the monarch butterfly population than to the size of the population of any other insect.
</b>


This choice goes beyond what’s supported by the passage. While the passage indicates that the size of the honeybee population is somehow related to the size of the monarch butterfly population, the passage provides no basis for comparing the closeness of that connection with the closeness of the connection between the size of the honeybee population and the size of the population of any other insect. For all we know from what the passage says, there is, for instance, some other insect the size of the population of which both always increases and always decreases with the size of the honeybee population.


> <b>(D) If monarch butterflies become extinct, domestic honeybees are likely to become extinct as well.
</b>


This choice is out of proportion with what the passage says. The passage says merely that a decline in the population of butterflies <em>was </em>always followed by a decline in the population of honeybees. It does not say that a decline in the butterfly population always <em>will be</em> or even always was followed by a decline <em>of the</em> <em>same magnitude</em> in the honeybee population. So, it could be that, when the monarch butterfly population declines a lot, the honeybee population also declines, but not as much. Alternatively, it could be that, in the future, butterfly population declines will not be followed by honeybee population declines. So, given what the passage says, it could be that, even if the butterflies were to die out completely, the honeybee population would decline by only a small percentage or not at all.


> <b>(E) The size of the honeybee population does not impact the size of the butterfly population.
</b>


This answer uses the words “honeybee population” and “butterfly population” to distract us. It is also quite tempting because it seems to recognize that the size of honeybee population is more dependent upon that of the butterfly population than the size of the butterfly population is upon that of the honeybee population. However, the fact that declines in the honeybee population are not always followed by declines in the butterfly population does not mean that the size of honeybee population “does not impact” the size of the butterfly population. The size of the honeybee population could somehow affect the size of the butterfly population without there being a clear, constant pattern to the interaction between the two. So, we can’t say that there is no impact. What this choice says does not have to be true.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

{{< /alert >}}
</details></h5>


<u>Changes in Tense Can Make an Answer Out of Proportion to the Stimulus</u>

Pay close attention to changes in tense, which will often make the answer out of proportion to the information in the original paragraph.

> <u>Stimulu</u><u>s</u>: <em>There <strong>has never been</strong> a successful invasion of Country X.</em>

> <u>Out of Proportion</u>: <em>There <strong>will never be</strong> a successful invasion of Country X.</em>

Notice the change in verb tense.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When answering Inference questions, pay close attention to changes in tense, which will often make the answer out of proportion to the information in the original paragraph.

##  Example 8 

In the United States, a customer has to pay sales tax to a state only when goods purchased are purchased from a retailer with a physical presence in the state in which the customer is located.

If the above statements are true, which of the following must also be true?

1. [ ] (A) States received more revenue in the form of sales taxes before the advent of online retailing.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Most purchases made online do not involve a customer’s having to pay sales tax to a state.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) It is possible that an online retailer located in the United States would never have to charge sales tax on any purchase made from it.
4. [ ] (D) As the number of purchases made online increases, states will see decreases in sales tax revenue.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Customers usually make online purchases from retailers without physical locations in the states in which the customers are located.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



Effects of the Statements in the Passage:


<u>Effect of the First Statement</u>: Defines when a customer has to pay sales tax – only when goods purchased are purchased from a retailer with a physical presence in the state in which the customer is located.


<u>Effect of the Second Statement</u>: Indicates that many purchases involve a customer in one state purchasing from a retailer in another state.


<u>Effect of the Third Statement</u>: Describes the results of customers’ making purchases from retailers in other states – in such cases, customers do not pay sales tax.


The correct answer to this question will follow directly from the information provided in these three statements. To avoid choosing an incorrect choice, we have to be careful not to choose a choice that says something out of proportion with what is said in the statements. For instance, a choice that said that <strong>most </strong>customers making purchases online do not pay sales tax would be out of proportion with the statement made in the passage that says that “in <strong>many</strong> cases, customers do not pay sales tax.”


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) States received more revenue in the form of sales taxes before the advent of online retailing.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, as, if we were to assume that online retailing involving customers and retailers in different states has taken business away from physical store retailing, then, given what the passage says about how sales taxes work, we might conclude that states received more revenue in the form of sales taxes before the advent of online retailing. However that assumption about online retailing taking business away from physical store retailing is not supported by the information in the passage. Thus, fully supporting this answer choice requires using information from outside the passage.


> <b>(B) Most purchases made online do not involve a customer’s having to pay sales tax to a state.
</b>


 What this choice says is out of proportion with what the passage says. The passage says that, “in many cases, customers do not pay sales tax on online purchases.” “Many” simply means “a great number.” A great number is not necessarily “most,” i.e., “more than half.” So, what this choice says about “Most purchases made online” is not supported by the passage.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) It is possible that an online retailer located in the United States would never have to charge sales tax on any purchase made from it.
</b>


 The passage says that a customer has to pay sales tax to a state only when goods purchased are purchased from a retailer with a physical presence in the state in which the customer is located. Since it is possible that all of an online retailer’s customers would be located in states other than any state in which the retailer has a physical location, it is possible that the retailer would never have to charge sales tax on any purchase made from it.


> <b>(D) As the number of purchases made online increases, states will see decreases in sales tax revenue.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, as, if one were to assume that online retailing is taking business away from physical store retailing, one might feel that it would make sense to infer from what the passage says that, as more purchases are made online, state sales tax revenue will decrease. However, the idea that online retailing is taking business away from physical store retailing is not supported by anything said in the passage. So, arriving at the conclusion stated in this choice would require adding information from outside the passage to the scenario.


> <b>(E) Customers usually make online purchases from retailers without physical locations in the states in which the customers are located.
</b>


 What this choice says is out of proportion with what the passage says. While the passage says that “many online purchases are made by customers in one state from retailers with physical locations only in other states,” “many online purchases” could be a small percentage of all online purchases. So, the fact that <em>many</em> online purchases involve customers’ purchasing from retailers in states other than those in which the customers are located does not necessarily mean that customers <em>usually</em> make online purchases from retailers without physical locations in the states in which the customers are located.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
