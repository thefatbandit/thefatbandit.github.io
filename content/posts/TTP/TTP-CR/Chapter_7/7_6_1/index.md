## Lesson Content
The first type of Inference correct answer that we’ll discuss is an answer that is a conclusion based on the entirety of the information presented in the passage.

Often, what is conveyed by the statements in the passage in an Inference question adds up logically to a conclusion.

Consider the following example:

> <em>The presence on Mars of certain clay minerals believed to form only in water gave scientists reason to believe that life may have once existed on Mars, in the water in which the clay minerals were formed. However, further investigation revealed that the clay minerals were formed in water present in molten magma. Since molten magma is extremely hot, any water present in magma would be too hot to support life.</em>

> <em>Which of the following can be properly concluded from the statements above?</em>

> <em>(<u>Trap Choice</u>)</em> <em>Life has never existed on Mars.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>The presence on Mars of certain clay minerals that form only in water is no reason to believe that life existed on Mars.</em>

Notice, both the trap choice and the correct answer are connected to all the information given in the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In some cases, the correct answers to Inference questions are supported by the entirety of what is said in the passages.

##  Example 12 

Crop rotation is the practice of growing in the same area a series of different types of crops, one type of crop one season and another type the next season.

Which of the following can be properly inferred from the statements above?

1. [ ] (A) If crop rotation is not used in an area, the presence of crop damaging organisms will not decrease in that area.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Whenever organisms cause extensive damage to crops in an area for two consecutive seasons, it must be the case that crop rotation was not employed.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Crop rotation can be just as effective in reducing the presence of crop damaging organisms as using chemical treatments generally is.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Crop rotation would not be effective in reducing the presence of organisms of a type that could live equally well on any type of crop.
5. [ ] (E) Farms that practice crop rotation tend to experience greater crop yields than are achieved by farms that do not practice crop rotation.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The passage presents us with a clear picture of what crop rotation is and how it affects organisms that may cause damage to crops.


<u>Effect of the First Statement</u>: Describes crop rotation as a practice involving planting different crops in the same area, one crop one season and another crop the next.


<u>Effect of the Second Statement</u>: Explains how crop rotation can make it difficult for crop damaging organisms to persist in an area – crop damaging organisms that live on one type of crop may not be able to live on another type, and so they die out.


<u>Effect of the Third Statement</u>:Presents the logical outcome of crop rotation – a reduction the presence of crop damaging organisms in a given area.


The correct answer to this question is an inference that is based on all three of the statements in the stimulus.


> <b>(A) If crop rotation is not used in an area, the presence of crop damaging organisms will not decrease in that area.
</b>


 While the passage indicates that crop rotation can result in a reduction in the presence of crop damaging organisms, the passage does not suggest that, without crop rotation, the presence of such organisms never decreases. So, it does not support the conclusion that, if crop rotation is not used in an area, the presence of crop damaging organisms will not decrease in that area.


> <b>(B) Whenever organisms cause extensive damage to crops in an area for two consecutive seasons, it must be the case that crop rotation was not employed.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because it seems to follow from what the passage says about the effects of crop rotation on the ability of crop damaging organisms to live in an area. However, the passage does not say that crop rotation <em>always</em> makes living in an area <em>impossible</em> for crop damaging organisms. So, for all we know from what the passage says, sometimes crop damaging organisms can persist in an area and damage crops even if crop rotation is practiced.


> <b>(C) Crop rotation can be just as effective in reducing the presence of crop damaging organisms as using chemical treatments generally is.
</b>


 While what this choice says seems likely to be true, this choice goes beyond what the passage supports. The facts in the passage indicate that crop rotation has certain effects on crop damaging organisms. It does not give us information that we can use to definitively compare those effects with the effects of chemical treatments.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(D) Crop rotation would not be effective in reducing the presence of organisms of a type that could live equally well on any type of crop.
</b>


 The passage indicates that the reason that crop rotation makes persisting in an area difficult for crop damaging organisms is that, often, organisms that can live on one kind of crop are not able to live on another type of crop. So, it follows that a kind of organism that could live on any type of crop would be able to continue living in an area even if crop rotation were practiced in that area. Thus, crop rotation would not be effective in reducing the presence of such an organism.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) Farms that practice crop rotation tend to experience greater crop yields than are achieved by farms that do not practice crop rotation.
</b>


 The passage indicates only that crop rotation has the effect of reducing the presence of crop damaging organisms. While it seems likely that, given the effects of crop rotation, farms that practice crop rotation tend to experience greater crop yields than are achieved by farms that do not practice crop rotation, the passage does not fully support this conclusion. In spite of what the passage says, it could be that some farmers do not practice crop rotation, and yet achieve similar results through the use of other methods.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

##  Example 13 

The degree to which smoking cigarettes is harmful to health is directly related to the amounts of damaging substances inhaled from cigarettes.

Which of the following can be properly concluded from the information provided by the passage?

1. [ ] (A) Many smokers who smoke cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke are not truly concerned about the harmful effects of smoking.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Cigarette companies that produce cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke consciously intend to mislead via their marketing messages.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) It is difficult to design a cigarette such that any person smoking that cigarette would avoid to a large degree the ill effects of smoking.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Certain implications of marketing messages associated with cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke do not hold true for all smokers.
5. [ ] (E) There is no actual difference between smoking regular cigarettes and smoking cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The passage includes information:


- that relates the harmful effects of smoking to the damaging substances inhaled from cigarettes.


- indicating that cigarette companies produce cigarettes with features designed to reduce the amounts of such damaging substances present in inhaled smoke.


- indicating that cigarette company marketing messages imply that people smoking such cigarettes will largely avoid the ill effects of smoking.


- indicating that people who smoke such cigarettes, by inhaling more smoke, defeat the features.


By combining the information provided by the statements in the passage, one can arrive at a variety of conclusions. However, not all of them will have to be true given what the passage says.


> <b>(A) Many smokers who smoke cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke are not truly concerned about the harmful effects of smoking.
</b>


 While the passage says that smokers who smoke cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke defeat the features by inhaling more smoke, the passage does not clearly indicate anything about the concerns of such smokers with regard to the harmful effects of smoking. It could be that, even though they defeat the features, they are concerned about the harmful effects of smoking.


> <b>(B) Cigarette companies that produce cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke consciously intend to mislead via their marketing messages.
</b>


 This answer could be tempting, because it seems plausible that cigarette companies intentionally mislead via their marketing messages associated with cigarettes with the features, given that many smokers defeat the features and so, in a way, the features don’t really make a difference. However, we are not looking for a statement that is merely plausible, but rather one that must be true, and, while the messages do seem to be misleading, nothing in the passage clearly indicates that the companies consciously intend to mislead.


> <b>(C) It is difficult to design a cigarette such that any person smoking that cigarette would avoid to a large degree the ill effects of smoking.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, given that the passage indicates that features apparently designed to make smoking less harmful have been defeated by some people smoking the cigarettes. However, the fact that those particular features have been defeated does not <em>necessarily </em>mean that it is difficult to design cigarettes such that any person smoking them would largely avoid the ill effects of smoking.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) Certain implications of marketing messages associated with cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke do not hold true for all smokers.
</b>


 One might be tempted to eliminate this choice because the way in which it is written obscures its connection to what is said in the passage. All the same, it is connected in the following way.


The passage says that the degree to which smoking is harmful is related to the amounts of damaging substances inhaled while smoking. The passage indicates that many people who smoke cigarettes with the features defeat the features by inhaling more smoke. We can conclude that, therefore, when smoking the cigarettes with the features, those people do not avoid the ill effects of smoking cigarettes.


If many people do not avoid the ill effects, then not all people avoid the ill effects. Therefore, the marketing messages’ implications that, by choosing the cigarettes with the features, one will avoid the ill effects of smoking do not hold true for all people.


> <b>(E) There is no actual difference between smoking regular cigarettes and smoking cigarettes with features designed to reduce amounts of damaging substances in inhaled smoke.
</b>


 This answer is a trap, because the statements made in the passage do indicate that smoking regular cigarettes and smoking cigarettes with the features are likely similar in terms of their harmful effects, and so it’s easy to decide that there is no difference between the smoking one of the types of cigarettes and smoking the other. However, the passage does not say that the two are the same in all ways, and, further, the passage tells us ways in which they are different. The smoke from one is different from the smoke from the other, and people inhale differently when smoking one type from how they would inhale if smoking the other type.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

##  Example 14 

When people are doing work that requires intense focus or involves learning new things, background music without lyrics may enhance their productivity, while background music with lyrics generally distracts them and thus negatively affects productivity.

Which of the following can be properly inferred from the statements above?

1. [ ] (A) When most of the people in a workspace are doing repetitive work, background music without lyrics will enhance the total productivity of the people in the workspace.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) The same background music that enhances the productivity of some people may cause a reduction in the productivity of others.
3. [ ] (C) Background music without lyrics enhances the productivity of people regardless of the type of work they are doing.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) Music with lyrics enhances the productivity of people doing repetitive tasks more than music without lyrics does.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Managers interested in maximizing company productivity should be focused on the type of background music played in company workspaces.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



<u>Fact 1</u>: Background music without lyrics may enhance the productivity of people doing work that requires intense focus or involves learning new things.


<u>Fact 2</u>: Background music with lyrics generally distracts and thus negatively affects the productivity of people doing work that requires intense focus or involves learning new things.


<u>Fact 3</u>: When people are doing repetitive work, background music with or without lyrics may enhance productivity.


The facts presented involve two kinds of background music and the effects of background music on the productivity of people doing two types of work.


The correct answer to this question, while not a summary of the passage, is based on all of the facts combined.


> <b>(A) When most of the people in a workspace are doing repetitive work, background music without lyrics will enhance the total productivity of the people in the workspace.
</b>


 This answer could be tempting, because the passage indicates that music without lyrics may enhance the productivity of people doing repetitive work, and may even enhance the productivity of people doing work that requires intense focus or involves learning new things. However, this choice is out of proportion with what the passage says, as this choice says “will enhance,” while the passage says “may enhance.” Since saying that something “may enhance” productivity does not mean that that thing definitely “will enhance” productivity, what this choice says is not fully supported by what the passage says.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) The same background music that enhances the productivity of some people may cause a reduction in the productivity of others.
</b>


 The passage says that music with lyrics may enhance the productivity of people doing repetitive work and that music with lyrics generally distracts people doing work requiring intense focus. Thus, one can infer from the facts in the passage that a type of music, music with lyrics, that enhances the productivity of some people may cause a reduction in the productivity of others.


> <b>(C) Background music without lyrics enhances the productivity of people regardless of the type of work they are doing.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because the passage indicates that background music without lyrics may enhance the productivity of both people doing work that requires intense focus and people doing repetitive work. However, in saying that background music without lyrics enhances the productivity of people regardless of the type of work they are doing, this choice goes too far. What if people were working on creating music? Would music playing while they were working cause them to be more productive? Probably not.


> <b>(D) Music with lyrics enhances the productivity of people doing repetitive tasks more than music without lyrics does.
</b>


 While one might be tempted to believe that what this choice says is true, the passage does not provide information that we can use to differentiate the effect of music with lyrics from that of music without lyrics.


> <b>(E) Managers interested in maximizing company productivity should be focused on the type of background music played in company workspaces.
</b>


 This choice goes well beyond anything said in the passage. The passage does not provide information that we can use to compare the importance to productivity of background music with the importance of any other factor that could affect productivity. So, it provides no basis for concluding that a manager interested in maximizing productivity should be focused on the type of background music played in company workspaces, rather than on something else.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

Let’s now look at correct answers that are fully supported by small portions of the passages.
