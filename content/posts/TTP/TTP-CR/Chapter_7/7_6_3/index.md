## Lesson Content
A third type of correct answer that we may encounter in an Inference question is an answer that is supported by numerical information located throughout the passage.

Finding the correct answer to such a question may require understanding the implications of a ratio or deciphering the meaning of data presented in percentage terms.

Consider the following example:

> <em>Roberta</em><em>’s Chocolate has always used two ounces of cocoa in making each of its chocolate bars. Recently, the company decided to decrease the size and weight of its chocolate bars. However, the company has continued to include two ounces of cocoa in each bar.</em>

> <em>If the above statements are true, which of the following conclusions can be properly drawn from them?</em>

> <em><u>(Correct Answer</u></em><em>)</em> <em>The ratio of cocoa to the other ingredients in the chocolate bars made by Roberta’s Chocolate has increased.</em>

In saying that Roberta’s Chocolate made the bars smaller but didn’t reduce the amount of cocoa per bar, the passage indicates that the amounts of other ingredients in the bars must have decreased.

Trap answers to questions containing numerical data may offer statements that may seem to follow from the numerical information presented but are not actually supported by what the passage says.

Consider the following example:

> <em>The FrootZoomer juicer company makes two types of juicers, Zoomers, on each of which the company makes a 33 percent gross margin, and SooperZoomers, on each of which the company makes a 41 percent gross margin. Last year’s average gross margin on sales of the two types of juicers was higher than the gross margin on sales of the two types of juicers for the year before last year.</em>

> <em>Which of the following can be properly inferred about FrootZoomer’s juicer sales in the past two years?</em>

> <em>(<u>Trap Choice</u>)</em> <em>FrootZoomer sold more SooperZoomers last year than it sold during the year before last year.</em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>) The ratio of SooperZoomers sold to Zoomers sold last year is greater than the same ratio for the year before last year.</em>

The key to finding the correct answer to this question is seeing that, since the average gross margin increased, the company’s sales must have changed in a way such that the ratio of SooperZoomers to Zoomers increased.

The key to avoiding choosing the trap choice is catching the fact that there are multiple ways in which the ratio of SooperZoomers sold to Zoomers sold could have increased, and that, therefore, while it’s possible that the sales of SooperZoomers increased, it does not have to be the case that sales of SooperZoomers increased.

Ways in which the ratio of SooperZoomers sold to Zoomers sold could have increased include:

> <u>Scenario 1</u>: The number of SooperZoomers sold remained the same, and the number of Zoomers sold decreased.

> <u>Scenario 2</u>: The number of SooperZoomers sold increased, and the number of Zoomers sold remained the same.

> <u>Scenario 3</u>: The number of SooperZoomers sold decreased, and the number of Zoomers sold also decreased, but by a percentage greater than the percentage decrease in SooperZoomer sales.

Yes, Scenario 2 above matches what the trap choice says.

On the other hand, since, for the average gross margin to increase, the ratio of SooperZoomers sold to Zoomers sold has to increase, what the correct answer says must be true.

##  Example 16 

Miracle Walnut Company sells two products – bags of walnuts in their shells, for each of which the company charges $6, and bags of walnuts unshelled, for each of which the company charges $11.

Which of the following is information provided by the statements above?

1. [ ] (A) Compared with Miracle Walnut Company’s total revenue for the year two years ago, the company’s total revenue last year was greater.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Last year the average price Miracle Walnut Company received for a bag of walnuts was greater than the average price it received the year before last.
3. [ ] (C) Last year, Miracle Walnut Company’s competitors sold walnuts in their shells at prices lower than that charged by Miracle Walnut Company for such walnuts.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) Most of Miracle Walnut Company’s customers prefer unshelled walnuts over walnuts in their shells.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Last year, the number of bags of walnuts sold by Miracle Walnut Company was the same as the number it sold during the year before last year.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



Analysis of the Information Given:


<u>Effect of the First Statement</u>: Defines that Miracle Walnut Company sells two products and provides descriptions of the products and the price of each. Products and Prices: Bags of Walnuts in Shells, $6. Bags of Walnuts Unshelled, $11.


<u>Effect of the Second Statement</u>: Defines only the directions of changes in the quantities of the two products sold in each of two years. Last year, fewer bags of walnuts in shells were sold than were in the year before last. Last year more bags of walnuts unshelled were sold than were in the year before last.


To arrive at the correct answer to this question, we’ll have to be careful not to draw unwarranted conclusions from the information given. We know that the quantities sold of the two products changed, and we know the directions of the changes, but we don’t know the magnitudes of the changes. Each of the changes could have been large or small.


> <b>(A) Compared with Miracle Walnut Company’s total revenue for the year two years ago, the company’s total revenue last year was greater.
</b>


If one were to assume that the decrease in the number of bags of walnuts in shells sold matched the increase in the number of bags of unshelled walnuts sold, then one might believe that this statement is true. For example, if the number of bags of walnuts in shells sold decreased by 1000, then revenue decreased by $6000. If the number of bags of unshelled walnuts increased by 1000, revenue increased by $11,000. Since the increase would be greater than the decrease in this scenario, the net change would be an increase in revenue. However, since the passage does not tell us anything about the magnitudes of the decrease and the increase, it does not support this type of calculation. So, it does not support what this choice says.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) Last year the average price Miracle Walnut Company received for a bag of walnuts was greater than the average price it received the year before last.
</b>


 No matter how many bags of walnuts in shells and bags of unshelled walnuts were sold in each of the two years, if the number of bags of walnuts in shells decreased from the first year to the second year, and the number of bags of unshelled walnuts increased, then the average price of a bag of walnuts sold by the company must have increased. To see why this increase in the average price must have occurred, you could consider the following. The first year’s average price would be calculated using a certain number of 6’s and a certain number of 11’s. Then the second year’s average would be based on fewer 6’s and more 11’s, as the number of bags sold for $6 decreased year over year, and the number of bags sold for $11 increased. So, from the first year to the second, the average price would move away from $6 and toward $11.


> <b>(C) Last year, Miracle Walnut Company’s competitors sold walnuts in their shells at prices lower than that charged by Miracle Walnut Company for such walnuts.
</b>


One might take the fact that fewer bags of walnuts in shells were sold last year than were the year before as an indication that competitors sold the same product at a lower price and, thus, took some business from Miracle Walnut Company. However, the passage does not give us a clear reason to believe that this specific factor caused the decrease.


> <b>(D) Most of Miracle Walnut Company’s customers prefer unshelled walnuts over walnuts in their shells.
</b>


One might take the fact that the price charged for a bag of unshelled walnuts is greater than the price charged for a bag of walnuts in their shells as an indication that customers prefer unshelled walnuts. However, it isn’t necessarily the case that customers prefer unshelled walnuts. It could be that the price for unshelled walnuts is higher because they cost more to produce, or for some other reason other than customer preference.


> <b>(E) Last year, the number of bags of walnuts sold by Miracle Walnut Company was the same as the number it sold during the year before last year.
</b>


What this choice says would be true only if the decrease in the number of bags of walnuts in shells sold exactly matched the increase in the number of bags of unshelled walnuts sold. Since the passage does not include information on the magnitudes of the changes in the number of bags sold, the passage does not provide support for the conclusion that the decrease matched the increase.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

##  Example 17 

The number of tourists from Japan who visited Paris during the first half of 2017 was 40.5 percent greater than the number from Japan who visited Paris during the first half of 2016.

Which of the following can be properly concluded from the statements above?

1. [ ] (A) The total number of tourists from Japan and the United Kingdom who visited Paris during the first half of 2017 exceeded the same total for the first half of 2016.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) During the first half of 2017 more people from Japan than from the United Kingdom visited Paris.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) The ratio of tourists from Japan who visited Paris to tourists from the United Kingdom who visited Paris was greater for the first half of 2017 than for the first half of 2016.
4. [ ] (D) Of all tourists who visited Paris, the proportion from Japan was greater in the first half of 2017 than the proportion from Japan was in the first half of 2016.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) The amount of money spent in Paris by tourists from the United Kingdom during the first half of 2017 was less than that spent by such tourists during the first half of 2016.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



Analysis of the Information Given:


<u>Effect of the First Statement</u>: States the percentage difference in the number of tourists from Japan visiting Paris during two time periods, first half of 2016 and first half of 2017. Direction: Increase from the first period to the second period. Numerical Value: 40.5 percent.


<u>Effect of the Second Statement</u>: States the percentage difference in the number of tourists from the United Kingdom visiting Paris during two time periods, first half of 2016 and first half of 2017. Direction: Decrease from the first period to the second period. Numerical Value: 1.7 percent.


To get the correct answer to this question, we have to be careful about the conclusions that we draw, as we have only information on percentages, not information on absolute numbers. So, for instance, even though the percentage change in people visiting from the United Kingdom was much smaller than the percentage change in the number from Japan, for all we know, the numerical change in people visiting from the United Kingdom was much greater than the numerical change in people from Japan.


> <b>(A) The total number of tourists from Japan and the United Kingdom who visited Paris during the first half of 2017 exceeded the same total for the first half of 2016.
</b>


Given that the passage says that the number from Japan increased substantially in percentage terms, while the number from the United Kingdom barely changed, one could be tempted to come to the conclusion that the total number visiting from the two countries increased. However, since the passage does not indicate how the absolute numbers of those visiting from the two countries compare, it does not support this conclusion. Given what the passage says, there could have been many more tourists from the United Kingdom than from Japan. So, a small percentage decrease in the tourists from the United Kingdom could actually represent a large decrease in absolute number terms, one that could outweigh a relatively small absolute number increase in those visiting from Japan.


> <b>(B) During the first half of 2017 more people from Japan than from the United Kingdom visited Paris.
</b>


The statements provide neither absolute numbers of tourists from Japan and the United Kingdom nor any comparison of the numbers of tourists from the two places. Therefore, they don’t support any conclusions about comparisons in absolute numbers of tourists from Japan and the United Kingdom.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) The ratio of tourists from Japan who visited Paris to tourists from the United Kingdom who visited Paris was greater for the first half of 2017 than for the first half of 2016.
</b>


 No matter what the actual numbers of tourists from Japan and the United Kingdom were in the two periods, if, as the passage says, the number from Japan increased from the first period to the second, and the number from the United Kingdom decreased, then the ratio of tourists from Japan to tourists from the United Kingdom must have increased.


> <b>(D) Of all tourists who visited Paris, the proportion from Japan was greater in the first half of 2017 than the proportion from Japan was in the first half of 2016.
</b>


Since the number of tourists from Japan increased by a substantial percentage, it might be tempting to conclude that the proportion of all tourists visiting Paris represented by the tourists from Japan increased. However, for all we know, the total number tourists increased by, for example, 100 percent, in which case the proportion represented by tourists from Japan would have decreased.


> <b>(E) The amount of money spent in Paris by tourists from the United Kingdom during the first half of 2017 was less than that spent by such tourists during the first half of 2016.
</b>


Since the passage does not provide information indicating how much tourists from the United Kingdom spent, it does not support any conclusion about how much they spent during the two time periods. For all we know from what the passage says, while the number of tourists from the United Kingdom decreased, it could be that the average amount spent per tourist from the United Kingdom increased, in which case the total amount spent by tourists from the United Kingdom could have increased rather than decreased.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Example 18 

Last year, Shoes Unlimited, which has always sold only shoes, sold online more shoes than it sold online during any previous year in the company’s history.

If the statements above are true, which of the following can be properly inferred from them?

1. [ ] (A) Last year, Shoes Unlimited’s expenses were greater than its expenses were during any other year in its history.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Last year, Shoes Unlimited sold via brick and mortar stores a larger number of shoes than it sold via brick and mortar stores during any previous year.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Over the last two years, Shoes Unlimited has seen its share of the shoe market decline.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Shoes Unlimited does not sell shoes to its customers via online transactions only.
5. [ ] (E) At the beginning of last year, Shoes Unlimited stopped aggressively marketing its products.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



<u>Fact</u>: Last year, Shoes Unlimited, which has always sold only shoes, sold online more shoes than it sold online during any previous year in the company’s history.


<u>Fact</u>: The average price at which the company sold shoes last year was higher than the average price at which it sold shoes during any previous year.


<u>Fact</u>: The company’s sales revenue for last year was the lowest sales revenue for any year in the company’s history.


In answering this question, we’re seeking a conclusion that MUST be true given the facts presented.


> <b>(A) Last year, Shoes Unlimited’s expenses were greater than its expenses were during any other year in its history.
</b>


Notice that the passage tells us only about the number of shoes the company sold, the prices of the shoes, and the company’s sales revenue. None of that information tells us anything concrete about the company’s expenses. So, this choice is not supported by what the passage says.


> <b>(B) Last year, Shoes Unlimited sold via brick and mortar stores a larger number of shoes than it sold via brick and mortar stores during any previous year.
</b>


The passage provides information only on Shoes Unlimited’s online sales of shoes.


Furthermore, the passage says that the company sold online more shoes than it sold online during any previous year in the company’s history, and that the average price at which the company sold shoes last year was higher than the average price at which it sold shoes during any previous year, but the company’s sale revenue for last year was the lowest sales revenue for any year in the company’s history.


If, even though the company sold a record high number of shoes online and sold its shoes at a record high average price, the company’s sales revenue was at a record low, the company must have seen a decrease in revenue from sales other than online sales, such as sales in brick and mortar stores.


So, while, given what the passage says, we don’t have sufficient information to be completely certain that, last year, the company sold via brick and mortar stores fewer shoes than it sold via brick and mortar stores during any previous year, the passage certainly does not support the conclusion that last year the company sold via brick and mortar stores a larger of number shoes than it sold via brick and mortar stores during any previous year.


> <b>(C) Over the last two years, Shoes Unlimited has seen its share of the shoe market decline.
</b>


The passage provides information only on Shoes Unlimited’s sales. It does not provide any information on the shoe market as a whole or any information on Shoes Unlimited’s share of the market. So, the passage provides no support for what this choice says.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D) Shoes Unlimited does not sell shoes to its customers via online transactions only.
</b>


We know that (Number of Units Sold) x (Price) = (Total Sales Revenue).


The passage says that Shoes Unlimited sold online more shoes than it sold online during any previous year in the company’s history.


The passage says also that the average price at which the company sold shoes last year was higher than the average price at which it sold shoes during any previous year.


Of course, if the company sells shoes online only, then the information we have would lead the following results for last year:


(Most Shoes Ever Sold) x (Highest Ever Average Price) = (Highest Ever Total Sales Revenue)


However, the passage tells us that the company’s sales revenue for last year was the <em>lowest</em> sales revenue for any year in the company’s history.


In order for the company’s sales revenue for last year to have been the lowest sales revenue for any year in the company’s history, the company must have seen a reduction in revenue somewhere, and we know that, if the company sold shoes online only, it would have had its highest ever sales revenue last year.


So, given what the passage says, we can tell that the company must sell shoes in ways other than via online transactions.


Thus, what this choice says, that Shoes Unlimited does not sell shoes to its customers via online transactions only, must be true.


> <b>(E) At the beginning of last year, Shoes Unlimited stopped aggressively marketing its products.
</b>


While the passage says that Shoes Unlimited’s sales revenue decreased, it provides no information, such as information on Shoes Unlimited’s marketing, on why the company’s sales revenue decreased. So, the passage does not support what this choice says.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

##  Example 19 

During each of the past two years, Bonanvia consumed 50 percent of the oranges that it produced that year and exported the other 50 percent, either in the form of whole fruit or in the form of orange juice.

If the statements above are true, which of the following must also be true?

1. [ ] (A) Last year, Bonanvia consumed less than 50 percent of the orange juice that it produced.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Last year, the average price of oranges in Bonanvia was higher than it was the year before.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Last year, Bonanvia exported a smaller quantity of oranges in the form of whole fruit than it exported during the year before.
4. [ ] (D) It is more profitable for Bonanvian orange producers to export orange juice than to export oranges in the form of whole fruit.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Bonanvian orange producers’ revenue from exported orange juice last year was the same as the revenue from exported orange juice the year before.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



Analysis of the Information Given:


<u>Effect of the First Statement</u>: Establishes that, during each of the past two years, Bonanvia consumed 50 percent of the oranges that it produced that year and exported the other 50 percent.


<u>Effect of the Second Statement</u>: Defines the two ways in which Bonanvia consumed and exported oranges – in the form of whole oranges and in the form of orange juice.


<u>Effect of the Third Statement</u>: Compares the orange production of last year with the orange production of the year before – last year’s production was 15 percent lower than that of the year before.


<u>Effect of the Fourth Statement</u>: Makes clear that the same amount of orange juice was exported in each of the two years.


In order to answer this question correctly, we have to be careful not to distort the information presented in the passage or to make unwarranted assumptions. Given the fact that there are a variety of interrelated numerical facts in the passage, it would be easy to arrive at an erroneous conclusion.


For instance, we have to be careful not to take the fact that Bonanvia consumed 50 percent of the oranges that it produced each year and exported the other 50 percent as indicating that it is also the case that whole oranges and orange juice each represented 50 percent of the oranges consumed and exported.


Overall, we have to be very careful and organized in our thinking in order to arrive at a conclusion that is truly supported by what the passage says.


> <b>(A) Last year, Bonanvia consumed less than 50 percent of the orange juice that it produced.
</b>


 This answer is very tempting. Since the passage says that orange production was lower last year than it was the year before, that Bonanvia exported 50 percent of the oranges that it produced each year, and that Bonanvia exported the same amount of juice that it had exported the year before, we might be tempted to conclude that last year Bonanvia exported more than half of the juice that it produced. But, wait a minute. We really don’t know how much juice was exported either year. All we know is that, each year, Bonanvia exported half of the oranges that it produced, either in the form of whole oranges or in the form of juice. For all we know, Bonanvia exported no juice two years ago and no juice last year, all the while making juice out of all the oranges that it did not export. So, we can’t conclude that Bonanvia consumed less than half of the juice it produced last year.


> <b>(B) Last year, the average price of oranges in Bonanvia was higher than it was the year before.
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because, since we know that the quantity of oranges produced last year was smaller than the quantity produced the year before, we might be tempted to conclude that price of oranges increased. However, even though it is plausible that a price increase would have occurred, the passage does not provide information that we can use to confirm that a price increase occurred.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) Last year, Bonanvia exported a smaller quantity of oranges in the form of whole fruit than it exported during the year before.
</b>


 The total quantity of oranges produced last year was smaller than the quantity produced the year before. Meanwhile, each year, Bonanvia exported half of the oranges it produced that year. Half of a smaller quantity is smaller than half of a larger quantity. So, the quantity of oranges exported in the form of whole oranges and juice last year was smaller than the quantity exported the year before.


Since the quantity of juice exported last year was the same as the quantity of juice exported the year before, the quantity of whole oranges exported last year must have been smaller than the quantity exported the year before. If the quantity of oranges exported were not smaller, then the total quantity of juice and oranges exported last year would not be smaller than the quantity exported the year before, and we know that the total quantity was, in fact, smaller.


> <b>(D) It is more profitable for Bonanvian orange producers to export orange juice than to export oranges in the form of whole fruit.
</b>


 This cannot be concluded from what the passage says. The passage does not give us any indication that it is more profitable to export orange juice.


> <b>(E) Bonanvian orange producers’ revenue from exported orange juice last year was the same as the revenue from exported orange juice the year before.
</b>


 This choice is tempting, because the passage says that, last year, Bonanvia exported the same amount of orange juice that it exported the year before. However, the price of orange juice could have change from one year to the next, and Revenue = Quantity x Price. So, since the passage does not tell us anything about the prices of orange juice in the two years, even though it provides information on the quantity of orange juice exported, it does not support any conclusion about revenue from exported orange juice.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

Let’s now discuss correct answers that are supported by mathematical information located in small portions of the passages.
