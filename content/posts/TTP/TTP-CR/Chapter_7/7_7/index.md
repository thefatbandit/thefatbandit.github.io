## Lesson Content
As is the case with other types of Critical Reasoning questions, the test-makers can increase the difficulty of Inference questions by using the word “EXCEPT” or “not” in question stems.

<em>If the statements above are true, all of the following can be properly inferred from them EXCEPT:</em>

or

<em>Given that the statements above are all true, which of the following is <u>not</u> an inference that can be properly made on the basis of them?</em>

When an Inference question stem uses the word “EXCEPT” or “not,” four of the five answers will be inferences that can be made given the information in the passage.

Meanwhile, since, in most cases, they wouldn’t want to make it too easy to find the correct answer by giving you a choice that is clearly untrue, the correct answers to such questions tend to be possibly, but not necessarily, true.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When an Inference Question uses the word “EXCEPT” or “not,” four of the five answers must be true. These four answers will be incorrect answers.
{{< /alert >}}

In these types of questions, the premises in the stimulus tend to make heavy use of words like “<em>sometimes</em>,” “<em>always</em>,” and “<em>never</em>.” They create conditions that must be met by the statements.

This concept might be easier to conceptualize with an example:

> <em>Animal Shelter Director: In our animal shelter we have only dogs and cats. None of our dogs is over 2 years old but many of our cats are.</em>

> <em>All of the following statements must be true EXCEPT:</em>

Remember, for a must be true (Inference) EXCEPT question, four of the answers will definitely be true, and these answers are <em>incorrect</em>.

Let’s consider a few possible answers:

> <em>All the dogs are at most 2 years old</em>

This answer <em>must be true.</em> If <em>none </em>of the dogs is over 2 years old, then all of them must be 2 years old or younger.

> <em>No dog is older than the oldest cat</em>

This answer <em>must be true</em>.

> <em>No cat is younger than the youngest dog</em>

This answer <em>could be true </em>or <em>could be false</em>.

Pay attention to the words as you analyze the answers, because the correct answer may well be a distortion of a sentence containing similar words.

The essential technique for this type of question is the negation technique: ask yourself whether it is possible for all the information in the stem to be true and for each answer to be false.

In fact, the correct answer in these question types may in fact contain any of the traps discussed earlier in the chapter.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In EXCEPT or not Inference questions, the correct answer will be the one that must be false or could be true. The four incorrect answers all must be true .
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following examples.

##  Example 23 

Delivereez and Boxrunners are the only two delivery services in town.

If the statements above are true, which of the following is <u>not</u> an inference that can be made on the basis of them?

1. [ ] (A) A large package delivered uptown by van was delivered by Boxrunners.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) A small package delivered to midtown by bicycle may have been delivered by Delivereez.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) A large package delivered to midtown by van was delivered by Delivereez.
4. [ ] (D) A medium sized package delivered to midtown by pedicab was delivered by Delivereez.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) A large package delivered downtown by van was not delivered by Boxrunners.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: medium



The stimulus to this question seems complicated and contains many facts. However, if we are organized and methodical, we can efficiently go through the answers to find the one that does not have to be true.


Facts Presented:


- There are only two delivery services in town, Delivereez and Boxrunners.


- Delivereez:


Bicycles – Small Packages


Pedicabs – Medium Packages


Vans – Large Packages


Delivers to: Downtown, Midtown


- Boxrunners:


Bicycles – Small Packages


Vans – Package of All Other Sizes


Delivers to: Midtown, Uptown


> <b>(A) A large package delivered uptown by van was delivered by Boxrunners.
</b>


 Of the only two delivery services, Boxrunners is the only one that delivers to uptown.


Must be true? Yes.


> <b>(B) A small package delivered to midtown by bicycle may have been delivered by Delivereez.
</b>


 Delivereez delivers to midtown and uses bicycles to deliver small packages.


Must be true? Yes.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) A large package delivered to midtown by van was delivered by Delivereez.
</b>


 Both services deliver to midtown, and both use vans for large packages. So, either one could have delivered a large package to midtown by van.


Must be true? No.


> <b>(D) A medium sized package delivered to midtown by pedicab was delivered by Delivereez.
</b>


 Of the only two delivery services in town, the only one that delivers by pedicab is Delivereez.


Must be true? Yes.


> <b>(E) A large package delivered downtown by van was not delivered by Boxrunners.
</b>


 Only Delivereez delivers to downtown.


Must be true? Yes.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Example 24 

Last year, Manufacturer W sold more units than it sold during the previous year, but its total revenue was the same as its total revenue for the previous year.

All of the following can be properly inferred from the information presented above EXCEPT:

1. [ ] (A) The average price per unit sold by Manufacturer W last year was lower than the average price per unit sold the previous year.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) The ratio of Manufacturer T’s profits to Manufacturer W’s profits for last year was greater than the same ratio for the year before.
3. [ ] (C) The ratio of the number of units sold by Manufacturer W to the number of units sold by Manufacturer T was greater last year than it was two years ago.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) The ratio of Manufacturer T’s revenue to Manufacturer W’s revenue was greater last year than it was two years ago.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Manufacturer T received last year an average price for each of its units sold higher than that received in the previous year.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



<u>Fact 1</u>: Manufacturer W’s total number of units sold increased.


<u>Fact 2</u>: Manufacturer W’s revenue remained the same.


<u>Fact 3</u>: Manufacturer T’s total number of units sold decreased.


<u>Fact 4</u>: Manufacturer T’s revenue increased.


What must be true?


From the information presented, we can make various inferences about the prices and sales of Manufacturer W and Manufacturer T.


In order to work with these statements, we must understand that total revenue is the product of number of units sold and price charged per unit.


U x P = R


So, if, for instance, U increases, and P increases, then R increases.


If U decreases, and P decreases, then R decreases.


If U remains the same, and P increases, R will increase even though U didn’t.


It is also the case that, if R has increased, but U has not changed, then we know that P must have increased.


On the other hand, if R has remained the same while U has increased, then P must have decreased.


So, for instance, since Manufacturer W’s units sold increased while Manufacturer W’s revenue remained the same, then the average price per unit sold by Manufacturer W must have decreased.


> <b>(A) The average price per unit sold by Manufacturer W last year was lower than the average price per unit sold the previous year.
</b>


As outlined above, since the number of units sold by Manufacturer W increased while Manufacturer W’s revenue remained the same, then the average price per unit sold by Manufacturer W must have decreased.


Must be true? Yes.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) The ratio of Manufacturer T’s profits to Manufacturer W’s profits for last year was greater than the same ratio for the year before.
</b>


This choice could seem to be true, as we know that Manufacturer T’s revenue increased last year while Manufacturer W’s revenue remained the same. However, revenue and profit, while related, are not the same thing. Profit is revenue minus cost. So, with only revenue information we have no way to make this inference about profits.


Must be true? No.


> <b>(C) The ratio of the number of units sold by Manufacturer W to the number of units sold by Manufacturer T was greater last year than it was two years ago.
</b>


Since we know that the number of units sold by Manufacturer W increased, while the number of units sold by Manufacturer T decreased, we can infer that the ratio of units sold by Manufacturer W to units sold by Manufacturer T increased, as, when we increase the number on the top of a ratio and decreased the number on the bottom of the ratio, we increase the ratio.


Must be true? Yes.


> <b>(D) The ratio of Manufacturer T’s revenue to Manufacturer W’s revenue was greater last year than it was two years ago.
</b>


Since we know that Manufacturer T’s revenue increased, while Manufacturer W’s revenue remained the same, we can infer that the ratio of Manufacturer T’s revenue to Manufacturer W’s revenue must have increased, as, when we increase the number on the top of a ratio and don’t change the number on the bottom of the ratio, we increase the ratio.


Must be true? Yes.


(E)<strong> Incorrect. </strong>If Manufacturer T was able to increase its revenue even as its sales declined, it must have received a higher price for each unit it sold. Remember, revenue is the product of number of products sold and price charged per product. If unit sales declined, yet revenue increased, then the price must have increased.


Must be true? Yes.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
