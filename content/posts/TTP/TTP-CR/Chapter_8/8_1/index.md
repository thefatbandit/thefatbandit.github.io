## Lesson Content
Many of the Critical Reasoning questions that we’ve examined up to this point have introduced arguments that contain clearly-stated conclusions.

Both names are fitting since these questions ask us to find the author’s logical conclusion or main point.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering Find the Conclusion/Main Point questions, our job is to select an answer choice that best captures the author’s conclusion, the main point of the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 1 

Once the root cause of an illness has been fully addressed, a person who has been experiencing that illness will no longer experience it.

If the statements above are true, which of the following is most strongly supported by them?

1. [ ] (A) Stress management counseling has no effect on people experiencing illness.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Some people experiencing illness become healthier without any treatment.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) There is no way to address the root causes of certain illnesses.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) In some cases, stress management counseling does not fully address the root cause of an illness.
5. [ ] (E) Stress is the sole cause of all forms of illness.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The argument in this question has three premises:


<u>Premise 1</u>: Once the root cause of an illness has been fully addressed, a person who has been experiencing that illness will no longer experience it.


<u>Premise 2</u>: In some cases, weekly stress management counseling will cause the progression of an illness to cease.


<u>Premise 3</u>: Often in such cases, once people experiencing illness stop undergoing the counseling, the progression will recommence.


What conclusion do these premises support?


They start off by telling us that, once the root cause of an illness is fully addressed, the person experiencing the illness will no longer experience it. Then they tell us that, while stress management counseling may cause the progression of an illness to cease, once the counseling is no longer going on, in some cases, the progression recommences.


Thus, the premises support the conclusion that, in at least some cases, stress management counseling does not fully address the root cause of an illness.


> <b>(A) Stress management counseling has no effect on people experiencing illness.
</b>


 This answer choice conflicts with what the passage says. The premises don’t support the conclusion that stress management counseling has no effect at all. Actually, they indicate that stress management does have some effects, though the effects may be somewhat temporary in some cases.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) Some people experiencing illness become healthier without any treatment.
</b>


 This answer choice states a conclusion with no support in the premises. They don’t say anything about people getting healthier without any treatment.


> <b>(C) There is no way to address the root causes of certain illnesses.
</b>


 What this choice says goes too far. The fact that stress management counseling may not always fully address the root causes of illnesses does not mean that there is no way to address the root causes of certain illnesses.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(D) In some cases, stress management counseling does not fully address the root cause of an illness.
</b>


 As discussed above, the first premise of the argument says that, when the root cause of an illness has been fully addressed, a person who has been experiencing that illness will no longer experience it. Thus, since, in some cases, when a person stops undergoing stress management counseling, the progression of an illness that the person has been experiencing recommences, the premises support the conclusion that, in some cases, stress management counseling does not fully address the root causes of illnesses.


> <b>(E) Stress is the sole cause of all forms of illness.
</b>


 This choice goes well beyond what the passage supports. The fact that, in some cases, stress management counseling causes the progression of an illness to cease, does not, on its own, support the conclusion that stress is the sole cause of all forms of illness.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
