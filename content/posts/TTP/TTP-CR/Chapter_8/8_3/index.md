## Lesson Content
As there are for answering Critical Reasoning questions of other types, there are best practices that we can follow when answering Find the Conclusion questions.

<u>Step 1 </u><u>– </u><u>Carefully Read and Completely Understand the Passage</u>

The foundation of choosing the right answer to a Conclusion question is understanding the passage.

<u>Step 2 – Identify the Question Type (What type of information is the question asking for?)</u>

Read the question stem to determine what role the correct answer to the question must play.

> <em>“Which of the following most logically concludes the argument?”</em>

<u>Step 3 – Carefully Analyze the Argument</u>

Once you’ve determined that you’re working on a Find the Conclusion question, your next job is to analyze the argument presented in the stimulus.

<u>Step 4 – Eliminate Wrong Answers</u>

Once we have analyzed the argument, we go through the answer choices, carefully asking, “<em>Does this answer follow logically from the statements, and is it the best possible conclusion to the argument?” </em>Use the obvious to your advantage: there can only be one correct answer choice.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Use the obvious to your advantage: there can be only one correct answer choice. One of the answers will be the best conclusion to the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

In a Find the Conclusion question, there are usually two, perhaps even three, answer choices that we can eliminate relatively quickly because they make statements that are too general, focus on only one sentence of the argument, make extreme logical leaps, conflict with all or part of the argument, or simply say something that is relatively obviously not supported by the passage.

It’s worth writing “A, B, C, D, E” on your dry erase pad.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

To avoid eliminating an answer choice only mistakenly to choose it as correct, write out “A, B, C, D, E” on your dry erase pad. As you eliminate the choices that are obviously wrong, physically cross them out.
{{< /alert >}}

Now – as you already know, one of the most common mistakes that people make is to eliminate the correct answer in their initial assessment of the answer choices.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

One of the most common mistakes that people make is to eliminate the correct answer in their initial assessment of the answer choices. So, even after you’ve crossed off an answer, you must stay open to the fact that you may have crossed off the correct answer. Always be willing to reassess the situation.
{{< /alert >}}

<u>Step 5 – Carefully Analyze the Final Answer Choice</u><u>s</u>

Of the answers remaining, the correct answer must do the best job of capturing the author’s logical conclusion to the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In a Find the Conclusion question, the correct answer will be the choice that most logically follows from the specific evidence provided in the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

At this point, one fundamental error that many people make is to simply re-read the two final choices and rely on “gut instinct” as to which is correct.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When you are down to two answers, you must not become complacent and make your best guess. No way. Now is the time to shine, to turn the jets to overdrive, and to relentlessly seek out the correct answer.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 4 

English Dictionary Editor: Many grammarians have recently stated that our dictionary should not have changed the definition of “they” to allow it to be used as a gender neutral singular pronoun on the grounds that using it in such a way is grammatically incorrect.

Which of the following most logically concludes the dictionary editor’s argument?

1. [ ] (A) Contrary to the grammarians’ claim, the dictionary’s new definition of “they” is not grammatically incorrect.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) Many other languages have a gender neutral, singular pronoun that is similar to the new definition of “they.”
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Grammarians eventually accept most changes to the ways in which words are used as grammatically correct.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) The grammarians were incorrect to claim that the dictionary should not have changed the definition of “they.”
5. [ ] (E) All dictionaries should change their definitions of “they” to allow it to be a gender neutral, singular pronoun.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

##### Question Level: hard



Let’s use the five-step process on this question.


<u>Step 1 </u><u>– </u><u>Carefully Read and Completely Understand the Passage</u>


At this point we have carefully read and understood what the passage says.


<u>Step 2 – Identify the Question Type</u>


The question stem asks: “<em>Which of the following most logically concludes the dictionary editor’s argument?</em>” We clearly see that we’re working with a Find the Conclusion question.


<u>Step 3 – Carefully Analyze the Argument</u>


<u>Statement One</u>: Many grammarians have recently stated that our dictionary should not have changed the definition of “they” to allow it to be used as a gender neutral singular pronoun on the grounds that using it in such a way is grammatically incorrect. – This statement describes the position of many grammarians.


<u>Statement Two</u>: Many English speakers now use “they” in such a manner. – This statement does not conflict directly with what the grammarians say but provides new information on the use of “they” as a singular pronoun.


<u>Statement Three</u>: The purpose of our dictionary is to reflect language as speakers currently use it. – This statement provides information about the dictionary and is related to what Statement Two says about how many English speakers use “they.”


Of course, the author has stopped short of providing us a conclusion.


Remember that the correct answer will be the one that fits the best after the word “therefore.”


<em>English Dictionary Editor: Many grammarians have recently stated that our dictionary should not have changed the definition of “they” to allow it to be used as a gender neutral singular pronoun on the grounds that using it in such a way is grammatically incorrect. However, many English speakers now use “they” in such a manner, and the purpose of our dictionary is to reflect language as speakers currently use it. <strong>Therefore,</strong></em>


<u>Step 4 – Eliminate the Wrong Answers</u>


Let’s look at each of the answer choices:


<strong>(A) Contrary to the grammarians’ claim, the dictionary’s new definition of “they” is not grammatically incorrect.</strong>


This answer seems to reflect the implications of the editor’s Statement Two. If, even though grammarians consider using “they” as a singular pronoun grammatically incorrect, many people use “they” in that way, maybe the author’s conclusion is that using “they” in that way is not actually grammatically incorrect. <strong>Keep while you look at the other answers.</strong>


<strong>(B) Many other languages have a gender neutral, singular pronoun that is similar to the new definition of “they.”</strong>


This is extra information. It neither logically follows from the statements nor synthesizes them. While it might aid the editor’s argument, it cannot be the conclusion, because the statements do not support a conclusion about word use in various languages. <strong>Eliminate this choice.</strong>


<strong>(C) Grammarians eventually accept most changes to the ways in which words are used as grammatically correct.</strong>


This answer might also strengthen the editor’s argument. However, we are not looking for a strengthener, but rather for a conclusion, and this statement does not logically follow from the statements in the passage. While it is possible that grammarians eventually change their minds about uses of words, the statements in the passage don’t lead to that conclusion. <strong>We can eliminate this choice.</strong>


<strong>(D) The grammarians were incorrect to claim that the dictionary should not have changed the definition of “they.”</strong>


This answer seems to follow from what the editor says. The editor’s says that people use “they” as a singular pronoun and that the purpose of dictionary is to reflect language as speakers currently use it. These statements are evidence support the dictionary’s changing its definition of “they.” So, they also support the conclusion that the grammarians were incorrect to claim that the dictionary should not have changed the definition. <strong>Let’s keep this one as well.</strong>


<strong>(E) All dictionaries should change their definitions of “they” to allow it to be a gender neutral, singular pronoun.</strong>


This choice goes beyond what the editor’s argument supports. The editor’s argument gives us information on only the editor’s own dictionary, not other dictionaries. For all we know, in determining how to define words, other dictionaries use guidelines different from those used by the dictionary mentioned in the passage. <strong>We can eliminate this choice.</strong>


<u>Step 5 – Carefully Analyze the Final Answer Choices</u>


Now that we are down to two answers, let’s carefully look at them to figure how they are different.


<strong>(A) Contrary to the grammarians’ claim, the dictionary’s new definition of “they” is not grammatically incorrect.</strong>


This answer focuses on whether the new definition of “they” is grammatically correct. The editor’s argument was focused on how the word “they” is actually used by speakers, not on the rules of grammar. This answer does not, therefore, follow from the statements. Let’s look at the other choice we kept.


<strong>(D) The grammarians were incorrect to claim that the dictionary should not have changed the definition of “they.”</strong>


This answer focuses on whether the dictionary was correct in changing the definition of “they.” The dictionary’s purpose is stated to be “to reflect language as speakers currently use it” and many people use “they” in the way that new definition describes. Therefore, it is logical to conclude from the editor’s argument that the dictionary was correct in its decision to change the definition, and that, therefore, the grammarians were incorrect in saying that the dictionary should not have changed the definition. This answer logically follows from the statements and is therefore correct.


Notice that this statement fits very well after “therefore.”


<em>English Dictionary Editor: Many grammarians have recently stated that our dictionary should not have changed the definition of “they” to allow it to be used as a gender neutral singular pronoun, on the grounds that using it in such a way is grammatically incorrect. However, many English speakers now use “they” in such a manner, and the purpose of our dictionary is to reflect language as speakers currently use it. <strong>Therefore, the grammarians were incorrect to claim that the dictionary should not have changed the definition of “they.”</strong></em>


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
