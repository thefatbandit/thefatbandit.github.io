## Lesson Content
As we have been discussing how to choose the correct answers to various types of Critical Reasoning questions, we have discussed the importance of not making up unsupported, convoluted stories in order to connect answer choices to passages.

Find the Conclusion questions turn the process around, by including incorrect answer choices that are unsupported stories that are related to what the premises say in convoluted ways.

To make you better prepared to tell the difference between unsupported stories and supported conclusions, let’s now go over some reasons why incorrect answer choices that appear in Find the Conclusion questions are not supported by the stimuli.

> - It requires the support of information that is not included in the stimulus.

> - It extends beyond what the statements in the stimulus actually say, i.e., it goes too far.

> - It is loosely related to what the statements say yet states a conclusion that is not actually supported by them.

> - It conflicts in some way with information that appears in the passage.

In order not to choose an answer choice that is wrong for one of the reasons described above, you have to notice:

> - key details.

> - the extent of what statements say.

> - the nature of what the statements say.

> - the subject matter of the information provided by the statements.

Let’s examine each of these reasons why choices are incorrect in greater detail.
