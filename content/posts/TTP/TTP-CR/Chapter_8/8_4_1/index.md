## Lesson Content
In Critical Reasoning questions of some types, such as Weaken the Argument questions or Strengthen the Argument questions, the correct answer will contain new information that is not found in the passage.

However, in a Find the Conclusion question, all of the information needed to answer the question must already be included in the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In a Find the Conclusion question, an answer that depends on outside information will be incorrect.
{{< /alert >}}

Further, as is the case in Inference questions, some incorrect answers to Find the Conclusion questions may very well be true and logical in real life yet not supported by the information provided in the passage.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Some answer choices in Find the Conclusion questions express things that may be true and logical in real life yet are not supported by the information provided in the passage. Such an answer will always be incorrect in a Find the Conclusion question.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s look at a basic example.

> <em>The most common treatment for headaches is some form of medicinal pain reliever, such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen, the use of either of which is usually effective in relieving headaches. In a recent study, however, a simple routine of stretching and light exercise was found to be more effective in reducing headaches than is either of these medications.</em>

> <em>If the statements above are true, they most strongly support which of the following conclusions?</em>

> <em>(A) Doctors recommend that people with certain medical conditions avoid taking acetaminophen due to potential negative side effects.</em>

> <em>(B) Headaches often result from psychological factors such as stress or anxiety.</em>

> <em>(C) In most cases, even people who, because of negative reactions, cannot use medicinal pain relievers will be able to solve headaches.</em>

Let’s break the argument down.

> <u>Statement 1</u>: The most common treatment for headaches is some form of medicinal pain reliever such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen.

> <u>Statement 2</u>: The use of ibuprofen or acetaminophen is usually effective in relieving headaches.

> <u>Statement 3</u>: A recent study found that a simple routine of stretching and light exercise was more effective at reducing headaches than is either of these medications.

What would a proper conclusion look like here? Such a conclusion would have to build upon these statements and add a synthesis.

> <em>The most common treatment for headaches is some form of medicinal pain reliever such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen, the use of either of which is usually effective in relieving headaches. In a recent study, however, a simple routine of stretching and light exercise was found to be more effective in reducing headaches than is either of these medications. <strong>Therefore,</strong></em>

An answer that depends on information not included in the statements will be incorrect.

> <em>(A) </em><strong><em>Incorrect.</em></strong><em> Doctors recommend that people with certain medical conditions avoid taking acetaminophen due to potential negative side effects.</em>

This answer adds new information that is not directly supported by the statements.

> <em>(B) </em><strong><em>Incorrect.</em></strong><em> Headaches often result from psychological factors such as stress or anxiety.</em>

This answer is a little more tempting than choice (A), because the fact that headaches can be solved via stretching and exercise could be perceived as indicating that headaches result from psychological factors.

> <em>(C) </em><strong><em>Correct.</em></strong><em> In most cases, even people who, because of negative reactions, cannot use medicinal pain relievers will be able to solve headaches.</em>

This answer adds an interpretation that builds directly upon the statements without introducing any new supporting information.

Notice that the conclusion would fit very well after “therefore.”

> <em>The most common treatment for headaches is some form of medicinal pain reliever such as ibuprofen or acetaminophen, the use of either of which is usually effective in relieving headaches. In a recent study, however, a simple routine of stretching and light exercise was found to be more effective in reducing headaches than is either of these medications. <strong>Therefore, in most cases, even people who, because of negative reactions, cannot use medicinal pain relievers will be able to solve headaches.</strong></em>

##  Example 6 

Over the past two decades, manufacturing capacity in Vesornia decreased by over 70 percent, as companies moved their manufacturing operations to other countries and, consequently, 70 percent of manufacturing jobs in Vesornia were lost.

If the statements above are true, which of the following predictions is most strongly supported by them?

1. [x] (A) The number of manufacturing jobs in Vesornia is not likely to return to its previous level in the near future.
2. [ ] (B) Becoming employed in a new manufacturing facility in Vesornia will be more difficult, on average, than was getting such a job in the past.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) The rate of unemployment in Vesornia will not change significantly in the short to medium term.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) The new manufacturing facilities in Vesornia will not produce products that are in the same categories as the products produced in the country in the past.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) The new manufacturing facilities in Vesornia will be more profitable for their owners than were manufacturing facilities in Vesornia in the past.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

##### Question Level: easy



The passage provides the following information:


<u>Statement 1</u>: Manufacturing capacity in Vesornia decreased by over 70 percent.


<u>Statement 2</u>: 70 percent of manufacturing jobs in Vesornia were lost.


<u>Statement 3</u>: Policies are likely to cause the country’s manufacturing capacity to return to its previous level.


<u>Statement 4</u>: Most of the new manufacturing facilities will be much more heavily automated than those that existed before the decline.


The correct answer will follow from these statements only. Notice, the statements mention only manufacturing capacity, manufacturing jobs, policies, and automation. A conclusion that depends on information other than the limited information provided by the passage will not be correct.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) The number of manufacturing jobs in Vesornia is not likely to return to its previous level in the near future.
</b>


 Manufacturing capacity in Vesornia is expected to return to its previous level. However, the new facilities will be much more heavily automated than manufacturing facilities in the country were previously, meaning that, operating the new facilities will require fewer employees per unit of capacity than did operating the facilities the existed in Vesornia in the past. Thus, even though capacity may return to its previous level, the number manufacturing jobs is not likely to return to its previous level in the near future.


> <b>(B) Becoming employed in a new manufacturing facility in Vesornia will be more difficult, on average, than was getting such a job in the past.
</b>


While the passage indicates that the new facilities will be more heavily automated than manufacturing facilities in Vesornia were in the past, arriving at the conclusion that it will be more difficult to get a manufacturing job than it was in the past requires information other than that provided in the passage. We don’t know how much competition for jobs there is, or what changes in manufacturing or employment in Vesornia might have occurred that would affect how difficult it will be to find employment in manufacturing.


> <b>(C) The rate of unemployment in Vesornia will not change significantly in the short to medium term.
</b>


 Arriving at a conclusion about Vesornia’s rate of unemployment requires information beyond what is provided by the passage. There are many factors that could affect the rate of unemployment in the country, and the passage mentions only a few specifics about manufacturing capacity and automation.


> <b>(D) The new manufacturing facilities in Vesornia will not produce products that are in the same categories as the products produced in the country in the past.
</b>


 While what this choice says could very well be true, the only difference between the previously existing facilities and new facilities that the passage mentions is the degree of automation. Information on the degree of automation provides little to no support for a conclusion about the categories of products that will be produced. To arrive at this conclusion, we would need information other than what the passage provides.


> <b>(E) The new manufacturing facilities in Vesornia will be more profitable for their owners than were manufacturing facilities in Vesornia in the past.
</b>


 This choice could be very tempting, because the passage says that the new facilities will be much more heavily automated than were the manufacturing facilities that existed in Vesornia in the past, and automation could result in greater efficiency and greater profitability, all other factors being held equal. However, the truth is that determining whether the new facilities will be more profitable requires information beyond how automated they will be, information such as how much competition there is.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
