## Lesson Content
As we’ve discussed in past chapters, when we read information, we may put our own spin on the statements that we are reading.

For instance, a passage might support the conclusion that a company “will become more profitable,” and an incorrect answer choice will say that the company “will become the most profitable company in its sector.” In this case, the incorrect answer choice goes too far when it says that the company will be the “most profitable,” as the passage supports only the conclusion that the company will become “more profitable.”

A passage could support the conclusion that a method that a person could use to get healthier when experiencing a certain type of illness is “very effective,” and an incorrect answer choice could say that “nobody will experience that type of illness ever again.” The conclusion that “nobody will experience that type of illness ever again” is beyond what is supported.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Find the Conclusion questions, some answer choices are incorrect because what they say extends past what is actually supported by the passages.
{{< /alert >}}

These answers can be extremely tempting because they can seem to be supported by what we just read.

Notice the differences between the extent of what the stimulus supports and what the answer choices say in the following example:

> <em>It’s easy to get the impression that someone who performs at a high level in a field has a special talent. The truth is, however, that people who perform at the top level in any field generally have engaged in consistent training to attain their levels of skill and, further, that anyone who trains consistently, using effective methods, will experience skill development as a result.</em>

> <em>If the statements above are all true, which of the following is most strongly supported by them?</em>

> <em>(A) Anyone can easily train to become as proficient in a field as those who perform at the top level in that field.</em>

> <em>(B) All differences in skill levels can be correctly attributed to differences in the amounts of training in which people have engaged.</em>

> <em>(C) Differences in people’s skill levels that have been attributed to differences in degrees of talent may actually be the results of differences in amounts of consistent training.</em>

Let’s break the argument down.

> <u>Statement 1</u>: It’s easy to get the impression that someone who performs at a high level in a field has a special talent.<em> </em>

> <u>Statement 2</u>: People who perform at the top level in any field generally have engaged in consistent training to attain their levels of skill.

> <u>Statement 3</u>: Anyone who trains consistently, using effective methods, will experience skill development as a result.

The correct answer is the one that presents a conclusion synthesized from the statements and does not go beyond what is supported by them.

Let’s look at each of the three answer choices:

> <em>(A) </em><strong><em>Incorrect.</em></strong><em> Anyone can easily train to become as proficient in a field as those who perform at the top level in that field.</em>

This answer goes beyond what is supported by the premises.

> <em>(B) </em><strong><em>Incorrect.</em></strong><em> All differences in skill levels can be correctly attributed to differences in the amounts of training in which people have engaged.</em>

Like choice (A), this choice goes beyond what is supported by the passage.

> <em>(C) </em><strong><em>Correct.</em></strong><em> Differences in people’s skill levels that have been attributed to differences in degrees of talent may actually be the results of differences in amounts of consistent training.</em>

This answer is a conclusion that follows directly from the statements and does not go beyond what they actually support.

> <em>It’s easy to get the impression that someone who performs at a high level in a field has a special talent. The truth is, however, that people who perform at the top level in any field generally have engaged in consistent training to attain their levels of skill and, further, that anyone who trains consistently, using effective methods, will experience skill development as a result. <strong>Therefore, differences in people’s skill levels that have been attributed to differences in degrees of talent may actually be the results of differences in amounts of consistent training.</strong></em>

##  Example 7 

Under normal circumstances, the immune system contributes to health by protecting the body from infection.

Which of the following is most strongly supported by the statements given?

1. [ ] (A) People are generally better off using holistic methods to solve autoimmune disease type health problems than they are using drugs to treat the symptoms.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) All drugs used in treating autoimmune disease cause undesirable side effects.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Once a person experiencing autoimmune disease begins taking drugs that block the functioning of the immune system, the body will begin to heal.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) Once the immune system begins to attack the body, the only way to prevent further damage to the body is to block the immune system’s functioning.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) The use of certain drugs used in treating autoimmune disease may result in a body’s becoming more vulnerable to infection.

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The passage includes three statements that could serve as premises:


<u>Statement 1</u>: Under normal circumstances, the immune system contributes to health by protecting the body from infection.


<u>Statement 2</u>: In cases of autoimmune disease, the immune system itself causes health problems, by attacking parts of the body.


<u>Statement 3</u>: Some drugs used in treating autoimmune disease work by blocking the functioning of the immune system.


The correct answer will be a synthesis these statements that does not go beyond what they actually support.


> <b>(A) People are generally better off using holistic methods to solve autoimmune disease type health problems than they are using drugs to treat the symptoms.
</b>


This answer might be true, but the passage does not provide information that supports this comparison between holistic methods and using drugs. In order to support this conclusion, we need information other than what is provided in the passage.


> <b>(B) All drugs used in treating autoimmune disease cause undesirable side effects.
</b>


This goes beyond what is supported by the statements. While it may make sense to conclude that, since some drugs used in treating autoimmune disease block the functioning of the immune system, some of the drugs used in treating autoimmune disease cause undesirable side effects, the passage does not provide information on “all” such drugs.


> <b>(C) Once a person experiencing autoimmune disease begins taking drugs that block the functioning of the immune system, the body will begin to heal.
</b>


This answer goes beyond what is supported by the passage. While what the passage says indicates that the immune system may be blocked from attacking the body once such drugs are taken, the passage does not say that as soon as a person starts taking such drugs the body will begin to heal.


> <b>(D) Once the immune system begins to attack the body, the only way to prevent further damage to the body is to block the immune system’s functioning.
</b>


This choice goes beyond what is supported by the passage.


While the passage indicates that drugs that block the immune system are used by people experiencing autoimmune disease, in saying that blocking the functioning of the immune system is the “only” way to prevent further damage to the body, this choice goes too far.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E) The use of certain drugs used in treating autoimmune disease may result in a body’s becoming more vulnerable to infection.
</b>


This choice follows directly from what the passage says.


If the immune system contributes to health by protecting the body from infection, then the use of some drugs used in treating autoimmune disease, which work by blocking the functioning of the immune system, may result in a body’s becoming more vulnerable to infection.


The complete argument is:


Under normal circumstances, the immune system contributes to health by protecting the body from infection. In cases of autoimmune disease, the immune system itself causes health problems, by attacking parts of the body, such as the nervous system or internal organs, and so, some drugs used in treating autoimmune disease work by blocking the functioning of the immune system. <strong>Therefore, the use of certain drugs used in treating autoimmune disease may result in a body’s becoming more vulnerable to infection</strong>.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
