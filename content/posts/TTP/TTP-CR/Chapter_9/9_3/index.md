## Lesson Content
Evaluate the Argument questions use predictable language, such as:

> <em>In evaluating the argument, it would be most useful to establish which of the following?</em>

> <em>Which of the following pieces of information would be most useful in evaluating the logic of the argument presented above?</em>

> <em>Which of the following would be most useful in determining whether the conclusion is valid?</em>

> <em>To evaluate the strength of the argument, it would be most helpful to know which of the following?</em>

> <em>In order to evaluate the force of the evidence presented, it would be most useful to determine which of the following?</em>

> <em>In assessing whether the data cited provide support for the position taken, it would be most useful to determine which of the following? </em>

> <em>Which of the following is most likely to yield information that would help in the evaluation of the researcher’s hypothesis?</em>

> <em>Which of the following is most likely to yield information that would help in the evaluation of the effectiveness of the new method?</em>

> <em>In deciding whether the plan will work as predicted, it would be most helpful to determine which of the following?</em>

> <em>The answer to which of the following questions would be most useful for evaluating the likelihood of success of these initiatives</em>?

> <em>In determining the impact of implementing the new strategy, it would be important to research which of the following?</em>

> <em>In assessing the plan’s chances of achieving its aim, it would be most useful to know which of the following?</em>

> <em>In relation to the claim above, the answer to which of the following questions would be most valuable in assessing the significance of the study?</em>

> <em>In evaluating the argument, it would be most useful to compare</em>

> <em>Which of the following, if true, would be most important to take into account in evaluating the result?</em>

> <em>Investigating which of the following would be most useful in choosing between the competing explanations described above?</em>

##  Example 4 

Each year, Steam and Beans holds its annual coffee contest in which coffee roasters from around the country enter their coffees to compete for the title of best new coffee.

In evaluating the argument, it would be most useful to establish which of the following?

1. [ ] (A) Whether Witchtown Roasters’ coffee is the most expensive coffee that will be entered into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Whether some of the coffee roasters that have entered but never won the Steam and Beans coffee contest will decide not to enter coffees into the contest this year
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Whether Witchtown Roasters will enter its coffee into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year
4. [ ] (D) Whether the people who own and operate Steam and Beans enjoy the taste and aroma of Witchtown Roasters’ coffee
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Whether the Steam and Beans coffee contest will be judged by spectators who attend the event or by coffee experts who will serve as formal judges
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Notice that the question stem makes clear to us that this question is an Evaluate the Argument question. In answering this question, our job will be to determine which answer choice mentions the thing that would be most useful to establish for the purpose of evaluating the argument.


<u>Background</u>: Each year, Steam and Beans holds its annual coffee contest in which coffee roasters from around the country enter their coffees to compete for the title of best new coffee.


<u>Premise</u>: Witchtown Roasters has won that title every year for the last five years.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Witchtown Roasters will win the title again this year.


Does the fact that Witchtown Roasters has won the title every year for the last five years allow the author to conclude that Witchtown Roasters will win the title again this year? In making the argument, what assumptions is the author making? What information might help us to determine whether those assumptions are sound?


> <b>(A) Whether Witchtown Roasters’ coffee is the most expensive coffee that will be entered into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year
</b>


 What if Witchtown Roasters’ coffee IS the most expensive coffee that will be entered into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year? Would that fact allow us to evaluate the argument? No, because we are not provided with any information that indicates that the prices of the coffees have any bearing on whether a coffee will win the contest this year.


Maybe it is true that the most expensive coffee entered into the contest always wins. However, maybe it’s true that the least expensive always wins. Or, perhaps there is no correlation whatsoever between the prices of the coffees and which coffee wins.


We must resist the urge to make any unwarranted assumptions.


> <b>(B) Whether some of the coffee roasters that have entered but never won the Steam and Beans coffee contest will decide not to enter coffees into the contest this year
</b>


 What if some of the coffee roasters that have entered but never won the Steam and Beans coffee contest will decide not to enter coffees into the contest this year? Would that change make it more or less likely that Witchtown Roasters’ coffee will win the contest this year? The effect is not clear, right?


Similarly, what if all of the coffee roasters that have entered but never won the Steam and Beans coffee contest will enter coffees into the contest again this year? Would that fact make it any more or less likely that Witchtown Roasters’ coffee will win the contest this year? The effect is not clear, right?


If you chose this choice, you may have done so because you made up a story such as that, if the roasters that have not won drop out of the contest, new roasters that might beat Witchtown Roasters will enter in place of the roasters that dropped out. However, a story like that one is not supported by the information we have. For all we know from what the passage says, if some roasters that have never won drop out, their dropping out won’t make much of a difference at all. After all, these roasters have never won anyway.


We have to be careful not to take information like the information provided by this choice and make up a story that seems to affect the support for the conclusion of the argument but isn’t really supported by the information we have.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C) Whether Witchtown Roasters will enter its coffee into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year
</b>


 If Witchtown Roasters will enter its coffee into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year, then the author’s argument is pretty solid. In fact, that information would make her argument stronger because it would serve to eliminate the possibility that Witchtown Roasters will not even enter its coffee into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year, in which case the conclusion would clearly be invalid.


However, if Witchtown Roasters will NOT enter its coffee into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year, then the argument falls apart, because, if Witchtown Roasters’ coffee is not even entered into the contest this year, it would not be possible for Witchtown Roasters to win the contest this year.


Notice that what this answer choice says allows us to determine whether an assumption made by the author is sound or unsound, allowing us to evaluate the validity of author’s argument. In other words, if we look back at the argument, we can see that the author assumes that Witchtown Roasters WILL actually enter its coffee into the Steam and Beans coffee contest this year.


> <b>(D) Whether the people who own and operate Steam and Beans enjoy the taste and aroma of Witchtown Roasters’ coffee
</b>


 Notice that the passage does not say that the people who own and operate Steam and Beans have anything to do with which coffee wins the contest. So, for all we know from what the passage says, whether the people who own and operate Steam and Beans enjoy the taste and aroma of Witchtown Roaster’s coffee has absolutely no impact on whether that coffee will win the contest.


Furthermore, it could be that the people who own and operate Steam and Beans enjoy the taste and aroma of all the coffees that are entered into the contest, and that, therefore, the fact that they enjoy the taste and aroma of a coffee does not mean that that coffee will win the contest.


We have no information indicating that, if the people who own and operate Steam and Beans’ enjoy a coffee, that coffee is likely to win the contest. So, we have no reason to believe that establishing whether the people who own and operate Steam and Beans enjoy the taste and aroma of Witchtown Roasters’ coffee will help us to evaluate the argument.


> <b>(E) Whether the Steam and Beans coffee contest will be judged by spectators who attend the event or by coffee experts who will serve as formal judges
</b>


 Notice that we do not have any information on how the contest’s being judged by either spectators or coffee experts would impact which coffee will win.


For example, if the contest were judged by spectators would it be any more or less likely that Witchtown Roasters’ coffee will win or lose the contest this year? We can’t say.


Alternatively, if the contest were judged by coffee experts would it be any more or less likely that Witchtown Roasters’ coffee will win or lose the contest this year? We can’t say.


We must resist the urge to make unwarranted assumptions.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Example 5 

People often blame increased consumption of high calorie foods for the increase in the percentage of people considered obese that has occurred over the past 100 years.

The answer to which of the following questions would be most helpful for evaluating the validity of the above conclusion?

1. [ ] (A) Is it the case that most people who consume greater quantities of high calorie foods than people generally consumed in the past do jobs that involve little physical activity?
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Is the percentage of people in jobs that involve significant physical activity who are obese significantly greater today than it was 100 years ago?
3. [ ] (C) Has the percentage of people considered obese increased steadily over the past 100 years?
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) Over the past 100 years have any medical methods that are effective in countering weight gain become available?
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Are there currently any people who are both in jobs that involve little physical activity and not obese?
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



The passage ends with the following conclusion:


<u>Conclusion</u>: It is the trend away from doing jobs that involve physical activity and toward doing jobs that involve little physical activity, rather than an increase in consumption of high calorie foods, that is responsible for the increase in the percentage of people considered obese.


The passage mentions two possible causes for the increase in the percentage of people considered obese: increased consumption of high calorie foods and a trend away from doing jobs that involve physical activity and toward doing jobs that involve little physical activity. The conclusion is that the second possible cause, the trend away from doing jobs that involve physical activity, is the one responsible for the increase in obesity. However, the passage provides no information that supports the conclusion.


The correct answer will express a question such that, depending on how the question is answered, the answer will provide support for the conclusion or provide a reason to question the validity of the conclusion.


> <b>(A) Is it the case that most people who consume greater quantities of high calorie foods than people generally consumed in the past do jobs that involve little physical activity?
</b>


No matter how this question is answered, the answer does not provide information that connects or disconnects the trend in obesity to either the trend in consumption of high calorie foods or the trend away from doing jobs that involve significant physical activity. All we can get from a “Yes” or “No” answer to this question is some sense of the degree to which people who do jobs involving little physical activity consume greater quantities of high calorie foods than people generally consumed in the past. That information does not help us to understand which of the two lifestyle trends is connected to the increase in the percentage of people considered obese.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B) Is the percentage of people in jobs that involve significant physical activity who are obese significantly greater today than it was 100 years ago?
</b>


 The answer to this question tells us what happens when we eliminate the variable “the trend away from doing jobs that involve significant physical activity,” because the question is about only people in jobs that involve significant physical activity.


So, if the answer to this question is “Yes, the percentage of people in jobs that involve significant physical activity who are obese is significantly greater today than it was 100 years ago,” then the answer weakens the argument, as, if even the percentage of people in jobs that involve significant physical activity who are obese is increasing, then there must be some factor other than lack of work related physical activity that is causing an increase in the percentage of people considered obese.


On the other hand, if the answer to this question is “No, the percentage of people in jobs that involve significant physical activity who are obese is not significantly greater today than it was 100 years ago,” then the answer strengthens the argument by showing that, when the suspected cause, the trend away from doing jobs that involve signidficant physical activity, is not present, the effect, an increase in the percentage of people considered obese, is not present either.


> <b>(C) Has the percentage of people considered obese increased steadily over the past 100 years?
</b>


 Since there is no reason to believe that either increased consumption of high calorie foods or a trend away from doing jobs that involve significant physical activity would result in either a steady or not steady increase in the percentage of people considered obese, neither a “Yes” answer nor a “No” answer to this question points to a particular cause of the increased rate of obesity.


> <b>(D) Over the past 100 years have any medical methods that are effective in countering weight gain become available?
</b>


 This question addresses the wrong conclusion. Regardless of whether medical methods for countering weight gain have become available, people are more likely to be obese today than they were 100 years ago. So, the answer to this question does not affect the argument.


> <b>(E) Are there currently any people who are both in jobs that involve little physical activity and not obese?
</b>


The answer to this question does not allow us to separate the effects of the two trends mentioned. Regardless of whether the answer to this question is that there are or are not people who do jobs that require little physical activity who are not obese, the answer gives us no way of determining whether consumption of high calorie foods or lack of physical activity is the cause of obesity among those who are obese and do jobs involving little physical activity. If, for instance, the answer is “No, there are no people who do jobs that involve little physical activity and are not obese,” we still don’t have information indicating whether the cause of everyone who does jobs involving little physical activity being obese is lack of physical activity or consumption of high calorie foods.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
