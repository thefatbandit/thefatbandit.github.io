## Lesson Content
To solve Evaluate the Argument Questions, follow this strategy:

<u>Step 1 – Carefully Read, Break Down, and Completely Understand the Argument</u>

In an Evaluate the Argument question, the passage will present a plan, proposal, or argument.

So, your first job in answering an Evaluate the Argument question is to carefully and methodically read the passage to determine what the point of the passage is and how any argument made in the passage is supported.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The foundation for getting the right answer to an Evaluate the Argument question is understanding the plan, proposal, or argument presented in the passage.
{{< /alert >}}

In reading the passage of an Evaluate the Argument question, you will generally find two main components of an argument:

> 1) Conclusion – Since correctly answering an Evaluate the Argument question requires identifying an assumption upon which the conclusion of the argument depends, identifying with precision the conclusion of the argument is paramount to correctly answering the question.

> 2) Evidence – We must also determine how the argument uses evidence to support the conclusion.

Remember that the conclusion and any evidence presented are always explicitly stated in the passage in an Evaluate the Argument question.

> 3) Assumptions – Of course, in these questions, there will be missing information that must be true if the logic of the argument is to work.

It can be helpful to make short, organized notes on the scratch pad, using abbreviations and symbols.

Remember - Critical Reasoning questions are not word matching or pattern identification questions.

<u>Step 2 – Read the Question Stem and Identify What it is Asking You to Do</u>

Read the question stem.

(Note: Some people prefer to read Critical Reasoning question stems before they read the passages.

<u>Step 3 – Remove the Most Clearly Incorrect Answer Choices by Using the Yes/No Test or Other Methods.</u>

In an Evaluate the Argument question, there are usually three answer choices that relatively clearly do not highlight information that would help us to evaluate the argument.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The correct answer to an Evaluate the Argument question highlights additional information that has the potential to strengthen or weaken the argument. Any choice that does not highlight such information is incorrect.
{{< /alert >}}

Notice something here.

On your first pass through the answer choices, keep choices that you are not sure about, even if doing so means that you are left with three or more choices with which to work.

It also makes sense to be open to revisiting answer choices that you have eliminated.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Eliminating answer choices that are clearly not right is not the same as eliminating choices that you are not sure about. On your first pass through the answer choices, keep choices that you are not sure about. Also, be open to revisiting answer choices that you have eliminated. If you are having trouble finding an answer choice that works, it may be that you have eliminated the correct answer.
{{< /alert >}}

As you read each choice, seek to demonstrate how the information that it highlights affects the support for the argument’s conclusion or that the information does not affect the support.

The information highlighted by four out of the five answer choices will not affect the support for the argument’s conclusion at all, and often you can prove that it does not.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

As you read each choice, seek to demonstrate how the information that it highlights affects the argument or that the information does not affect the argument.
{{< /alert >}}

The key to getting Critical Reasoning questions right is to SEE.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

It is much better to SEE WHY the information highlighted by an answer choice cannot affect an argument than to say that you don’t see how it could. In the former case, you are eliminating the choice because you see something. In the latter case you are eliminating it because you do not see something.
{{< /alert >}}

At all times in answering GMAT questions of any type you must seek to SEE as much as possible.

If you find that you are not seeing reasons to eliminate or keep choices, it may help to go back to the passage and look through it to see what it might say that you have yet to see in a way that results in your choosing the correct answer.

<u>Step 4 – Choose the Answer Choice That Is Most Clearly What We Would Want to Know in Order to Properly Evaluate the Argument. </u>

After eliminating answer choices that are clearly incorrect, you are at the final stage of getting the right answer to an Evaluate the Argument question.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Memorize and practice the 4-step strategy for attacking Evaluate the Argument questions.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Example 6 

Since, in order for polar bears to effectively hunt seals, their main food source, polar bears must use arctic sea ice as a platform, and, since, as global temperatures increase, arctic sea ice is disappearing, many people are concerned that polar bears will die out.

Which of the following would it be most useful to establish in order to evaluate the argument?

1. [x] (A) Whether the caloric needs of polar bears can be satisfied via the bears’ consumption of things other than seals
2. [ ] (B) Whether the seal population will increase if polar bears are unable to use sea ice as a platform for hunting seals
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) Whether arctic animals other than polar bears have successfully adjusted their eating habits in response to climate related environmental changes
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Whether it is likely that humans will change their activities in such a way as to cause global temperature increases to slow or cease
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Whether there are animals that can effectively hunt for seals without using sea ice as a platform
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

##### Question Level: easy



<u>Step 1 – Carefully Read, Break Down, and Completely Understand the Argument</u>


Breakdown of the Argument:


<u>Background</u>: Polar bears must use arctic sea ice as a platform for hunting seals.


<u>Background</u>: Arctic sea ice is disappearing, and therefore people are concerned that polar bears will die out.


<u>Premise</u>: Polar bears have been observed eating things other than seals.


<u>Conclusion</u>: Even if sea ice disappears entirely, polar bears will survive.


In this argument, the conclusion that polar bears will survive is based on the fact that polar bears have been observed eating things other than seals.


<u>Step 2 – Read the Question Stem and Identify What it is Asking You to Do</u>


Since the question stem is asking us to find a choice that highlights information that helps with evaluating the argument, it’s an Evaluate the Argument question.


<u>Step 3 – Remove the Most Clearly Incorrect Answer Choices by Using the Yes/No Test Or Other Methods</u>


(A) <strong>Keep.</strong> This choice is interesting. If the answer to this question is “Yes,” then we have some confirmation that polar bears could survive by consuming things other than seals. If the answer is “No,” then we have information that weakens the argument, because, if the caloric needs of polar bears cannot be satisfied via the bears’ consumption of things other than seals, then the conclusion is likely incorrect. Notice that this choice highlights an assumption on which the support for the conclusion depends, that the bears’ caloric needs can be satisfied via the bears’ consumption of things other than seals.


> <b>(B) Whether the seal population will increase if polar bears are unable to use sea ice as a platform for hunting seals
</b>


 This choice is a little tempting, because we might think that, if the answer is “Yes,” then maybe there will be so many seals that the polar bears will be able to eat them even without sea ice to use as a platform for hunting. However, given that the passage says that, in order for polar bears to effectively hunt seals, polar bears <em>must</em> use arctic sea ice as a platform, it seems highly unlikely that an abundance of seals would save the polar bears.


(C) <strong>Keep.</strong> This choice is interesting. If the answer is “No,” then maybe, for some reason, arctic animals in general have difficulty changing their eating habits. If the answer is “Yes,” then perhaps this choice helps to confirm that polar bears can do the same.


> <b>(D) Whether it is likely that humans will change their activities in such a way as to cause global temperature increases to slow or cease
</b>


 This choice could be temping, because one could perceive a “Yes,” answer as providing reason to believe that polar bears will survive. However, the point of the argument is not that polar bears will survive in general. The conclusion is that polar bears can survive <em>even if arctic sea ice disappears entirely</em>. Information indicating that the sea ice will not disappear would not strengthen this conclusion, which is about what will happen if sea ice <em>does </em>disappear.


> <b>(E) Whether there are animals that can effectively hunt for seals without using sea ice as a platform
</b>


 Regardless of whether the answer to the question posed by this choice is “Yes” or “No,” we already know from the passage that polar bears must have arctic ice in order to hunt effectively. Even if other animals can hunt seals without sea ice, polar bears won’t do so, and, if other animals don’t hunt seals without using sea ice, we don’t have information that affects the support for the conclusion. Thus, neither a “Yes” answer nor a “No” answer to this question provides information that affects the support for the conclusion.


<u>Step 4 – Choose the Answer Choice That Is Most Clearly What We Would Want to Know in order to Properly Evaluate the Argument.</u>


Choice (A) clearly matters here. If the answer is “Yes,” we have some additional support for the conclusion that polar bears could survive without arctic ice by eating things other than seals. If the answer is “No,” then support for the conclusion that polar bears could survive even if arctic sea is disappears completely is essentially destroyed.


Choice (C) is interesting, but the truth is that, even if other arctic animals have not been able to adapt in this way, polar bears might be able to, and even if other animals have been able to adapt in this way, polar bears could easily be different from those animals in some key way that would keep them from surviving by changing their eating habits. So, the answer to this question doesn’t really tell us much.


Therefore, the correct answer is (A).


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
