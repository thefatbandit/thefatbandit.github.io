## Lesson Content
Comparisons can often be used to strengthen or weaken support for conclusions and, thus, can be helpful for evaluating arguments.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Comparisons can often be used to strengthen or weaken support for conclusions and, thus, can be helpful for evaluating arguments. Of course, only a relevant comparison will be useful in this way, and the incorrect answer choices to Evaluate questions often highlight comparisons that indicate nothing that is useful for evaluating the arguments.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider, for example, an argument with the conclusion that too much water is bad for the health of tomato plants.

Let’s consider an answer choice that highlights an irrelevant comparison in the context of the San Alonso air pollution example:

> <em>The serious air pollution problems of San Alonso are to a large degree caused by the approximately 400,000 motor vehicles that are used in the city each day. Fortunately, it is estimated that, within five years, 200,000 of the motor vehicles used in San Alonso each day will be non-air-polluting electric vehicles. Therefore, within five years, the amount of air pollution typically seen in San Alonso will be greatly reduced.</em>

> <em>The answer to which of the following questions would be most useful for evaluating the likelihood that the prediction will prove to be correct</em>?

> <em>(<u>Incorrect Choice that Brings Up an Irrelevant Comparison</u>)</em> <em>Is the level of air pollution in San Alonso greater than the levels of air pollution in most cities? </em>

> <em>(<u>Correct Answer</u>)</em> <em>Is the number of motor vehicles used in San Alonso each day five years from now predicted to be fewer than 600,000?</em>

We can see how a test-taker’s mind could start running to create a story that explains how the comparison brought up by the incorrect choice would affect the argument.

The truth is, however, that that line of thinking doesn’t really make sense.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In answering Evaluate the Argument questions, beware of incorrect choices that highlight irrelevant comparisons.
{{< /alert >}}

When answering an Evaluate question, to avoid choosing a choice that highlights an irrelevant comparison, we have to be careful not to come up with a convoluted story that seems to connect that comparison to the argument but doesn’t really make sense.

As you answer the following question, notice the difference between the relevance of the comparisons made in the incorrect answers and that of the comparison made in the correct answer.

##  Example 13 

Elephants, like certain other animals, disperse the seeds of trees, thus enabling the parent trees whose seeds the elephants disperse to reproduce in areas at a distance from the locations of the parent trees.

Determining which of the following would be most helpful in assessing the degree to which the conservationists’ concerns are well founded?

1. [ ] (A) Whether elephants more effectively disperse seeds than do animals of other species
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) How much decreases in elephant populations that have been resulting from hunting by poachers who sell their body parts will slow in the near future.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Whether trees that grow far from the locations of their parent trees are healthier than trees that grow near to their parent trees
4. [ ] (D) Whether the types of trees that grow in forests historically inhabited by elephants tend to grow taller than do trees that grow in forests never inhabited by elephants
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Which approach to maintaining elephant populations is the most likely to result in success
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: hard



We have to evaluate the support for the following conclusion:


<u>Conclusion</u>: The reduction in seed dispersal that has resulted from decreases in elephant populations will negatively impact the numbers of healthy trees in forests that elephants inhabited historically.


The correct answer will highlight information that will add support for or serve to call into question the link between the reduction in seed dispersal and the numbers of healthy trees.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A) Whether elephants more effectively disperse seeds than do animals of other species
</b>


 This choice could be tempting, because the comparison that it brings up is related to the topic of the argument. Notice, however, that the argument is about the effects of<em> the reduction in seed dispersal that has resulted </em>from reductions in elephant populations. This reduction and its effects will be the same regardless of whether elephants disperse seeds more effectively than do animals of other species. If elephants are relatively great at dispersing seeds, the reduction in seed dispersal will have a certain effect. If elephants do not disperse seeds as effectively as do other animals, the reduction in seed dispersal will still have the same effect.


> <b>(B) How much decreases in elephant populations that have been resulting from hunting by poachers who sell their body parts will slow in the near future.
</b>


 This choice is tempting, because the degree to which decreases in elephant populations will slow could seem to affect the degree to which the conservationist’s concerns are well founded. However, by noticing a key detail in the passage, we can eliminate this choice. The concerns are about the reduction in seed dispersal that has <em>already </em>resulted from decreases in elephant populations that have <em>already </em>occurred. This choice is about the degree to which decreases in elephant populations will slow <em>in the future</em>. The degree to which decreases in elephant populations will slow in the future has no clear connection to the effects of a reduction in seed dispersal that has already occurred. Regardless of whether the decreases will slow a lot or not at all, the reduction that has already occurred will have the effects that it will have.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C) Whether trees that grow far from the locations of their parent trees are healthier than trees that grow near to their parent trees
</b>


 Let’s apply the Yes/No test to this choice. If the answer is “Yes,” and trees that grow far from their parent trees are healthier, then seed dispersal helps trees to be healthier. Thus, a “Yes” answer to the question posed by this choice adds to the support for the concerns. If the answer is “No,” then we have reason to believe that dispersing seeds does not benefit the health of trees, and thus, a “No” answer weakens support for the conclusion.


> <b>(D) Whether the types of trees that grow in forests historically inhabited by elephants tend to grow taller than do trees that grow in forests never inhabited by elephants
</b>


 This choice could be very tempting, because a test-taker could associate “taller” with “healthier” and conclude that having elephants around makes trees healthier. Notice, however, that a “Yes” answer to this question merely indicates that the <em>types</em> of trees in forest in which elephants live tend to grow taller than the trees that grow in other forests. All that information would indicate is that the best place for elephants may be the best place for the types of trees that grow tall. A “No” answer would indicate the opposite, that shorter types of trees thrive where elephants also thrive. Whatever the case, the truth is that neither “shorter” nor “taller’ is the equivalent of “healthier.” So, this comparison gives us no information that is useful for evaluation of the support for the conclusion.


> <b>(E) Which approach to maintaining elephant populations is the most likely to result in success
</b>


 The question posed by this choice is pertinent in general to a discussion of the effects of decreases in elephant populations, but the answer to the question posed by this choice does not provide information that affects the support for the specific concerns of the conservationists. What we have to see in order to eliminate this choice are that the concerns discussed in the passage are about the effects of decreases in elephant populations that have occurred, and that the answer to the question of how best to stem future decreases will not affect the degree to which concerns about the effects of past decreases are well founded.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
