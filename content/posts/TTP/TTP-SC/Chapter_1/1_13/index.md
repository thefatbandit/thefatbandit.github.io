## Lesson Content
Incorrect choices in Sentence Correction questions are incorrect because of clear errors in meaning, logic, or grammar.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Often, when a person does not see what’s wrong with an option in a Sentence Correction question, he labels the version as “awkward.” 
{{< /alert >}}

We’ve been teaching the GMAT for over 15 years, and to this day, we still haven’t seen anyone clearly articulate what the word “awkward” means about an incorrect sentence version.

Let’s be clear.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In Sentence Correction questions, incorrect sentences are only very rarely incorrect because they are “awkward.” In general, sentence versions are incorrect for clear, definable reasons; they are incorrect because of errors in grammar, structure, or meaning. Claiming that a sentence is incorrect because it is awkward generally indicates that we have failed to see why it is incorrect.
{{< /alert >}}

Thus, as you proceed through this course, you’ll notice that we always provide at least one (and usually more than one) clear reason why a sentence is wrong.

We want you to hold yourself to the same high standard.

We’ll explore this concept much more in the chapters to come.
