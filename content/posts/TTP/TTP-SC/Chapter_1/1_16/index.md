## Lesson Content
Often, in answering Sentence Correction questions, people fall into choosing choice (E), the last answer choice, without a clear reason to choose choice (E).

As you can imagine, falling into choosing choice (E) in this way often results in a person’s getting a question incorrect.

To be clear, answer choice (E) will be correct just as frequently as the other four answer choices.

However, don’t choose (E) just because you haven’t decided that another choice is correct, and (E) is the only one left.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Although answer choice (E) will be correct just as frequently as the other four answer choices, you should not choose answer choice (E) as correct simply because you do not see why any of the other answer options are correct.
{{< /alert >}}

So, when you get to choice (E), if you haven’t found clear reasons why choice (E) or other choices are incorrect or correct, then the move is to go back through the choices and find clear reasons for choosing one choice over the others.

Consider the following example.

##  Sentence Correction Example 20  

<u>Policymakers in government has largely ignored the dire predictions the latest climate change assessment make, both because of the current global economic downturn and</u> corporate influence.

1. [ ] (A) Policymakers in government has largely ignored the dire predictions the latest climate change assessment make, because of both the current global economic downturn and
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) The dire predictions the latest climate change assessment make have largely been ignored by government policymakers, both because of the current global economic downturn and
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) The dire predictions made in the latest climate change assessment have largely been ignored by government policymakers, both because of the current global economic downturn and because of
4. [ ] (D) Policymakers in government is largely ignoring the dire predictions the latest climate change assessment make, both as a result of the current global economic downturn as well as of
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) The dire predictions made in the latest climate change assessment has largely been ignored by government policymakers, both because of the current global economic downturn and also
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



In evaluating the answer choices in this Sentence Correction question, we must be sure to find specific reasons why one choice is better than the others, rather than simply select choice (E) because nothing else seems correct. If we get to choice (E) without identifying a sentence version that’s clearly correct, we may have to go through all the choices again.


Be careful not to just fall into choosing choice (E)!


> <b>(A)</b> <b>Policymakers in government has largely ignored the dire predictions the latest climate change assessment make, because of both the current global economic downturn and
</b> corporate influence.



 The first flaw we see in this version is that the plural subject “policymakers” does not agree with the singular verb “has.” A plural subject must be paired with a plural verb.


Continuing through the sentence, we can see also that the singular subject “climate change assessment” is incorrectly paired with the plural verb “make.” After all, the thing making “the dire predictions” is the “latest climate change assessment.” So, the wording should instead be “the latest climate change assessment <em>makes</em>.”


> <b>(B)</b> <b>The dire predictions the latest climate change assessment make have largely been ignored by government policymakers, both because of the current global economic downturn and
</b> corporate influence.



If you spotted the second subject-verb agreement error in choice (A), you probably noticed that this choice commits the same error: the singular noun “latest climate change assessment” does not properly pair with the plural verb “make.” The correct pairing would be “the latest climate change assessment makes.”


Notice also the “both … and” construction in this version of the sentence. The elements of this construction should be parallel, meaning that, since “both” is followed by “because of,” “and” should also be followed by “because of.” So, since “and” is followed by only the phrase “corporate influence,” the “both … and” construction is not correctly parallel.


If you didn’t spot the error in parallelism, don’t worry; you’ll get plenty of practice with this skill in the Parallelism chapter.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C)</b> <b>The dire predictions made in the latest climate change assessment have largely been ignored by government policymakers, both because of the current global economic downturn and because of
</b> corporate influence.



This version is the only one that does not contain any subject-verb agreement errors.


Also, the “both … and” construction in this version is correctly parallel since “both” and “and” are both followed by “because of.”


Notice also that the plural noun “dire predictions” is correctly paired with the plural verb “have.”


The sentence is grammatically correct and its meaning is logical.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>Policymakers in government is largely ignoring the dire predictions the latest climate change assessment make, both as a result of the current global economic downturn as well as of
</b> corporate influence.



The first flaw we see in this version is that the plural subject “policymakers” does not agree with the singular verb “is.” A plural subject must be paired with a plural verb.


Also we must be careful to pinpoint which noun in the sentence pairs with the plural verb “make.” The thing making “the dire predictions” is the singular “latest climate change assessment.” Thus, since the singular “assessment” cannot pair with the plural verb “make,” this choice is incorrect. The correct pairing would be “the latest climate change assessment makes.”


This choice also contains the illogical phrase “both … as well as.” The correct wording is “both … and.”


> <b>(E)</b> <b>The dire predictions made in the latest climate change assessment has largely been ignored by government policymakers, both because of the current global economic downturn and also
</b> corporate influence.



If you have not decided on another choice, you may be particularly tempted by this choice because it changes the incorrect “make” we saw in other choices to the correct “made.”


However, this choice commits a different subject-verb agreement error, incorrectly pairing the plural noun “dire predictions” with the singular verb “has.” The “predictions” are the thing ignored by policymakers, not the singular “assessment.”


If you had trouble seeing the subject-verb agreement error, it is helpful to cross off the phrase separating the noun from the main verb:


The dire predictions <s>made in the latest climate change assessment</s> has … been ignored


Now, we can clearly see that “dire predictions … has” is not grammatical. “Dire predictions … have” would be correct.


Additionally, this choice contains a redundancy: the phrase “both … and <em>also</em>” is incorrect, because “and” and “also” have the same meaning in that context. The correct wording is simply “both … and.”


Be careful not to fall into selecting choice (E) just because you’re having trouble finding a choice that seems correct. You must evaluate each answer choice on its own merits.


<strong>Key Concept: </strong>Confirm that singular subjects are paired with singular verbs and that plural subjects are paired with plural verbs. 


<strong>Key Concept:</strong> Make sure that the two halves of correlative constructions such as “both X and Y,” “not only X but also Y,” and “not X but Y” are parallel in structure.


<strong>Key Concept:</strong> The second half of the idiom “both X and Y” cannot use “also” or “as well as” in the place of or in addition to “and.”


<strong>Key Concept:</strong> When checking for subject-verb agreement, cross off any noun modifiers that come between the subject and finite verb.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Sentence Correction Example 21  

Although it is clear that koan meditation is becoming more widely used, <u>it is also clear that meditation of that form is not likely as becoming</u> the most popular with people in general.

1. [ ] (A) it is also clear that meditation of that form is not likely as becoming
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) what is clear also is that that form of meditation is unlikely to become
3. [ ] (C) it is clear also that meditation of that form is unlikely for becoming
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) also clear is that form of meditation is not likely in becoming
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) though it is also clear that meditation of that form is unlikely to become
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



As we go through the choices in this question, choices (A), (B), (C), and (D) may all seem strange and incorrect to us. So, since choice (E) “sounds” OK, when we get to choice (E), we may be tempted to quickly choose it without much analysis.


Choice (E) has a major error, however. So, to get this question correct, we have to be careful not to “fall into choosing choice (E).”


> <b>(A)</b> Although it is clear that koan meditation is becoming more widely used, <b>it is also clear that meditation of that form is not likely as becoming
</b> the most popular with people in general.



We can see that this version uses the wording “not likely as becoming,” which is not correct English and does not convey a meaning that makes sense.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B)</b> Although it is clear that koan meditation is becoming more widely used, <b>what is clear also is that that form of meditation is unlikely to become
</b> the most popular with people in general.



 Because this version uses “what is,” and because “that that” may seem odd, we may initially be reluctant to choose this choice.


However, using “what is” the way this version uses it is correct.


Also, the first “that” in “that that” is part of “what is clear is that.” Then, the second “that” is used to indicate that the sentence is continuing to discuss “that form of medi<ins>t</ins>ation.” So, using “that” twice in a row makes sense.


So, this version is grammatically correct and conveys a meaning that makes sense.


> <b>(C)</b> Although it is clear that koan meditation is becoming more widely used, <b>it is clear also that meditation of that form is unlikely for becoming
</b> the most popular with people in general.



We can see that this version uses the wording “unlikely for becoming,” which is not correct English and does not convey a meaning that makes sense.


> <b>(D)</b> Although it is clear that koan meditation is becoming more widely used, <b>also clear is that form of meditation is not likely in becoming
</b> the most popular with people in general.



We can see that this version uses the wording “not likely in becoming,” which is not correct English and does not convey a meaning that makes sense.


> <b>(E)</b> Although it is clear that koan meditation is becoming more widely used, <b>though it is also clear that meditation of that form is unlikely to become
</b> the most popular with people in general.



This version does not “sound” strange or incorrect the way all the other versions do, but this version has a clear error. So, we have to be careful not to fall into choosing this choice (E) just because it seems OK when we first read it.


The problem with this version is that it uses both “although” and “though” even though they mean essentially the same thing and serve the same purpose in the sentence.


We can more easily see that using both “although” and “though” is incorrect by simplifying the sentence.


Although it is clear …, though it is also clear ….


In order for the sentence to be correct, we need to use one or the other as in:


Although it is clear …, it is also clear ….


or


It is clear …, though it is also clear ….


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
