## Lesson Content
On test day, each Sentence Correction question will take you, on average, about 1.5 to 1.75 minutes to complete.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

On test day, each Sentence Correction question will take you about 1.5 to 1.75 minutes each to complete. Keep in mind that this time is an average; some will be solved faster, and some will require more time.
{{< /alert >}}

That said, answering Sentence Correction questions when you are taking the GMAT and practicing answering Sentence Correction questions when you are preparing to take the GMAT are two different activities.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

As you are working through the learning phase of the TTP Sentence Correction course, you should work through the chapter examples and the chapter test questions untimed.
{{< /alert >}}

This information is very important.

No matter what you have heard from anyone else about how to practice Sentence Correction, <strong>do Sentence Correction practice questions untimed, focus on understanding and accuracy, and let speed come with skill.</strong> Here’s why.

If you can’t solve a question within the recommended time frame, then you don’t yet have the skills you need to solve that question in that amount of time.

To make matters worse, blindly marking a question just to answer it within a two- or three-minute time frame and then getting it wrong can kick your self-confidence down.

So, go slowly and methodically.

You won’t learn to succeed in Sentence Correction by getting questions incorrect in under two minutes each.

Your study plan will begin to nudge you when you should begin practicing these questions under time pressure.

Let’s now discuss leveraging the predictability of Sentence Correction questions.
