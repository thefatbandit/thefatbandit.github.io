## Lesson Content
When answering a Sentence Correction question, begin by reading the entire original sentence to identify whether there are any clear errors of grammar or structure present.

If the original version seems to be grammatically correct, then we keep choice (A) as a contender and move on to the other choices.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When we are answering a Sentence Correction question, if the original version seems to be grammatically correct, then we keep choice (A) as a contender and move on to the other choices. However, if the underlined portion of the original sentence is clearly incorrect, we eliminate choice (A).
{{< /alert >}}

For example, if the original version has a clear error in subject-verb agreement, a clear error in verb tense, or any other clear error, we can confidently eliminate (A).

We then read the other four versions vertically for that same error.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

If that exact grammatical error is present in any other versions, we eliminate those answer choices in which that same error is repeated.
{{< /alert >}}

Now, and we will talk more about this soon, we MUST BE SURE that we are eliminating ONLY the EXACT same grammatical error.

For example, let’s say that the original sentence uses the plural pronoun “they” with a singular noun.

The reason is that one (or more) of the other choices may have adjusted the underlined words so that now “they” works correctly in the sentences.

Instead of matching words only, we must read each version carefully, seeking to determine whether, in each case, the use of the word “they” really is an error.

Now, often, and even more often than not in harder questions, any errors that we find in the original sentence won’t appear in any of the other four versions.

Our next move is scanning the rest of the versions for other grammar errors.

Having scanned the five sentence versions for clear grammar errors and eliminated any in which we found grammar errors, we may have eliminated all versions but one, in which case we have found the correct answer to the question.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Having scanned the five sentence versions for clear grammar errors and eliminated any in which we found grammar errors, we may have eliminated all versions but one, in which case we have found the correct answer to the question.
{{< /alert >}}

However, in many Sentence Correction questions, two or more sentence versions will be grammatically correct, and so, even after we have eliminated any versions in which we have spotted grammar errors, we may be left with multiple choices to choose among.

In the chapters that follow, we will discuss ways of determining whether a sentence conveys a meaning that makes sense.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In many cases, our final step in answering a Sentence Correction question will be to eliminate versions that don’t convey meanings that make sense and find a version that does convey a meaning that makes sense.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s now apply this approach to answering a Sentence Correction question.

##  Sentence Correction Example 4  

<u>The whale shark is the largest shark of all, but they are, to a human, less dangerous than the white shark by eating</u> only small fish, shrimp, and plankton.

1. [ ] (A) The whale shark is the largest shark of all, but they are, to a human, less dangerous than the white shark by eating
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) Whale sharks are the largest of all sharks but are, to a human, less dangerous than white sharks since whale sharks eat
3. [ ] (C) The whale shark is the largest shark of all, but they are, to a human, less dangerous than a white shark, eating
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) The whale shark, the largest of all sharks, are, to a human, less dangerous than the white shark because of eating
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) Whale sharks are the largest of all sharks, but they are, to a human, less dangerous than white sharks by eating
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

##### Question Level: medium



> <b>(A)</b> <b>The whale shark is the largest shark of all, but they are, to a human, less dangerous than the white shark by eating
</b> only small fish, shrimp, and plankton.



 Notice that this version starts off talking about “the whale shark,” which is singular, but then jumps to using the plural pronoun “they.” As a result, since “they” cannot refer to the singular “the whale shark,” “they” has nothing to refer to.


So, we can eliminate this choice because it has a clear grammar error, since a plural pronoun needs a plural noun to refer to.


If we scan the other versions, we can see that choice (C) has the same pronoun issue: “they” has nothing to refer to.


Notice also that, while choice (E) also uses the pronoun “they,” choice (E) does not have a pronoun issue, because (E) uses the plural “whale sharks.” So, “they” in (E) correctly refers to “whale sharks.”


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B)</b> <b>Whale sharks are the largest of all sharks but are, to a human, less dangerous than white sharks since whale sharks eat
</b> only small fish, shrimp, and plankton.



This choice is grammatically correct and uses the conjunction “since” to effectively convey a meaning that makes sense.


> <b>(C)</b> <b>The whale shark is the largest shark of all, but they are, to a human, less dangerous than a white shark, eating
</b> only small fish, shrimp, and plankton.



Like the version created via the use of choice (A), this version starts off talking about “the whale shark,” which is singular, but then jumps to using the plural pronoun “they.” As a result, since “they” cannot refer to the singular “the whale shark,” “they” has nothing to refer to.


So, we can eliminate this choice because it has a clear grammar error, since a plural pronoun needs a plural noun to refer to.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>The whale shark, the largest of all sharks, are, to a human, less dangerous than the white shark because of eating
</b> only small fish, shrimp, and plankton.



 In this version, the singular subject “the whale shark” does not agree with the plural verb “are,” as we can more easily see by crossing off what appears between “the whale shark” and “are.”


The whale shark<s>, the largest of all sharks,</s> are, to a human, less dangerous


We can confidently eliminate this choice because it has a clear grammar error.


> <b>(E)</b> <b>Whale sharks are the largest of all sharks, but they are, to a human, less dangerous than white sharks by eating
</b> only small fish, shrimp, and plankton.



This choice may be more challenging to eliminate than (A), (C), or (D), because, whereas those choices have grammar errors, this version is grammatically correct. So, we have to use meaning to choose between this choice and the correct answer, (B).


The way to eliminate this choice is to see that this version says that whale sharks “are … less dangerous than white sharks by eating only small fish, shrimp, and plankton.” Notice that saying that whale sharks are less dangerous “by eating” certain things does not really make sense. Whale sharks would not make themselves less dangerous by eating.


Choice (B), on the other hand, uses “since” rather than “by” to convey a meaning that makes sense.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
