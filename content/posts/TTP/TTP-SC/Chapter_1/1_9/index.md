## Lesson Content
In most incorrect answer choices in Sentence Correction questions, there are multiple flaws.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In most incorrect answer choices in Sentence Correction questions, there are multiple flaws. So, if we are not certain that one possible flaw is actually a flaw, we can generally find a flaw that we are more sure about.
{{< /alert >}}

So, if we are having trouble deciding whether one characteristic of a sentence choice renders that choice incorrect, rather than spend time wondering about that one characteristic, we can look for a stronger or clearer reason to eliminate that choice.

Let’s see how this process can work by considering an example:

> Researchers studying the spider monkey have noticed them to live in matriarchal troops.

When we read that sentence, we see that it includes the expression “noticed them to live.” Does that expression make sense? Is it idiomatically correct? It is incorrect because “noticed X to Y” is not an expression used in English, but if we were to see the above sentence in a Sentence Correction question, we might not be sure whether the presence of “noticed them to live” in the sentence makes the sentence incorrect.

So, let’s look for a stronger decision point.

There is another flaw in the sentence.

OK, so now, because we see that the use of “them” is clearly incorrect, we have a strong decision point, and therefore we would be able to confidently decide that the above example sentence is incorrect if it were a sentence version in a Sentence Correction question.

##  Sentence Correction Example 12  

<u>Researchers evolution is being studied by whom have found that development of new traits do not always result</u> in increased fitness of a species.

1. [ ] (A) Researchers evolution is being studied by whom have found that development of new traits do not always result
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Researchers who study evolution and find that development of a new trait does not always result
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) In studying evolution, researchers have found that development of a new trait does not always result
4. [ ] (D) Researchers who study evolution has found development of new traits not always resulting
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Studying evolution, development of new traits has been found not always to result by researchers
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: easy



In some cases, we can easily tell that characteristics of sentence versions in this question constitute flaws that make the versions incorrect. In other cases, however, whether characteristics constitute flaws is less clear. In a case in which we aren’t sure whether a characteristic constitutes a flaw, it makes sense to look for another characteristic that we can tell for sure constitutes a flaw.


> <b>(A)</b> <b>Researchers evolution is being studied by whom have found that development of new traits do not always result
</b> in increased fitness of a species.



When we read this version, the wording “Researchers evolution is being studied by whom” jumps out at us as convoluted, but is it incorrect? That wording means “researchers who study evolution.” So, it seems possible that that wording is just an alternative way of saying, “researchers who study evolution” and that the presence of that wording in this version does not make this version incorrect.


So, let’s look for a clearer reason to eliminate this choice.


As we continue reading this version, we see that the singular subject “development” and the plural verb “do” do not agree. We can more easily see that they do not agree by crossing off what appears between “development” and “do”:


development <s>of new traits</s> do not always result in increased fitness


By finding this subject-verb agreement error, we have found a clear reason to eliminate this choice.


> <b>(B)</b> <b>Researchers who study evolution and find that development of a new trait does not always result
</b> in increased fitness of a species.



Notice that this version says, “Researchers who study evolution and find …” without ever completing the thought. It leaves the reader wondering, “Researchers who study and find do what?”


In other words, in this version, there’s no verb to go with the subject “researchers,” and thus this version is not a complete sentence.


The fact that this version is not a complete sentence is a clear reason to eliminate this choice.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C)</b> <b>In studying evolution, researchers have found that development of a new trait does not always result
</b> in increased fitness of a species.



The plural subject “researchers” agrees with the plural verb “have,” the singular subject “development” agrees with the singular verb “does,” and the rest of this version is grammatically correct.


Also, this version effectively conveys a meaning that makes sense.


So, this version is the correct answer.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>Researchers who study evolution has found development of new traits not always resulting
</b> in increased fitness of a species.



 Toward the end of this version, we see “found development of new traits not always resulting in increased fitness of a species.” Is “found development of new traits not always resulting” correct or incorrect? It certainly sounds incorrect, but whether it’s incorrect may not be entirely clear to us.


So, let’s look for a clearer reason to eliminate this choice.


Toward the beginning of this version, we see “Researchers who study evolution has found.” Notice that the plural subject “researchers” does not agree with the singular verb “has found.”


By finding this subject-verb agreement error, we have found a clear reason to eliminate this choice.


> <b>(E)</b> <b>Studying evolution, development of new traits has been found not always to result by researchers
</b> in increased fitness of a species.



This version has some clear errors.


Notice what it says at the beginning: “Studying evolution, development of new traits has been found ….” For reasons that we’ll discuss later in the course, that part conveys that “development of new traits” has been studying evolution, a nonsensical meaning.


Further along, this version says, “development of new traits has been found not always to result by researchers.” So, this version seems to say that development of new traits somehow results “by researchers,” which is nonsense.


So, this version is clearly incorrect because it conveys nonsensical meanings.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
