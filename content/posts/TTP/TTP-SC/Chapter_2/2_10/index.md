## Lesson Content
We have just discussed that one way to connect two independent clauses is by using a comma and a coordinating conjunction (FANBOYS).

Consider the following example:

                               IC                                 IC

> My breakfast was cold; the door had been left open.

The semicolon here acts in the same way as a comma and a coordinating conjunction would.

When a semicolon is used as a substitute for a comma and coordinating conjunction, an independent clause <strong>must</strong> be found on each side of the semicolon.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When a semicolon is used as a substitute for a comma and coordinating conjunction, an independent clause must be found on each side of the semicolon.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s look at a few more example sentences:

> The company’s year-end financials were just released; the company earned more this year than it earned last year.

> I have a boxing match tomorrow; I can’t go dancing with you tonight.

> The GMAT is a game of determination; the highest scores are often earned by those willing to prepare the most thoroughly.

Notice that, in each of the example sentences above, there are independent clauses on both sides of the semicolon.

### Targeted Practice Solutions

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>Targeted Practice: 1</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

<b>Explanation:</b>


In this case, two independent clauses and joined with a comma. So, we have a comma splice. One way to make the structure a correct sentence is to connect the two clauses with a semicolon rather than a comma. A correct version is:


The drop spindle is an ancient tool for spinning fiber into yarn or thread<strong>;</strong> it has been around for a very long time and has changed little since its first invention.

A semicolon is incorrectly used to connect a clause to a phrase. While what precedes the semicolon is an independent clause, what follows the semicolon includes no main verb. The sentence could be fixed through the addition of a main verb to what follows the semicolon.


Improving nutrition and reducing stress prompted changes in the behavior of genes; among them <strong>were</strong> some genes believed to be tumor suppressors.
