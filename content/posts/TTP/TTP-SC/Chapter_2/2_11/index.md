## Lesson Content
We’ll encounter the colon from time to time in Sentence Correction questions.

Whenever we have a colon, what precedes the colon must be able to stand on its own as a complete sentence.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Whenever we have a colon, what precedes the colon must be able to stand on its own as a complete sentence.
{{< /alert >}}

For example:

> <strong>The board of directors made its final decision:</strong> the CEO will be replaced immediately.

> <strong>Aditya had a productive day:</strong> he completed his studying and walked the dogs.

> <strong>My dad tells me the same thing every morning before school:</strong> “Don’t talk to strangers.”

> <strong>Breakfast provided the guests with some lovely choices:</strong> English muffins, fresh fruit salad, and skillet potatoes.

> <strong>Here is a simple rule for being happy: </strong>find gratitude in your life.

Notice that, in each of those examples, an independent clause precedes the colon.

Now, the colon may be followed by an independent clause, but it need not be; it is also grammatically correct for the colon to be followed by an example or series of examples.

For example:

> I love everything about strawberries: <strong>the taste, the texture, and the smell</strong>.

> Please tell us one thing: <strong>how many people will attend</strong>.

> Last night they served a delicious dinner: <strong>kelp noodles with red sauce</strong>.

> Students who earn top GMAT scores usually do three things: <strong>study with TTP, work hard, and persist until they succeed</strong>.

> They had a great day: <strong>beaches, boating, and a balloon ride</strong>.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A colon must be preceded by an independent clause. It may be followed by an independent clause, but it need not be. The colon can also be followed by an example or series of examples.
{{< /alert >}}

Watch out for a common mistake made with the colon.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: My morning routine included: pushups and sit ups.

Of course, “My morning routine included” is not a complete thought and could not stand on its own as a sentence.

We could correctly say the following:

> My morning routine included some exercises: pushups and sit ups.

Notice that now what comes before the colon expresses a complete thought.

This sentence is also incorrectly using the colon:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: My favorite activity was: tumbleweed chasing.

Similarly, in this context, the verb “was” needs to be followed by either a noun or an adjective.

In general, it’s important to understand that a colon cannot be used if the sentence preceding the colon is not complete.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A colon cannot be used if the sentence preceding the colon is not complete.
{{< /alert >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>Targeted Practice: 1</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

<b>Explanation:</b>


What precedes the colon is not a complete thought. “The administration used” has to be followed by a noun that names what the administration used, but in this case, the nouns that name what the administration used are incorrectly separated from “used” by a colon.


A correct version would omit the colon.


In order to address the issue of violence in the school, the administration used conflict resolution classes, hall monitors, and counseling.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)

The committee <strong>defined</strong> their goals for the year: to balance the budget, to support entrepreneurship, and to address environmental issues.
