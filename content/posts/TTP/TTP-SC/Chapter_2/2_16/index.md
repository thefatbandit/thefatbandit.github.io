## Lesson Content
A noun clause is a clause that plays the role of a noun.

Many noun clauses begin with the following words:

> that, which, how, who, whom, where, whether, what, why

For example, in each of the following sentences the noun clauses are bolded:

> Marty feels <strong>that spiritual development has no limits</strong>.

> <strong>That holiday leave had been cancelled</strong> depressed the entire company.

> The crowd was unhappy with <strong>what happened next</strong>.

> <strong>Why she left in the middle of the night</strong> was a mystery.

> <strong>Whether a vaccine will be developed</strong> is yet to be determined.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Many noun clauses begin with the words “that,” “which,” “how,” “who,” “whom,” “where,” “whether,” “what,” and “why.”
{{< /alert >}}

Like nouns, noun clauses can perform a variety of roles in sentences.
