## Lesson Content
Many people believe that the subject of a sentence must be a noun.

> <strong>That Napoleon’s ruse would fail in its intended objective</strong> had not occurred to the French generals.

This is a perfectly acceptable sentence.

Notice that each of the following sentences has a noun clause serving as the subject:

> <strong>That she had the nerve to attend the party</strong> is shocking.

> <strong>What few believed could exist</strong> was eventually created.

> <strong>Whichever movie you choose</strong> is fine by me.

> <strong>That I would be chosen the winner</strong> came as a surprise to all.

> <strong>Whoever made this course </strong>is a real genius.

> <strong>Whether we can go to Lanai</strong> is debatable.

> <strong>That sales have increased this quarter</strong> is good news.

> <strong>What Jeff said</strong> is confusing.

> <strong>Why he had gone to Madrid</strong> was what everyone wanted to know.

> <strong>Where we met</strong> is none of your business.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A noun clause can fulfill the role of the subject of a sentence.
{{< /alert >}}
