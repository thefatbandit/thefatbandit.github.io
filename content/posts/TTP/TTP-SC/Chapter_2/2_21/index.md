## Lesson Content
When three or more items appear in a series, a comma and a coordinating conjunction must appear before the last item in the series.

> For breakfast, I had cereal, toast<strong>, and</strong> juice.

Notice that, in this sentence, a comma and the coordinating conjunction “and” precede the final noun, “juice.” The comma just before the coordinating conjunction is referred to as the “Oxford Comma.” It must be present in such a list on the GMAT.

In each of the following sentences, notice how a comma precedes the coordinating conjunction prior to the final item in the list.

> The politician bribed the <strong>directors, Tom, and Nancy</strong>.

> Paul loves <strong>hot peppers, peanut butter, cocoa, and bananas</strong>.

> The team loves to <strong>meditate, play ping pong, and swim</strong>.

> The GMAT rewards <strong>hard work, determination, and organization</strong>.

> Having an MBA helps people to <strong>earn a higher salary, become promoted more quickly, and garner respect from their colleagues</strong>.

The following sentences, therefore, are incorrect because they include lists of three items but lack the “Oxford” comma:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: Planes have engines, wings and wheels.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: Justine ordered avocados, beets and kale.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: Baseball, soccer and polo are Rafi’s favorite sports.

This sentence is missing the comma before the coordinating conjunction “and” that precedes the last item in the list, “millet.” A correct version is:


The major staple crops of Nigeria are cassava, yam, maize, sorghum, rice<strong>,</strong> and millet.

Many comics involving Donald Duck, Scrooge McDuck<strong>,</strong> or Donald’s children were drawn by cartoonist Carl Barks.

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>Targeted Practice: 5</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

<b>Explanation:</b>


We can see that the list in this sentence is incorrectly constructed because there is no comma before the coordinating conjunction “and” that appears before the last item “sesame seeds.”


At the same time, because “and” appears twice, the list appears to be composed of two items, “rice and beans” and “chickpeas and sesame seeds.” So, one way to correct the list is to replace the comma between those two items with the conjunction “or.”


By combining rice and beans <strong>or</strong> chickpeas and sesame seeds, one can achieve amino acid balance.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
