## Lesson Content
There are two major hallmarks of an independent clause:

> · An independent clause contains both a subject and a finite verb.

> · An independent clause can stand alone as a sentence.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

An independent clause contains a subject and a finite verb and can stand alone as a sentence.
{{< /alert >}}

Why are independent clauses important? Well, every English sentence must have at least one independent clause, and the correct version of a sentence in a Sentence Correction question always contains at least one independent clause.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Every English sentence must have at least one independent clause, and the correct version of a sentence in a Sentence Correction question always contains at least one independent clause.
{{< /alert >}}

The following are examples of independent clauses, with their subjects and finite verbs bolded.

> <strong>I respect </strong>you.

> <strong>I love</strong> you.

> <strong>The crowd laughed</strong>.

> <strong>Jeff and Sara were</strong> walking the dog.

> <strong>Bears hibernate</strong> during the winter.

> <strong>The dog and the cat are</strong> eating their dinner.

> <strong>Neither Mashid nor her sisters are</strong> at the beach.

> <strong>Eating</strong> <strong>bananas in the sun</strong> <strong>is</strong> my favorite pastime.

> <strong>To arrive at the restaurant before ten would</strong> have been close to a miracle.

Notice that each of these independent clauses contains a subject and a finite verb.

At this point, let’s introduce an important term: “main verb.” A main verb is the finite verb found in an independent clause.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In any independent clause, the finite verb in that clause is called a main verb.
{{< /alert >}}

For example:

> The leopard <strong>slept</strong> in the tree.

> The building <strong>will </strong>sway in the wind.

> My computer <strong>runs</strong> quickly.

> The stores <strong>were</strong> crowded with shoppers.

> Morning fog <strong>is</strong> beautiful.

In the clauses above, “slept,” “will,” “runs,” “were,” and “is” are the main verbs.

There is one more required characteristic of an independent clause: there must be no word that removes the clause’s independence.
