## Lesson Content
Clauses that cannot constitute sentences on their own are, quite logically, called dependent clauses.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Clauses that cannot constitute sentences on their own are called dependent clauses. These clauses fulfill specific functions within sentences and serve to enhance or modify the meaning expressed by an independent clause.
{{< /alert >}}

There are three distinct types of dependent clauses: 

(1) Subordinate clauses

(2) Relative clauses 

(3) Noun clauses

Let’s continue by discussing subordinate clauses first.
