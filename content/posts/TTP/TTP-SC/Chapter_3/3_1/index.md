## Lesson Content
At this point, we already know that any English sentence must contain at least one subject and at least one finite verb serving as the main verb of the sentence.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Any English sentence must contain at least one subject and at least one finite verb serving as the main verb of the sentence.
{{< /alert >}}

We also know that finite verbs are special in the sense that they allow us to determine when in time a subject performs an action.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Finite verbs are special in the sense that they allow us to determine when in time a subject performs an action.
{{< /alert >}}

In addition, we know that the finite verb found in an independent clause is referred to as a main verb.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The finite verb found in an independent clause is referred to as a main verb.
{{< /alert >}}

Finally, we know that one of the tricks of the GMAT is to present us with collections of words lacking either a subject or a main verb to see whether we’ll accept these collections of words as sentences.

We’re going to begin this chapter with a discussion of how a subject and its finite verb in any clause must agree in number.
