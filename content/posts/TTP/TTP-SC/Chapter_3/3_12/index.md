## Lesson Content
Like other collective nouns that we have discussed, the collective nouns “majority” and “minority” can be considered singular or plural depending on the contexts in which they appear.

In the following sentences, “majority” and “minority” are plural:

> A <strong>majority of the students</strong> are already applying for jobs.

> In today’s world, in which most people have some knowledge of geography, only a <strong>small minority</strong> believe Earth to be flat.

Logic dictates that the first sentence is conveying that it is the individual students who are applying for jobs, not the majority itself that is applying for jobs.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: A <strong>majority</strong> of the students <strong>is</strong> already applying for jobs.

So, in this case, we need the plural “are” to go with “A majority of the students.”

Similarly, in the second sentence, the minority itself is not what believes Earth to be flat.

In the following sentences, “majority” and “minority” are singular:

> A two-thirds <strong>majority of the ministers</strong> <strong>was</strong> required for the passage of a new amendment.

> A large enough <strong>minority is</strong> sufficient for blocking passage of a new amendment.

In the two examples above, the sentences are not focused on the ministers but rather on the size of the majority as a whole and size of the minority as a whole.

💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Like other collective nouns that we have discussed, the collective nouns “majority” and “minority” can be considered singular or plural depending on the contexts in which they appear. Also, like other collective nouns, “majority,” and “minority” can be used alone or combined with prepositional phrases.

##  Sentence Correction Example 12  

Even <u>if it is only a small minority of people who has extreme political beliefs, when</u> they are vocal, they can gain significant political power.

1. [ ] (A) if it is only a small minority of people who has extreme political beliefs, when
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) if there is only a small minority of people who has extreme political beliefs, if
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) if only a small minority of people have extreme political beliefs, when 
4. [ ] (D) when being only a small minority of people that have extreme political beliefs, if
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) when only a small minority of people has extreme political beliefs, when
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



> <b>(A)</b> Even <b>if it is only a small minority of people who has extreme political beliefs, when
</b> they are vocal, they can gain significant political power.



Logically, it is the people who make up the “small minority,” not the minority itself, who have “beliefs.” Thus, we don’t need the singular verb “has” to pair with the singular “minority.” Rather, we need the plural “have” to pair with the plural “people.”


Notice also that the phrasing “Even if <em>it is</em> only a small minority of people …” is unnecessarily wordy, and thus the meaning conveyed is somewhat muddled. A clearer, more straightforward phrasing would be “Even if only a small minority of people ….” 


> <b>(B)</b> Even <b>if there is only a small minority of people who has extreme political beliefs, if
</b> they are vocal, they can gain significant political power.



Logically, it is the people who make up the “small minority,” not the minority itself, who have “beliefs.” Thus, we don’t need the singular verb “has” to pair with the singular “minority.” Rather, we need the plural “have” to pair with the plural “people.”


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C)</b> Even <b>if only a small minority of people have extreme political beliefs, when 
</b> they are vocal, they can gain significant political power.



This choice correctly pairs the plural verb “have” with the plural noun “people,” logically indicating that the people themselves, not the “minority,” have “beliefs.”


This choice also does not include any of the unnecessary wordiness or unclear meanings we see in some other choices. The sentence meaning is clear and logical, and the sentence contains no grammatical errors.


> <b>(D)</b> Even <b>when being only a small minority of people that have extreme political beliefs, if
</b> they are vocal, they can gain significant political power.



This choice correctly pairs the plural verb “have” with the plural noun “people,” logically indicating that the people themselves, not the “minority,” have “beliefs.” However, it is never correct to refer to people using the pronoun “that.” We need the pronoun “who” to refer to “people.”


Additionally, the phrasing “when <em>being</em> only a small minority of people” conveys an unclear and illogical meaning, seeming to indicate that people are actively making some kind of personal choice to either be or not be a small minority at certain times. Clearly, such a meaning is nonsensical. 


> <b>(E)</b> Even <b>when only a small minority of people has extreme political beliefs, when
</b> they are vocal, they can gain significant political power.



 This choice may be attractive because it is so succinct and because, whereas choice (C) — the actually correct choice — uses “if” and “when,” this choice uses “when” in both the first and second half of the sentence. However, in reality, there is nothing illogical or incorrect about using “if” and “when,” as opposed to repeating “when,” in this context. 


More importantly, this choice incorrectly uses the singular verb “has,” illogically indicating that the “minority,” not the “people,” is what has “beliefs.” Of course, the people themselves have “beliefs,” and so we need the plural verb “have” to pair with the plural noun “people.”


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

##  Sentence Correction Example 13  

Realizing <u>that the undecided voters, becoming a smaller group as Election Day approached, with a majority of the votes was required</u> for winning, the candidates campaigned intensely.

1. [ ] (A) that the undecided voters, becoming a smaller group as Election Day approached, with a majority of the votes was required
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) that the group of undecided voters were becoming smaller as Election Day approached and that a majority of the votes were required
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) that the group of undecided voters was becoming smaller as Election Day approached and that a majority of the votes was required
4. [ ] (D) that, as a group, the undecided voters were becoming smaller as Election Day approached and that the majority of the votes were a requirement
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) that the undecided voters was becoming a smaller group as Election Day approached and that a majority of the votes were required
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



The sentence mentions two collective entities, “the group of undecided voters” and “a majority of the votes.”


The sentence says that “the group of undecided voters” was becoming smaller. Since it does not make sense that the undecided voters themselves were becoming smaller, it must be that it is the group as a whole that was becoming smaller. So, the correct verb to go with “the group of undecided voters” is the singular “was,” as in “the group … was becoming smaller.”


Regarding “a majority of the votes,” the point of the sentence is not that certain individual votes were required for winning. The point is that winning requires a particular thing, “a majority of the votes.” So, “a majority of the votes” is best understood to be a singular subject that goes with the singular verb “was.”


> <b>(A)</b> Realizing <b>that the undecided voters, becoming a smaller group as Election Day approached, with a majority of the votes was required
</b> for winning, the candidates campaigned intensely.



This awkwardly constructed version says that “the undecided voters … with a majority of the votes was required for winning.” What that statement means is not entirely clear. Are the undecided voters without a majority of the votes not required for winning?


Also, “voters” does not agree with the singular “was.”


Also, it does not quite make sense to describe “the undecided voters” as “becoming a smaller group.” A group itself would become a smaller group. The voters in the group would not become a smaller group.


> <b>(B)</b> Realizing <b>that the group of undecided voters were becoming smaller as Election Day approached and that a majority of the votes were required
</b> for winning, the candidates campaigned intensely.



In this version, “the group of undecided voters” is paired with the plural verb “were becoming.” Since “were becoming” does not agree with the singular “group,” “were becoming” must go with the plural “voters.” Thus, version conveys the nonsensical meaning that the undecided voters themselves were becoming smaller.


Also, “a majority of the votes were required for winning” does not clearly convey that a majority of the votes was required for winning. Rather, the use of “were” indicates that the focus is on the individual votes themselves. So, that clause conveys the nonsensical meaning that most of the votes were required for winning, but some votes were not, as if there were two kinds of votes, required ones and not required ones.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C)</b> Realizing <b>that the group of undecided voters was becoming smaller as Election Day approached and that a majority of the votes was required
</b> for winning, the candidates campaigned intensely.



By pairing “the group of undecided voters” with the singular “was” this version indicates that the group of undecided voters as a whole was becoming smaller, a meaning that makes sense.


Also, “a majority of the votes was required for winning” clearly conveys that, in order to win, a candidate had to have a majority of the votes.


> <b>(D)</b> Realizing <b>that, as a group, the undecided voters were becoming smaller as Election Day approached and that the majority of the votes were a requirement
</b> for winning, the candidates campaigned intensely.



By saying, “as a group, the undecided voters were becoming smaller,” this version conveys the nonsensical meaning, that, as a group, the undecided voters themselves were becoming smaller people.


Also, “the majority of the votes would be a requirement for winning” is awkward and not quite logical. It seems to convey that some votes, the majority of them, are “a requirement” while other votes are not “a requirement.”


> <b>(E)</b> Realizing <b>that the undecided voters was becoming a smaller group as Election Day approached and that a majority of the votes were required
</b> for winning, the candidates campaigned intensely.



In this version, the plural “undecided voters” is incorrectly paired with the singular “was.”


Also, in saying, “the undecided voters was becoming a smaller group,” this version does not clearly convey that the group was becoming smaller. The statement illogically focuses on what the individual voters were becoming, as if voters themselves could become a smaller group.


Also, “a majority of the votes were required for winning” does not clearly convey that a majority of the votes was required for winning. Rather, because the use of “were” indicates that the focus is on the individual votes themselves, that clause conveys the somewhat nonsensical meaning that most of the votes were required, but some votes were not, as if there were two kinds of votes, required ones and not required ones.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

Let’s now briefly discuss a strategy to use in a case in which whether a collective noun should be considered singular or considered plural is not clear.
