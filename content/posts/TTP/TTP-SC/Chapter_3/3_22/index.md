## Lesson Content
Like “number,” “percentage,” in a sentence subject, can be preceded by either “the” or “a.”

When, in a subject, “percentage” is preceded by “the,” the sentence is about the percentage itself, and so the subject is singular, as are the subjects in the following examples.

> <strong>The percentage</strong> of people in America who do note vote <strong>is </strong>astounding.

> Most experts believed that <strong>the percentage</strong> of cars represented by the electric car category <strong>was</strong> going to increase rapidly.

In each of the examples above, “the percentage of” is used, because each sentence is conveying something about a percentage, which is, of course, a singular subject.

Note that the presence of “the percentage of” does not affect a verb contained within a relative clause modifying a noun in the subject.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A subject that begins with “the percentage of” takes a singular verb.
{{< /alert >}}

In contrast, when, in a subject, “percentage” is preceded by “a,” the sentence is focused not on the percentage itself but rather on what the percentage is a percentage of.

Consider the following examples:

> <strong>A percentage</strong> of the water <strong>is</strong> not contaminated.

> <strong>A percentage</strong> of the lakes <strong>are</strong> relatively unpolluted.

The first sentence is about “a percentage of the water.” Since “water” is singular, the subject, “a percentage of the water,” is paired with the singular “is.”

The second sentence is about “a percentage of the lakes.” Since “lakes” is plural, the subject is paired with the plural “are.”

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A subject that begins with “a percentage of” takes a verb that agrees in number with what the percentage is a percentage of.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Sentence Correction Example 22  

While the number of milkweed plants available to monarch <u>butterflies have decreased with increased herbicide use, an increase in the percentage of gardeners surveyed who indicate</u> that they are planting native milkweed to help with addressing the issue.

1. [ ] (A) butterflies have decreased with increased herbicide use, an increase in the percentage of gardeners surveyed who indicate
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) butterflies has been decreased from increased herbicide use, an increase in the percentage of gardeners surveyed indicates
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) butterflies has been decreasing by increasing herbicide use, as an increasing percentage, the gardeners surveyed indicate
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) butterflies has decreased with increased herbicide use, an increasing percentage of gardeners who are surveyed indicate
5. [ ] (E) butterflies have decreased by increased herbicide use, the increasing percentage of gardeners surveyed indicate
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

##### Question Level: medium



The correct version of the sentence consists of a subordinate clause, “while the number … has …,” and an independent clause, “an increasing percentage of gardeners … indicate ….”


The subject of the subordinate clause is “the number of milkweed plants.” Since “the number” is always singular, in the correct version, the main verb of the subordinate clause is the singular “has decreased.”


In the correct version, the subject of the independent clause is “an increasing percentage of gardeners.” Since this subject begins with “an … percentage,” what the percentage is a percentage of determines whether the subject is singular or plural. Since the percentage is a percentage of gardeners, which is plural, the use of the plural verb “indicate” is correct.


Meanwhile, since there are in this question multiple answer choices that are basically grammatically correct, once we determine which choices have subjects and verbs that agree, we have to use meaning to find our way to the one correct answer.


> <b>(A)</b> While the number of milkweed plants available to monarch <b>butterflies have decreased with increased herbicide use, an increase in the percentage of gardeners surveyed who indicate
</b> that they are planting native milkweed to help with addressing the issue.



The singular subject “the number of milkweed plants” is incorrectly paired with the plural verb “have.”


Also, this version has no independent clause. It begins with a subordinate clause, “while the number … have …,” and then what follows is simply a phrase that begins with “an increase” and includes a relative clause, “who indicate …,” but does not include a main verb or independent clause.


> <b>(B)</b> While the number of milkweed plants available to monarch <b>butterflies has been decreased from increased herbicide use, an increase in the percentage of gardeners surveyed indicates
</b> that they are planting native milkweed to help with addressing the issue.



While the singular subject “the number of milkweed plants” is correctly paired with the singular verb “has,” the wording “has been decreased from increased herbicide use” conveys an unclear and not quite logical meaning.


How exactly would a “number” have “been decreased <em>from</em> increased herbicide use”? Since “from” means, basically, “starting at,” if anything, this version seems to convey the nonsensical meaning that “the number” started at “increased herbicide use” and “has been decreased from” there.


The sentence might almost make sense if “by” were used instead of “from,” as in, “the number … has been decreased by increased herbicide use,” but, as written, this version does not convey anything logical.


Also, the second clause conveys the illogical meaning that an increase in the percentage of gardeners surveyed (as in, more gardeners are surveyed than were surveyed previously), indicates that they are planting native milkweed to help with addressing the issue. How would the fact that the percentage of gardeners who are surveyed has increased indicate that gardeners are planting native milkweed? It wouldn’t.


> <b>(C)</b> While the number of milkweed plants available to monarch <b>butterflies has been decreasing by increasing herbicide use, as an increasing percentage, the gardeners surveyed indicate
</b> that they are planting native milkweed to help with addressing the issue.



The singular subject “the number of milkweed plants” is correctly paired with the plural verb “has been decreasing,” and this version is otherwise grammatically correct. However, we have to be careful not to be tricked into choosing this choice by the grammatical correctness of this version.


Notice that “the number of milkweed plants … has been decreasing by increasing herbicide use” conveys the nonsensical idea that the number of milkweed plants has been decreasing itself by increasing herbicide use.


Also, while “the gardeners” is correctly paired with the plural verb “indicate,” “as an increasing percentage, the gardeners surveyed” suggests the nonsensical meaning that all of the gardeners surveyed are “an increasing percentage.” How would gardeners surveyed <em>be</em> an increasing percentage? They wouldn’t, and even if they would, the sentence doesn’t say what the gardeners surveyed are an increasing percentage of.


So, this version is an illogical mess.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D)</b> While the number of milkweed plants available to monarch <b>butterflies has decreased with increased herbicide use, an increasing percentage of gardeners who are surveyed indicate
</b> that they are planting native milkweed to help with addressing the issue.



The singular subject “the number of milkweed plants” is correctly paired with the singular verb “has,” the plural subject “an increasing percentage of gardeners” is correctly paired with the plural verb “indicate,” and the sentence is otherwise grammatically correct and conveys a meaning that makes sense.


> <b>(E)</b> While the number of milkweed plants available to monarch <b>butterflies have decreased by increased herbicide use, the increasing percentage of gardeners surveyed indicate
</b> that they are planting native milkweed to help with addressing the issue.



The singular subject “the number of milkweed plants” is incorrectly paired with the plural verb “have.”


Also, by using the preposition “by,” the sentence seems to suggest the nonsensical idea that the number of milkweed plants has decreased itself “by” increased herbicide use.


Finally, the singular “the increasing percentage” is incorrectly paired with the plural “indicate.”


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)
