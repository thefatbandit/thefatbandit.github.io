## Lesson Content
We have discussed that subjects that begin with the SANAMM pronouns can be singular or plural depending on the nouns to which the pronouns refer.

Consider the following examples:

> <strong>Half of the class</strong> <strong>is</strong> going on a field trip.

> <strong>One-third of the students have</strong> shown some interest in geology.

In the first sentence, the fraction “half” is part of a single entity, “the class.” Therefore, the subject, “half of the class,” takes a singular verb, “is.”

In the second sentence also, we have a fraction, “one-third,” but the fraction is a fraction of a plural noun, “students.” Therefore, the subject, “one-third of the students,” must take a plural verb, “have.”

Notice that, in these situations involving fractions, we cannot ignore the prepositional phrase that comes after the fraction in the subject.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A subject that begins with a fraction such as “half” or “one-fourth” can take a singular or plural verb depending on what the fraction is a fraction of. When a subject begins with a fraction, we cannot ignore a prepositional phrase that comes between the fraction and the main verb.

##### Question Level: medium



Every version of the sentence in this question includes two fractions that function as the subjects of clauses.


The first fraction is “one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet of vehicles on the roads.” Since the fraction “one hundredth” is a fraction of the singular “fleet,” this fraction takes a singular verb. Notice, this fraction is not a fraction of the vehicles on the roads. “Vehicles on the roads” is a part of a prepositional phrase, “of the vehicles on the roads,” that modifies “fleet.”


The second fraction is “one-tenth of the cars on the road.” Since the fraction “one-tenth” is a fraction of the plural “cars,” this fraction takes a plural verb.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(A)</b> <b>Whereas not even one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet of vehicles on the roads is made up of electric vehicles, in Norway, by 2018, one-tenth of the cars on the road were
</b> electric.



The singular “is” agrees with the singular “one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet,” the plural “were” agrees with the plural “one-tenth of the cars on the road,” and this version conveys a meaning that makes sense.


> <b>(B)</b> <b>While not even one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet of road vehicles makes up electric vehicles, in Norway, as of 2018, one-tenth of the cars on the road were once
</b> electric.



While the singular “makes” agrees with the singular “one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet,” and the plural “were” agrees with the plural “one-tenth of the cars on the road,” the meaning conveyed by this version does not make sense.


First this version conveys the nonsensical meaning that one-hundredth of the fleet of vehicles makes up electric cars, as if electric cars are made of a fleet of vehicles.


Then, by using the wording “were once,” this version conveys the illogical idea that one-tenth of the cars on the road in Norway “were once” electric, implying that the same cars are no longer electric.


> <b>(C)</b> <b>Since not even one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet of vehicles on the roads is made up of electric vehicles, in Norway, by 2018, one-tenth of the cars on the road had been
</b> electric.



This version is basically grammatically correct but conveys a meaning that does not make sense.


First the conjunction “since” indicates that what’s expressed by the second clause is the effect of what’s expressed in the first clause, but it does not make sense that one-tenth of the cars on the roads in Norway would be electric “since” not even one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet of vehicles is made up of electric vehicles.


Also, this version expresses that the cars in Norway “had been” electric, as if the cars were electric and then changed to not electric.


> <span class="incorrect-answer"><i class="fal fa-times-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D)</b> <b>Though not even one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet of road vehicles are made up of electric vehicles, in Norway, as of 2018, one-tenth of the cars on the road was
</b> electric.



The plural “are” does not agree with the singular “one-hundredth of the fleet.”


Also, the singular “was” does not agree with the plural “one-tenth of the cars.”


> <b>(E)</b> <b>With not even one-hundredth of the worldwide fleet of road vehicles making up electric vehicles, in Norway, since 2018, one-tenth of the cars on the road has been
</b> electric.



The singular “has been” does not agree with the plural “one-tenth of the cars.”


Also, the beginning of this sentence conveys a meaning that does not make sense, that the fleet of vehicles “make up” electric vehicles, as if electric vehicles are made of a fleet of vehicles.


Also, the use of “with” at the beginning of the sentence does not make sense, as the sentence mentions nothing that is “with” anything else.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
