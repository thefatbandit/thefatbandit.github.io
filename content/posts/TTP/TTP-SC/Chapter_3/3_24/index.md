## Lesson Content
The rules we follow in situations in which portions are described via the use of the word “percent” are similar to the ones we follow in situations in which subjects involve fractions.

Consider the following examples:

> <strong>Ninety percent of the board was</strong> present.

> <strong>Fifty percent of the board members are</strong> in favor of the acquisition.

Notice that the first sentence is about “ninety percent” of “the board.” Since “the board” is singular, “ninety percent of the board” agrees with the singular verb “was.”

The second sentence, on the other hand, is about “fifty percent” of “the board members.” Since “board members” is plural, we need the plural verb “are.”

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In a situation involving a subject that is a portion described via the use of the word “percent,” whether the subject is singular or plural depends on whether what the portion is a portion of is singular or plural.
{{< /alert >}}

##### Question Level: medium



In the various versions of the sentence, two portions are mentioned, “80 percent of those responding to the survey” and “half of the respondents.” While the wording changes somewhat from version to version, in all versions, both portions are portions of plural entities, “those responding” and “respondents.” So, both portions are considered plural subjects.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A)</b> <b>While close to 80 percent of the survey respondents indicate that they take calls while driving, only about half of the respondents believe that their
</b> doing so does not negatively impact their driving performance.



The plural subject “80 percent of those responding” is correctly paired with the plural “indicate,” and the plural “half of the respondents” is correctly paired with the plural “believe,” and the sentence conveys a meaning that makes sense.


Notice that the possibly unfamiliar wording “their doing so” is used to make this correct answer seem a little off.


> <b>(B)</b> <b>Close to 80 percent of respondents to the survey indicates taking calls while driving, though only about half believes that
</b> doing so does not negatively impact their driving performance.



The plural subject “80 percent of respondents” is incorrectly paired with the singular “indicates.”


Also, in the second clause, the singular “believes” does not agree with “half,” even though “half” may seem to be singular, because “half” is understood to mean “half of the respondents,” which is plural.


> <b>(C)</b> <b>With close to 80 percent of the survey respondents who indicate that they take calls while driving, only about half of the respondents believe that
</b> doing so does not negatively impact their driving performance.



While this version is basically grammatically correct, it does not convey a logical meaning, as it conveys that half of the respondents believe that taking calls while driving does not negatively affect their driving performance “with” 80 percent of the respondents, and it does not make sense that half of the respondents are “with” 80 percent of the respondents.


In order not to be fooled into choosing a choice like this one, we have to be careful to go beyond checking for grammar issues to considering the entire version of the sentence and the meaning that it conveys.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>Although close to 80 percent of the respondents to the survey indicate taking calls while driving, only about half of them believes that their
</b> doing so does not negatively impact their driving performance.



The plural “half of the them” is incorrectly paired with the singular “believes.”


Also, “indicate taking calls while driving” does not convey a clear meaning. Are the respondents indicating that they are “taking calls while driving,” that others are “taking calls while driving”? What exactly do they indicate about taking calls while driving?


> <b>(E)</b> <b>Though there are close to 80 percent of the respondents to the survey who indicate having taken calls while driving, only about half believes
</b> doing so does not negatively impact their driving performance.



The plural “indicate” agrees with what the relative pronoun “who” refers to, the plural “respondents.”


However, the first clause, “there are close to 80 percent of the respondents who indicate …,” conveys the illogical meaning that close to 80 percent of the respondents who indicate having taken calls while driving simply exist. In other words, “there are close to 80 percent of the respondents” means, basically, that 80 percent of the respondents exist.


Also, in the second clause, the singular “believes” does not agree with “half,” even though “half” may seem to be singular, because “half” is understood to mean “half of the respondents,” which is plural.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)
