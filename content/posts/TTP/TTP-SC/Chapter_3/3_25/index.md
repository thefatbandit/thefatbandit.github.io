## Lesson Content
In English, nouns that end in -s are often plural.

<sup>1 </sup>Datum/Data: While “datum” and “data” are, strictly speaking, the singular and plural of “datum,” usage has evolved, and currently, “data” can be used to convey a singular meaning or a plural meaning.

<sup>2</sup> Medium/Media: When “media” refers to the press, it is often considered a collective noun that takes a singular verb (e.g., “The media is out in full force”).

Let’s look at some examples of these words in sentences:

> An alumnus <strong>receives</strong> 10 percent off of all costs of admission to university events.

> The alumni <strong>receive</strong> 10 percent off of all costs of admission to university events.

> The criterion for the competition <strong>is</strong> too strict.

> The criteria for the competition <strong>are</strong> too strict.

> The syllabus <strong>is</strong> very detailed.

> The syllabi <strong>are</strong> very detailed.

Furthermore, there are some nouns that appear to be plural, but are actually singular.

> Mathematics <strong>is</strong> my worst subject.

> Good news <strong>makes</strong> me smile.

> Diabetes <strong>is</strong> on the rise in the United States.

> The mumps <strong>is</strong> a disease that affects only humans.

Note that some of these nouns that appear to be plural are fields of study that end in –ics, such as “politics,” “economics,” “ethics,” “statistics,” “linguistics,” and the aforementioned “mathematics.” When used to denote a branch of study, these nouns are always singular.

> His politics <strong>get</strong> in the way of his personal relationships.

> There <strong>aren’t</strong> any statistics to bolster the claim.

> The ethics of eating meat <strong>are</strong> a primary concern for many vegetarians and vegans.

> The economics of restaurant ownership <strong>make</strong> success a one-in-a-million shot.

##### Question Level: medium



A key element of correctly answering this question is understanding that “nuclei” is not a singular noun. We don’t necessarily have to choose a choice on the basis of whether a verb agrees with “nuclei,” but if we were to mistakenly think that “nuclei” is a singular noun, then we would rule out the correct answer, in which “nuclei” is the subject of and is paired with a plural verb in a clause.


Meanwhile, some of the incorrect choices in this question have subject-verb agreement errors, and all of the incorrect choices convey illogical or nonsensical meanings.


> <b>(A)</b> For many years, few considered <b>the possible disease that occurs when cell nuclei is not functioning well, because people thought nuclei was
</b> so important for cell function that cells without properly functioning nuclei would simply die.



Since “nuclei” is plural, neither “nuclei is” nor “nuclei was” is correct, since “is” and “was” are singular.


Also, this version does not convey a clear, logical meaning. For one thing, what “few considered the possible disease that occurs” means is not clear, as it does not clearly convey in what sense or in what context people were not considering the disease.


Also, “the possible disease that occurs” is not logical. If the disease occurs, then it is clearly possible, so saying that the disease is a possible disease that occurs does not make sense.


So, this version is written to sound as if it says something but actually doesn’t convey much of anything.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B)</b> For many years, few considered <b>the possibility that diseases occur when cell nuclei are not functioning well, because people thought nuclei to be
</b> so important for cell function that cells without properly functioning nuclei would simply die.



In this version, the plural “nuclei” is correctly paired with the plural verb “are.”


Also “few considered the possibility that” makes sense, and this version correctly uses the idiomatic expression “thought … to be” to clearly convey a logical meaning.


> <b>(C)</b> For many years, few considered <b>the disease that occurs, possibly when cell nuclei are not functioning well, because of people thinking that nuclei were
</b> so important for cell function that cells without properly functioning nuclei would simply die.



In this version, the plural “nuclei” is correctly paired with the plural “are” and “were.”


However, this version conveys a nonsensical meaning, that few people considered <em>the disease that occurs because of people thinking that nuclei were important</em>.


> <b>(D)</b> For many years, few considered <b>diseases possibly occurring when cell nuclei was not functioning well, because people thinking about nuclei were
</b> so important for cell function that cells without properly functioning nuclei would simply die.



In this version, the plural “nuclei” is incorrectly paired with the singular “was.”


Also, this sentence does not convey a logical meaning, the most blatant meaning issue being the fact that the sentence conveys that <em>people thinking about nuclei were so important</em> for cell function that cells without properly functioning nuclei would simply die.


> <b>(E)</b> For many years, few considered <b>diseases that possibly occur when cell nuclei is not functioning well, because of people who were thinking that nuclei is
</b> so important for cell function that cells without properly functioning nuclei would simply die.



In this version, the plural “nuclei” is twice incorrectly paired with the singular “is.”


Also, because this version uses the wording “because of people who were thinking that nuclei is so important,” this version is not quite logical, as it does not make sense that few people considered something <em>because of people </em>who were thinking something. The point to be made is that few people considered something about certain diseases because <em>people in general were thinking </em>something else about those diseases.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
