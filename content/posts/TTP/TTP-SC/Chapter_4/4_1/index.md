## Lesson Content
As we already know, a pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun. We use pronouns so that we don’t have to repeat nouns in sentences.
{{< /alert >}}

For example:

> <strong>Sara</strong> is a skilled artist, and <strong>she </strong>consistently produces intriguing work.

The pronoun “she” takes the place of the noun “Sara.” We refer, then, to “Sara” as the antecedent (or the referent) of the pronoun “she.” Having the pronoun “she” available allows us not to have to repeat the noun “Sara.”

So, pronouns give us flexibility in writing.

However, errors in pronoun usage are common, and such errors significantly erode the clarity of sentences, and flawed pronoun usage is common in Sentence Correction questions.

As this chapter unfolds, we’ll examine a number of pronoun usage issues that commonly appear in Sentence Correction questions.
