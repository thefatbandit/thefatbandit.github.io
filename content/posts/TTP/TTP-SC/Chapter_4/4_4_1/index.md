## Lesson Content
Unlike many other authorities on English grammar, which do not consider it acceptable for a noun in the possessive form to be the antecedent of a pronoun, the GMAT does consider it acceptable for a noun in the possessive form to be the antecedent of a pronoun.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The GMAT considers it acceptable for a noun in the possessive form to be the antecedent of a pronoun.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s unpack this idea.

Nouns in the possessive form are also adjectives.

> The <strong>boss’s </strong>dog is running fast.

> The <strong>fish’s </strong>eggs will hatch soon.

> <strong>Jim Morrison’s</strong> music is delightful.

> <strong>California’s</strong> climate is beautiful.

> <strong>Sara’s </strong>car is very clean.

For example, which dog? “The boss’s dog.” For example, which climate? “California’s climate.” For example, which car? “Sara’s car.”

Although these nouns in the possessive form are essentially adjectives, the GMAT would consider it acceptable for pronouns to have these nouns as antecedents.

> The <strong>boss’s </strong>dog is running fast, and <strong>his </strong>cat is taking a nap.

> The <strong>fish’s </strong>eggs will hatch soon; <strong>it</strong> will then begin to swim upstream with <strong>its</strong> babies.

> <strong>Jim Morrison’s</strong> music is delightful, but <strong>his</strong> poetry is ethereal.

> <strong>California’s</strong> climate is beautiful; <strong>it</strong> is a wonderful place to live.

> <strong>Sara’s </strong>car is very clean because <strong>she</strong> spent all day washing it.

So, what does this information mean to us? What this information means to us is that, if in answering a Sentence Correction question, we see a choice that has a pronoun referring to a noun in the possessive form, we should not eliminate that choice just because it has a pronoun referring to a noun in the possessive form.

Many students come to TTP under the illusion that it is not acceptable for a noun in the possessive form to be the antecedent of a pronoun.

Let’s now discuss another type of pronoun error: pronoun-antecedent disagreement.
