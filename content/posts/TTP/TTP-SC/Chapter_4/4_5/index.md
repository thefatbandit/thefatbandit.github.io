## Lesson Content
Just as subjects must agree in number with their verbs, pronouns and antecedents must agree.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A pronoun must agree in number with its antecedent. A singular pronoun must refer to a singular antecedent, and a plural pronoun must refer to a plural antecedent.
{{< /alert >}}

To ensure pronoun-antecedent agreement, when there is a pronoun in a sentence, identify its antecedent and check for agreement.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: The prices at the department store are set high, and <strong>it</strong> is marked down to normal levels on sale days.

We see the singular pronoun “it.” Once we find the pronoun, the next step is to look for the antecedent to ensure that it agrees in number with the pronoun.

> The <strong>prices</strong> at the department store are set high, and <strong>they</strong> are marked down to normal levels on sale days.

Of course, in a GMAT question, the agreement problem might be solved in a less obvious way, such as through eliminating the pronoun entirely:

> The prices at the department store are set high and then marked down to normal levels on sale days.

Let’s consider another example:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: Because the city is well managed, their tax revenue often exceeds their expenses.

We see the plural possessive pronoun “their.” The antecedent is “city,” a singular noun, so the pronoun and its antecedent do not agree.

> Because the <strong>city</strong> is well managed, <strong>its</strong> tax revenue often exceeds <strong>its</strong> expenses.

##  Sentence Correction Example 9  

<u>When the company, releasing three new models, their profits did not grow</u> as much as had been anticipated.

1. [ ] (A) When the company, releasing three new models, their profits did not grow
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) The company’s releasing three new models had profit growth that was not
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) Although the company has released three new models, their profits have not grown
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) The company, though releasing three new models, its profits did not grow
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) Though the company had released three new models, its profits did not grow

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



A company is a single entity. So, unless a sentence somehow indicates that “company” refers to the people at the company, it makes the most sense to consider “company” singular and to refer to it with the singular pronoun “it.”


In this case in particular, the sentence is mentioning the profits of the company as a whole. So, clearly, the use of the singular possessive pronoun “its” with “profits” makes the most sense.


> <b>(A)</b> <b>When the company, releasing three new models, their profits did not grow
</b> as much as had been anticipated.



Since three new models would not have profits that would grow when the company released the models, the profits mentioned would be the company’s profits. Thus, the plural “their” incorrectly refers to the singular “company.”


Also, the first part of the sentence does not make sense, as “when the company” should begin a clause, but there is no verb present to complete such a clause. “Releasing,” a participle, cannot on its own serve as the main verb of a clause and is instead part of the modifier “releasing three new models.”


> <b>(B)</b> <b>The company’s releasing three new models had profit growth that was not
</b> as much as had been anticipated.



While this version is concise and grammatically correct, it conveys a nonsensical meaning, that the releasing of three new models had profit growth. Releasing three new models might result in profit growth, but releasing three new models would not itself have profit growth.


> <b>(C)</b> <b>Although the company has released three new models, their profits have not grown
</b> as much as had been anticipated.



Since three new models would not have profits that would grow when the company released the models, the profits mentioned would be the company’s profits. Thus, the plural “their” incorrectly refers to the singular “company.”


Notice that the use of the singular “has released” with “company” provides some confirmation that “company” is considered singular in this sentence. “Company” must be singular or plural. So, either “has” or “their” must be incorrect.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>The company, though releasing three new models, its profits did not grow
</b> as much as had been anticipated.



While this version correctly uses the singular “its” with the singular antecedent “company,” the sentence is illogically constructed. Notice that the subject of the sentence is “it’s profits” and that “the company” is simply tacked onto the beginning of the sentence and not connected to the sentence in any logical way.


Also, the modifier “though releasing three new models,” which is in the present tense, does not clearly convey that the company released the new models before experiencing lower than expected profit growth. So, the sentence does not effectively indicate the sequence of events.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E)</b> <b>Though the company had released three new models, its profits did not grow
</b> as much as had been anticipated.



In this version, the singular “its” agrees with its antecedent, “company.”


Also, this version clearly indicates the sequence of events and the relationship between the release of the new models and the lower than anticipated profit growth.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)
