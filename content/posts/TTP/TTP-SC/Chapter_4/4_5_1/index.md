## Lesson Content
Pronouns that refer to “each” or “every” must be singular, regardless of whether “each” or “every” appears at the beginning of a phrase that contains a plural noun or that describes a group.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Pronouns that correctly refer to “each” or “every” are always singular, regardless of whether “each” or “every” begins a phrase that contains a plural noun or that describes a group.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following sentences:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: <strong>Each </strong>of the theories <strong>were </strong>examined, and then <strong>they were</strong> categorized according to degree of plausibility.

> Correct: <strong>Each </strong>of the theories <strong>was </strong>examined, and then <strong>it was</strong> categorized according to degree of plausibility.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: <strong>Every one</strong> of the chemicals discussed in the report <strong>are</strong> widely used even though <strong>they are </strong>highly toxic.

> Correct: <strong>Every one</strong> of the chemicals discussed in the report <strong>is</strong> widely used even though <strong>it</strong> is highly toxic.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: <strong>Every</strong> ecologist affiliated with the organization <strong>should</strong> attend the meeting and <strong>provide</strong> an update on the progress <strong>their</strong> team has made.

> Correct: <strong>Every</strong> ecologist affiliated with the organization should attend the meeting and provide an update on the progress <strong>her</strong> team has made.

Notice that, although these sentences contain plural nouns, such as “theories” or “chemicals,” or mention groups, the pronouns used are singular.

Here are some more examples of sentences that contain phrases that begin with “each” or “every” and contain singular pronouns that refer to “each” or “every”:

> They will photograph <strong>each</strong> of the houses before they paint <strong>it</strong>.

> <strong>Each</strong> poet may submit up to five of <strong>his</strong> poems.

> They mentioned that <strong>every</strong> one of the animals that they had observed rears <strong>its</strong> young in a unique way.

> <strong>Every</strong> cyclist on the team was tested for drug use regardless of how <strong>she</strong> had placed.
