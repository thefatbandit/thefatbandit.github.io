## Lesson Content
A pronoun’s case is its categorization into one of three types: subject, object, and possessive.

To see how pronouns of these types function in sentences, consider the following examples:

> <strong>I</strong> invited <strong>you</strong> to <strong>our</strong> Halloween party.

“I” is the subject pronoun.

> <strong>Everybody </strong>is hoping that the star player stays on <strong>his</strong> current team.

“Everybody” is the subject pronoun because it is doing the action of hoping.

> <strong>You</strong> should give <strong>your</strong> mother the flowers that <strong>they</strong> picked.

“You” is a subject pronoun.

> The SEAL Team refused to leave any of <strong>its</strong> members behind.

“Its” is a possessive pronoun.

> <strong>We</strong> asked <strong>him</strong> where <strong>their</strong> keys were.

“We” is a subject pronoun.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

If the pronoun is doing the action, a subject pronoun must be used. If the pronoun is being acted upon or is the object of a preposition, an object pronoun must be used. If the pronoun is indicating ownership, a possessive pronoun must be used.
{{< /alert >}}
