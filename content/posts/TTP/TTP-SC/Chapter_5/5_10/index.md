## Lesson Content
An absolute phrase is a phrase that:

> • consists of only a noun and its modifiers, and

> • modifies an entire clause.

To remember that absolute phrases modify entire clauses, people sometimes say, “Absolute phrases modify absolutely everything.”

The following sentence contains an absolute phrase.

> <strong>His work completed</strong>, John headed home for the night.

In this example, “his work completed” is an absolute phrase.

Here’s another:

> Jennifer sat waiting in the dentist chair, <strong>her arms and legs crossed</strong>.

The absolute phrase “her arms and legs crossed” contains the nouns “arms and legs,” along with modifiers “her” and “crossed.” The phrase modifies the entire clause “Jennifer sat waiting in the dentist chair,” describing how Jennifer sat in the dentist chair.

Here’s another:

> The staff members were tired and stressed out, <strong>the tense negotiations having dragged on for days</strong>.

The absolute phrase “the tense negotiations having dragged on for days” contains the noun “negotiations,” along with modifiers “tense” and “having dragged on for days.” The phrase modifies the entire clause “the staff members were tired and stressed out,” providing information explaining why the staff members were tired and stressed out.

Let’s now discuss two common ways in which absolute phrases are used.

<u>Two Ways in Which Absolute Phrases Are Used</u>

Absolute phrases are used in two ways:

> • To add descriptive information to a clause.

> • To add explanatory information to a clause.

In each of the following two examples, an absolute phrase adds descriptive information.

> The boat sat anchored, <strong>its sails billowing in the wind</strong>.

<u>Absolute Phrase</u>: “its sails billowing in the wind”

<u>Effect of Phrase</u>: Adds information describing how the boat was as it sat anchored.

> The chorus, <strong>their voices resonant and mellifluous</strong>, brought the house down with their rendition of the work.

<u>Absolute Phrase</u>: “their voices resonant and mellifluous”

<u>Effect of Phrase</u>: Describes how the voices of the chorus sounded as the chorus brought the house down.

In each of the following examples, an absolute phrase adds explanatory information.

> <strong>The weather being sunny and beautiful</strong>, we decided to walk rather than drive.

<u>Absolute Phrase</u>: “the weather being sunny and beautiful”

<u>Effect of Phrase</u>: Explains why we decided to walk rather than drive.

> <strong>Our ravenous appetites satiated</strong>, we asked the waiter to pack the rest of the five-course meal to go.

<u>Absolute Phrase</u>: “our ravenous appetites satiated”

<u>Effect of Phrase</u>: Explains why we asked the waiter to pack the rest of the meal.

> <strong>His flight canceled</strong>, Jack was forced to take a red-eye that had a two-hour layover in Phoenix.

<u>Absolute Phrase</u>: “his flight canceled”

<u>Effect of Phrase</u>: Explains why Jack was forced to take a different flight.
