## Lesson Content
Adjectives are modifiers that can modify only nouns or pronouns, while adverbs are modifiers that can modify verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Adjectives can modify only nouns or pronouns. Adverbs can modify verbs, adjectives, and other adverbs.
{{< /alert >}}

Here are some examples of common adjectives: hard, bright, pretty, thoughtless, spurious, enthusiastic, yellow, tentative, sonorous, new, good.

> The <strong>new </strong>book is now in stores.

> She made a <strong>thoughtless</strong> comment.

> The <strong>pretty </strong>horse is sleeping.

Here are some examples of common adverbs: occasionally, often, seldom, always, never, clearly, famously, judiciously, slowly, well.

> We run <strong>occasionally</strong>.

> The car approached them <strong>slowly</strong>.

> He spoke <strong>convincingly</strong>.

The most common use of an adverb is to modify a verb.

As you may have noticed from the adverb examples above, many, but not all, adverbs end with -ly.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A word ending in –ly typically will not modify a noun, because most –ly words are adverbs.
{{< /alert >}}
