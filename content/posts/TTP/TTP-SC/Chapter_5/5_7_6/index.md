## Lesson Content
The relative pronoun “whose” can refer to things or people.

> The car <strong>whose fender I dented</strong> belongs to the police chief.

> My mother, <strong>whose bracelet I borrowed</strong>, has always been willing to share.

> The dog <strong>whose paws are big</strong> also has a loving heart.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The relative pronoun “whose” can refer to things or people. “Whose” is the English language’s only possessive relative pronoun.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Sentence Correction Example 23  

The contestant <u>with the total number of points that are the highest</u> wins the Icarus Cup, a competition in which each person competing flies on his or her own power.

1. [ ] (A) with the total number of points that are the highest
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) whose total number of points is highest
3. [ ] (C) the total number of points of which is highest
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) that has the highest total number of points
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) having a total number of points being the highest
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



In this question, all of the answer choices modify “contestant.” The sentence indicates that people compete in the Icarus Cup. Thus, we can presume that “the contestant” is a human. Therefore, some answer choices can be eliminated on the basis of the fact that the relative pronouns “which” and “that” cannot be correctly used to refer to a human contestant.” The other answer choices can be evaluated on the basis of the degree to which the meanings that they convey are logical.


> <b>(A)</b> The contestant <b>with the total number of points that are the highest
</b> wins the Icarus Cup, a competition in which each person competing flies on his or her own power.



While this version may sound correct, a careful reading reveals that it is illogical, as it conveys that the winner is selected on the basis of how high the points themselves are rather than on the basis of how high the total number of points is. Points would not be high. The number of points would be.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(B)</b> The contestant <b>whose total number of points is highest
</b> wins the Icarus Cup, a competition in which each person competing flies on his or her own power.



This version correctly uses the possessive relative pronoun “whose” to refer to “contestant” and conveys a logical meaning.


> <b>(C)</b> The contestant <b>the total number of points of which is highest
</b> wins the Icarus Cup, a competition in which each person competing flies on his or her own power.



This version is a bit awkward, but it might be OK if it did not incorrectly use “which” to refer to the noun “contestant,” which represents a human.


> <b>(D)</b> The contestant <b>that has the highest total number of points
</b> wins the Icarus Cup, a competition in which each person competing flies on his or her own power.



This version incorrectly uses “that” to refer to the noun “contestant,” which represents a human.


> <b>(E)</b> The contestant <b>having a total number of points being the highest
</b> wins the Icarus Cup, a competition in which each person competing flies on his or her own power.



The use of the word “being” in this version results in the sentence’s conveying a somewhat illogical meaning, that the points or, possibly, the total number of points is actively “being the highest.” In other words, the version conveys that the contestant with the points that are “being the highest” wins.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
