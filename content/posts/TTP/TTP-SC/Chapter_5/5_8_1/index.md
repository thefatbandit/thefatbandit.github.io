## Lesson Content
Appositives usually, though not always, appear beside what they modify.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: My brother, running through the park, Eduardo saw a rare California condor.

The separation of “Eduardo” from what it modifies, “my brother,” results in an awkward, illogical sentence.

> Running through the park, my <strong>brother Eduardo</strong> saw a rare California condor.

Let’s look at another example:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: A brown dog, jumping up and down, Brownie was excited to see its owners.

The separation of the “brown dog” and what it modifies, “Brownie,” results in an awkward, illogical sentence.

> Jumping up and down, <strong>Brownie, a brown dog,</strong> was excited to see its owners.

Notice that, now, the appositive “a brown dog” clearly modifies “Brownie.”

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

An appositive will usually, but not always, appear directly beside what it modifies.
{{< /alert >}}

##  Sentence Correction Example 24  

<u>In 1901, the first working version of the radio was built by Marconi, an invention originally developed by Tesla.</u>

1. [ ] (A) In 1901, the first working version of the radio was built by Marconi, an invention originally developed by Tesla.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) The first working version of the radio, being an invention originally developed by Tesla, was built by Marconi in 1901.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Marconi built the first working version of the radio in 1901, which was an invention originally developed by Tesla.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) In 1901, Marconi built the first working version of the radio, an invention originally developed by Tesla.
5. [ ] (E) In 1901, the first working version of the radio, built by Marconi, had been an invention originally developed by Tesla.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Correctly answering this question takes noticing that only one version makes sense. The placement of modifiers in and the wording of other versions results in sentences that convey illogical meanings.


> <b>(A)</b> <b>In 1901, the first working version of the radio was built by Marconi, an invention originally developed by Tesla.
</b>


The placement of the appositive “an invention originally developed by Tesla” directly after “Marconi” results in the sentence’s seeming to convey the illogical meaning that Marconi was an invention developed by Tesla.


> <b>(B)</b> <b>The first working version of the radio, being an invention originally developed by Tesla, was built by Marconi in 1901.
</b>


This version illogically conveys that the first working version of the radio was being an invention developed by Tesla. The radio is an invention developed by Tesla, but not the first working version of the radio, and, also, saying that either the “first working version of the radio” or simply “the radio” was “being an invention” does not really make sense.


> <b>(C)</b> <b>Marconi built the first working version of the radio in 1901, which was an invention originally developed by Tesla.
</b>


Notice that “radio in 1901” is not a noun phrase that could be modified by “which was an invention …” since “in 1901” does not modify “radio” but, rather, modifies “built,” expressing when Marconi built. Thus, “which was an invention …” is illogically separated from what it’s meant to modify, “radio,” by “in 1901.”


As a result, “which was an invention …” seems to modify “1901,” rather than “the radio.”


So, this version seems to convey the nonsensical meaning that 1901 was an invention developed by Tesla.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D)</b> <b>In 1901, Marconi built the first working version of the radio, an invention originally developed by Tesla.
</b>


The appositive “an invention originally developed by Tesla” is placed so as to modify “the radio,” resulting in a sentence that effectively conveys a logical meaning.


> <b>(E)</b> <b>In 1901, the first working version of the radio, built by Marconi, had been an invention originally developed by Tesla.
</b>


This version conveys a somewhat illogical meaning: that, in 1901 the first working version of the radio built by Marconi had been an invention developed by Tesla.


For one thing, it would make much more sense to say that the radio was developed by Tesla and that the first working version was built by Marconi.


Also, even if we were to decide that it makes sense to say that the first working version was an invention developed by Tesla, saying that the radio had been an invention developed by Tesla in 1901 does not make sense, since it does not make sense to restrict the time when it was an invention developed by Tesla to 1901. Once it had been developed by Tesla, it was an invention developed by Tesla from then on, not just in 1901.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

##  Sentence Correction Example 25  

<u>Alan Blumlein is best known for his stereophonic recording research,</u> a method that encodes the relative positions of the sound sources recorded.

1. [ ] (A) Alan Blumlein is best known for his stereophonic recording research,
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) Alan Blumlein, best known for his research into stereophonic recording, which is
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) Alan Blumlein is best known for his research into stereophonic recording,
4. [ ] (D) Alan Blumlein’s research into stereophonic recording is how he is best known,
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Alan Blumlein, best known for his research into stereophonic recording, is
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



Correctly answering this question takes finding a version in which the combination of the non-underlined portion with an answer choice results in a logical sentence.


> <b>(A)</b> <b>Alan Blumlein is best known for his stereophonic recording research,
</b> a method that encodes the relative positions of the sound sources recorded.



In this version, the non-underlined portion is used as an appositive. This appositive, “a method …” is placed directly after and, therefore, seems to modify “research.” Since “research” is not a method that encodes the relative positions of sounds, this version does not make sense.


> <b>(B)</b> <b>Alan Blumlein, best known for his research into stereophonic recording, which is
</b> a method that encodes the relative positions of the sound sources recorded.



This version is composed of only a noun, “Alan Blumlein,” and a long modifier, “best known ….” Thus, this version has no main verb and, thus, is not a complete sentence.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C)</b> <b>Alan Blumlein is best known for his research into stereophonic recording,
</b> a method that encodes the relative positions of the sound sources recorded.



In this version, the non-underlined portion is used as an appositive. This appositive, “a method …” is placed directly after and, therefore, seems to modify “stereophonic recording.” Since, stereophonic recording is a type of recording, even if we are not sure what exactly stereophonic recording is, it seems reasonable to describe stereophonic recording as a method that does something in particular.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>Alan Blumlein’s research into stereophonic recording is how he is best known,
</b> a method that encodes the relative positions of the sound sources recorded.



 In this version, the non-underlined portion is used as an appositive. This appositive, “a method …,” is placed directly after and, therefore, seems to modify “how he is best known.” Since “how he is best known” is not a method that encodes the relative positions of sounds, this version does not make sense.


> <b>(E)</b> <b>Alan Blumlein, best known for his research into stereophonic recording, is
</b> a method that encodes the relative positions of the sound sources recorded.



This version conveys the nonsensical meaning that Alan Blumlein is a method.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)
