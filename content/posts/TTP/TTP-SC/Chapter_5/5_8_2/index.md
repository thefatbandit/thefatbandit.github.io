## Lesson Content
So far, the appositives that we have discussed have renamed and modified specific words or phrases.

Consider the following example:

> Earth’s population of humans is expected to increase substantially over the coming decades, <strong>a development that could have significant negative effects on quality of life on the planet</strong>.

The phrase “a development that could have significant negative effects on quality of life” is an abstract appositive.

Let’s consider another example:

> After the judge announced her decision, the governor spent much of the rest of the day ranting on social media, <strong>a spectacle that shocked not only his supporters but also his detractors</strong>.

Like the phrase in the previous example, the phrase “a spectacle that shocked not only his supporters but also his detractors” does not rename and modify any specific word or phrase that appears before it.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

GMAT Sentence Correction questions often contain structures called “abstract appositives,” which, rather than rename and modify specific words or phrases, rename and modify entire ideas presented in clauses.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s now discuss a way in which we may see abstract appositives used in Sentence Correction questions.
