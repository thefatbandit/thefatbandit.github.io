## Lesson Content
A participle can never on its own function as the main verb in a clause.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A participle can never on its own function as the main verb (finite verb) in a clause. So, a sentence in which the only verb form is a participle will not be complete.
{{< /alert >}}

Because participles look like verbs, sometimes a participle can be perceived to be the main verb in a sentence when, in fact, no main verb (finite verb) actually exists.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: The man, swimming in a sea of uncertainty without any good options.

Upon careful inspection, we see that the above construction is missing a finite verb; the construction is not a sentence.

> The man, swimming in a sea of uncertainty without any good options, <strong>cried</strong>.

Now that we have added a finite verb, the sentence is complete.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In considering a sentence version, identify the subject and finite verb to ensure that the sentence is complete.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s look at another example sentence:

> The members of the team, disheartened by the loss, hung their heads.

We have two verb forms: “disheartened” and “hung.” However, only one is functioning as a finite verb, while the other is acting as a modifier.

In this case, the subject of the sentence is “members” and the action performed is “hung.” The other verb form in the sentence, “disheartened,” is a past participle that describes the members.

> <u>Incorrect</u>:<em> </em>The members of the team, disheartened by the loss.

Of course, one way to correct these types of sentences is to insert a helping verb between the noun and the participle.

> The members of the team <strong>were</strong> disheartened by the loss.

> The man <strong>was swimming</strong> in a sea of uncertainty without any good options.

Another fix is to change a participle to a past tense or present tense verb.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: The man, <strong>swimming</strong> in a sea of uncertainty without any good options.

> <u>Correct</u>: The man <strong>swam</strong> in a sea of uncertainty without any good options.

> <u>Correct</u>: The man <strong>swims</strong> in a sea of uncertainty without any good options.

By changing the participle to a past tense verb or a present tense verb, we can correct the sentence fragment.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

A sentence can be rendered complete through the insertion of a helping verb before a participle or through changing a participle to a present or past tense verb.
{{< /alert >}}
