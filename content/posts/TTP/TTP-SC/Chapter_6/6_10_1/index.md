## Lesson Content
The present continuous tense is used for describing events that are temporarily ongoing in the present.

> The children <strong>are playing </strong>outside.

> The dog <strong>is</strong><strong> running</strong> on the beach.

> The sun <strong>is setting</strong> over the Pacific Ocean.

> The members <strong>are taking</strong> their placement tests.

Notice that, in each of the above sentences, the event is occurring “right now.” In addition, the events are continuous.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The present continuous tense is used to describe events that are temporarily ongoing in the present. This tense is formed by joining a present tense form of the verb “to be” with the present participle (-ing form) of the base verb.
{{< /alert >}}
