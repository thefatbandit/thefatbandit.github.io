## Lesson Content
The past continuous tense is used to describe an action or event that was ongoing but has ended.

> The children <strong>were playing</strong> outside yesterday.

> The plane <strong>was flying</strong> over Paris one hour ago.

> Last night, the sun <strong>was setting</strong> over the ocean.

Notice that the events described by these examples have already occurred and that, when the events occurred, they were ongoing, or continuous.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The past continuous tense is used to describe an action or event that was ongoing but has ended.
{{< /alert >}}

This tense can also be used to describe an event that was occurring in the background when a more notable event occurred.

> The woman <strong>was washing</strong> clothes when the police arrived.

> The dog <strong>was chewing</strong> his bone when the kids came running into the room.

> The plane <strong>was flying</strong> over Colombia when the pilot decided to descend quickly.

> The tiger <strong>was watching</strong> the herd of buffalo as they swam in the lake.

Notice that, in the above examples, the past continuous tense is used in describing events that were already occurring when more notable events occurred, and that all of the events occurred in the past.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The past continuous tense can also be used to describe an event that was occurring in the background when a more notable event occurred.
{{< /alert >}}
