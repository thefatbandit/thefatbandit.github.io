## Lesson Content
The future continuous tense is used to describe an action or event that will be ongoing but has not yet begun; the action or event will occur in the future.

> The children <strong>will be playing</strong> outside tomorrow.

> The plane <strong>will be flying</strong> over Madrid in about five hours.

> Astronomers <strong>will be studying</strong> the comet as it passes through the inner solar system.

Notice that the events described by these examples will be ongoing at some point in the future.
