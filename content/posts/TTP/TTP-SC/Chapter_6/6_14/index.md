## Lesson Content
There is a major difference between a hypothetical situation and a situation in which the outcome is real, yet the speaker is unsure of that outcome.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

There is a major difference between a hypothetical situation and a situation in which the outcome is real, yet the speaker is unsure of that outcome.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following example sentence:

> If this painting <strong>was painted </strong>by Rembrandt, then it is an amazing find.

In this situation, the painting mentioned was or was not painted by Rembrandt.

How can we use this knowledge to score points on test day? We can eliminate any sentence version that uses the subjunctive mood when describing a situation that is uncertain, not hypothetical.

<strong><u>Key Strategy</u></strong><strong>:</strong> Eliminate any sentence version that uses the subjunctive mood when describing a situation that is uncertain, not hypothetical.

Consider the following:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: If our representative <strong>were </strong>there that day, he can report what occurred.

> <u>Correct</u>: If our representative <strong>was </strong>there that day, he can report what occurred.

Notice that this sentence is not describing something hypothetical.

Let’s consider another example:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: If the song <strong>were written </strong>by Mike Posner, then some royalties should go to him.

> <u>Correct</u>: If the song <strong>was written </strong>by Mike Posner, then some royalties should go to him.

This sentence is not about a hypothetical situation.

##  Sentence Correction Example 18  

Investors suspect that <u>it would increase the supply of government bonds if the government will pass</u> another stimulus package.

1. [ ] (A) it would increase the supply of government bonds if the government will pass
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) the supply of government bonds would increase if the government is passing
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) it would increase the supply of government bonds if the government passes
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) the supply of government bonds would increase if the government were to pass
5. [ ] (E) the supply of government bonds would increase if the government has passed
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



> <b>(A)</b> Investors suspect that <b>it would increase the supply of government bonds if the government will pass
</b> another stimulus package.



The two clauses of this version communicate conflicting meanings and do not work logically with each other.


The use of “would increase” in the first clause of the sentence indicates that the sentence is about a hypothetical outcome that investors suspect <em>would</em> occur if the government were to take a certain action, not about an outcome that may actually occur.


However, the use of the future indicative “will pass” in “if the government <em>will pass</em> another stimulus package” implies that the sentence is about events that may actually happen, not about a hypothetical situation.


Additionally, the pronoun “it” in “<em>it</em> would increase the supply of government bonds” does not have a logical antecedent. The sentence does not mention anything that “would increase the supply of government bonds.”


> <b>(B)</b> Investors suspect that <b>the supply of government bonds would increase if the government is passing
</b> another stimulus package.



The two clauses of this version communicate conflicting meanings and do not work logically with each other.


The use of “would increase” in the first clause of the sentence indicates that the sentence is about a hypothetical outcome that investors suspect <em>would</em> occur if the government were to take a certain action, not about an outcome that may actually occur.


However, the use of the present continuous indicative “is passing” in “if the government <em>is passing</em> another stimulus package” indicates that the sentence is about events that may actually be happening, not about a hypothetical situation.


> <b>(C)</b> Investors suspect that <b>it would increase the supply of government bonds if the government passes
</b> another stimulus package.



The two clauses in this version communicate conflicting meanings and do not work logically with each other.


The use of “would increase” in the first clause of the sentence indicates that the sentence is about a hypothetical outcome that investors suspect <em>would</em> occur if the government were to take a certain action, not about an outcome that may actually occur.


However, the use of the indicative “passes” in “if the government <em>passes</em> another stimulus package” indicates that the sentence is about events that may actually happen, not about a hypothetical situation.


Additionally, the pronoun “it” in “<em>it</em> would increase the supply of government bonds” does not have a logical antecedent. The sentence does not mention anything that “would increase the supply of government bonds.”


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D)</b> Investors suspect that <b>the supply of government bonds would increase if the government were to pass
</b> another stimulus package.



The two clauses of this version work logically together.


The use of “would increase” in the first clause of the sentence indicates that the sentence is about a hypothetical outcome that investors suspect <em>would</em> occur if the government were to take a certain action.


Similarly, the use of the subjunctive “were to pass” in “if the government <em>were to pass</em> another stimulus package” communicates that the sentence is about a hypothetical situation.


Also, this version eliminates the antecedent-lacking pronoun “it” and, thus, conveys a logical meaning.


> <b>(E)</b> Investors suspect that <b>the supply of government bonds would increase if the government has passed
</b> another stimulus package.



The two clauses in this version communicate conflicting meanings and do not work logically with each other.


The use of “would increase” in the first clause of the sentence indicates that the sentence is about a hypothetical outcome that investors suspect <em>would</em> occur if the government were to take a certain action, not about an outcome that may actually occur.


However, the use of the present perfect indicative “has passed” in “if the government <em>has </em><em>passed</em> another stimulus package” indicates that the sentence is about events that may actually happen, not about a hypothetical situation.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

##  Sentence Correction Example 19

2. [ ] (B) When Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, is placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he will not have a size advantage, which contributes
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) If Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, was placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he would not have a size advantage contributing

4. [ ] (D) When Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, were placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he did not have the size advantage, which contributed
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) If Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, were to be placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he would not have the size advantage that contributed

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



Since the sentence is about placing someone who played baseball in the early twentieth century into the league as it is in the twenty-first century, the scenario is almost certainly hypothetical. So, unless, at over 100 years old, Babe Ruth might still be capable of playing baseball professionally, the correct voice to use in the “if” clause must be the subjunctive. Also, as the question is written, even if Babe Ruth’s playing baseball in the twenty-first century were possible, because of tense and modifier issues, none of the versions that use the indicative in the “if” clause would work anyway.


> <b>(A)</b> <b>If Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, was placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he will not have a size advantage, contributing
</b> to his dominance.



The meaning conveyed by the wording of this version, “if Babe Ruth … was placed into the league … he will,” is illogical, because the implications of the meaning are that Babe Ruth, who played baseball around 100 years before the statement was made, may have actually been placed into the league around the time the statement was made. Since the placing into the league of a baseball player who played around 100 years before the statement was made is almost certainly purely hypothetical, the use of the indicative “was placed” does not make sense. The subjunctive should be used instead.


Also, the use of the participial phrase “contributing to his dominance” after the clause “he will not have a size advantage” and separated from that clause by a comma results in the sentence’s conveying the illogical meaning that, by not having a size advantage, Babe Ruth will contribute to his dominance.


> <b>(B)</b> <b>When Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, is placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he will not have a size advantage, which contributes
</b> to his dominance.



The meaning conveyed by the wording of this version, “when Babe Ruth … is placed into the league … he will,” is illogical, because the implications of the meaning are that Babe Ruth, who played baseball around 100 years before the statement was made, is actually going to be placed into the league after the time the statement was made. Since the placing into the league of a baseball player who played around 100 years before the statement was made is almost certainly purely hypothetical, the use of the indicative “is placed” does not make sense. The subjunctive should be used instead.


Also, the use of the relative clause “which contributes to his dominance” does not make sense. “Which contributes to his dominance” seems to modify “size advantage.” However, the clause that precedes “which contributes to his dominance” says that Babe Ruth will <em>not</em> have a size advantage. So, there is no size advantage for “which contributes to his dominance” to modify, because the point that the preceding clause makes is that no such size advantage will exist.


> <b>(C)</b> <b>If Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, was placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he would not have a size advantage contributing
</b> to his dominance.



The meaning conveyed by the wording of this version, “if Babe Ruth … was placed into the league … he would not have,” is illogical for two reasons.


One reason is that the use of “he would” makes clear that the statement is about what “would” be the case in a hypothetical scenario, whereas the indicative “if he was” implies that the scenario presented is possibly actual.


The other reason is that the implications of the meaning conveyed by “if he was placed into the league” are that Babe Ruth, who played baseball around 100 years before the statement was made, may actually have been placed into the league around the time the statement was made. Since, the placing into the league of a baseball player who played around 100 years before the statement was made is almost certainly purely hypothetical, the use of the indicative does not make sense.


For the above two reasons, the use of the indicative “was placed” is incorrect, and the use of the subjunctive “were placed,’’ which would indicate that the scenario described is hypothetical, makes more sense.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>When Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, were placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he did not have the size advantage, which contributed
</b> to his dominance.



Since the use of “when” before a statement implies that the events described by the statement did or will in fact occur, the use of the subjunctive in a statement beginning with “when” does not make sense, because events described via the use of the subjunctive are understood to be hypothetical, not actual. So, saying “when Babe Ruth … were placed” is not logical. “Were” is either the subjunctive form or the plural form of “to be,” neither of which works in the context of this version.


Also, the statement “he did not have the size advantage, which contributed to his dominance” is not logical. “He did not have the size advantage, which contributed to his dominance” expresses (1) that, in the universe, there is one size advantage, “the size advantage;” (2) that Babe Ruth did not have that size advantage; and (3) that, apparently, somehow, that size advantage contributed to Babe Ruth’s dominance, perhaps at some time when he did have that size advantage.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(E)</b> <b>If Babe Ruth, the professional baseball player who was a star in the early twentieth century, were to be placed into the league as it is now, in the twenty-first century, he would not have the size advantage that contributed
</b> to his dominance.



This version uses the subjunctive “were to be placed” in conveying the logical meaning, that, in a hypothetical situation in which Babe Ruth is placed into today’s league, he would not have “the sized advantage that contributed to his dominance.”


Notice, the use of the restrictive relative clause “that contributed to his dominance” effectively expresses that the size advantage mentioned is a specific one, the size advantage that contributed to Babe Ruth’s dominance.


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

Let’s now discuss how the subjunctive mood is used in command situations.
