## Lesson Content
At this point, we know well that we’ll encounter situations in which the correct answers are written in the passive voice.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In SC questions, we will encounter situations in which the correct answers are written in the passive voice, so don’t rule out a sentence simply because it is written in the passive voice.
{{< /alert >}}

In fact, one way in which a question writer can make the incorrectness or correctness of answer choices less obvious is to use the active voice in incorrect choices and the passive voice in the correct answer to a question, thus tempting test-takers to choose the more conventional sounding, active voice incorrect choice rather than the less conventional sounding, passive voice correct answer.

So, when answering Sentence Correction questions, we have to be careful not to be fooled by such a setup.

Consider the following pair of sentence versions:

> <u>Passive Voice</u>: In the end, the more skilled candidate was elected by the citizens to the office of governor, an electoral outcome that boded well for the prospects of the state.

> <u>Active Voice</u>: In the end, the citizens elected the more skilled candidate to the office of governor, resulting in an electoral outcome that boded well for the prospects of the state.

The active voice construction might sound a little more “normal,” or more “right,” than the slightly less concise but still correct passive voice construction.

The moral of the story is that we have to be careful not to be tempted into choosing an incorrect answer choice just because it uses the active voice and, therefore, sounds a little more “normal” than other choices.

##  Sentence Correction Example 28  

<u>As one of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh was not painting before his late twenties and produced most of his best-known works</u> during his final two years.

1. [ ] (A) As one of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh was not painting before his late twenties and produced most of his best-known works
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) As one of history's greatest painters and as an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh would not begin painting until his late twenties, and he produced most of his best-known works
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) One of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh did not begin painting until his late twenties, and most of his best-known works were produced
4. [ ] (D) Though one of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh had not begun to paint before his late twenties, producing most of his best-known works
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) Even though he was one of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh did not begin painting until his late twenties, and he produced most of his best-known works
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

##### Question Level: medium



There are a few tempting trap answers in this question. Choice (E) in particular seems attractive with “even though” setting up the sentence to indicate a contrast between Van Gogh’s accomplishments and his commencing painting at a relatively late age. Another aspect of choice (E) and some other incorrect choices that makes them tempting is the use of the straightforward active voice “he produced” rather than the passive voice “his best-known works were produced.” At the same time, only the choice that uses the passive voice makes sense, as all the other choices convey illogical meanings.


> <b>(A)</b> <b>As one of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh was not painting before his late twenties and produced most of his best-known works
</b> during his final two years.



This choice conveys the illogical meaning that, as one of history’s greatest painters, Vincent Van Gogh was not painting.


Also, the use of the past continuous wording “was not painting before his late twenties” does not convey a clear meaning. It doesn’t clearly indicate that he did not paint until his late twenties. Rather it expresses that, at some point before his late twenties, he was not painting.


> <b>(B)</b> <b>As one of history's greatest painters and as an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh would not begin painting until his late twenties, and he produced most of his best-known works
</b> during his final two years.



With its nicely parallel wording, “as one of history’s … and as an important …,” this choice may be tempting. Also, it may “sound right” because it uses the active voice “he would not begin to paint.” At the same time, this version conveys the illogical meaning that, as one of history’s greatest painters, Van Gogh would not begin painting until his late twenties, as if he was already one of history’s greatest painters before he began.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(C)</b> <b>One of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh did not begin painting until his late twenties, and most of his best-known works were produced
</b> during his final two years.



This choice may not seem as appealing as some others because it contains a somewhat “funny sounding” appositive “one of history's greatest painters and an important contributor” and the passive voice wording “most of his best-known works were produced.” All the same, it effectively conveys a logical meaning.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>Though one of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh had not begun to paint before his late twenties, producing most of his best-known works
</b> during his final two years.



This version conveys the illogical meaning that, though Van Gogh was one of history’s greatest painters, he had not begun to paint before his late twenties, as if he had been one of history’s greatest painters when he had not begun to paint.


Also, the wording “had not begun to paint before his late twenties” conveys a muddled meaning that does not really make sense, and the sentence does not successfully indicate that he actually did begin to paint upon reaching his late twenties. All the sentence says is that, before his late twenties, he had not begun.


Also, as a result of the use of the closing modifier, “producing some of his best-known works …,” the sentence conveys the nonsensical meaning that Van Gogh had not begun painting, producing some of his best-known works.


> <b>(E)</b> <b>Even though he was one of history's greatest painters and an important contributor to the foundations of modern art, Vincent Van Gogh did not begin painting until his late twenties, and he produced most of his best-known works
</b> during his final two years.



This choice may be tempting, as “even though” nicely sets up the sentence to describe a contrast between Van Gogh’s accomplishments and his commencing painting at a relatively late age, and as the active voice “he produced” may “sound right.” However, this version conveys the illogical meaning that, even though Van Gogh was one of history’s greatest painters, he did not start painting until his late twenties, as if he was one of the greatest painters before he started painting.


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

Let’s now discuss verb voice issues being combined with other issues to create clearly incorrect choices.
