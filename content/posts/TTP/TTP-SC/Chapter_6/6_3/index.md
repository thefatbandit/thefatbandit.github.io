## Lesson Content
When a simple past tense verb is used, we’re describing an event that occurred – and ended – in the past.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The simple past tense is used to describe events that occurred – and ended – in the past.
{{< /alert >}}

The simple past tense is most commonly formed by placing the ending –ed after the stem of a regular verb.

> He <strong>finished</strong> his paper just before the deadline.

> We <strong>walked</strong> the dog before sunrise.

> Last year, attendance at the festival <strong>exceeded</strong> expectations.

> In 1929, the stock market <strong>crashed</strong>.

> Earlier this morning, Ernie the mule <strong>jumped </strong>into the swimming pool.

In each of these examples, notice that the action began – and ended – in the past.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In the cases of most verbs, the simple past tense form is created by adding –ed to the stem of the verb.
{{< /alert >}}
