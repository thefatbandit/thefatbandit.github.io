## Lesson Content
The future tense is used for discussing events that have not yet occurred but will occur in the future.

> The little boy <strong>will go</strong> to kindergarten next year.

> John and Rebecca <strong>will be </strong>at the movies tonight.

> Blaze and Molly <strong>will eat</strong> their dinner a bit later.

> The rabbit <strong>will rest</strong> for a few minutes next to the brook.

> The sun <strong>will burn</strong> out one day.

Notice that each of these examples describes an event that will occur in the future.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The future tense is used when discussing events that have not yet occurred but will occur in the future. The future tense is formed by preceding the base verb with the helping verb “will.”
{{< /alert >}}

Note that, to describe an event in the future, we can also use the following construction:

> a form of “to be” + “going to” + the base verb

A form of “to be” + “going to” + the base verb is generally used when future events are planned or seem likely to occur.

> I <strong>am going to go</strong> to the beach later today.

> Sara <strong>is going to</strong> <strong>start </strong>taking French lessons.

> Because oil producing countries have agreed to increase output, commodity prices<strong> are going to decline</strong>.

> Since the government has kept interest rates too low for too long, inflation <strong>is going to begin eroding</strong> the purchasing power of citizens.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The construction a form of “to be” + “going to” + the base verb can be used to create future tense expressions. The future tense expressed in this way is generally used in sentences about future events that are planned or that seem likely to occur.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s now discuss how to decide whether the use of a tense in a sentence version makes sense.
