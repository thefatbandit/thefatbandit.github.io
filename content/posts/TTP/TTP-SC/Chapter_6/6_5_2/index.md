## Lesson Content
In addition to verb context clues, GMAT questions will provide us with all sorts of other helpful clues that will allow us to rule out sentence versions with verb tenses that can’t possibly work.

Consider the following sentence versions:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: <strong>In the past</strong>, Rome’s senate <u>experiences</u> high levels of corruption.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: <strong>In the past</strong>, Rome’s senate <u>will experience </u>high levels of corruption.

> <u>Correct</u>: <strong>In the past</strong>, Rome’s senate <u>experienced</u> high levels of corruption.

Notice that by saying, “in the past,” the sentence provides us with a valuable clue that signals that the action occurred in the past.

We also eliminate the first sentence version immediately because the present tense “experiences” conveys that the action occurs regularly, but an action “in the past” can’t occur regularly.

We can also eliminate the second sentence version immediately because the future tense “will experience” conveys that the action did not yet occur, but an action “in the past” has already begun and ended.

The third sentence version works well because the past tense “experienced” makes sense given that the action occurred “in the past.”

Notice that the simple context clue, “in the past” was all we needed to see to get to the correct version.

Let’s try another:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: Regardless of when the current CEO <strong>decides</strong> to retire, the board <u>found</u> a replacement quickly, so that the company <strong>will not face</strong> the same vulnerabilities that it <strong>faced</strong> after the last CEO resigned.

> <u>Incorrect</u>: Regardless of when the current CEO <strong>decides</strong> to retire, the board <u>finds</u> a replacement quickly, so that the company <strong>will not face</strong> the same vulnerabilities that it <strong>faced</strong> after the last CEO resigned.

> <u>Correct</u>: Regardless of when the current CEO <strong>decides</strong> to retire, the board <u>will find</u> a replacement quickly, so that the company <strong>will not face</strong> the same vulnerabilities that it <strong>faced</strong> after the last CEO resigned.

We must look for keywords and other context clues to determine which tense logically fits the timeline of events.

We can eliminate version one because if the CEO has not yet decided to retire, how could the board have found a replacement?

We can also eliminate version two because the present tense “finds” illogically conveys that the board habitually “finds” a new CEO.

Sentence version three works well because the future tense “will find” make sense considering that the CEO has not yet decided to retire.

As we can see, by paying attention to context clues, we can assess the timeline and choose correctly among the past, present, and future tenses, even when two or more of those tenses are already contained in a sentence.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

In considering a sentence containing a combination of past, present, and future tenses, use keywords, key phrases, and other context clues to arrive at a logical timeline of events.
{{< /alert >}}

As the chapter progresses, we’ll discuss how to find the correct verb tense in more complicated scenarios, but for now, it’s important that we can effectively choose between the simple past, present, and future tenses.

##  Sentence Correction Example 1  

<u>As many feel that elephants used in its acts are the circus engaging in cruelty to animals, the fall in ticket sales was dramatic, with the circus eventually shutting down.</u>

1. [ ] (A) As many feel that elephants used in its acts are the circus engaging in cruelty to animals, the fall in ticket sales was dramatic, with the circus eventually shutting down.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
2. [ ] (B) With many feeling that the use of elephants in the circus’s acts engaged in cruelty to animals, ticket sales fell dramatically and eventually shut the circus down.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
3. [ ] (C) Once the use of elephants in its acts had many feeling that the circus will engage in cruelty to animals, ticket sales fell dramatically, eventually shutting down the circus.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)
4. [x] (D) Since many felt that, by using elephants in its acts, the circus was engaging in cruelty to animals, ticket sales fell dramatically, and the circus eventually shut down.
5. [ ] (E) Because many felt that, by using elephants in its acts, the circus is engaging in cruelty to animals, ticket sales fell dramatically, with the circus eventually shut down.
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (D)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



The correct version of the sentence will use tenses in ways such that all of the events mentioned are presented in a logical timeline, and the correct version will be otherwise logical and grammatically correct.


> <b>(A)</b> <b>As many feel that elephants used in its acts are the circus engaging in cruelty to animals, the fall in ticket sales was dramatic, with the circus eventually shutting down.
</b>


Context clues, such as the use of the past tense “was” and the fact that the circus has shut down, indicate that these events occurred in the past. Therefore, the use of the present tense “feel” and “are engaging” in discussing actions engaged in by the circus does not make sense.


Also, this version conveys the illogical meaning that people feel that elephants are the circus engaging in cruelty to animals.


> <b>(B)</b> <b>With many feeling that the use of elephants in the circus’s acts engaged in cruelty to animals, ticket sales fell dramatically and eventually shut the circus down.
</b>


Since it makes sense that all the events mentioned would have occurred in the past, this version’s use of the past tense in all clauses makes sense.


However, this version conveys the illogical meaning that the “use of elephants” itself “engaged in cruelty to animals.” The use of elephants may constitute or involve cruelty to animals, but would not “engage in” cruelty to animals.


Also, this version conveys the illogical meaning that “ticket sales … shut the circus down.”


> <b>(C)</b> <b>Once the use of elephants in its acts had many feeling that the circus will engage in cruelty to animals, ticket sales fell dramatically, eventually shutting down the circus.
</b>


Context clues, such as the use of the past tense “fell” and the fact that the circus has been shut down, indicate that these events occurred in the past. Therefore, the use of the future tense “will engage” in discussing an action engaged in by the circus does not make sense.


Also, this version conveys the nonsensical meaning that, by falling, ticket sales shut down the circus.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(D)</b> <b>Since many felt that, by using elephants in its acts, the circus was engaging in cruelty to animals, ticket sales fell dramatically, and the circus eventually shut down.
</b>


Since it makes sense that all the events mentioned would have occurred in the past, this version’s use of the past tense in all clauses makes sense.


Also, all aspects of the cause-and-effect scenario presented in this version make sense.


> <b>(E)</b> <b>Because many felt that, by using elephants in its acts, the circus is engaging in cruelty to animals, ticket sales fell dramatically, with the circus eventually shut down.
</b>


Context clues, such as the use of the past tense “felt” and “fell” and the fact that the circus has shut down, indicate that these events occurred in the past. Therefore, the use of the present tense “is engaging” in discussing actions engaged in by the circus does not make sense.


> ##### Correct Answer: (D)

##  Sentence Correction Example 2  

<u>Previously, Pittsburgh was known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, though currently, the city, having spent the last several decades reinventing itself, is</u> considered one of America’s most livable cities.

1. [x] (A) Previously, Pittsburgh was known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, though currently, the city, having spent the last several decades reinventing itself, is
2. [ ] (B) Unlike previously, when Pittsburgh is known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, currently, the city, which has spent the last several decades reinventing itself, was
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
3. [ ] (C) Previously, Pittsburgh is known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun is not visible; however, currently, the city spent the last several decades reinventing itself and is
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
4. [ ] (D) Whereas, previously, Pittsburgh was known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, currently, the city that has spent the last several decades reinventing itself was
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)
5. [ ] (E) Although previously, Pittsburgh, known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, currently, the city, spending the last several decades reinventing itself, is
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (A)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: easy



In this question, the answer choices contain adverb context clues “previously,” which indicates that events discussed occurred in the past, and “currently,” which indicates that events discussed are occurring in the present.


So, the tenses of the main verbs of the clauses that follow these adverbs must fit these adverbs, and other verb forms in the sentence must then work with the main verbs of the clauses to express a coherent a logical timeline.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">YOUR CHOICE</span></span><b>(A)</b> <b>Previously, Pittsburgh was known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, though currently, the city, having spent the last several decades reinventing itself, is
</b> considered one of America’s most livable cities.



In this version, the past tense “Pittsburgh was known” and “the sun was not visible” work with the adverb “previously,” and the present tense “the city … is considered” works with the adverb “currently.”


Also, the present participial phrase “having spent the last several decades reinventing itself” works logically with “the city … is considered.”


> <b>(B)</b> <b>Unlike previously, when Pittsburgh is known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, currently, the city, which has spent the last several decades reinventing itself, was
</b> considered one of America’s most livable cities.



The present tense “Pittsburgh is known” does not work with the adverb “previously,” which indicates that the events discussed occurred, not in the present, but in the past.


Also, the past tense “the city … was” does not work with “currently,” since “currently” indicates that the events discussed are occurring in the present.


> <b>(C)</b> <b>Previously, Pittsburgh is known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun is not visible; however, currently, the city spent the last several decades reinventing itself and is
</b> considered one of America’s most livable cities.



The present tense “Pittsburgh is known” and “the sun is” do not work with the adverb “previously,” which indicates that the events discussed occurred, not in the present, but in the past.


Also, the past tense “the city spent” does not work with the adverb “currently,” as “currently” indicates that the events discussed are occurring in the present.


> <b>(D)</b> <b>Whereas, previously, Pittsburgh was known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, currently, the city that has spent the last several decades reinventing itself was
</b> considered one of America’s most livable cities.



The past tense “Pittsburgh was known” and “the sun was” work with the adverb “previously.”


However, the past tense “the city was” does not work with the adverb “currently,” since “currently” indicates that the events discussed are occurring in the present.


Also, the restrictive modifier “that has spent the last several decades reinventing itself” specifies a city without indicating that that city is Pittsburgh, and as a result, the sentence illogically seems to be about two different cities, Pittsburgh and “the city that has spent the last several decades reinventing itself.”


> <b>(E)</b> <b>Although previously, Pittsburgh, known for having a sky so polluted with smoke that the sun was not visible, currently, the city, spending the last several decades reinventing itself, is
</b> considered one of America’s most livable cities.



The structure of this version is flawed, as we can see by removing the nonessential modifier “known for having … visible.” When we do so, we get the following:


Although previously, Pittsburgh, currently, the city, spending the last several decades reinventing itself, is considered …


This structure has two subjects, “Pittsburgh” and “the city” for one verb “is considered.” It also illogically employs two adverbs, “previously” and “currently,” to open one clause.


Also, the present participle “spending” does not work logically with “the last several decades,” since the last several decades are in the past rather than in the present. “Having spent” is required instead.


> ##### Correct Answer: (A)

Let’s now move on to the perfect tenses: present perfect, past perfect, and future perfect.
