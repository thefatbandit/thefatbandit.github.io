## Lesson Content
There are a few other words that are clues indicating that the use of the present perfect tense may make sense.

For example:

> Native Americans have lived in what is now the United States <strong>for at least the last 15,000 years.</strong>

> Because Ale has lived in Vietnam <strong>for more than two years</strong>, she is fluent in Vietnamese.

> <strong>Over the last one million years</strong>, human civilizations have undergone evolutionary change.

> <strong>Over the course of last few days</strong>, it has become clear the people in this office have become a team.

> <strong>During </strong>the semester, Juan has gone snorkeling in Mexico many times.

> <strong>During </strong>this presidential election, our country has lost some of its cohesion.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The words “for,” “over,” and “during” are clues indicating that the use of the present perfect may make sense.
{{< /alert >}}

Let’s now discuss the past perfect tense.
