## Lesson Content
Sometimes we’ll encounter a list within a list.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Sometimes we’ll encounter a list within a list. We call these structures nested lists. In other words, a nested list is a list within one of the elements of another list.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following example sentence:

> Please leave <strong>your name and your number</strong> and explain why you called.

Notice that we have a two-part list.

> Please leave (<strong>your name and your number</strong>) and explain (why you called).

The nested list will always be a secondary list within a larger list, which we refer to as the outer list.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The nested list will always be a secondary list within a larger list, which we refer to as the outer list.
{{< /alert >}}

Notice that there are two “ands” in the nested list.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

The presence of more than one “and” is a hallmark of a nested list and is an important parallelism trigger.
{{< /alert >}}

It’s important to keep the outer list and the nested list clear in our minds.

> Please:

<strong>leave</strong> (your name and your number)

<strong>explain</strong> why you called

What’s the nested list?

> leave:

your name

your number

As we progress to more complicated examples, it’s important to take the necessary time to keep the outer list and the nested list straight.

Let’s look at another example:

> The managing partner instructed the traders to sell the financial stocks, purchase <strong>precious metal stocks and technology stocks</strong>, and determine the yield to maturity of ten-year government bonds.

First notice that the presence of the word “and” twice alerts us that we might have a nested list (we do).

It’s important to keep the outer list and the nested list clear in our minds.

The managing partner instructed the traders to:

> <strong>sell</strong> the financial stocks

> <strong>purchase </strong>(precious metal stocks and technology stocks)

> <strong>determine </strong>the yield to maturity of ten-year government bonds

Now, what’s the nested list? The nested list is present in the second item in the larger list.

> purchase:

precious metal stocks

technology stocks

Again, notice the presence of two “and”s.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Given a nested list, it’s important to keep straight the outer list and the nested list. Always ask, “What’s the outer list?” and “What’s the nested list?”
{{< /alert >}}
