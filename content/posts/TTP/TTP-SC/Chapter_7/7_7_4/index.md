## Lesson Content
When “not only … but also” is used, we must pay careful attention to any words in between “but” and “also,” because the placement of words between “but” and “also” can mess up the parallelism.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

When “not only … but also” is used, we must pay careful attention to any words in between “but” and “also,” because the placement of words between “but” and “also” can mess up the parallelism.
{{< /alert >}}

Consider the following example:

> <u>Correct</u>: The chef not only prepares dinners but also bakes pastries.

We can see that this sentence is logical by distributing “the chef” after “not only” and “but also.”

> Not only <strong>the chef</strong> prepares dinners but also <strong>the chef</strong> bakes pastries.

However, look what happens when “he” is placed between the “but … also.”

> <u>Incorrect</u>: The chef not only prepares dinners but <strong>he</strong> also bakes pastries.

When we distribute “the chef,” we get the following:

> Not only <strong>the chef</strong> prepares dinners but <strong>he</strong> also <strong>the chef</strong> bakes pastries.

Now, the second part of the “not only … but also” construction is broken since “but he also the chef bakes pastries” does not make sense.

Let’s consider another set of examples:

> <u>Correct</u>: People who solve world problems not only heal the world but also heal themselves.

The above example clearly and logically conveys that people who solve world problems accomplish two ends, as we can see by distributing “people who solve world problems” after “not only” and “but also.”

> Not only <strong>people who solve world problems</strong> heal the world but also <strong>people who solve world problems</strong> heal themselves.

Now consider what happens if we put “they” between “but” and “also.”

> <u>Incorrect</u>: People who solve world problems not only heal the world but <strong>they</strong> also heal themselves.

When we distribute “people who solve world problems,” we get the following:

> Not only <strong>people who solve world problems</strong> heal the world but <strong>they</strong> also <strong>people who solve world problems</strong> heal themselves.

Notice that the parallelism is broken since “but they also people who solve world problems heal themselves” does not make sense.

##  Sentence Correction Example 16  

During her period of field study, the primatologist <u>observed that not only do chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but also that they seek</u> to address unfair treatment of others.

1. [ ] (A) observed that not only do chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but also that they seek
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
2. [x] (B) not only observed chimpanzees appearing to have a sense of fairness but also observed them seeking
3. [ ] (C) observed not only that chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but observed also that they seek
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
4. [ ] (D) not only observed chimpanzees appearing to have a sense of fairness but also seeking
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)
5. [ ] (E) not only observed that chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but she also observed that they seek
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (B)

##### Question Level: easy



> <b>(A)</b> During her period of field study, the primatologist <b>observed that not only do chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but also that they seek
</b> to address unfair treatment of others.



 Since “that” appears before “not only,” it is understood to appear after “but also.” So, the repetition of “that” after “but also” does not make sense, as we can see by carrying over the “that” that appears before “not only”.


<strong>that</strong> (not only do chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but also that they seek)


not only <strong>that </strong>chimpanzees (do) appear to have a sense of fairness but also <strong>that </strong>that they seek


We can see that “that that they seek” does not make sense.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(B)</b> During her period of field study, the primatologist <b>not only observed chimpanzees appearing to have a sense of fairness but also observed them seeking
</b> to address unfair treatment of others.



 In this version, what appears after “not only” is parallel to what appears after “but also” and works with what introduces the correlative structure, as we can see by considering the following:


<strong>the primatologist</strong> (not only observed chimpanzees appearing to have a sense of fairness but also observed them seeking)


not only <strong>the primatologist</strong> observed chimpanzees appearing to have a sense of fairness but also <strong>the primatologist</strong> observed them seeking


So, the sentence is logically constructed.


> <b>(C)</b> During her period of field study, the primatologist <b>observed not only that chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but observed also that they seek
</b> to address unfair treatment of others.



 In this version, since “observed” appears before “not only,” the repetition of “observed” between “but” and “also” is illogical, as we can see by considering the following:


<strong>observed</strong> (not only that chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but observed also that they seek)


not only <strong>observed</strong> that chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but observed also <strong>observed</strong> that they seek


We can see that “observed also observed that they seek” does not make sense.


> <b>(D)</b> During her period of field study, the primatologist <b>not only observed chimpanzees appearing to have a sense of fairness but also seeking
</b> to address unfair treatment of others.



 In this version, both “observed” and “chimpanzees” appear after “not only.” So, they are not understood to appear after “but also.” As a result, this version is illogical, as we an see by considering the following:


<strong>the primatologist</strong> (not only observed chimpanzees appearing to have a sense of fairness but also seeking to address unfair treatment of others.)


not only <strong>the primatologist</strong> observed chimpanzees appearing to have a sense of fairness but also <strong>the primatologist</strong> seeking to address unfair treatment of others.


We can see that “the primatologist seeking to address unfair treatment of others” does not make sense in this context.


> <b>(E)</b> During her period of field study, the primatologist <b>not only observed that chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but she also observed that they seek
</b> to address unfair treatment of others.



 In this version, since “the primatologist” appears before “not only,” the placement of “she” between “but” and “also” does not make sense, as we can see by considering the following:


<strong>the primatologist</strong> (not only observed that chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but she also observed that they seek)


not only <strong>the primatologist</strong> observed that chimpanzees appear to have a sense of fairness but she also <strong>the primatologist</strong> observed that they seek


We can see that “she also the primatologist observed that they seek” does not make sense.


> ##### Correct Answer: (B)
