## Lesson Content
It’s hard to get through the Verbal section of the GMAT without encountering Sentence Correction questions that involve comparisons.

In a comparison, two or more things, ideas, concepts, entities, or people are compared.

For example:

> John is like Melissa; they both love Caribbean beaches.

> The teeth of the gray wolf are similar in size to the teeth of the red wolf.

> Federico can run the 40-yard dash as quickly as Johanna can.

> The Magna Carta is a set of laws older than the Bill of Rights.

> Swimming is similar to hiking in that both elevate a person’s heart rate.

We see that a comparison is easy to recognize.

> - Logical

> - Unambiguous

> - Properly constructed

> - Grammatical

A comparison is logical when it compares similar things, using two or more items.

Let’s begin by discussing how to identify comparisons.
