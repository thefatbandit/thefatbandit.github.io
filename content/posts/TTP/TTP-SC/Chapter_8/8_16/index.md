## Lesson Content
As we have seen, to a large degree, GMAT Sentence Correction is a test of skill in noticing issues.

Another way in which Sentence Correction questions test the same skills is through the use of incorrect combinations of words in comparison expressions.

So, in order to ensure that we will correctly answer Sentence Correction questions involving comparisons, we have to maintain awareness of whether the comparison expressions in the sentences use correct combinations of words.

Consider the following example:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: The rate at which the tides rise in the Bay of Fundy is much <strong>greater as </strong>the rate at which tides rise in most other locations.

While, if read quickly, the above sentence may seem to be fine, if we look carefully, can see that the comparison in the above example incorrectly uses “as” with “greater.” Of course, with “greater,” “than” should be used instead of “as,” as in:

> The rate at which the tides rise in the Bay of Fundy is much <strong>greater than </strong>the rate at which tides rise in other locations.

This following example has a similar issue:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: There may be <strong>more </strong>life forms in a handful of earth <strong>as </strong>there are people living on the planet Earth.

The above sentence does not make sense.

> There may be <strong>more </strong>life forms in a handful of earth <strong>than </strong>there are people living on the planet Earth.

Let’s consider one more example:

> <u>Incorrect</u>: While people may believe poodles to be small dogs, the truth is that some poodles are <strong>as large</strong> in terms of height and weight <strong>than</strong> dogs of breeds commonly considered large.

In expressing a comparison of poodles and dogs of other breeds, this example incorrectly pairs “as large” with “than.” “As large” should be paired with “as,” as in:

> While people may believe poodles to be small dogs, the truth is that some poodles are <strong>as large </strong>in terms of height and weight <strong>as </strong>dogs of breeds commonly considered large.

{{< alert type="warning" >}}
💡<b>MUST KNOW:</b>

Incorrect choices in Sentence Correction questions involving comparisons may include incorrect combinations of comparison words, such as “greater as” rather than the correct “greater than,” “more … as” rather than the correct “more … than,” “so … as,” rather than the correct “as … as,” “as … than,” rather than the correct “as … as,” or “such … like,” rather than the correct “such … as.”

##  Sentence Correction Example 25  

Analysis of the proportions of carbon isotopes present in the structure of a tomb located in Scotland <u>have indicated that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is thousands of years as old as similar tombs found in Greece</u>.

1. [ ] (A) have indicated that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is thousands of years as old as similar tombs found in Greece
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
2. [ ] (B) has indicated, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb, compared with similar tombs found in Greece, as being thousands of years older
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
3. [ ] (C) indicate that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is thousands of years older than similar tombs found in Greece
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
4. [ ] (D) has indicated that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is thousands of years older as similar tombs found in Greece
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (E)
5. [x] (E) indicates that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is older, by thousands of years, than similar ones found in Greece

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: medium



> <b>(A)</b> Analysis of the proportions of carbon isotopes present in the structure of a tomb located in Scotland <b>have indicated that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is thousands of years as old as similar tombs found in Greece
</b>.



 The singular subject “analysis” does not agree with the plural verb “have indicated.”


Also, the expression “thousand of years as old as” does not make sense. Since “as old as” is used to express that two things are the same age, there is no reason to mention “thousands of years” along with saying “as old as.”


> <b>(B)</b> Analysis of the proportions of carbon isotopes present in the structure of a tomb located in Scotland <b>has indicated, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb, compared with similar tombs found in Greece, as being thousands of years older
</b>.



This version does not effectively convey a logical meaning. If we consider the core sentence without any nonessential modifiers, we have “analysis … has indicated the tomb as being thousands of years older. That sentence basically means that analysis has pointed out the tomb “as being thousands of years older,” and does not effectively convey what exactly the analysis has indicated.


> <b>(C)</b> Analysis of the proportions of carbon isotopes present in the structure of a tomb located in Scotland <b>indicate that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is thousands of years older than similar tombs found in Greece
</b>.



This version is correct except for the fact that the singular subject “analysis” does not agree with the plural verb “indicate.”


> <b>(D)</b> Analysis of the proportions of carbon isotopes present in the structure of a tomb located in Scotland <b>has indicated that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is thousands of years older as similar tombs found in Greece
</b>.



The use of “as” with “thousand of years older” is incorrect. The correct expression is “thousands of years older <strong>than</strong>.”


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(E)</b> Analysis of the proportions of carbon isotopes present in the structure of a tomb located in Scotland <b>indicates that, contrary to conclusions based on analysis of artifacts, the tomb is older, by thousands of years, than similar ones found in Greece
</b>.



 The singular subject “analysis” agrees with the singular verb “indicates,” and this version correctly uses “than” with “older, by thousands of years.”


> ##### Correct Answer: (E)

##  Sentence Correction Example 26  

While the Chrysler Building in New York has <u>twenty-five fewer floors than those of the more well-known Empire State Building, for eleven months following completion, the Chrysler building was as tall or taller than any other building</u> in the city or the world.

1. [ ] (A) twenty-five fewer floors than those of the more well-known Empire State Building, for eleven months following completion, the Chrysler building was as tall or taller than any other building
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
2. [ ] (B) twenty-five floors less than the more well-known Empire State Building has, for eleven months following completion, the Chrysler building was as tall as or taller than any building
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
3. [x] (C) twenty-five floors fewer than are had by the more well-known Empire State Building, for eleven months following its completion, the Chrysler building was as tall as or taller than any other building
4. [ ] (D) twenty-five fewer floors as the more well-known Empire State Building has, for eleven months following being completed, the Chrysler building was tall as or taller than any other building
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)
5. [ ] (E) floors that are twenty-five fewer than those of the more well-known Empire State Building, for eleven months following its completion, the Chrysler building was just as tall or taller than other buildings
    > <br> <b>Correct Answer:</b> (C)

{{< /quizdown >}}

   <h5><details><summary><b><u>View Solution</u></b></summary>
   {{< alert type="secondary" >}}

##### Question Level: hard



> <b>(A)</b> While the Chrysler Building in New York has <b>twenty-five fewer floors than those of the more well-known Empire State Building, for eleven months following completion, the Chrysler building was as tall or taller than any other building
</b> in the city or the world.



“The Chrysler Building in New York has twenty-five fewer floors than those of the … Empire State Building” does not convey any meaning very well but seems to convey the nonsensical meaning that the Chrysler Building has twenty-five fewer floors than the floors of the Empire State Building, or “those,” have.


Also, “completion” does not effectively express that it was the Chrysler Building that was completed. This idea would be better expressed via “its completion,” which, via the use of “its,” indicates that what’s mentioned is the completion of the Chrysler Building.


Also, “as tall” is missing the second “as.” It should be “as tall as.” We have to avoid being fooled by the fact that “or taller than” follows “as tall” into thinking that the second “as” is not necessary.


> <b>(B)</b> While the Chrysler Building in New York has <b>twenty-five floors less than the more well-known Empire State Building has, for eleven months following completion, the Chrysler building was as tall as or taller than any building
</b> in the city or the world.



“Less” is incorrectly used with the countable noun “floors.”


Also, “completion” does not effectively express that it was the Chrysler Building that was completed. This idea would be better expressed via “its completion,” which, via the use of “its,” indicates that what’s mentioned is the completion of the Chrysler Building.


Also, since the Chrysler Building is a building that is “any building,” by saying “the Chrysler Building was as tall as or taller than any building,” the sentence illogically compares the Chrysler Building with itself.


> <span class="correct-answer"><i class="fal fa-check-circle"></i><span class="ms-1 fw-semibold correctness-text">CORRECT ANSWER</span></span><b>(C)</b> While the Chrysler Building in New York has <b>twenty-five floors fewer than are had by the more well-known Empire State Building, for eleven months following its completion, the Chrysler building was as tall as or taller than any other building
</b> in the city or the world.



The comparisons expressed by this version are logical, and the comparison words used are used correctly.


> <b>(D)</b> While the Chrysler Building in New York has <b>twenty-five fewer floors as the more well-known Empire State Building has, for eleven months following being completed, the Chrysler building was tall as or taller than any other building
</b> in the city or the world.



This version incorrectly uses “as” with “twenty-five fewer.” With “fewer,” “than” should be used rather than “as.”


Also, “being completed” does not effectively express that it was the Chrysler Building that was completed. This idea would be better expressed via “its being completed” or “its completion,” either of which, via the use of “its,” indicates that what’s mentioned is the completion of the Chrysler Building.


Also, “tall as” is missing an “as” before “tall.” It should be “as tall as.”


> <b>(E)</b> While the Chrysler Building in New York has <b>floors that are twenty-five fewer than those of the more well-known Empire State Building, for eleven months following its completion, the Chrysler building was just as tall or taller than other buildings
</b> in the city or the world.



This version expresses the illogical idea that the floors of the Chrysler Building themselves are somehow “fewer than” the floors of the Empire State Building. The number of floors can be fewer than another number of floors, or the floors can be fewer in number than other floors, but floors themselves cannot simply be fewer than other floors, as if being few is an attribute that floors can have to a greater degree than other floors have that attribute.


Also, “just as tall” is missing the second “as.” It should be “just as tall as.” We have to avoid being fooled by the fact that “or taller than” follows “just as tall” into thinking that the second “as” is not necessary.


Also, while not a strong reason for eliminating this choice, the sentence’s saying that the Chrysler Building was taller than “other buildings” does not result in the expression of a clear meaning. Is “taller than other buildings” meant to express that the Chrysler Building was taller than some other buildings in the city or the world, perhaps as few as two buildings? Is it meant to express that the Chrysler Building was taller than all other buildings?


> ##### Correct Answer: (C)

Let’s now discuss how “whereas,” “while,” and “although” differ from “unlike.”
