CR-3 The Question Stem and Answer Choices

  • MBA
  • GMAT
  • CR

The question stem poses a question directed at the stimulus. It specifies the task you must perform in order to get credit for the problem.

Question Stem Tasks
  • Identify details of the stimulus
  • Describe the structure of the argument
  • Strengthen or weaken the argument
  • Identify inferences, main points, and assumptions
  • Recognize errors of reasoning
  • Reconcile conflicts
  • Find arguments that are identical in nature

Analyze the Question Stem

It’s important to correctly classify the question into one of the types since that’ll help us in deciding which part of the stimulus to focus on. It allows us to proceed quickly with confidence and sometimes, even help in determining the correct answer before reading any options.

Legend: V. Important Important Not that frequent

🚨 10 Critical Reasoning Question Types (& example Q-Stems)
Must Be True/ Most Supported
  • “If the statements above are true, which of the following must also be true?”
  • “Which of the following can be properly inferred from the passage?”
Main Point
  • “The main point of the argument is that”
Assumption
  • “The argument in the passage relies on which of the following assumptions?”
Strengthen/Support
  • “Which of the following, if true, most strengthens the argument?”
  • “Which of the following, if true, would most strongly support the position above?”
Resolve the Paradox
  • “Which of the following, if true, would most help to explain the rise in revenues last year?”
Weaken
  • “Which of the following, if true, most seriously weakens the argument above?”
Method of Reasoning
  • “Which of the following describes how the argument above is developed?”
Flaw in Reasoning
  • “The reasoning in the chemist’s argument is flawed primarily because of which argument?”
Parallel Reasoning
  • “Which of the following arguments is most similar in its pattern of reasoning to the argument above?”
Evaluate the Argument
  • “The answer to which of the following questions would contribute most to an evaluation of the argument”

Family of Questions

ABSOLUTE TRUTH → SUSPISION (Flow of Information): The part at the end of the arrow is affected/determined. Whatever is given before the arrow is simply accepted.

Prove (Stimulus → Answer Choices)

  1. Use the information in the stimulus to select one of the answer choices.
  2. Any answer choice that does not appear either directly in the stimulus or as a combination of items in the stimulus is incorrect
  3. Must be True, Main Point, Method of Reasoning, Flaw in Reasoning, Parallel Reasoning

Help (Answer Choices → Stimulus)

  1. Based on helping the author’s argument in some way.
  2. Identify reasoning errors or “gaps” in the stimulus. These will be shored up by the answer choices
  3. New information in the answer choices is alright.
  4. Assumption, Strengthen, Resolve the paradox

Hurt (Answer choices -/> Stimulus)

  1. Based on attacking the author’s argument.
  2. Approach is the same as Help. But here we try expanding upon/pointing out the “gaps”.
  3. Weaken

Identifying the Question Type

  • Must be True and Resolve the Paradox are frequently connected to stimuli that do not contain conclusions. Then based on whether there’s a paradox or not we can decide between Resolve the Paradox & Must be True.

Practice

  1. “Which of the following, if true, most helps to explain the viewpoint described above?”
Solution
Resolve the Paradox

Given the statement must “explain”, the question is from the Assumption, Strengthen, and Resolve the paradox. The idea of explaining is most closely aligned with Resolving the Paradox.

  1. “Which of the following is most like the argument above in its logical structure?”
Solution
Parallel Reasoning
  1. “The argument is flawed because it”
Solution
Flaw in Reasoning
  1. “The advertisement proceeds by”
Solution
Method of Reasoning
  1. “Mary challenges Shaun’s reasoning by”
Solution
Method of Reasoning

Although the questions “challenges”, this is not a Weaken question since the stem asks for a description of the way Shaun’s reasoning was challenged. Thus you’re asked to identify Mary’s method of reasoning.

  1. “The answer to which of the following questions would most help in evaluating the philosopher’s argument?”
Solution
Evaluate the Argument

”Except” and “Least” in Q Stem (QuestionX type)

These question are specially encountered in the higher-difficulty problems.

EXCEPT
“except” in a Q literally turns the intent of the Q stem upside down.

🚨 QUESTIONX ≠ OPPOSITE QUESTION (eg. WEAKENX ≠ STRENGTHEN)

Having an “except” in the Q stem doesn’t mean we have to find the polar opposite. For eg. “Each of the following statements weaken the argument EXCEPT”.

A “Weaken EXCEPT” question does not ask you to find the statement that strengthen the argument. We just have to choose other than weaken.

LEAST (treat the same as “except”)
“least” has a similar effect. Although it’s meaning differs from “except”, treat it the same as “except”. This is so because if the test-makers allow all the options to weaken to a certain degree, it leaves room for challenging them.
💡 For both “except” & “least”, there will be 4 options that will clearly meet the stated criteria (weaken, strengthen, resolve, etc.) and the 1 correct answer will not.

Practice

  1. “Which one of the following, if all of them are true, is LEAST helpful in establishing that the conclusion above is properly drawn?”
Solution
StrengthenX
  1. ““Which one of the following, if true, does NOT help to resolve the apparent discrepancy between the two surveys discussed?”
Solution
ResolveX

Answer Choices

Pre-phasing Answers

It’s highly effective in quickly finding correct answer choices and avoiding incorrect answer choices. Prephasing also helps you keep alert and in touch with the elements of the problem.

🚨 Always read all the answer choices

Contenders Vs. Losers

Sort the answer choices into contenders & losers.

  • Contender: Answer choices that seem somewhat attractive, interesting or even confusing
  • Losers: Answer choices that strike as incorrect.

Deciding the final Ans

As we encounter a contender, we check it’s merit against the previous best contender. This process may seem tedious, but keeps us on our toes & saves time in the long run.

🚨 NEVER DWELL ON THE PREVIOUS QUESTION!!!!!

Fill In the Blank Questions

We will occasionally encounter questions where the stimulus is preceded by the question stem & then the stimulus ends with an underlines blank space.

The question stem in these problems directs you to fill in the blank with the answer that best completes the passage.

These often throw the test-takers a curveball since it seems new. But these are in fact Main Point, Must Be True, Strengthen, Assumption… question types.
