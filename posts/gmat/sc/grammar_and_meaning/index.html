SC-2 Grammar and Meaning

  • MBA
  • GMAT

Must-Know Grammar Terms

Clause

Set of words containing a subject and a working verb

Eg: She applied for the job.
She- subject & applied- working verb
💡 Using the question “Who did what?” will point to the subject and working verb

Independent Clause: Standalone correct sentence.

Dependent Clause: Also contains a verb. But not standalone in a sentence. These are your sentence fragments.

Modifier

Provides additional information in a sentence. Adjectives are a modifier.

Essential Modifiers: They are essential to understanding the sentence meaning

Eg: “The job that she started last week is much harder than her previous job”

Here, “that she started last week” is an essential modifier.

Since without it, “The job that is much harder than her previous job”

We are left with the question “What Job?”

Non-essential Modifiers: These modifiers can be removed from the sentence without any change to its meaning.

Eg: “The cat, which lives next door, is very noisy.”
In this sentence, “which lives next door” is a non-essential clause. If we know which cat is being referred to we can directly remove it to make the sentence “The cat is very noisy.”
💡 The “comma which” structure is an example of a non-essential modifier.

Sentence Core

  • Combination of independent clauses along with some essential modifiers forms a are the bare minimum needed in order to have a coherent sentence.
  • All non-essential modifiers are removed from the sentence.

Conjunction

Words that help to stick parts of the sentences together.

  • Coordinating Conjunctions: (Ind. Clause ↔ Ind. Clause) Join independent clauses together.
  • Subordinating Conjunctions: (Ind. Clause ↔ Modifier)

Marker (Not an official grammar term)

A flag to show that a certain kind of issue is being tested.

E.g: Unlike is a comparison marker.

💡 Keep a record of all markers & the grammar issue they point at.

Meaning: A Closer Look

The correct answer choice should convey a logical & clear meaning.

Meaning errors can primarily be classified into:

  1. Choose your words
  2. Place your words
  3. Match your words

Choose Your Words

Is the right word being used for the right meaning?

Vocabulary Based

E.g:
✅ “My decision to drive a hybrid car was motivated by ECONOMIC considerations.”

❌ “ECONOMICAL considerations motivated my decision to drive a hybrid car.”

Economical mean “thrifty, efficient” here. It’s not distant from the original meaning economic considerations- that is, monetary considerations.

Some Common Word Discrepancies
Aggravate (Worsen)Aggravating (Irritating)
known as (named)known to be (acknowledged as)
loss of (no longer in possession of)loss in (decline in value)
mandate (command)have a mandate (have authority from voters)
native of (person of)native to (species that originated in)
range of (variety of)ranging (varying)
rate of (speed/frequency of)rates for (prices for)
rise (general increase)raise (a bet/ salary increase)
try to do (seek to accomplish)try doing (experiment with)

May, Will, Must, Should

These express the various levels of certainty, obligation & reality. The meaning changes entirely on swapping these words with each other.

Eg:

✅ The court ruled that the plaintiff MUST pay full damages.

❌ The court ruled that the plaintiff SHOULD pay full damages.

A court never suggests its final decision. It always gives a ruling which is strictly followed by everyone. But the “should” in the second sentence makes the decision a suggestion.

🚨 On the GMAT, “SHOULD” → Moral Obligation

Place Your Words

Placement of some words can change the entire meaning of a sentence.

Eg:

“ALL the children are covered in mud.”

”The children are ALL covered in mud.”

Here, the intent shifts from “how many” children (all of them) to “how much” the children were covered in mud (all over).

💡 Word Place Change ⇒ Check for possible meaning change The correct word placement should resolve all sentence ambiguity.

Eg:

❌ The council granted the right to make legal petitions TO CITY OFFICIALS.

✅ The council granted CITY OFFICIALS the right to make legal petitions.

✅ The right to make legal petitions TO CITY OFFICIALS was granted by the council.

Match Your Words

Sentences contain pairs of words or phrases that must match.

Eg: ❌ “Unlike Alaska, where the winter is quite cold, the temperature in Florida rarely goes below freezing.”

Here the state Alaska is being compared with the temperature in Florida. It’s illogical to compare a state to the temperature in another state.

💡 Connected words must always make sense together.

Avoiding Redundancy

Words which can be removed without changing the sentence meaning should be removed.

Words with same meaning

Most common redundancy trap.

❌ The value of the stock ROSE by a 10% INCREASE.

✅ The value of the stock INCREASED by 10%.

✅ The value of the stock ROSE by 10%.

Expressions of time

PASTPreviouslyFormerlyIn the pastBefore now
PRESENTNowCurrentlyPresentlyAt present
YEARLYAnnualEach yearA year (3 launches a year)

These are the most sneaky. It is easy to sneak in 2 redundant time expressions.

💡 One of the expressions being in the non-underlined part are specially sneaky.

But there can be representations, provided the meaning supports it.

Contrast Words

These are also sneaky since they are quite spaced apart.

❌ “Although she studied night and day for three months, yet she did not do well on her exam.”

Solutions

Q1

She is the most dedicated gardener on the block, watering the more than 50 plants in her yard every day. (watering more than the 50 plants in her yard every day)

(D)

The original version contains the phrase the more than 50 plants. Here the words more than modify the number 50. The sentence therefore means that she waters her plants, of which there are more than 50. In the boldface version, the phrase changes to watering more than the 50 plants. Here the words more than are separated from the number 50, and therefore do not modify that number. The new version says that she waters something more than (i.e., in addition to) the plants-for instance, she might water her gravel walkway or her garden gnomes or the neighbours’ plants. Both versions potentially make sense (though one implies she is a very dedicated gardener), so either version could be correct.

Q2

Students at Carver High School are encouraged to pursue only those extracurricular activities from which stems success in college applications. (success in college applications stems)

(D)

Both versions are correct!

The original sentence inverts the normal order of subject (success) and verb (stems = “originates”), but this inversion is possible after which or in similar positions. There is no change in meaning.

Q3

Hector remembers San Francisco as it was when he left 10 years ago. (as though he had left ten years ago)

(D)

The boldface version changes to the original version, but the new version could make sense.

  • The original sentence says that Hector actually DID leave San Francisco 10 years ago.
  • The revised version says that he did NOT leave San Francisco 10 years ago: the expression as though is used to discuss things that are untrue or did not happen (You behave as though you were richer than Bill Gates!).
  • Another important change in meaning comes because the revised version takes out the words it (i.e., San Francisco) was, and therefore does not refer directly to the state of affairs in San Francisco 10 years ago.

Practice-II

Fix the following four sentences. Look for issues outlined in the above sections.

  1. After the test format was changed, scores subsequently dropped by more than a 25% decrease.
Solution

Redundancy (Same Meaning Words): “dropped” & “decrease”.

After the test format was changed, scores subsequently dropped by more than 25%.

  1. Though canals have experienced a severe decline in barge traffic over the past several decades, yet with the rise in fuel costs, “shipping” by actual ships may once again become an important means of transporting goods within the country.
Solution

Redundancy (Contrast Words): “Though” & “yet”

Canals have experienced a severe decline in barge traffic over the past several decades, yet with the rise in fuel costs, “shipping” by actual ships may once again become an important means of transporting goods within the country.
