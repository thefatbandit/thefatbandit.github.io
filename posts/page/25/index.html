5.1 Introduction to Resolve the Paradox Questions

Lesson Content A Resolve the Paradox question is based on a passage that presents an apparent paradox, which, in GMAT CR, is a situation in which there exist two facts that seemingly contradict each other or that seemingly cannot both be true at the same time. In other words, a paradox is composed of two seemingly incompatible statements of fact; the facts, at least without more information, don’t seem to make sense together.

September 10, 2022 Read
5.2 Common Resolve the Paradox Question Stems

Lesson Content The faster we can recognize what a Critical Reasoning question is asking us to find, the faster we can answer it. Here are some of the most common question stems you’ll see: Which of the following, if true, most helps to resolve the apparent discrepancy in the passage above? Which of the following, if true, would explain the discrepancy described above? Which one of the following, if true, does most to justify this apparently paradoxical belief?

5.4 Strategy for Answering Resolve the Paradox Questions

Lesson Content To answer Resolve the Paradox questions correctly, follow this 5-step strategy. Step 1 – Carefully read and completely understand the passage. As is the case in answering any other type of Critical Reasoning question, the foundation of choosing the right answer to a Paradox question is understanding the passage. Step 2 – Read the question stem and identify what it is asking you to do. The nature of the information presented in the passage, along with the question stem, should make it easy to tell that you’re faced with a Paradox question.

5.5 In answering a Paradox question, we must understand exactly what the paradox is that we must resolve.

Lesson Content Precision in reading is one of the most important Critical Reasoning skills to master. 💡MUST KNOW: In Resolve the Paradox questions, every word matters. Accordingly, when test-takers get Paradox questions incorrect, they often do so because, when reading the passage of a Paradox question, they do not develop a precise understanding of what the paradox is that they need to resolve. In answering a Paradox question, we must have a precise understanding of exactly what the paradox is that we must resolve.

5.6 Don’t Look for a Conclusion in a Resolve the Paradox Question.

Lesson Content As we answer a Paradox question, there is no need to look for a conclusion either in the stimulus or in the correct answer, because the author is not making an argument in the way in which he or she does in a Strengthen or Weaken question. 💡MUST KNOW: We don’t need to find a conclusion to correctly answer a Resolve the Paradox question. So, don’t look for one!

5.7 In Answering Resolve the Paradox Questions, Look for What Could be True, Not What Must be True.

Lesson Content For any Resolve the Paradox question, there will likely be many possible ways in which we could resolve the paradox. 💡MUST KNOW: For any Resolve the Paradox question, there will likely be many possible ways in which we could resolve the paradox. Our job is just to find one of them. We’re not trying to find the one answer that must be true. 💡MUST KNOW: In answering a Resolve the Paradox question, we’re looking for an answer that could be true.
