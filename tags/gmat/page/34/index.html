12.5 Boldface Method of Reasoning Questions

Lesson Content Some Method of Reasoning Questions present us with stimuli that include one or two sections that are in boldface type. 💡MUST KNOW: Some Method of Reasoning Questions present us with stimuli that include one or two sections that are in boldface type. In answering such a question, our job is to identify the role that any section in boldfaced type plays in the argument. Thus, in Boldface Method of Reasoning questions, the correct answer is the one that accurately describes the role, or roles, of any sections in boldface.

12.5.2 Identifying Conclusions in order to Efficiently and Correctly Answer Boldface Questions

Lesson Content The stimuli of Boldface questions can be hodgepodges of background information, premises, conclusions, and information of other types, and often it’s not obvious what roles the various parts of the passages play. So, what’s the key to determining what roles the statements in a Boldface stimulus play? The key is finding any conclusions that appear in the passage, and in particular, the main conclusion. The roles of all the statements in an argument tend to depend on how the statements are related to the conclusion of the argument.

12.5.5 The conclusions are inconspicuous.

Lesson Content In most of the Critical Reasoning arguments that we have seen, the conclusions have appeared at or near to the ends of the passages, and the fact that they are conclusions has often been signaled by words such as “Therefore” or “Clearly.” This pattern does not hold for most arguments seen in Boldface questions. 💡MUST KNOW: Often, in the arguments used in Boldface questions, main conclusions are not preceded by conclusion signaling words like “therefore,” and the conclusions are worded so as to be barely noticeable.

Lesson Content Another way in which the creators of Boldface questions trick test-takers by taking advantage of common perceptions of how arguments work is by making the boldfaced portions look as if they play roles other than the roles that they play. 💡MUST KNOW: In the passages in Boldface questions, the portions in boldface can be worded and positioned to appear to play roles other than the roles that they play.
