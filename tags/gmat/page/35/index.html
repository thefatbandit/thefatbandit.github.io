12.5.7 The passages often contain multiple conclusions, along with premises to support the conclusions.

Lesson Content Another factor that can make sorting through the statements in a Boldface question and keeping everything straight challenging is the presence of multiple conclusions and the premises that support them. Differentiating between premises and conclusions. Determining which conclusions are supported by the arguments and which ones the arguments oppose. Determining which premises support which conclusion. Consider the following example: There is a common perception that, because higher than normal water temperatures can kill off coral reefs, and because climate change related emissions are causing increases in ocean water temperatures, other than reducing climate change causing emissions, there is not much that can be done to save reefs.

12.5.8 Incorrect answer choices often say things that are partly correct or closely related to what is correct.

Lesson Content As we just discussed, sorting out the roles of the statements in Boldface question passages can be complicated by the fact that there can be multiple conclusions with premises that support each of them. For instance, if a portion in boldface is a premise that supports a conclusion opposed by the main conclusion of the argument, an answer choice that says that that boldfaced portion is itself a conclusion opposed by the conclusion of the argument could be a tempting trap.

SC-4 Modifiers

A modifier describes/provides extra information about something else in the sentence. Incorrectly used modifiers lead to ambiguity or illogical meaning. 💡 Many modifiers answer the questions who, what, when, where, or why Adjectives & Adverbs These are one-word modifiers. Adjective → ONLY Noun/Pronoun Adverb → Almost anything EXCEPT Noun/Pronoun Verbs, Adjectives Prepositional Phrases Entire Clauses Many adverbs are formed by adding “-ly” to the adjective 💡 Often Adjective ↔ Adverb use will be interchanged as the sentence error.

CR-9 Assumption

An assumption is simply an unstated premise that must be true in order for the argument to be true. It is an integral component of the argument that the author takes for granted and leaves unsaid. 💡 The answer to an assumption question must be something that the author must believe in order for the conclusion to be properly drawn. The assumptions are minimal & can’t contain extraneous statements Diff. b/w Strengthen & Assumption Questions Strengthen questions ask us to support the argument in any way possible.

CR-8 Strengthen

These questions ask you to identify the answer choice that best supports the argument. The correct answer choice does not necessarily justify the arguments, nor is the correct answer choice necessarily an assumption of the argument. 💡 The correct answer choice simply helps the argument in some way. Rules to Solve (Same as Weaken) The stimulus will always contain an argument since you are asked to weaken the author’s reasoning. Focus on the conclusion.

CR-7 Cause and Effect Reasoning

Cause & effect reasoning asserts/denies whether one thing causes another, or, is caused by another. 💡 Most tested logical concept in GMAT CR stimuli. What is Causality? Causality occurs when one event is said to make another occur. The cause is the event that makes the other occur; the effect is the event that follows from the cause. By definition, the cause must occur before the effect. Recognizing Causality Cause & Effect relationship has a signature characteristics- a single cause makes the effect happen.

CR-6 Weaken

Weaken type question require you to select the answer choice that undermines the author’s argument as decisively as possible. 💡 MOST FREQUENT CR QUESTION TYPE Rules to Solve The stimulus will always contain an argument since you are asked to weaken the author’s reasoning. Focus on the conclusion. The more we know about its specifics, the better armed we will be to deciding the correct answer choice. Information in the stimulus is suspect.

CR-4 Must Be True

Must Be True Must by True questions require you to select an answer choice that is proven by the stimulus information. The correct answer choice maybe: Paraphrase of the stimulus Logical consequence of one or more parts of the stimulus Fact Test The correct answer choice can always be proven by referring to the facts stated in the stimulus. Any answer choice we select should not be selected on the basis that it “sounds good”.
