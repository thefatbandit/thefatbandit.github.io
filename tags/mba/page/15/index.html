7.7.2 Whatever Precedes the First Part of a “Not Only … But Also” Structure Carries Over to the Second Part.

Lesson Content Whatever precedes the first part of a “not only … but also” structure is understood to carry over to the second part of the structure, and thus does not have to be repeated in order for a complete, logical thought to be expressed. 💡MUST KNOW: Whatever precedes the first part of a “not only … but also” correlative structure is understood to carry over to, without being written in, the second part of the correlative structure.

Lesson Content In the last section, we saw that an essential element that precedes “not only” is understood to carry over, without being written, to what comes after “but also.” In contrast, an element that comes directly after “not only” does not carry over to after “but also.” So, if any such element is necessary after “but also,” it must be repeated. 💡MUST KNOW: In a “not only … but also” structure, elements that follow “not only” are not understood to carry over to after “but also.
