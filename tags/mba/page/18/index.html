1.2 The Basic Layout of Critical Reasoning Questions

Lesson Content Each Critical Reasoning question begins with a written stimulus, which is usually, though not always, in the form of a short passage of about 100 words or fewer. 💡MUST KNOW: Each Critical Reasoning question begins with a written stimulus, usually in the form of a short passage of about 100 words or fewer. Accompanying the stimulus is a question and five answer choices. The question will always ask us to determine which of the five answer choices is logically related to the stimulus in a particular way.

1.11 What to Do If you Have Eliminated All of the Answer Choices

Lesson Content Sometimes we find that we have eliminated all the answer choices. 💡MUST KNOW: If you have eliminated all the answer choices, simply take a moment to be sure that you have properly understood the passage. Then go back through the answer choices and carefully reevaluate them. This time through you need to really bear down on each answer choice and rigorously evaluate its merits. By continuing to consider the differences between the choices and by going back to the passage to find details that you missed in previous readings, you’ll find that one choice that is the correct answer to the question.
