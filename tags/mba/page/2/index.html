1.16 Be Careful of Falling Into Answer Choice (E).

Lesson Content Often, in answering Sentence Correction questions, people fall into choosing choice (E), the last answer choice, without a clear reason to choose choice (E). As you can imagine, falling into choosing choice (E) in this way often results in a person’s getting a question incorrect. To be clear, answer choice (E) will be correct just as frequently as the other four answer choices. However, don’t choose (E) just because you haven’t decided that another choice is correct, and (E) is the only one left.

2.3 Independent Clauses

Lesson Content There are two major hallmarks of an independent clause: · An independent clause contains both a subject and a finite verb. · An independent clause can stand alone as a sentence. 💡MUST KNOW: An independent clause contains a subject and a finite verb and can stand alone as a sentence. Why are independent clauses important? Well, every English sentence must have at least one independent clause, and the correct version of a sentence in a Sentence Correction question always contains at least one independent clause.

2.5 The Subordinate Clause

Lesson Content Consider one of our last examples of an independent clause: The crowd laughed. The clause has a subject and a finite verb - all that is needed, in this instance, for a complete sentence. Incorrect: Because the crowd laughed. Although you may not know exactly what is missing here, it’s almost certainly clear to you that we don’t have a sentence. “Because” is a member of a class of words called subordinating conjunctions, words that can take away the independence of a clause when placed at the head of the clause.
