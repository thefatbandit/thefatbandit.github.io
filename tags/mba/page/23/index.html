3.9.2 Choices That are Pseudo-Weakeners

Lesson Content In a Weaken question, along with the correct answer choice, which actually weakens the argument, there can be one or more pseudo-weakener answer choices, which may give the impression that they weaken the argument but don’t really get the job done. Consider the following example: The football coach at Verdantville high school wants the players on the team to gain weight. So, he is providing the players with all the chocolate bars that they can eat.

3.9.3 Frameshifts – Choices that Weaken the Support for the Wrong Conclusion

Lesson Content Consider an argument that supports Conclusion X. When a frameshift occurs, an incorrect answer choice will cast doubt on a conclusion that is related to or very similar to Conclusion X but is not Conclusion X. If we’re not careful, it’s quite easy to fall for a frameshift because of the similarity between the actual conclusion of the argument and the conclusion called into question by the incorrect choice.

3.9.4 Pertinent but Incorrect Choices

Lesson Content Some of the most tempting incorrect choices in Weaken questions are choices that mention things that one would likely consider in a real-world review of a situation. At the same time, while these answer choices may say things worthy of consideration, they do not weaken the arguments that they follow. 💡MUST KNOW: We must be careful not to be tempted into choosing an answer choice that says something that is valid and is pertinent to what the passage discusses but does not affect the argument.

3.9.5 Answer Choices That Appear to Attack Premises

Lesson Content As we have discussed, the correct answer to a Weaken the Argument question will never be one that attacks a premise of the argument, because premises in GMAT arguments are taken to be true and therefore cannot be undermined. 💡MUST KNOW: Premises in GMAT arguments are taken to be true and therefore cannot be undermined. Therefore, an answer choice that says something that appears to undermine a premise will not weaken the argument.

3.10 Tricky Weaken Question Stems: EXCEPT and NOT

Lesson Content Weaken the Argument questions will sometimes include the word “except” or “not” in their question stems to make the questions a bit more challenging. Any of the following, if true, undermines support for the speaker’s claim EXCEPT: Which of the following, if true, does not weaken the argument above? When a Weaken question has the word “except” or “not” in the stem, four out of the five answer choices will weaken the argument.

Lesson Content To find the correct answer to a Strengthen question, follow this 6-step strategy. Step 1 – Carefully read and understand the passage. 4.6 Provide a Premise to Support or Add Support for the Conclusion

Lesson Content In some Strengthen the Argument questions, the passages contain conclusions without any supporting premises. 💡MUST KNOW: In some Strengthen the Argument questions, the passages contain conclusions without any supporting premises. In other questions, the premises within the arguments only partially support the conclusions. In any of these cases, an effective way for an answer choice to strengthen the argument is to provide a premise, which will either provide the only support for the conclusion or provide further support for the conclusion alongside existing premises.
