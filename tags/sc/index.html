1.1 Introduction

Lesson Content In the verbal section of the GMAT, between 9 and 13 of the 36 questions will be Sentence Correction questions. In this course, we will cover all of the concepts, strategies, and skills that you’ll need to correctly answer every Sentence Correction question you see on test day. 1.2 The Basic Layout of Sentence Correction Questions

Lesson Content Each Sentence Correction question presents us with a sentence, and all or part of the sentence is underlined. The first answer choice, answer choice (A), offers phrasing that is a repeat of the underlined portion of the original sentence. 💡MUST KNOW: In Sentence Correction questions, the first answer choice offers phrasing that is a repeat of underlined portion of the original sentence. Each of the other four choices presents an alternative way of wording the underlined portion of the sentence.

1.4 When Answering Sentence Correction Questions, Read the Sentence Versions Holistically.

Lesson Content To consistently succeed in identifying issues in the sentence versions in Sentence Correction questions, we have to read the sentences in their entirety, or holistically. 💡MUST KNOW: To consistently succeed in identifying issues in the sentence versions in Sentence Correction questions, we have to read the sentences in their entirety, or holistically. 1.5 How To Approach Sentence Correction Questions

Lesson Content When answering a Sentence Correction question, begin by reading the entire original sentence to identify whether there are any clear errors of grammar or structure present. If the original version seems to be grammatically correct, then we keep choice (A) as a contender and move on to the other choices. 💡MUST KNOW: When we are answering a Sentence Correction question, if the original version seems to be grammatically correct, then we keep choice (A) as a contender and move on to the other choices.

1.7 The Non-Underlined Portion of the Sentence is just as Important as the Answer Choices.

Lesson Content Don’t for a second think that our job is to evaluate only the portion of the sentence that appears in the answer choices. In fact, often when a person falls into a Sentence Correction trap, she does so because she failed to carefully consider the non-underlined portion of the sentence. 💡MUST KNOW: When answering a Sentence Correction question, we must carefully consider both the underlined and the non-underlined portions of a sentence.

1.9 Looking for Strong Decision Points When Choosing Among Sentence Correction Answer Choices

Lesson Content In most incorrect answer choices in Sentence Correction questions, there are multiple flaws. 💡MUST KNOW: In most incorrect answer choices in Sentence Correction questions, there are multiple flaws. So, if we are having trouble deciding whether one characteristic of a sentence choice renders that choice incorrect, rather than spend time wondering about that one characteristic, we can look for a stronger or clearer reason to eliminate that choice.
