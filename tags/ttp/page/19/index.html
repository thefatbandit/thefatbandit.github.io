1.15 Do Not Skip Words When Reading Critical Reasoning Passages, Question Stems, or Answer Choices in an Attempt to Save Time.

Lesson Content Often, because they want to save time, or simply because they don’t feel like reading every word, test-takers skip or gloss over words in Critical Reasoning passages, question stems, or answer choices or decide not to read the last several words of a passage, question stem, or answer choice. In fact, what often happens is that a test-taker who has skipped words in a Critical Reasoning question flails around and repeatedly goes through the answer choices without finding the correct answer because, in skipping those words, the test-taker has failed to acquire the information necessary for answering the question.

1.18 We Don’t Need Specialized, Outside Knowledge to Answer Critical Reasoning Questions

Lesson Content Correctly answering Critical Reasoning questions does not require any outside, specialized knowledge. 💡MUST KNOW: Correctly answering Critical Reasoning questions does not require any outside, specialized knowledge. Any outside information necessary for answering these questions is considered “common knowledge” among people with common sense and a basic level of education. In other words, yes, we will encounter passages on economics, science, music theory, politics, and psychology, to name a few topics, but any outside information we’ll need to answer Critical Reasoning questions will be information that is considered generally known, and widely accepted, by the general population.
