5.8.3 Paradox Incorrect Choice Type 3: A Choice that Makes the Situation More Paradoxical

Lesson Content Be on the lookout for choices that actually make the situation described even more paradoxical. 💡MUST KNOW: Beware of choices that actually make the situation described even more paradoxical, that deepen the paradox. A situation can be made more paradoxical by information making the two facts appear to be more contradictory or by the addition of what seems to be more conflict to the scenario presented. Remember, our job is to resolve the paradox, not to add to it.

5.8.4 Paradox Incorrect Choice Type 4: A Choice that Seems to Contradict One of the Two Facts

Lesson Content In answering Resolve the Paradox questions, our job is not to call into question the credibility of any statement, nor is it to reinforce the credibility of any statement. 💡MUST KNOW: In answering Resolve the Paradox questions, we accept the statements in the passages as true, even though they apparently conflict. Accordingly, we never resolve the paradox in a Paradox question by challenging one of the facts stated in the passage.

5.8.5 Paradox Incorrect Choice Type 5: A Choice That Introduces Information That Has No Effect On The Paradox

Lesson Content Among the answer choices in a Paradox question can be choices that have no effect on the paradox presented by the stimulus. 💡MUST KNOW: Information that has no effect on the paradox will never resolve the paradox. To tell the difference between incorrect choices that have no effect on the paradox and the correct answer, we have to carefully consider what effect each choice has on the scenario presented in the stimulus and be careful not to make up convoluted stories that give answer choices effects that they don’t really have.

6.2 The Assumptions Inherent to All Cause-and-Effect Arguments

Lesson Content As we learned in prior chapters, when an author makes a cause-and-effect claim, she assumes that her cause-and-effect claim is actually correct; she assumes that there could not be an alternative explanation for the observed events.
